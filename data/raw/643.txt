<p><b>John Henry Bonham</b> (31 May 1948 – 25 September 1980) was an English musician and songwriter, best known as the drummer of <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a>. Bonham was esteemed for his speed, power, fast bass-drumming, distinctive sound, and "feel" for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Groove_(popular_music)" title="Groove (popular music)">groove</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> He is considered one of the greatest rock drummers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moderndrummer.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-moderndrummer.com-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Greatest_Drummers_Of_All_Time.21_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Greatest_Drummers_Of_All_Time.21-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> readers named him "best drummer of all time" in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RS_reader.27s_poll_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-RS_reader.27s_poll-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Bonham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Bonham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bonham was born on 31 May 1948, in <a href="/wiki/Redditch" title="Redditch">Redditch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Worcestershire" title="Worcestershire">Worcestershire</a>, England, to Joan and Jack Bonham. He began learning to play at five, making a kit of containers and coffee tins, imitating his idols <a href="/wiki/Max_Roach" title="Max Roach">Max Roach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gene_Krupa" title="Gene Krupa">Gene Krupa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Buddy_Rich" title="Buddy Rich">Buddy Rich</a>. His mother gave him a snare drum at the age of ten. He received his first drum kit from his father at 15, a <a href="/wiki/Premier_Percussion" title="Premier Percussion">Premier Percussion</a> set. Bonham never took formal drum lessons, though as a teen he had advice from other Redditch drummers. Between 1962 and 1963, still at school, Bonham joined the Blue Star Trio,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> and Gerry Levene &amp; the Avengers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Bonham attended Lodge Farm Secondary Modern School, where his headmaster wrote in his report that "He will either end up a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dustman" title="Dustman">dustman</a> or a millionaire."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-d.26c_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-d.26c-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> After leaving school in 1964, he worked for his father as an apprentice carpenter<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> between drumming for local bands. In 1964, Bonham joined his first semi-professional band, Terry Webb and the Spiders, and met his future wife Pat Phillips around the same time. He played in other <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a> bands such as The Nicky James Movement and The Senators, who made a single, "She's a <a href="/wiki/Mod_(subculture)" title="Mod (subculture)">Mod</a>," in 1964. Bonham then took up drumming full time. Two years later, he joined A Way of Life, but the band folded. Needing a regular income, he joined a <a href="/wiki/Blues" title="Blues">blues</a> group called Crawling King Snakes, whose lead singer was <a href="/wiki/Robert_Plant" title="Robert Plant">Robert Plant</a>.</p>
<p>In 1967, A Way of Life asked Bonham to return to the group, and he agreed, while keeping in touch with Plant. Plant formed <a href="/wiki/Band_of_Joy" title="Band of Joy">Band of Joy</a> and chose Bonham as drummer. The band recorded demos but no album. In 1968, American singer <a href="/wiki/Tim_Rose" title="Tim Rose">Tim Rose</a> toured Britain and asked <a href="/wiki/Band_of_Joy" title="Band of Joy">Band of Joy</a> to open his concerts. When Rose returned months later, Bonham was invited by the singer to drum for Rose's band, which gave him a regular income.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Led_Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Bonham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Led Zeppelin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a></div>
<p>After break-up of <a href="/wiki/The_Yardbirds" title="The Yardbirds">The Yardbirds</a>, guitarist <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Page" title="Jimmy Page">Jimmy Page</a> formed another band and recruited Plant, who in turn suggested Bonham. Page's choices for drummer included <a href="/wiki/Procol_Harum" title="Procol Harum">Procol Harum</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B.J._Wilson" title="B.J. Wilson">B.J. Wilson</a>, and session drummers <a href="/wiki/Clem_Cattini" title="Clem Cattini">Clem Cattini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aynsley_Dunbar" title="Aynsley Dunbar">Aynsley Dunbar</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ginger_Baker" title="Ginger Baker">Ginger Baker</a> was also rumoured to be on Page's list. However, on seeing Bonham drum for <a href="/wiki/Tim_Rose" title="Tim Rose">Tim Rose</a> at a club in <a href="/wiki/Hampstead" title="Hampstead">Hampstead</a>, north London, in July 1968, Page and manager <a href="/wiki/Peter_Grant_(music_manager)" title="Peter Grant (music manager)">Peter Grant</a> were convinced he was perfect for the project, first known as the New Yardbirds and later as Led Zeppelin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Snow1990_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-Snow1990-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Bonham was reluctant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amgbio_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-amgbio-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Plant sent eight <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telegram" title="Telegram">telegrams</a> to Bonham's pub, the "Three Men in a Boat", in <a href="/wiki/Walsall" title="Walsall">Walsall</a>, which were followed by 40 telegrams from Grant. Bonham was also receiving offers from <a href="/wiki/Joe_Cocker" title="Joe Cocker">Joe Cocker</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Farlowe" title="Chris Farlowe">Chris Farlowe</a> but he accepted Grant's offer. He recalled, "I decided I liked their music better than Cocker's or Farlowe's."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>During Led Zeppelin's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin_North_American_Tour_1968/1969" title="Led Zeppelin North American Tour 1968/1969">first tour of the United States</a> in December 1968, Bonham became friends with <a href="/wiki/Vanilla_Fudge" title="Vanilla Fudge">Vanilla Fudge's</a> drummer, <a href="/wiki/Carmine_Appice" title="Carmine Appice">Carmine Appice</a>. Appice introduced him to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ludwig_drums" title="Ludwig drums">Ludwig drums</a>, which he then used for the rest of his career. Bonham used the longest and heaviest sticks, which he called "trees." His hard hitting was on many Led Zeppelin songs, including "<a href="/wiki/Immigrant_Song" title="Immigrant Song">Immigrant Song</a>" (<i><a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin_III" title="Led Zeppelin III">Led Zeppelin III</a></i>), "<a href="/wiki/When_the_Levee_Breaks" title="When the Levee Breaks">When the Levee Breaks</a>" (<i><a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin_IV" title="Led Zeppelin IV">Led Zeppelin IV</a></i> / <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Zoso.svg"><img alt="Zoso.svg" data-file-height="172" data-file-width="744" height="16" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Zoso.svg/70px-Zoso.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Zoso.svg/105px-Zoso.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Zoso.svg/140px-Zoso.svg.png 2x" width="70"/></a>), "<a href="/wiki/Kashmir_(song)" title="Kashmir (song)">Kashmir</a>" (<i><a href="/wiki/Physical_Graffiti" title="Physical Graffiti">Physical Graffiti</a></i>), "<a href="/wiki/The_Ocean_(Led_Zeppelin_song)" title="The Ocean (Led Zeppelin song)">The Ocean</a>" (<i><a href="/wiki/Houses_of_the_Holy" title="Houses of the Holy">Houses of the Holy</a></i>), and "<a href="/wiki/Achilles_Last_Stand" title="Achilles Last Stand">Achilles Last Stand</a>" (<i><a href="/wiki/Presence_(album)" title="Presence (album)">Presence</a></i>). Page let Bonham use a double bass drum in an early demo of "<a href="/wiki/Communication_Breakdown" title="Communication Breakdown">Communication Breakdown</a>" but scratched the track because of Bonham's "over-use" of it. The studio recording of "<a href="/wiki/Misty_Mountain_Hop" title="Misty Mountain Hop">Misty Mountain Hop</a>" captures his <a href="/wiki/Dynamics_(music)" title="Dynamics (music)">dynamics</a>, similarly exhibited on "<a href="/wiki/No_Quarter_(song)" title="No Quarter (song)">No Quarter</a>". On cuts from later albums, Bonham handled <a href="/wiki/Funk" title="Funk">funk</a> and <a href="/wiki/Latin_American_music" title="Latin American music">Latin</a>-influenced drumming. Songs like "<a href="/wiki/Royal_Orleans" title="Royal Orleans">Royal Orleans</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Fool_in_the_Rain" title="Fool in the Rain">Fool in the Rain</a>" are examples, respectively displaying a New Orleans shuffle and a samba.</p>
<p>His drum solo, first entitled "Pat's Delight," later "<a href="/wiki/Moby_Dick_(instrumental)" title="Moby Dick (instrumental)">Moby Dick</a>", often lasted 30 minutes. He used bare hands for different sounds. Bonham's sequence for the film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Song_Remains_the_Same_(film)" title="The Song Remains the Same (film)">The Song Remains the Same</a></i>, featured him in a drag race at <a href="/wiki/Santa_Pod_Raceway" title="Santa Pod Raceway">Santa Pod Raceway</a> to the sound of his solo, "Moby Dick". In Led Zeppelin tours after 1969, Bonham included <a href="/wiki/Conga" title="Conga">congas</a>, orchestral <a href="/wiki/Timpani" title="Timpani">timpani</a> and a symphonic <a href="/wiki/Gong" title="Gong">gong</a>. He was is credited by the <i><a href="/wiki/Dallas_Times_Herald" title="Dallas Times Herald">Dallas Times Herald</a></i> with the first concert use of electronic timpani drum synthesisers during "<a href="/wiki/Kashmir_(song)" title="Kashmir (song)">Kashmir</a>" in <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, Texas, in 1977.</p>
<p>Bonham collected antique sports cars and motorcycles, which he kept on his family's farm, The Old Hyde. He bought the Plough pub in nearby <a href="/wiki/Shenstone,_Worcestershire" title="Shenstone, Worcestershire">Shenstone</a>, near Kidderminster, which was converted to allow him to drive his bikes or cars right behind the bar.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Work_outside_Led_Zeppelin">Work outside Led Zeppelin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Bonham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Work outside Led Zeppelin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1969 Bonham appeared on <a href="/wiki/The_Family_Dogg" title="The Family Dogg">The Family Dogg</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/A_Way_of_Life_(album)" title="A Way of Life (album)">A Way of Life</a></i>, with Page and Jones. Bonham also played for <a href="/wiki/Screaming_Lord_Sutch" title="Screaming Lord Sutch">Screaming Lord Sutch</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/Lord_Sutch_and_Heavy_Friends" title="Lord Sutch and Heavy Friends">Lord Sutch and Heavy Friends</a></i> in 1970. He played on <a href="/wiki/Lulu_(singer)" title="Lulu (singer)">Lulu</a>'s 1971 song "Everybody Clap," written by <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Gibb" title="Maurice Gibb">Maurice Gibb</a> and Billy Lawrie. In 1972, he played on a Maurice Gibb-produced album by <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Stevens_(musician)" title="Jimmy Stevens (musician)">Jimmy Stevens</a> called <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Freak_Me_Out" title="Don't Freak Me Out">Don't Freak Me Out</a></i> in the UK and <i>Paid My Dues</i> in the US, credited as "Gemini" (his star sign). He drummed for his <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a> friend, <a href="/wiki/Roy_Wood" title="Roy Wood">Roy Wood</a>, on his 1979 album, <i>On the Road Again</i>, and on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wings_(band)" title="Wings (band)">Wings</a>' album <i><a href="/wiki/Back_to_the_Egg" title="Back to the Egg">Back to the Egg</a></i> on the tracks "<a href="/wiki/Rockestra_Theme" title="Rockestra Theme">Rockestra Theme</a>" and "So Glad to See You Here". Bonham was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wedding_ceremony_participants#Best_man" title="Wedding ceremony participants">best man</a> of <a href="/wiki/Black_Sabbath" title="Black Sabbath">Black Sabbath's</a> <a href="/wiki/Tony_Iommi" title="Tony Iommi">Tony Iommi</a> at his wedding ceremony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1974, Bonham appeared in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Son_of_Dracula_(1974_film)" title="Son of Dracula (1974 film)"><i>Son of Dracula</i></a></i>, playing drums in Count Downe's (<a href="/wiki/Harry_Nilsson" title="Harry Nilsson">Harry Nilsson</a>) band. This was an <a href="/wiki/Apple_Corps" title="Apple Corps">Apple</a> film directed by <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Francis" title="Freddie Francis">Freddie Francis</a>. Bonham appeared in a drum line-up including <a href="/wiki/Keith_Moon" title="Keith Moon">Keith Moon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ringo_Starr" title="Ringo Starr">Ringo Starr</a> on the soundtrack album.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death">Death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Bonham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 24 September 1980, Bonham was picked up by Led Zeppelin assistant Rex King to attend rehearsals at <a href="/wiki/Bray_Studios_(UK)" title="Bray Studios (UK)">Bray Studios</a> for a tour of North America, to begin 17 October in Montreal, Canada — the band's first since 1977. During the journey, Bonham asked to stop for breakfast, where he drank four quadruple <a href="/wiki/Vodka" title="Vodka">vodkas</a> (16 <a href="/wiki/Shot_glass" title="Shot glass">shots</a>, between 400–560 ml). He then drank after arriving at rehearsals. The band stopped rehearsing late in the evening and the band retired to Page's house, the Old Mill House in <a href="/wiki/Clewer" title="Clewer">Clewer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Berkshire" title="Windsor, Berkshire">Windsor</a>. After midnight on 25 September, Bonham fell asleep; someone took him to bed and placed him on his side. Benji LeFevre, Led Zeppelin's tour manager, and John Paul Jones found him dead the next afternoon.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Welch_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Welch-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Bonham was 32.</p>
<p>The inquest on 27 October 1980 showed that in 24 hours Bonham had drunk around 40 shots (1–1.4 litres) of 40% <a href="/wiki/Alcohol_by_volume" title="Alcohol by volume">ABV</a> vodka, after which he vomited and choked. The finding was accidental death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Welch_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Welch-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> An <a href="/wiki/Autopsy" title="Autopsy">autopsy</a> found no other drugs in Bonham's body.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Bonham was cremated and his ashes interred on 12 October 1980, at <a href="/wiki/Rushock" title="Rushock">Rushock</a> parish church, <a href="/wiki/Worcestershire" title="Worcestershire">Worcestershire</a>.</p>

<p>The remaining members disbanded Led Zeppelin rather than replace him. They said on 4 December 1980: "We wish it to be known that the loss of our dear friend and the deep respect we have for his family, together with the sense of undivided harmony felt by ourselves and our manager, have led us to decide that we could not continue as we were." It was signed "Led Zeppelin".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mojo2_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Mojo2-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Family">Family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Bonham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bonham had a younger brother, Mick Bonham (1950-2000), a disc jockey, author and photographer, and a younger sister, <a href="/wiki/Deborah_Bonham" title="Deborah Bonham">Deborah Bonham</a> (born 1962) a singer-songwriter.</p>
<p>Bonham was married to Pat Phillips, and the couple had two children; <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Zo%C3%AB_Bonham&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Zoë Bonham (page does not exist)">Zoë Bonham</a> (born 10 June 1975), is a singer-songwriter who appears at Led Zeppelin conventions, and <a href="/wiki/Jason_Bonham" title="Jason Bonham">Jason Bonham</a> (born 15 July 1966), a drummer who has played with <a href="/wiki/UFO_(band)" title="UFO (band)">UFO</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foreigner_(band)" title="Foreigner (band)">Foreigner</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bonham_(band)" title="Bonham (band)">Bonham</a>. He previously played in <a href="/wiki/Black_Country_Communion" title="Black Country Communion">Black Country Communion</a> with <a href="/wiki/Glenn_Hughes" title="Glenn Hughes">Glenn Hughes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Derek_Sherinian" title="Derek Sherinian">Derek Sherinian</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Joe_Bonamassa" title="Joe Bonamassa">Joe Bonamassa</a>. They recorded an album called <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Country_(album)" title="Black Country (album)">Black Country</a></i> in 2010. On 10 December 2007, he played with <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Ahmet_Ertegun_Tribute_Concert" title="Ahmet Ertegun Tribute Concert">Ahmet Ertegun Tribute Concert</a>, as well as their reunion at the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records_40th_Anniversary" title="Atlantic Records 40th Anniversary">Atlantic Records 40th Anniversary</a> show on 15 May 1988. A 1970 film clip of four-year-old Jason playing drums appears in the <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Song_Remains_the_Same_(film)" title="The Song Remains the Same (film)">The Song Remains the Same</a></i>. Zoë and Jason appeared at the induction ceremony for the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a> in 1995 with the surviving members of Led Zeppelin.</p>
<p>Bonham's mother, Joan, died aged 81 on 10 February 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dead_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-Dead-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> She sang for <a href="/wiki/The_Zimmers" title="The Zimmers">the Zimmers</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> a 40-member band set up as a result of a BBC documentary on the treatment of the elderly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Bonham's cousin, Billy Bonham (born 1950), played keyboards for <a href="/wiki/Terry_Reid" title="Terry Reid">Terry Reid</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ace_Kefford" title="Ace Kefford">Ace Kefford</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Bonham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards_and_accolades">Awards and accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Bonham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Awards and accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2007, <i><a href="/wiki/Stylus_Magazine" title="Stylus Magazine">Stylus</a></i> magazine rated Bonham number 1 of 50 great rock drummers,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stylusmagazine.com_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-stylusmagazine.com-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> as did <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gigwise.com" title="Gigwise.com">Gigwise.com</a></i> in 2008,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Greatest_Drummers_Of_All_Time.21_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-The_Greatest_Drummers_Of_All_Time.21-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> and a <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i> reader's poll where he "led the list by a significant margin" in 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RS_Readers_Poll_2011_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-RS_Readers_Poll_2011-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> Bonham was ranked no. 1 on <i><a href="/wiki/Classic_Rock_(magazine)" title="Classic Rock (magazine)">Classic Rock's</a></i> 2005 list of 50 Greatest Drummers in Rock,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Modern_Drummer" title="Modern Drummer">Modern Drummer</a></i> describes him as "the greatest rock 'n' roll drummer in history."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-moderndrummer.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-moderndrummer.com-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> In September 2008, Bonham topped the <i><a href="/wiki/Blabbermouth.net" title="Blabbermouth.net">Blabbermouth.net</a>'</i>s list of "Rockers fans want brought back to life", ahead of <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Mercury" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Rhythm_(music_magazine)" title="Rhythm (music magazine)">Rhythm</a></i> magazine voted him the greatest drummer ever, topping a readers' poll to determine the "50 greatest drummers of all time" in October 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> At the end of the <a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a> series <i><a href="/wiki/I%27m_in_a_Rock_%27n%27_Roll_Band!" title="I'm in a Rock 'n' Roll Band!">I'm in a Rock 'n' Roll Band!</a></i> on 5 June 2010, Bonham was named best drummer of all time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Bonham has been described by <a href="/wiki/AllMusic" title="AllMusic">AllMusic</a> as one of the most important, well-known and influential drummers in rock.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Adam Budofsky, <a href="/wiki/Managing_editor" title="Managing editor">managing editor</a> of <i>Modern Drummer</i>, writes "If the king of rock 'n' roll was Elvis Presley, then the king of rock drumming was certainly John Bonham."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The surviving members of Led Zeppelin reunited to play <a href="/wiki/Live_Aid" title="Live Aid">Live Aid</a> in 1985 and employed two drummers, <a href="/wiki/Phil_Collins" title="Phil Collins">Phil Collins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Thompson_(drummer)" title="Tony Thompson (drummer)">Tony Thompson</a>, to take his place.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Influence_on_notable_musicians_and_tributes">Influence on notable musicians and tributes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Bonham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Influence on notable musicians and tributes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rock drummers influenced by Bonham include <a href="/wiki/Alex_Van_Halen" title="Alex Van Halen">Alex Van Halen</a> (<a href="/wiki/Van_Halen" title="Van Halen">Van Halen</a>), <a href="/wiki/Joey_Kramer" title="Joey Kramer">Joey Kramer</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Aerosmith" title="Aerosmith">Aerosmith</a>), <a href="/wiki/Lee_Kerslake" title="Lee Kerslake">Lee Kerslake</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Uriah_Heep_(band)" title="Uriah Heep (band)">Uriah Heep</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ozzy_Osbourne" title="Ozzy Osbourne">Ozzy Osbourne</a>), <a href="/wiki/Tony_Thompson_(drummer)" title="Tony Thompson (drummer)">Tony Thompson</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AllMusic_Biography:_Neil_Peart_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-AllMusic_Biography:_Neil_Peart-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Chic_(band)" title="Chic (band)">Chic</a>), <a href="/wiki/Dave_Grohl" title="Dave Grohl">Dave Grohl</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Nirvana_(band)" title="Nirvana (band)">Nirvana</a>, <a href="/wiki/Foo_Fighters" title="Foo Fighters">Foo Fighters</a>), <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Lee" title="Tommy Lee">Tommy Lee</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6tley_Cr%C3%BCe" title="Mötley Crüe">Mötley Crüe</a>), <a href="/wiki/Peter_Criss" title="Peter Criss">Peter Criss</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Kiss_(band)" title="Kiss (band)">Kiss</a>), <a href="/wiki/Chad_Smith" title="Chad Smith">Chad Smith</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Red_Hot_Chili_Peppers" title="Red Hot Chili Peppers">Red Hot Chili Peppers</a>), <a href="/wiki/Dave_Lombardo" title="Dave Lombardo">Dave Lombardo</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Slayer" title="Slayer">Slayer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fant%C3%B4mas_(band)" title="Fantômas (band)">Fantômas</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Brad_Wilk" title="Brad Wilk">Brad Wilk</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Rage_Against_the_Machine" title="Rage Against the Machine">Rage Against the Machine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Black_Sabbath" title="Black Sabbath">Black Sabbath</a>).</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Dave_Grohl" title="Dave Grohl">Dave Grohl</a> said:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>John Bonham played the drums like someone who didn't know what was going to happen next—like he was teetering on the edge of a cliff. No one has come close to that since, and I don't think anybody ever will. I think he will forever be the greatest drummer of all time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Chad Smith remarked:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>To me, hands down, John Bonham was the best rock drummer ever. The style and the sound was so identifiable to one person. Any drum set that he would play, it sounded like him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Other musicians also paid tribute. John Paul Jones said Bonham was a "bass player's dream".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rapallo_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-Rapallo-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> Jimmy Page has also commented:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>One of the marvellous things about John Bonham which made things very easy [for a producer] was the fact that he really knew how to tune his drums, and I tell you what, that was pretty rare in drummers in those days. He really knew how to make the instrument sing, and because of that, he could just get so much volume out of it by just playing with his wrists. It was just an astonishing technique that was sort of pretty holistic if you know what I mean.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NPRPage_41-0"><a href="#cite_note-NPRPage-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>"Bonzo: The Groove Remains the Same—A Night In Honor of John Henry Bonham" was produced by <a href="/wiki/Whitesnake" title="Whitesnake">Whitesnake</a> drummer <a href="/wiki/Brian_Tichy" title="Brian Tichy">Brian Tichy</a> in Los Angeles on 25 September 2010 — the 30th anniversary of his death. Notable drummers that appeared at the tribute included <a href="/wiki/Steven_Adler" title="Steven Adler">Steven Adler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vinny_Appice" title="Vinny Appice">Vinny Appice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Aronoff" title="Kenny Aronoff">Kenny Aronoff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frankie_Banali" title="Frankie Banali">Frankie Banali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fred_Coury" title="Fred Coury">Fred Coury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_D%27Anda" title="Jimmy D'Anda">Jimmy D'Anda</a>, <a href="/wiki/James_Kottak" title="James Kottak">James Kottak</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Slade" title="Chris Slade">Chris Slade</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chad_Smith" title="Chad Smith">Chad Smith</a>, Joe Travers, <a href="/wiki/Simon_Wright_(musician)" title="Simon Wright (musician)">Simon Wright</a>, and John's son, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Bonham" title="Jason Bonham">Jason Bonham</a>. <a href="/wiki/Carmine_Appice" title="Carmine Appice">Carmine Appice</a> performed via video.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gibson_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gibson-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1988 the <a href="/wiki/Pulitzer_Prize" title="Pulitzer Prize">Pulitzer laureate</a> <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Rouse_(composer)" title="Christopher Rouse (composer)">Christopher Rouse</a> composed <a href="/wiki/Bonham_(Rouse)" title="Bonham (Rouse)"><i>Bonham</i></a> in tribute.</p>
<p>Bonham's drumming has been widely <a href="/wiki/Sampling_(music)" title="Sampling (music)">sampled</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop music</a>; for example, the <a href="/wiki/Beastie_Boys" title="Beastie Boys">Beastie Boys</a> sampled "Moby Dick," "The Ocean," and "When the Levee Breaks." on their debut album <i><a href="/wiki/Licensed_to_Ill" title="Licensed to Ill">Licensed to Ill</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Equipment">Equipment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Bonham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Equipment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Drums">Drums</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Bonham&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Drums">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">

<p>Bonham initially used Premier drums, but in the late 1960s was introduced to Ludwig by Carmine Appice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> Throughout the remainder of his career, Bonham endorsed of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ludwig_drums" title="Ludwig drums">Ludwig Drums</a>. At times, Bonham's <a href="/wiki/Bass_drum" title="Bass drum">kick drum</a> pedal squeaked. Jimmy Page later commented:</p>
<blockquote class="templatequote">
<p>The only real problem I can remember encountering was when we were putting the first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin_(box_set)" title="Led Zeppelin (box set)">boxed set</a> together. There was an awfully squeaky bass drum pedal on "Since I've Been Loving You". It sounds louder and louder every time I hear it! [laughs]. That was something that was obviously sadly overlooked at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-page93_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-page93-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>In 2005, Ludwig reissued Bonham drum kits in several styles and, in 2007, stainless steel kits similar to those Bonham used on the last Led Zeppelin tours in the 1970s.</p>
