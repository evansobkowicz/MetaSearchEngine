<p>The <a href="/wiki/Discography" title="Discography">discography</a> of the English <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin" title="Led Zeppelin">Led Zeppelin</a> consists of nine <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio albums</a>, four <a href="/wiki/Live_album" title="Live album">live albums</a>, nine <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilation albums</a>, sixteen <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">singles</a> and eight <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">music downloads</a>. Formed in London in 1968, the group consisted of guitarist <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Page" title="Jimmy Page">Jimmy Page</a>, singer <a href="/wiki/Robert_Plant" title="Robert Plant">Robert Plant</a>, bass player <a href="/wiki/John_Paul_Jones_(musician)" title="John Paul Jones (musician)">John Paul Jones</a>, and drummer <a href="/wiki/John_Bonham" title="John Bonham">John Bonham</a>. The band pioneered the concept of <a href="/wiki/Album-oriented_rock" title="Album-oriented rock">album-oriented rock</a> and often refused to release popular songs as singles.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Led_Zep_Bio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Led_Zep_Bio-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Their debut album, <i><a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin_(album)" title="Led Zeppelin (album)">Led Zeppelin</a></i> (1969), released by <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Records" title="Atlantic Records">Atlantic Records</a>, charted at number six on the <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">UK Albums Chart</a> and at number ten on the United States <a href="/wiki/Billboard_200" title="Billboard 200"><i>Billboard</i> 200</a>. It received several <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">sales certifications</a>, including an 8 times multi-platinum from the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) and Diamond from the <a href="/wiki/Music_Canada" title="Music Canada">Music Canada</a>. Led Zeppelin's second studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin_II" title="Led Zeppelin II">Led Zeppelin II</a></i>, recorded when the band were on tour, was released a few months after the first. It reached number one in several countries, including the UK and the US, where it was certified 12 times multi-platinum. The album produced Led Zeppelin's most successful single, "<a href="/wiki/Whole_Lotta_Love" title="Whole Lotta Love">Whole Lotta Love</a>", which peaked at several music charts in the top 10. <i><a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin_III" title="Led Zeppelin III">Led Zeppelin III</a></i> (1970) was a softer, more <a href="/wiki/Folk_music" title="Folk music">folk</a>-based effort compared to the <a href="/wiki/Hard_rock" title="Hard rock">hard rock</a> of the band's previous releases.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Led_Zeppelin_III_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Led_Zeppelin_III-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> It also peaked at number one in the UK and in the US.</p>
<p>Led Zeppelin's <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin_IV" title="Led Zeppelin IV">untitled fourth album</a>, often called <i>Led Zeppelin IV</i> and released on 8 November 1971, is their most commercially successful album. It received a 23 times multi-platinum certification from RIAA, the third-highest of all albums.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Led_Zeppelin_IV_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Led_Zeppelin_IV-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The band's fifth album, <i><a href="/wiki/Houses_of_the_Holy" title="Houses of the Holy">Houses of the Holy</a></i>, was released in 1973. Again a transatlantic chart-topper, it has received an 11 times multi-platinum certification from RIAA. In 1974, the band set up their own <a href="/wiki/Vanity_label" title="Vanity label">vanity label</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swan_Song_Records" title="Swan Song Records">Swan Song Records</a>, which would release the rest of Led Zeppelin's studio albums. The first was the 1975 <a href="/wiki/Double_album" title="Double album">double album</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Physical_Graffiti" title="Physical Graffiti">Physical Graffiti</a></i>, which has received a 16 times platinum certification from RIAA. However, Zeppelin's seventh album, <i><a href="/wiki/Presence_(album)" title="Presence (album)">Presence</a></i> (1976), did not perform as well as their previous releases, receiving a triple Platinum certification from RIAA. On 20 October 1976 Led Zeppelin released their first <a href="/wiki/Concert_film" title="Concert film">concert film</a> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Song_Remains_the_Same_(film)" title="The Song Remains the Same (film)">The Song Remains the Same</a></i>. The recording of the film took place during three nights of concerts at <a href="/wiki/Madison_Square_Garden" title="Madison Square Garden">Madison Square Garden</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, during the band's <a href="/wiki/Led_Zeppelin_North_American_Tour_1973" title="Led Zeppelin North American Tour 1973">1973 concert tour of the United States</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Welch2_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Welch2-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The band's eighth album, <i><a href="/wiki/In_Through_the_Out_Door" title="In Through the Out Door">In Through the Out Door</a></i>, received a sextuple platinum certification. This was the last album released by the band before Bonham died of <a href="/wiki/Alcohol_intoxication" title="Alcohol intoxication">alcohol intoxication</a> in 1980; Led Zeppelin disbanded immediately afterwards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-John_the_Bonham_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-John_the_Bonham-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1982 Led Zeppelin released the album <i><a href="/wiki/Coda_(album)" title="Coda (album)">Coda</a></i>, a compilation of outtakes from the band's previous recordings, but still considered to be a studio album. Since their break-up, the band have released numerous compilation and live albums from older concerts, including <i><a href="/wiki/How_the_West_Was_Won_(Led_Zeppelin_album)" title="How the West Was Won (Led Zeppelin album)">How the West Was Won</a></i>, which peaked at number one on the <i>Billboard</i> charts, and the compilation album <i><a href="/wiki/Mothership_(album)" title="Mothership (album)">Mothership</a></i>, which produced seven music downloads and was released on the same day Led Zeppelin's entire catalog became available in digital stores, including in the <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Billboard_iTunes_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-Billboard_iTunes-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> "<a href="/wiki/Stairway_to_Heaven" title="Stairway to Heaven">Stairway to Heaven</a>", which had never been released as an official single before, was one of these songs released in digital stores.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-never_released_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-never_released-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The band has sold over 300 million albums worldwide,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-led_zeppelin_net_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-led_zeppelin_net-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> including 111.5 million certified units in the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RIAA_total_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-RIAA_total-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
