<h2><a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/2011/06/08/simple-pleasures-of-life/" rel="bookmark">The Simple Pleasures of Life</a></h2>
<p class="post-info">
						Posted in <a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/category/edwardian-era/" rel="category tag">Edwardian era</a>, <a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/category/literature-2/" rel="category tag">Literature</a>, tagged <a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/tag/anne-of-green-gables/" rel="tag">Anne of Green Gables</a>, <a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/tag/lucy-maud-montgomery/" rel="tag">Lucy Maud Montgomery</a>, <a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/tag/simple-pleasures/" rel="tag">simple pleasures</a> on June 8, 2011 | 
													<a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/2011/06/08/simple-pleasures-of-life/#respond" title="Comment on The Simple Pleasures of Life">Leave a Comment »</a> </p>
</div>
<div class="entry">
<blockquote><p>“After all,” Anne had said to Marilla once, “I believe the nicest and sweetest days are not those on which anything very splendid or wonderful or exciting happens but just those that bring simple little pleasures, following one another softly, like pearls slipping of a string.” Life at Green Gables was full of such days…</p></blockquote>
<p style="text-align:right;"><em>Anne of Windy Willows</em> – Lucy Maud Montgomery</p>
<p style="text-align:left;">I remember one day lamenting over the fact that special and fun things hardly ever happened in my life. Then I wondered if I could change that.</p>
<p style="text-align:left;">I decided that, in order to be able to be excited about SOMETHING, I would be excited about ANYTHING that was mildly pleasing. And it worked! Every week I had several things to be excited about… sometimes it was payday, sometimes an afternoon in a cafe with a friend, or reading inside with the sound of rain on the roof, or even a sleep-in on the weekend. Everything seemed to have suddenly become so much more exciting! And it was all because I looked forward to it and enjoyed the moment when it arrived!</p>
<div class="wp-caption alignright" id="attachment_172" style="width: 250px"><a href="https://teainateacup.files.wordpress.com/2011/06/anne-of-green-gables1.jpg"><img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-172" src="https://teainateacup.files.wordpress.com/2011/06/anne-of-green-gables1.jpg?w=500" title="Anne of Green Gables"/></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Diana Barry and Anne Shirley in Anne of Green Gables, played by Schuyler Grant and Megan Follows.</p></div>
<p style="text-align:left;">It is for this reason that this passage from <em>Anne</em> resounds with me. She didn’t need anything fantastically exciting to happen to her each day in order for her to be happy. She just focussed on the “simple little pleasures” of life. Sometimes it was a stroll in the fields, or seeing springtime bring out new blooms, or having some raspberry cordial with a bosom friend (hehe). There are tonnes of otherwise menial moments in Anne’s life that filled her with raptures.</p>
<p style="text-align:left;">She didn’t wait for something exciting to happen to her, she just made every day exciting.</p>
<p style="text-align:left;">Green Gables was full of such days…. is your home full of such days?</p>

<h2><a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/2011/05/12/a-bend-in-the-road/" rel="bookmark">A Bend in the Road</a></h2>
<p class="post-info">
						Posted in <a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/category/edwardian-era/" rel="category tag">Edwardian era</a>, <a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/category/literature-2/" rel="category tag">Literature</a>, tagged <a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/tag/anne-of-green-gables/" rel="tag">Anne of Green Gables</a>, <a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/tag/bend-in-the-road/" rel="tag">Bend in the Road</a>, <a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/tag/literature/" rel="tag">literature</a>, <a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/tag/lucy-maud-montgomery/" rel="tag">Lucy Maud Montgomery</a>, <a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/tag/megan-follows/" rel="tag">Megan Follows</a>, <a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/tag/quote/" rel="tag">quote</a> on May 12, 2011 | 
													<a href="https://teainateacup.wordpress.com/2011/05/12/a-bend-in-the-road/#comments" title="Comment on A Bend in the Road">4 Comments »</a> </p>
</div>
<div class="entry">
<blockquote><p><em>When I left Queen’s my future seemed to stretch out before me like a straight road. I thought I could see along it for many a milestone. Now there is a bend in it. I don’t know what lies around the bend, but I’m going to believe that the best does. It has a fascination of its own, that bend, Marilla. I wonder how the road beyond it goes – what new landscapes – what new beauties – what curves and hills and valleys further on. </em></p></blockquote>
<p align="right">Anne of Green Gables – Lucy Maud Montgomery</p>
<div class="wp-caption aligncenter" id="attachment_40" style="width: 360px"><a href="https://teainateacup.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/anne.jpg"><img alt="Anne of Green Gables" class="size-full wp-image-40 " src="https://teainateacup.files.wordpress.com/2011/05/anne.jpg?w=500" title="anne"/></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Anne of Green Gables (Megan Follows)</p></div>
<p align="right" style="text-align:left;">Anne of Green Gables has always been one of my favourite characters.</p>
<p align="right" style="text-align:left;">Anne inspires me. She is stoic in the face of adversity. She has courage to rise against any attempt at intimidation. She keeps hope alive down deep. She perseveres despite enormous hardship. She is true to herself, even when people don’t understand her.  And even when life around her changes – when there is a bend in the road – she is never shaken.</p>
<p align="right" style="text-align:left;">I have recently had an unexpected (and unwanted) bend in my own road. The sort of “bend” that makes you worry, cry, feel uncertain and really wish it would all go away.</p>
<p align="right" style="text-align:left;">We all have unwanted “bends” in our lives, and none of us knows what is on the other side of them. But can we say <em>I don’t know what lies around the bend, but I’m going to believe that the best does? </em>Can the bend hold a fascination for you, like it did for Anne? Can you wonder how the road beyond it goes? What new beauties and landscapes you will discover?</p>
<p align="right" style="text-align:left;">I guess it is all about choosing to live on the positive side of life!</p>
<p align="right" style="text-align:left;">Has your road got a bend in it?</p>
