<p><b>John Jacob "Jack" Astor IV</b> (July 13, 1864 – April 15, 1912), often dubbed "Jack Ass" in the press,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> was an American businessman, real estate builder, investor, inventor, writer, lieutenant colonel in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish-American_War" title="Spanish-American War">Spanish-American War</a>, and a prominent member of the <a href="/wiki/Astor_family" title="Astor family">Astor family</a>.</p>
<p>In April 1912, Astor earned a prominent place in history when he embarked on the ocean liner <i><a href="/wiki/RMS_Titanic" title="RMS Titanic">RMS Titanic</a></i>, which <a href="/wiki/Sinking_of_the_RMS_Titanic" title="Sinking of the RMS Titanic">struck an iceberg and sank</a> in the early hours of April 15. Astor was among the 1,514 people on board who did not survive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> He was the richest passenger aboard the <i>Titanic</i>, and was thought to be among the richest people in the world at that time, with a net worth of $85,000,000 when he died.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life.2C_education.2C_and_family">Early life, education, and family</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Jacob_Astor_IV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life, education, and family">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>John Jacob Astor IV was born on July 13, 1864. He was the youngest of five children and only son of businessman, collector, and race horse breeder/owner <a href="/wiki/William_Backhouse_Astor,_Jr." title="William Backhouse Astor, Jr.">William Backhouse Astor, Jr.</a> (1829–1892) and socialite <a href="/wiki/Caroline_Webster_Schermerhorn_Astor" title="Caroline Webster Schermerhorn Astor">Caroline Webster "Lina" Schermerhorn</a> (1830–1908). His four elder sisters were Emily (1854–1881), Helen (1855–1893), Charlotte (1858–1920), and Caroline (Carrie) (1861–1948). He was a great-grandson of fur-trader <a href="/wiki/John_Jacob_Astor" title="John Jacob Astor">John Jacob Astor</a> (1763–1848) and Sarah Cox Todd (1761–1834), whose fortune made the Astor family one of the wealthiest families in the United States. Jack's paternal grandfather <a href="/wiki/William_Backhouse_Astor,_Sr." title="William Backhouse Astor, Sr.">William Backhouse Astor, Sr.</a> (1792–1875) was a prominent real estate businessman. Through his paternal grandmother Margaret Rebecca Armstrong (1800–1872), Jack was also a great-grandson of Senator <a href="/wiki/John_Armstrong,_Jr." title="John Armstrong, Jr.">John Armstrong, Jr.</a> (1758–1843) and Alida Livingston (1761–1822) of the <a href="/wiki/Livingston_family" title="Livingston family">Livingston family</a>. Lina's parents were Abraham Schermerhorn and Helen White. Astor attended <a href="/wiki/St._Paul%27s_School_(Concord,_New_Hampshire)" title="St. Paul's School (Concord, New Hampshire)">St Paul's School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Concord,_New_Hampshire" title="Concord, New Hampshire">Concord, New Hampshire</a> and later attended <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-titanic_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-titanic-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Jack was also a nephew of financer/philanthropist <a href="/wiki/John_Jacob_Astor_III" title="John Jacob Astor III">John Jacob Astor III</a> (1822–1890) and grandnephew of occasional poet John Jacob Astor, Jr. (1791–1869). Helen's husband was diplomat <a href="/wiki/James_Roosevelt_Roosevelt" title="James Roosevelt Roosevelt">James Roosevelt "Rosey" Roosevelt</a> (1854–1927), half-brother of President <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin Delano Roosevelt</a> (1882–1945) of the <a href="/wiki/Roosevelt_family" title="Roosevelt family">Roosevelt family</a>. Carrie, a noted philanthropist, was the wife of Marshall Orme Wilson (1860–1926), brother of banker <a href="/wiki/Richard_Thornton_Wilson,_Jr." title="Richard Thornton Wilson, Jr.">Richard Thornton Wilson, Jr.</a> (1866–1929) and socialite <a href="/wiki/Grace_Vanderbilt" title="Grace Vanderbilt">Grace Graham Wilson</a> (1870–1953). Jack was also a first cousin of Viscount <a href="/wiki/William_Waldorf_Astor,_1st_Viscount_Astor" title="William Waldorf Astor, 1st Viscount Astor">William Waldorf "Willy" Astor</a> (1848–1919).</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriages">Marriages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Jacob_Astor_IV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Marriages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>On February 17, 1891,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TitanicProfile_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-TitanicProfile-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Astor married socialite <a href="/wiki/Ava_Lowle_Willing" title="Ava Lowle Willing">Ava Lowle Willing</a> (1868–1958), a daughter of Edward Shippen Willing and Alice Barton. The couple had two children:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vincent_Astor" title="Vincent Astor">William Vincent Astor</a> (1891–1959), businessman and philanthropist</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ava_Alice_Muriel_Astor" title="Ava Alice Muriel Astor">Ava Alice Muriel Astor</a> (1902–1956), socialite and heiress</li>
</ul>
<p>Jack and Ava divorced in November 1909. Compounding the scandal of their divorce was Astor's announcement that he would remarry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-titanic_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-titanic-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> At the age of 47, Jack married 18-year-old socialite <a href="/wiki/Madeleine_Astor" title="Madeleine Astor">Madeleine Talmage Force</a> (1893–1940), the sister of real estate businesswoman and socialite <a href="/wiki/Katherine_Emmons_Force" title="Katherine Emmons Force">Katherine Emmons Force</a> (1891–1948). Katherine and Madeleine's parents were William Hurlbut Force and Katherine Arvilla Talmage. They were married in his mother's ballroom at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beechwood_(mansion)" title="Beechwood (mansion)">Beechwood</a>, the family's <a href="/wiki/Newport,_Rhode_Island" title="Newport, Rhode Island">Newport, Rhode Island</a>, house. There was also much controversy over their 29-year age difference.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-titanic_survivor_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-titanic_survivor-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> His son Vincent despised Madeleine,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Vincent_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Vincent-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> yet he served as best man at Jack and Madeleine's wedding.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-encyclopedia_titanica_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-encyclopedia_titanica-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The couple took an extended honeymoon in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> to wait for the gossip to calm down. Among the few Americans who did not spurn him at this time was <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Brown" title="Margaret Brown">Margaret Brown</a>, later fictionalized as <i><a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/The_Unsinkable_Molly_Brown" title="The Unsinkable Molly Brown">The Unsinkable Molly Brown</a></i>. She accompanied the Astors to Egypt and <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and, by coincidence, was called home to the U.S. at the same time the Astors also found it necessary to abbreviate their touring.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-titanic_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-titanic-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Careers">Careers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Jacob_Astor_IV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Careers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Among Astor's accomplishments was <i><a href="/wiki/A_Journey_in_Other_Worlds" title="A Journey in Other Worlds">A Journey in Other Worlds</a></i>, an 1894 science fiction novel about life in the year 2000 on the planets <a href="/wiki/Saturn" title="Saturn">Saturn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">Jupiter</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> He also patented several inventions, including a bicycle brake in 1898, a "vibratory disintegrator" used to produce gas from <a href="/wiki/Sphagnum" title="Sphagnum">peat moss</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Pneumatics" title="Pneumatics">pneumatic</a> road-improver, and helped develop a <a href="/wiki/Turbine" title="Turbine">turbine engine</a>. Astor made millions in real estate. In 1897, Astor built the Astoria Hotel, "the world's most luxurious hotel",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WaldorfAstoriaHotel_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-WaldorfAstoriaHotel-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> in New York City, adjoining the Waldorf Hotel owned by Astor's cousin and rival, Willy. The complex became known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waldorf-Astoria_Hotel" title="Waldorf-Astoria Hotel">Waldorf-Astoria Hotel</a>, coincidentally becoming the host location to the U.S. inquiries into the sinking of the <i><a href="/wiki/RMS_Titanic" title="RMS Titanic">RMS Titanic</a></i>, in which Colonel Astor was killed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-titanic_4-3"><a href="#cite_note-titanic-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>From 1894 to 1896, he was on the staff of <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_New_York" title="Governor of New York">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Levi_P._Morton" title="Levi P. Morton">Levi P. Morton</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Shortly after the outbreak of the Spanish-American War in 1898, Astor personally financed a volunteer artillery unit known as the "Astor Battery", which saw service in the Philippines. Astor was appointed a <a href="/wiki/Lieutenant_colonel" title="Lieutenant colonel">lieutenant colonel</a> in the U.S. Volunteers and served as an officer on the staff of Major General <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/William_Shafter" title="William Shafter">William Shafter</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">Cuba</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Santiago" title="Siege of Santiago">Santiago Campaign</a>. He was later given a brevet (honorary promotion) to colonel in recognition of his services.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>During the siege, he allowed his <a href="/wiki/Yacht" title="Yacht">yacht</a>, the <i>Nourmahal</i>, to be used by the U.S. government. During the war, Astor appeared in the films <i>President McKinley's Inspection of Camp Wikoff</i> in 1898 and <i>Col. John Jacob Astor, Staff and Veterans of the Spanish-American War</i> in 1899.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-titanic_4-4"><a href="#cite_note-titanic-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> As a result of his military service, Astor was entitled to the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Campaign_Medal" title="Spanish Campaign Medal">Spanish Campaign Medal</a>.</p>

<p>Astor was a member of several military and hereditary societies. He was an early member of the New York <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Society_of_Colonial_Wars" title="Society of Colonial Wars">Society of Colonial Wars</a> and was assigned membership number 138. He was also a member of the <a href="/wiki/Military_Order_of_Foreign_Wars" title="Military Order of Foreign Wars">Military Order of Foreign Wars</a>, Society of the Army of Santiago and the Society of the American Wars of the United States.</p>
<p>His estate "Ferncliff," north of the town center of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhinebeck,_New_York" title="Rhinebeck, New York">Rhinebeck, New York</a>, with a mile and a half of Hudson River frontage in the picturesque Lower <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hudson_River_Valley" title="Hudson River Valley">Hudson River Valley</a>, had been purchased <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Piecemeal" title="Piecemeal">piecemeal</a> by his father in the mid-19th century; Astor was born there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> His father's Italianate house of 1864 was partly rebuilt in 1904 to designs by <a href="/wiki/Stanford_White" title="Stanford White">Stanford White</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/McKim,_Mead,_and_White" title="McKim, Mead, and White">McKim, Mead, and White</a>, retaining its conservative exterior, and a sports pavilion in Louis XVI style was added.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> The "Casino" or "Astor Courts" reportedly housed the first residential indoor swimming pool in the U.S., an indoor tennis court with vaulting of <a href="/wiki/Guastavino_tile" title="Guastavino tile">Guastavino tile</a>, and guest bedrooms; in the lower level were a bowling alley and a shooting range.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> The estate, reduced to 50 acres (200,000 m<sup>2</sup>) and renamed "Astor Courts," eventually became a wedding venue. The wedding of <a href="/wiki/Chelsea_Clinton" title="Chelsea Clinton">Chelsea Clinton</a> took place there on July 31, 2010.<br style="clear:both;"/></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Titanic"><i>Titanic</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Jacob_Astor_IV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Titanic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While traveling, Madeleine became pregnant, and wanting the child born in the U.S., the Astors boarded the <a href="/wiki/RMS_Titanic" title="RMS Titanic">RMS <i>Titanic</i></a> on her maiden voyage to New York. They embarked in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cherbourg" title="Cherbourg">Cherbourg</a>, France, in first class and were the wealthiest passengers aboard. Accompanying the Astors were Astor's valet, Victor Robbins; Madeleine's maid, Rosalie Bidois; and her nurse, Caroline Louise Endres. They also took their pet <a href="/wiki/Airedale_Terrier" title="Airedale Terrier">Airedale</a>, Kitty. The Astors were deeply fond of their dog and had come close to losing her on a previous trip when she went missing in Egypt. Kitty did not survive the sinking.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> A short while after the <i>Titanic</i> hit the iceberg that caused her to sink, Astor informed his wife of the collision, but told her that the damage did not appear to be serious. Some time later as the ship's lifeboats for first class were being manned, Colonel Astor remained unperturbed; he and his family played with the mechanical horses in the gymnasium. At some point Astor is thought to have sliced the lining of an extra lifebelt with a pen knife to show his wife its contents, either to prove that they were not of use or to reassure her that they were.</p>
<p>When Second Officer <a href="/wiki/Charles_Lightoller" title="Charles Lightoller">Charles Lightoller</a> later arrived on A Deck to finish loading Lifeboat 4, Astor helped his wife, with her maid and nurse, into it. He then asked if he might join his wife because she was in 'a delicate condition'; however, Lightoller told him that men were not to be allowed to board until all the women and children had been loaded. After Lifeboat 4 was lowered at 1:55 am, Astor is said to have stood alone while others tried to free the remaining collapsible boats;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-titanic_4-5"><a href="#cite_note-titanic-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> he was last seen alive on the starboard bridge wing, smoking a cigarette with <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Futrelle" title="Jacques Futrelle">Jacques Futrelle</a>. A mere half hour later, the ship disappeared beneath the ocean. Madeleine, her nurse, and her maid survived. Colonel Astor and his valet, Victor Robbins, did not.</p>
<p>According to <i>Titanic</i> passenger <a href="/wiki/Archibald_Gracie_IV" title="Archibald Gracie IV">Archibald Gracie IV</a>, "She was lifted up through the window, and her husband helped her on the other side, and when she got in, her husband was on one side of this window and I was on the other side, at the next window. I heard Mr Astor ask the second officer whether he would not be allowed to go aboard this boat to protect his wife. He said, 'No, sir, no man is allowed on this boat or any of the boats until the ladies are off.' Mr Astor then said, 'Well, tell me what is the number of this boat so I may find her afterwards,' or words to that effect. The answer came back, "No. 4."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the aftermath, ships were sent out to retrieve the bodies from the site of the sinking; of the 1,517 passengers and crew that perished in the sinking, only 333 bodies were ever recovered. Colonel Astor's body was recovered on April 22 by the cable ship <i><a href="/wiki/CS_Mackay-Bennett" title="CS Mackay-Bennett">Mackay-Bennett</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-titanic_4-6"><a href="#cite_note-titanic-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Astor was identified by the initials sewn on the label of his jacket. Among the items found on him was a gold <a href="/wiki/Pocket_watch" title="Pocket watch">pocket watch</a> which his son Vincent claimed and wore the rest of his life.</p>

<p>Astor was buried in <a href="/wiki/Trinity_Church_Cemetery" title="Trinity Church Cemetery">Trinity Church Cemetery</a> in New York City. Four months after the <i>Titanic</i> sank, Madeleine Astor gave birth to his second son, socialite <a href="/wiki/John_Jacob_Astor_VI" title="John Jacob Astor VI">John Jacob "Jakey" Astor VI</a> (1912–1992).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-titanic_4-8"><a href="#cite_note-titanic-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>For many years there has been conflicting and contradictory information regarding the exact manner in which Astor perished in the sinking. Many researchers have expressed the belief that Astor was crushed to death by one of the ship's falling <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Smokestack" title="Smokestack">funnels</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> this opinion being based on the claim that his body was recovered in a mangled state, but all who examined his body maintained that it was in perfect condition with no bruising.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-titanic_4-9"><a href="#cite_note-titanic-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> Survivor Philip Mock claimed to have seen Astor in the water clinging to a raft with <a href="/wiki/William_Thomas_Stead" title="William Thomas Stead">William Thomas Stead</a>. "Their feet became frozen," said Mock, "and they were forced to release their hold. Both were drowned."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Will">Will</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Jacob_Astor_IV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Will">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Astor left $69 million of his $85-million estate (equivalent to approximately $1,686,217,241 in 2015 dollars)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-inflation-US_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-inflation-US-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> to Vincent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> This value included his estate in Rhinebeck and his yacht, the <i><a href="/wiki/USS_Noma_(SP-131)" title="USS Noma (SP-131)">Noma</a></i>. To Madeleine, he left a $5-million trust fund and an annual paycheck of $500,000, as well as use of his New York mansion at 65th &amp; Fifth Avenue, all its furnishings, and his Newport mansion <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beechwood_(mansion)" title="Beechwood (mansion)">Beechwood</a> and all its furnishings, pick of whichever luxury limousine she wanted from his collection and five of his prized horses—as long as she did not remarry. His daughter Ava (who lived with her mother, also named Ava) received a $10-million trust fund. Upon turning 21, Jakey inherited the trust fund Colonel Astor had set aside for him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MoneyForJakey_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-MoneyForJakey-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">Legacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Jacob_Astor_IV&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Legacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:John_Jacob_Astor_1909.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="491" data-file-width="408" height="265" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/John_Jacob_Astor_1909.jpg/220px-John_Jacob_Astor_1909.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/John_Jacob_Astor_1909.jpg/330px-John_Jacob_Astor_1909.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/91/John_Jacob_Astor_1909.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:John_Jacob_Astor_1909.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
John Jacob Astor IV in 1909.</div>

<p>Astor's prominence led to the creation of many exaggerated and unsubstantiated accounts about his actions during the sinking of the <i>Titanic</i>. One story alleges that he opened the ship's <a href="/wiki/Kennel" title="Kennel">kennel</a> and released the dogs; in another, he placed a woman's hat on a boy to make sure the child was able to get into a lifeboat. Another legend claims that after the ship hit the iceberg, he quipped, "I asked for ice, but this is ridiculous." These stories appeared in newspapers, magazines, and even books about the sinking. In reality, none of the claims about Astor's actions were substantiated, as nobody who recognized him survived other than the women who boarded lifeboats relatively early on.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wade_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-wade-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> Wade wrote that the ice joke is almost certainly apocryphal, as Astor was not known for making jokes, and that the story about the hat (like many other 'survivor stories' published shortly after the sinking) may have been invented by the reporter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wade_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-wade-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
