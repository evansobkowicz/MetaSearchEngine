<h4><strong>PHYSICAL GRAFFITI DELUXE EDITION ARRIVES EXACTLY 40 YEARS AFTER DEBUT,   <br>
    PRODUCED AND NEWLY REMASTERED BY JIMMY PAGE, WITH PREVIOUSLY UNRELEASED   COMPANION AUDIO</br></strong><br>
<br>
    Multiple CD, Vinyl, And Digital Formats, Including Limited Edition Super Deluxe Boxed Set, Available February 24 </br></br></h4>
<p>The Led Zeppelin reissue campaign continues in 2015, turning the spotlight on the double album <em>Physical Graffiti</em>.   The deluxe edition of the group’s sixth studio album will arrive 40   years to the day after the original debuted on February 24, 1975. As   with the previous deluxe editions, <em>Physical Graffiti</em> has been   newly remastered by guitarist and producer Jimmy Page and is accompanied   by a disc of companion audio comprising previously unreleased music   related to the original release.<br>
<br>
Certified 16x platinum in the U.S., the commercial success of <em>Physical Graffiti</em> was equaled by its critical reception. Generally regarded as one of the   greatest double albums of all time, the original 15 tracks represent a   creative tour de force that explores the band’s dynamic musical range,   from the driving rock of “Custard Pie” and an acoustic arrangement of “Bron-Yr-Aur” to the Eastern raga of “Kashmir” and funky groove of   “Trampled Under Foot.”<br>
<br>
The companion audio disc that accompanies the deluxe edition of <em>Physical Graffiti</em> has seven unreleased tracks, including rough mixes of “In My Time Of   Dying” and “Houses Of The Holy,” as well as an early mix of “Trampled   Under Foot” called “Brandy &amp; Coke.” All the unreleased companion   tracks offer fans a chance to hear well-known songs from a different   perspective, including the Sunset Sound mix of “Boogie With Stu” and   “Driving Through Kashmir,” a rough orchestra mix of the band’s eight   minute opus “Kashmir.” Also featured is “Everybody Makes It Through,” a   strikingly different early version of “In The Light” with alternate   lyrics.</br></br></br></br></p>
