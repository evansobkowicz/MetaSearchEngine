<h3 class="post-title entry-title" itemprop="name">
CRACKER?  The Eagle Has Landed
</h3>
<div class="post-header">
<div class="post-header-line-1"></div>
</div>
<div class="post-body entry-content" id="post-body-5520024624879854512" itemprop="description articleBody">
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://www.imfdb.org/w/images/8/8d/The_Eagle_Has_Landed_poster.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="400" src="http://www.imfdb.org/w/images/8/8d/The_Eagle_Has_Landed_poster.jpg" width="260"/></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<br/>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto; mso-margin-top-alt: auto;">
<span style='font-family: "Times New Roman","serif"; font-size: 10pt; mso-fareast-font-family: "Times New Roman";'>               <span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: small;"> “The Eagle
Has Landed” is an action/adventure war movie released in 1976  It is firmly in the suicide mission subgenre.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>It was directed by John Sturges (<a href="http://warmoviebuff.blogspot.com/2012/01/44-great-escape.html">"The Great Escape"</a>) and was his last film.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>It is
based on the novel by Jack Higgins. <span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>It
opens with a reference to Otto Skorzeny’s daring rescue of Mussolini from
captivity.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>This inspires Hitler to try
to capture “his greatest enemy” – Churchill.</span></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto; mso-margin-top-alt: auto;">
<span style='mso-fareast-font-family: "Times New Roman";'><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">                </span>The head of military
intelligence, Admiral Canaris (Anthony Quayle), is assigned the task of
implementing Hitler’s dream.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>He meets
with an officer named Radl (Robert Duvall), but denigrates the whole fantasy
and wants to just go through the motions.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>However, Himmler (Donald Pleasence) gets involved and green-lights the
project.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Radl warms to the possibilities
when information arrives that Churchill will be visiting an isolated British
village.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Radl chooses a decorated war
hero named Steiner (Michael Caine) to lead the mission.</span></span></div>
<table cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="float: right; margin-left: 1em; text-align: right;"><tbody><span style="font-family: inherit;">
</span>
<tr><td style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.imfdb.org/w/images/5/58/TEHLFG42.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><img border="0" height="145" src="http://www.imfdb.org/w/images/5/58/TEHLFG42.jpg" width="320"/></span></a></td></tr>
<span style="font-family: inherit;">
</span>
<tr><td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: blue; font-family: inherit; font-size: small;">Steiner does not like Nazis</span></td></tr>
<span style="font-family: inherit;">
</span></tbody></table>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto; mso-margin-top-alt: auto;">
<span style='mso-fareast-font-family: "Times New Roman";'><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">       </span>We meet Steiner (the same
Steiner who appears in <a href="http://warmoviebuff.blogspot.com/2011/05/64-cross-of-iron.html">"Cross of Iron"</a>) as he bucks authority by trying to save
a Jewish woman from the S.S.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Steiner is
insubordinate, anti-Nazi, anti-brass, cynical, and worshiped by his men.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>He ends up getting court-martialed and he and
his men are sent to a penal colony.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>Similar to the plot of “The Dirty Dozen”, they are promised forgiveness
if they undertake the suicide mission.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>Radl also recruits an IRA operative named Devlin (Donald Sutherland) to
participate.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>He considers himself an
Irish patriot, not a traitor.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto; mso-margin-top-alt: auto;">
<span style='mso-fareast-font-family: "Times New Roman";'><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">                </span>Devlin goes on ahead and makes
contact with a sleeper agent, a woman named Gray.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>He also meets and charms a lass named Molly
(Jenny Agutter) who inexplicably falls so hard and so quickly that she kills
her beau when he is going to rat out Devlin.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>Granted, he was a lummox, but her treason for love is a bit too pat.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto; mso-margin-top-alt: auto;">
<span style='mso-fareast-font-family: "Times New Roman";'><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">                </span>Steiner and his men parachute into England 
dressed as Polish soldiers.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>The sleepy
villagers are welcoming and then grateful when one of the Germans saves the
life of a local girl.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Unfortunately, the
gratefulness is short-lived as the incident results in discovery of their true
identities.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>The populace is quickly
rounded up and held hostage in the local church.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>One of the townspeople manages to escape and
goes up the road to inform an American Rangers unit led by a buffoonish Colonel
Pitt (Larry Hagman)<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>His daddy must be a
general.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>He looks at the situation as a
chance for glory and disregards the sane Capt. Clark’s (Treat Williams) advice
to go slow.</span></span><span style="font-family: inherit;"><br/></span></div>
<table cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="float: left; margin-right: 1em; text-align: left;"><tbody><span style="font-family: inherit;">
</span>
<tr><td style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.imfdb.org/w/images/1/12/TEHLBazooka3.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><img border="0" height="145" src="http://www.imfdb.org/w/images/1/12/TEHLBazooka3.jpg" width="320"/></span></a></td></tr>
<span style="font-family: inherit;">
</span>
<tr><td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: blue; font-family: inherit; font-size: small;">a bazooka in a cemetery - shame</span></td></tr>
<span style="font-family: inherit;">
</span></tbody></table>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto; mso-margin-top-alt: auto;">
<span style='mso-fareast-font-family: "Times New Roman";'><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">                </span>Pitt does his Custer imitation
resulting in a nicely staged fire-fight that features graphic wounds and lots
of fireworks including some nifty bazooka work.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>It’s a disaster and Pitt can’t even defeat Mrs. Gray.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>His encounter with the spy has a twisted
ending that almost compensates for the tomfoolery.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto; mso-margin-top-alt: auto;">
<span style='mso-fareast-font-family: "Times New Roman";'><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">                </span>Capt. Clark arrives with
reinforcements and a brain, so now the German’s are trapped with the hostages
in the church.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Steiner, being a human
being, not a Nazi, releases the hostages.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>Molly shows him a secret passage out of the church so he can go kill
Churchill (the things a British girl will do for an Irish spy she just
met).<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Steiner’s crew offer to stay
behind to delay the Rangers and provide us with more violence.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Devlin also escapes.</span></span></div>
<table cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="float: right; margin-left: 1em; text-align: right;"><tbody><span style="font-family: inherit;">
</span>
<tr><td style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.imfdb.org/w/images/1/1b/TEHLP382.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><img border="0" height="132" src="http://www.imfdb.org/w/images/1/1b/TEHLP382.jpg" width="320"/></span></a></td></tr>
<span style="font-family: inherit;">
</span>
<tr><td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: blue; font-family: inherit; font-size: small;">the last thing Churchill saw?</span></td></tr>
<span style="font-family: inherit;">
</span></tbody></table>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto; mso-margin-top-alt: auto;">
<span style='mso-fareast-font-family: "Times New Roman";'><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">                </span>Steiner, now disguised as an
American, manages to get to Churchill.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>Does he win the war for Germany?<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>Hey, if the Inglorious Basterds can kill Hitler, why not?</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto; mso-margin-top-alt: auto;">
<span style='mso-fareast-font-family: "Times New Roman";'><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">                </span>“The Eagle Has Landed” is not on
a level with “Guns of Navarone” or “Where Eagles Dare”.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>It is even more implausible than most of the
suicide mission subgenre.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>The romance
that is thrown in is ridiculous as well.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>The acting is a strength.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Caine
ably fills James Coburn’s shoes as Steiner.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>He has a lot of charisma and it is easy to see why his superiors are
enraged by him and his subordinates are willing to die for him.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Paired with “Cross of Iron”, Steiner is one
of the great anti-heroes of war movies.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>Quayle is good as Canaris and accurately portrays his disgust with Nazi
schemes.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Although the movie is pure
imagination, Canaris was a leading figure in the resistance against Hitler and
even collaborated with the Allies.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>His
involvement in the plot to assassinate Hitler cost him his life.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Devlin is a nice addition and offers a
different perspective.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Sutherland plays
him as a charming rogue.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>The one false note
is Hagman as Pitt.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>The sudden injection
of comic relief is a bit jarring in an otherwise serious movie.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto; mso-margin-top-alt: auto;">
<span style='mso-fareast-font-family: "Times New Roman";'><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">                </span>The movie was filmed in an
English village, so the setting is picturesque.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>The action set pieces are well done, but too brief.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>The weapons appear authentic except the bazooka is a later model..<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>The score is typical for this type of
movie.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>It is satisfactory, but does not
stand out like in “Where Eagles Dare”.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>The intrigue is also inferior to WED.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>It lacks real suspense.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>You know
Steiner will not hurt the hostages, for instance.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>One questionable element is the introduction
of American Rangers into a story that should have been limited to Germans and
British.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>This seems to be a marketing
decision to help with the box office.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>It
is reminiscent of Sturges’ “The Great Escape” where three Americans characters
were inaccurately added.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Can’t the
British defend their own Prime Minister?<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;"> 
</span>I suspect American’s would have been upset if a plot against FDR was
foiled by British forces.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto; mso-margin-top-alt: auto;">
<span style='font-family: inherit; mso-fareast-font-family: "Times New Roman";'>               In conclusion, "The Eagle Has Landed" is entertaining, but certainly not one of the 100 Best War Movies.  Kurt Steiner will have to be content with his "Cross of Iron" making the list.  But then again, he probably would not give a damn about such a list.</span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto; mso-margin-top-alt: auto;">
<span style='mso-fareast-font-family: "Times New Roman";'><strong><span style="color: red; font-family: inherit;">Rating
-<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>6/10</span></strong></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal; margin: 0in 0in 10pt 0.5in; mso-margin-bottom-alt: auto; mso-margin-top-alt: auto;">
<span style='font-family: inherit; mso-fareast-font-family: "Times New Roman";'>Here is my
updated ranking of the great suicide mission movies: </span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt; text-indent: 0.5in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit; line-height: 115%;">       1.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  <a href="http://warmoviebuff.blogspot.com/2012/05/cracker-where-eagles-dare.html">Where Eagles Dare</a></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
<span style="line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">                </span>2.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Kelly’s Heroes</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
<span style="line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">                </span>3.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>Inglorious Basterds</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
<span style="line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">                </span>4.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>The Dirty Dozen</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
<span style="line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">                </span>5.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  <a href="http://warmoviebuff.blogspot.com/2010/09/93-guns-of-navarone.html">Guns of Navarone</a></span></span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin: 0in 0in 10pt;">
<span style="line-height: 115%;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="mso-tab-count: 1;">                </span>6.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">  </span>The Eagle Has Landed</span></span></div>
