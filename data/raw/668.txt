<h2 class="rm">Biography</h2>
<h3 class="remove-top-margin"><span class="locality">London</span>, <span class="country-name">United Kingdom</span>    
    (<span itemprop="foundingDate">1970</span> – 1997)</h3> </hgroup>
</header>
<div class="wiki">
<div class="wiki-abstract" data-require="music/wiki" id="wikiAbstract">
<div class="wiki-text">
 Queen were an English rock band originally consisting of four members: vocalist <a class="bbcode_artist" href="http://www.last.fm/music/Freddie+Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a>, guitarist <a class="bbcode_artist" href="http://www.last.fm/music/Brian+May">Brian May</a>, bass guitarist <a class="bbcode_artist" href="http://www.last.fm/music/John+Deacon">John Deacon</a>, and drummer <a class="bbcode_artist" href="http://www.last.fm/music/Roger+Taylor">Roger Taylor</a>.<br/>
<br/>
The band formed in London in 1970 after May and Taylor’s former band <a class="bbcode_artist" href="http://www.last.fm/music/Smile">Smile</a> split after having released an album and single. Freddie replaced lead vocalist <a class="bbcode_artist" href="http://www.last.fm/music/Tim+Staffell">Tim Staffell</a>, after the latter’s departure from the original trio.
 <span class="hide"> <br/><br/> There was much deliberation as to what the band’s name would be. May and Taylor had settled upon the Grand Dance, derived from a C.S. Lewis Trilogy of books Out Of The Silent Planet.<br/>
<br/>
Ultimately, the band went with Queen, a name that was entirely Freddie’s. Originally Roger and, especially, Brian did not like the name, though Mercury would eventually persuade them both that it was “right” for the band.<br/>
<br/>
“Years ago I thought up the name Queen… It’s just a name, but it’s very regal, and it sounds splendid. It’s a strong name, very universal and immediate. It had a lot of visual potential and was open to all sorts of interpretations. I was certainly aware of the gay connotations, but that was just one facet of it. “<br/>
- Freddie Mercury.<br/>
<br/>
Queen went through several bass players before a young electronics major by the name of John Deacon arrived on the scene in January of 1971.
