<p>The <b>Moorish idol</b> (<i>Zanclus cornutus</i>), is a marine <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a> <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monotypic" title="Monotypic">sole</a> <a href="/wiki/Extant_taxon" title="Extant taxon">extant</a> representative of the <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <b>Zanclidae</b> (from the <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> ζαγκίος, <i>zagkios</i>, "oblique") in <a href="/wiki/Order_(biology)" title="Order (biology)">order</a> <a href="/wiki/Perciformes" title="Perciformes">Perciformes</a>. A common inhabitant of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical" title="Tropical">tropical</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Subtropical" title="Subtropical">subtropical</a> <a href="/wiki/Reef" title="Reef">reefs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lagoon" title="Lagoon">lagoons</a>, the Moorish idol is notable for its wide distribution throughout the <a href="/wiki/Indo-Pacific" title="Indo-Pacific">Indo-Pacific</a>. A number of <a href="/wiki/Butterflyfish" title="Butterflyfish">butterflyfishes</a> (<a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Heniochus" title="Heniochus">Heniochus</a></i>) closely resemble the Moorish idol. It is closely related to, if not a direct descendant of, the extinct <i><a href="/wiki/Eozanclus" title="Eozanclus">Eozanclus</a> brevirhostris</i>, from the Middle <a href="/wiki/Eocene" title="Eocene">Eocene</a> of <a href="/wiki/Monte_Bolca" title="Monte Bolca">Monte Bolca</a>.</p>
<p>The Moorish idol got its name from the <a href="/wiki/Moors" title="Moors">Moors</a> of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, who purportedly believed the fish to be a bringer of happiness. Moorish idols are also popular <a href="/wiki/Aquarium" title="Aquarium">aquarium</a> fish, but despite their popularity, they are notorious for short aquarium lifespans and sensitivity.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_description">Physical description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moorish_idol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Physical description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ZanclusCornutus.png"><img alt="ZanclusCornutus.png" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="418" data-file-width="557" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/ZanclusCornutus.png/220px-ZanclusCornutus.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/ZanclusCornutus.png/330px-ZanclusCornutus.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/ZanclusCornutus.png/440px-ZanclusCornutus.png 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ZanclusCornutus.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>With distinctively compressed and disk-like bodies, Moorish idols stand out in contrasting bands of black, white, and yellow, which makes them attractive to aquarium keepers. The fish have relatively small fins, except for the dorsal fin, whose six or seven spines are dramatically elongated to form a trailing, <a href="/wiki/Sickle" title="Sickle">sickle</a>-shaped crest called the <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Philomantis&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Philomantis (page does not exist)">philomantis</a> extension. Moorish idols have small terminal mouths at the end of long, tubular snouts; many long bristle-like teeth line the mouth. The Moorish idol differs from <a href="/wiki/Butterflyfish" title="Butterflyfish">butterflyfish</a> in having a prominent black, triangular anal fin.</p>
<p>The eyes are set high on the fish's deeply keeled body; in adults, perceptible bumps are located above each. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anal_fin" title="Anal fin">anal fin</a> may have two or three spines. Moorish idols reach a maximum length of 23 cm (9.1 in). The sickle-like dorsal spines shorten with age.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moorish_idol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Generally denizens of shallow waters, Moorish idols prefer flat reefs. The fish may be found at depths from 3 to 180 m (9.8 to 590.6 ft), in both murky and clear conditions.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Their range includes <a href="/wiki/East_Africa" title="East Africa">East Africa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ducie_Island" title="Ducie Island">Ducie Islands</a>; <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>, southern <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and all of <a href="/wiki/Micronesia" title="Micronesia">Micronesia</a>; they are also found from the southern <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_California" title="Gulf of California">Gulf of California</a> south to <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding">Feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moorish_idol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_sponge" title="Sea sponge">Sponges</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coral" title="Coral">coral</a> polyps, <a href="/wiki/Tunicate" title="Tunicate">tunicates</a>, and other <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benthic" title="Benthic">benthic</a> <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a> constitute the bulk of the Moorish idol's diet.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behavior">Behavior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moorish_idol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behavior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Often seen alone, Moorish idols also form pairs or occasionally small schools, especially as juveniles. They are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Diurnal_animal" title="Diurnal animal">diurnal</a> fish, sticking to the bottom of the <a href="/wiki/Reef" title="Reef">reef</a> at night, adopting a drab coloration. Like <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Butterfly_fish" title="Butterfly fish">butterfly fish</a>, they mate for life. Adult males display aggression toward one another.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moorish_idol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Moorish idols are <a href="/wiki/Pelagic_zone" title="Pelagic zone">pelagic</a> spawners; that is, they release eggs and sperm in the water column, leaving fertilized eggs to drift away with the currents. The range of these fish may be explained by the unusually long <a href="/wiki/Larva" title="Larva">larval</a> stage.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The fish reach a length of 7.5 cm (3.0 in) before becoming free-swimming juveniles. Captive</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_aquarium">In the aquarium</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moorish_idol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: In the aquarium">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Moorish-pennant-bannerfish.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="600" data-file-width="800" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Moorish-pennant-bannerfish.jpg/220px-Moorish-pennant-bannerfish.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Moorish-pennant-bannerfish.jpg/330px-Moorish-pennant-bannerfish.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Moorish-pennant-bannerfish.jpg/440px-Moorish-pennant-bannerfish.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Moorish-pennant-bannerfish.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
A comparison of the three remarkably similar fish: the Moorish idol (left), schooling bannerfish (top), and pennant coralfish(bottom)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Moorish idols are notoriously difficult to maintain in <a href="/wiki/Captivity_(animal)" title="Captivity (animal)">captivity</a>. They require large tanks, often exceeding 100 US gal (380 l; 83 imp gal),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-saltaquarium.about.com_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-saltaquarium.about.com-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> are voracious eaters, and can become destructive.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-saltaquarium.about.com_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-saltaquarium.about.com-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Some aquarists prefer to keep substitute species that look very similar to the Moorish idol. These substitutes are all butterflyfishes of the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Heniochus" title="Heniochus">Heniochus</a></i>, and include the <a href="/wiki/Pennant_coralfish" title="Pennant coralfish">pennant coralfish</a>, <i>H. acuminatus</i>; threeband pennantfish, <i><a href="/wiki/Heniochus_chrysostomus" title="Heniochus chrysostomus">H. chrysostomus</a></i>; and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/False_Moorish_idol" title="False Moorish idol">false Moorish idol</a>, <i>H. diphreutes</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Moorish idols typically are very picky eaters. They will either eat nothing (common) and perish or eat everything (uncommon).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Synonyms">Synonyms</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moorish_idol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Synonyms">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<li><i>Zanclus cornutus</i> (Linnaeus, 1758) accepted senior, new combination<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fishbase_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fishbase-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></li>

</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Moorish_idol&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Cultural references">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>In the 2003 <a href="/wiki/Pixar" title="Pixar">Pixar</a> film <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Nemo" title="Finding Nemo">Finding Nemo</a></i>, a Moorish idol named Gill (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Willem_Dafoe" title="Willem Dafoe">Willem Dafoe</a>) was one of Nemo's tank inhabitants. Gill was depicted having a very strong desire for freedom outside of the aquarium and was constantly scheming to achieve this, possibly referencing real-life Moorish idols' difficulties keeping in captivity. He will also appear in the sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Dory" title="Finding Dory">Finding Dory</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>Moorish idols have long been among the most iconic of <a href="/wiki/Coral_reef" title="Coral reef">coral reef</a> fauna. They have graced all types of products, such as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shower_curtain" title="Shower curtain">shower curtains</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blanket" title="Blanket">blankets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Towel" title="Towel">towels</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Wallpaper" title="Wallpaper">wallpaper</a> made with an ocean or underwater theme.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
</ul>
