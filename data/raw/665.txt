<p><i><b>Queen</b></i> is the <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack_album" title="Soundtrack album">soundtrack album</a> by <a href="/wiki/Amit_Trivedi" title="Amit Trivedi">Amit Trivedi</a>, to the 2014 <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_India" title="Cinema of India">Hindi</a> film of the <a href="/wiki/Queen_(film)" title="Queen (film)">same name</a> directed by Vikas Bahl and starring Kangana Ranaut in lead role. The album features eight tracks in a different array of <a href="/wiki/Genre" title="Genre">genres</a>. It was released digitally on 30 January 2014, and physically on 2 February 2014 at the <a href="/wiki/Kala_Ghoda_Arts_Festival" title="Kala Ghoda Arts Festival">Kala Ghoda Arts Festival</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>, attended by the cast and crew of the film and preceded by Trivedi's performance.</p>
<p>Trivedi travelled to European cities like <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> in search of inspiration for the album, while also working on four different film projects at the time. The album received positive reviews from critics. All the tracks are composed by Trivedi and written by Anvita Dutt, with the exception of "Ranjha" written by Raghu Nath, composed by Rupesh Kumar Ram and the producer of the film <a href="/wiki/Anurag_Kashyap" title="Anurag Kashyap">Anurag Kashyap</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Amit_Trivedi" title="Amit Trivedi">Amit Trivedi</a> composed the music soundtrack simultaneously working on four other ones like <a href="/wiki/Ghanchakkar_(film)#Soundtrack" title="Ghanchakkar (film)">Ghanchakkar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lootera#Soundtrack" title="Lootera">Lootera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bombay_Talkies_(film)#Soundtrack" title="Bombay Talkies (film)">Bombay Talkies</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kusar_Prasad_Ka_Bhoot" title="Kusar Prasad Ka Bhoot">Kusar Prasad Ka Bhoot</a> in a time span of 5 months.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-background_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-background-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The film based on a woman's self-discovery while travelling; Trivedi also travelled to Europe in Amsterdam, the film's shooting location<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> so that he could understand the vibe and musical demand of the place in accordance with the local philosophy of "free living [..] especially in their [European] nightclubs".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-background_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-background-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> During the visit he also spent time in Paris and bonded with the locals there while working over music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Trivedi completed <i>Ghanchakkar</i> and <i>Lootera</i> before <i>Queen</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The last track, Ranjha composed by Rupesh Kumar Ram, a composer from Simla; discovered by <a href="/wiki/Anurag_Kashyap" title="Anurag Kashyap">Anurag Kashyap</a> and used in the soundtrack with few arrangements made by Trivedi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> An additional track was released on March 1, it was the remixed version of hit cabaret number of the 1970s <i>Hungama Ho Gaya</i> sung by <a href="/wiki/Asha_Bhosle" title="Asha Bhosle">Asha Bhosle</a> for the film, <i><a href="/wiki/Anhonee_(1973_film)" title="Anhonee (1973 film)">Anhonee</a></i> (1973) was remixed by Amit Trivedi for the film, with additional vocals by <a href="/wiki/Arijit_Singh" title="Arijit Singh">Arijit Singh</a>. It was shot at Club NL, in Amsterdam, where a Bollywood number was played for the first time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The copyright of the soundtrack was purchased by <a href="/wiki/T-Series" title="T-Series">T-Series</a>; long-time collaborator of <a href="/wiki/Viacom_18_Motion_Pictures" title="Viacom 18 Motion Pictures">Viacom 18 Motion Pictures</a>, the distributor of the film, in March 2013 along with that of few other films.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>The first track, <i>London Thumakda</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Wedding_music" title="Wedding music">wedding song</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dance_music" title="Dance music">dance number</a> was released on Viacom 18 Motion pictures official YouTube channel on 6 January 2014;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> followed by <i>Badra Bahar</i> and <i>O Gujariya</i> on 17 January 2014<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> and 24 January 2014 respectively.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> The music album was physically released on 2 February 2014 at the <a href="/wiki/Kala_Ghoda_Arts_Festival" title="Kala Ghoda Arts Festival">Kala Ghoda Arts Festival</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a> and is an official part of the festival.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Musiclauch_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-Musiclauch-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Preceding the launch was, Trvidei's first live stage performance along with his livewire team at Cross Maidan including Queen's songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Musiclauch_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-Musiclauch-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> The entire cast of the film along with the director were present at the launch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Musiclauch_16-2"><a href="#cite_note-Musiclauch-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> The song saw a digital release on 30 January 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-iTunes_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-iTunes-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The album received mostly positive reviews. In MusicAlouds review, the soundtrack was described as one that "treads familiar soundscapes and yet produces an engaging set of songs".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MA_18-0"><a href="#cite_note-MA-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> Rajiv Vijayankar for <a href="/wiki/Bollywood_Hungama" title="Bollywood Hungama">Bollywood Hungama</a> passed the album as "pleasant album by Amit Trivedi" picking <i>London Thumakda</i>, <i>Badra bahaar</i> and <i>Harjaiyaan</i> as the best of the eight tracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BH_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-BH-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> Mohar Basu stated in her review for <a href="/wiki/Koimoi" title="Koimoi">Koimoi</a> that "Every song in this album is a winner in its own right. Amit Trivedi is unusual and is known to create his own norm by refusing to fall into any random moulds of stereotypical understanding. Once again after last year’s poetry in music, Trivedi has evolved another album that matches the marvel expected of him. Giving us amusing, delightful, enthralling composition Trivedi’s memorable music from Queen wins my heart. I am going with a 4/5 and wishes that [<i><a href="/wiki/Sic" title="Sic">sic</a></i>] Trivedi is more frequent in the commercial music scenario".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_performance">Chart performance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Queen_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chart performance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two songs, <i>Jugni</i> and <i>Kinare</i> from the soundtrack debuted at number 10 and eight at Planet Bollywood Top 10 chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> The album itself debuted at number ten on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes" title="ITunes">ITunes India</a> Top 100 Indian chart.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> The album ranked number four on the "Top 5 Albums of the year" list compiled by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times_of_India" title="The Times of India">The Times of India</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
