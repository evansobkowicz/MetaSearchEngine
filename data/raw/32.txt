<h2><a href="https://mcliterature.wordpress.com/2012/03/08/the-little-prince-2-2/" rel="bookmark">The Little Prince 2</a></h2>
<small class="date">
<span class="date_day">8</span>
<span class="date_month">03</span>
<span class="date_year">2012</span>
</small>
<div class="entry">
<p><a href="https://mcliterature.files.wordpress.com/2011/04/fox.jpg"><img alt="" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-60" height="215" src="https://mcliterature.files.wordpress.com/2011/04/fox.jpg?w=300&amp;h=215" title="fox" width="300"/></a>In <em>The Little Prince, </em>the fox and the prince have a conversation about what makes that one particular boy, or that one particular fox, or that one particular flower special.</p>
<p>In the end, the fox told the prince: “Here is my secret.  It’s quite simple:  One sees clearly only with the heart.  Anything else essential is invisible to the eyes.”</p>
<p>In your own words, what does that quote mean to you?  Apply it to your own life or other pieces of literature if you need to give examples in your explanation.</p>
<p><em>Be sure to type out your answers in complete sentences.  Be sure to back up your ideas with some examples, evidence, or proof.  Be sure to check back and see what other people say too.  Be sure to comment on what they say.</em></p>
<p>Your answer should be 3-5 paragraphs long and give examples from both The Little Prince and other places (other books, movies, the real world…)</p>

<h2><a href="https://mcliterature.wordpress.com/2012/02/28/the-little-prince-2012/" rel="bookmark">The Little Prince – 2012</a></h2>
<small class="date">
<span class="date_day">28</span>
<span class="date_month">02</span>
<span class="date_year">2012</span>
</small>
<div class="entry">
<p><em><a href="https://mcliterature.files.wordpress.com/2011/02/the_little_prince.jpg"><img alt="" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-44" src="https://mcliterature.files.wordpress.com/2011/02/the_little_prince.jpg?w=288&amp;h=300" title="The_Little_Prince"/></a></em>The Little Prince has traveled around to a bunch of small planets.  On each of them he met one strange person, and almost all of those people made him think that grown-ups are pretty strange.  As we talked about in class, the characters in the book may be symbols that represent real people or groups of people in the real world.  The author may be using this story to tell us something about those real people by showing you these ridiculous characters in the book.</p>
<p>In the comments section below, share with the class what you think Antoine de Saint Exupery might be trying to say.  Who do the king, the vain man, the drunkard, the business man, the lamplighter, and the geographer really represent.</p>
<p><em>Be sure to type out your answers in complete sentences.  Be sure to back up your ideas with some examples, evidence, or proof from both the story and real life.  Your answers should be 3-4 paragraphs (each paragraph between 5-7 sentences).</em></p>
<p><em> Be sure to check back and see what other people say too.  Be sure to comment on what they say.  Part of your assignment is to comment on what at least 2 other kids wrote.  </em></p>
</div>

<h2><a href="https://mcliterature.wordpress.com/2011/04/20/the-little-prince-4/" rel="bookmark">The Little Prince 4</a></h2>
<small class="date">
<span class="date_day">20</span>
<span class="date_month">04</span>
<span class="date_year">2011</span>
</small>
<div class="entry">
<p><a href="https://mcliterature.files.wordpress.com/2011/04/the-little-prince1.gif"><img alt="" class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-66" height="132" src="https://mcliterature.files.wordpress.com/2011/04/the-little-prince1.gif?w=300&amp;h=132" title="the-little-prince1" width="300"/></a>We talked about <em>The Little Prince </em>for nearly two weeks.  While we discussed the story, we decided that we understand the story is about a little boy from a far away planet, two active volcanoes, one extinct volcano (but you never know), baobabs, a flower, a sheep, a king, a fox, a pilot, and a snake.  We also realized that the story is not about boys, planets, volcanoes (extinct or not), trees with funny sounding names, flowers, sheep, kings, foxes, any sort of airplanes, or snakes.</p>
<p>If <em>The Little Prince </em>is about all those things, but isn’t about any of those things… What is <em>The Little Prince </em>about?</p>
<p><em>Be sure to type out your answers in complete sentences.  Be sure to back up your ideas with some examples, evidence, or proof.  Be sure to check back and see what other people say too.  Be sure to comment on what they say. </em></p>

<h2><a href="https://mcliterature.wordpress.com/2011/04/12/the-little-prince-2/" rel="bookmark">The Little Prince 2</a></h2>
<small class="date">
<span class="date_day">12</span>
<span class="date_month">04</span>
<span class="date_year">2011</span>
</small>
<div class="entry">
<p><a href="https://mcliterature.files.wordpress.com/2011/04/snake.jpg"><img alt="" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-50" src="https://mcliterature.files.wordpress.com/2011/04/snake.jpg?w=217&amp;h=300" title="snake"/></a>After traveling to several planets and meeting a series of grown-ups that baffled the him, The little prince has finally arrived on Earth.  He’s met a tiny snake that claims to be more powerful than a king, a flower that seems terribly unaware, an echo that confuses the prince, and a whole garden full of roses that make the prince feel ordinary and unworthy of being a prince.</p>
<p>In his short time on Earth, the little prince has already begun to seem like a different person.  He is no longer as optimistic, as happy, or as curious.</p>
<p>Tell me how has Earth, and these creatures he’s met there, changed the little prince?</p>
<p>Is this a good thing or a bad thing?  Why?</p>
<p><em>Be sure to type out your answers in complete sentences.  Be sure to  back up your ideas with some examples, evidence, or proof.  Be sure to  check back and see what other people say too.  Be sure to comment on  what they say. </em></p>
