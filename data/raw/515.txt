<h1 class="entry-title">A Little Fish and a Big God: Finding Nemo and the Prodigal Son</h1>
<div class="entry-meta">

<div class="entry-content">
<div addthis:title="A Little Fish and a Big God: Finding Nemo and the Prodigal Son " addthis:url="http://kierstiplog.com/nemo-and-prodigal-son/" class="addthis_toolbox addthis_default_style addthis_32x32_style"><a class="addthis_button_facebook"></a><a class="addthis_button_twitter"></a><a class="addthis_button_email"></a><a class="addthis_button_pinterest_share"></a><a class="addthis_button_compact"></a><a class="addthis_counter addthis_bubble_style"></a></div><p><span style="font-size: medium;">My sister and I just watched <i>Finding Nemo</i> for the, ahem, first time. (I know, I know, it’s a classic, please don’t hate us.)</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;"> </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">But we really enjoyed it, and la<a href="http://kierstiplog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Finding-Nemo.jpg"><img alt="Finding Nemo" class="size-medium wp-image-1539 alignright" height="300" src="http://kierstiplog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Finding-Nemo-207x300.jpg" width="207"/></a>st night after we finished it, my sister said to me, “It’s kind of like a Prodigal Son/parallel to Jesus story, you know?”</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;"> </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">“How?” I asked. I was a little dense last night.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;"> </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">She started explaining, and my eyes got wide. I think she was a little astonished at my ecstatic reaction until I exclaimed, “I’ve been wondering what to blog about!”</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;"> </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">Yes. I get excited about these things.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;"> </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">But really, parallels do abound in this little movie. A young son who gets fed up with his father and runs away…only to land in big trouble. A father who never gives up on him…though Marlin’s unwearying search for little Nemo brings to mind even more the shepherd searching for his lost sheep in that same set of parables Jesus told. Relentless, unwavering, pursuing through countless dangers and hardships, never giving up <a href="http://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Luke+15&amp;version=NASB">“until he finds it.”</a></span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;"> </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">That is God’s heart toward His children. Our Father’s heart toward us. </span></p>
<table cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">

<p><span style="font-size: medium;">As we approach this Father’s Day, I’m thankful for my loving and faithful human Daddy. I know many do not have that. But whatever our relationships with our earthly fathers have been, I hope this weekend turns our hearts toward the Father who ultimately gave us life, and the depth and height of His love that pursues us, even when the trouble we’re in is of our own making.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;"> </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">I’m so thankful His grace isn’t based on what I deserve, just as Marlin’s love for little Nemo wasn’t. He pursued him and constantly risked his life just because Nemo was his son…and he loved him.</span></p>
<div class="wp-caption alignleft" id="attachment_1540" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://kierstiplog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Prodigal-Son-Murillo.jpg"><img alt="Prodigal Son Murillo" class="size-medium wp-image-1540 " height="275" src="http://kierstiplog.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/06/Prodigal-Son-Murillo-300x275.jpg" width="300"/></a><p class="wp-caption-text"></p></div></div></article></div></div></div></div></div></div></div></div></div></div></div></div></body></html> <span style="font-size: medium;">The Return of the Prodigal Son by Murillo</span>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;"> </span></p>
<blockquote><p><span style="font-size: medium;">“He has not dealt with us according to our sins,</span><br/>
<span style="font-size: medium;"> Nor rewarded us according to our iniquities.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">For as high as the heavens are above the earth,</span><br/>
<span style="font-size: medium;"> So great is His lovingkindness toward those who fear Him.</span><br/>
<span style="font-size: medium;"> As far as the east is from the west,</span><br/>
<span style="font-size: medium;"> So far has He removed our transgressions from us.</span><br/>
<span style="font-size: medium;"> Just as a father has compassion on his children,</span><br/>
<span style="font-size: medium;"> So the Lord has compassion on those who fear Him.</span><br/>
<span style="font-size: medium;"> For He Himself knows our frame;</span><br/>
<span style="font-size: medium;"> He is mindful that we are but dust.”</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">~Psalm 103:10-14</span></p></blockquote>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;"> </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: medium;">What are some movies you think of around Father’s Day? What stories remind you of our Father’s love for us? Please comment and share!</span></p>
