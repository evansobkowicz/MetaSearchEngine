<p>The discography of <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a> starts on July 19, 1954, with the release of his first commercial <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a>, and ends in February 1978. Presley's final album, <i><a href="/wiki/Elvis_in_Concert_(album)" title="Elvis in Concert (album)">Elvis in Concert</a></i>, and final two singles, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/My_Way_(song)" title="My Way (song)">My Way</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Unchained_Melody" title="Unchained Melody">Unchained Melody</a>", although released after his death are considered part of the discographical canon as they were projects Presley himself knew about in the planning stages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> All other releases are included in the posthumous sections, and obviously all <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compact_Disc" title="Compact Disc">compact disc</a> <a href="/wiki/Box_set" title="Box set">box sets</a> were also released after Presley's death. Without including album reissues, during this period of 1954 to 1978 American Presley releases consisted of 108 singles, 30 <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended-play singles</a>, 70 albums on <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>, four albums on the <a href="/wiki/Pickwick_Records" title="Pickwick Records">Pickwick</a> budget label, and five singles on the <a href="/wiki/Sun_Records" title="Sun Records">Sun Records</a> label.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="RIAA_certifications_and_sales">RIAA certifications and sales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elvis_Presley_singles_discography&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: RIAA certifications and sales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) began to certify <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">gold albums and singles</a> in 1958,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> and Elvis Presley's first RIAA award, a gold single for "<a href="/wiki/Hard_Headed_Woman" title="Hard Headed Woman">Hard Headed Woman</a>", was certified on August 11, 1958.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> In August 1992, he was awarded with 110 gold, platinum and multi-platinum albums and singles, the largest presentation of gold and platinum record awards in history.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> According to Presley's record label, RCA, he is <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_music_artists" title="List of best-selling music artists">the best selling artist of all time</a>, with sales over 1 billion records worldwide in all formats (600 million in the United States alone).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AP_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-AP-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
