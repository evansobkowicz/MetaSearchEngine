<p><b>René Angélil</b>, <span style="font-size:85%;"><a href="/wiki/Order_of_Canada" title="Order of Canada">CM</a> <a href="/wiki/National_Order_of_Quebec" title="National Order of Quebec">OQ</a></span> (born January 16, 1942) is a <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> singer and manager. He is the husband and former manager of singer <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine Dion</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gazette_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Gazette-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ren%C3%A9_Ang%C3%A9lil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Angélil was born in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Qu%C3%A9bec" title="Québec">Québec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, of a father of <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syrian</a> descent and a Canadian mother of <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanese</a> origin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> <span class="citation-needed-content" style="background-color: #fff9f9; color: DarkSlateGray; border: 1px solid #ffdcdc;">He is <a href="/wiki/Melkite_Greek_Catholic_Church" title="Melkite Greek Catholic Church">Melkite Catholic</a> and studied at College Saint-Viateur (high school), in Outremont and at College André-Grasset (post secondary), in Montreal.</span><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ren%C3%A9_Ang%C3%A9lil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Angélil started out as a pop singer in the 1960s in Montréal, Angélil formed a pop rock group, "The Baronets", with childhood friends Pierre Labelle and Jean Beaulne. They were known as "Québec's <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Smithtones&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Smithtones (page does not exist)">Smithtones</a>"<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> by the name of Les Baronnets. This group had some hits in the 1960s as <a class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r6SC-s2I8cY" rel="nofollow"><i>"C'est fou, mais c'est tout"</i></a> on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> (a translation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beatles" title="Beatles">Beatles</a>' song "<a href="/wiki/Hold_Me_Tight" title="Hold Me Tight">Hold Me Tight</a>") catering to the Quebec market for translations of English-language pop hits from the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> or the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. After the dissolution of the group, Angélil and best friend <a href="/wiki/Guy_Cloutier" title="Guy Cloutier">Guy Cloutier</a> began managing artists.</p>
<p>Together they managed the career of two successful Québec entertainers <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Simard" title="René Simard">René Simard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ginette_Reno" title="Ginette Reno">Ginette Reno</a>, among many other pop stars of the time. They parted ways in 1981 to each become solo managers. At the end of 1980 (not long after being replaced as Ginette's manager) René heard <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine Dion</a>'s <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Demo_tape" title="Demo tape">demo tape</a>. In 1987 and 1988, he won the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9lix_Award" title="Félix Award">Félix Award</a> for Manager of the Year.</p>
<p>In July 2008, Angélil was named the director for the reality television show <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Star_Academie" title="Star Academie">Star Academie</a>'</i>s fourth season, scheduled to start in February 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ren%C3%A9_Ang%C3%A9lil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1966, he married his first wife, Denyse Duquette, the couple had one son, Patrick Angélil (born 1968). In 1974, he married singer Manon Kirouac, called Anne Renée on the stage:<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> the couple had two children, Jean Pierre Angélil (born 1974) and Anne Marie Angélil (born 1977), whose husband is Quebec singer-songwriter Marc Dupré. On December 17, 1994, he married <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine Dion</a>. Celine met him when she was twelve. Dion is 26 years younger than Angélil. Their son, René Charles Angélil, was born January 25, 2001. On August 18, 2009, the couple announced that Dion was pregnant with their second child. It was announced three months later that Dion had miscarried. On May 30, 2010, Celine Dion's representative announced that Dion was 14 weeks pregnant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> On October 23, 2010, Dion and Angélil welcomed twin boys. The boys were named Eddy Angelil, after <a href="/wiki/Eddy_Marnay" title="Eddy Marnay">Eddy Marnay</a>, who produced Dion's first five albums, and Nelson Angelil, after <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Mandela" title="Nelson Mandela">Nelson Mandela</a>. Angélil is fan of <a href="/wiki/National_Hockey_League" title="National Hockey League">NHL</a> hockey, and he is a good friend of former <a href="/wiki/Colorado_Avalanche" title="Colorado Avalanche">Colorado Avalanche</a> president and general manager, <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Lacroix_(ice_hockey_b._1948)" title="Pierre Lacroix (ice hockey b. 1948)">Pierre Lacroix</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Illness">Illness</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ren%C3%A9_Ang%C3%A9lil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Illness">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Angélil suffered a heart attack in 1992 at age 50.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> He was engaged to Celine Dion and staying with her at a Los Angeles hotel at the time.</p>
<p>In April 1998 doctors in Dallas confirmed that a lump in Angélil's neck was malignant<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. He underwent surgery as Dion was booked for her Texas concert<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. He suffered from <a href="/wiki/Esophageal_cancer" title="Esophageal cancer">throat cancer</a> (resulting in Dion temporarily halting her career to be with him), but has recovered<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. He appeared in the video for <a href="/wiki/Simple_Plan" title="Simple Plan">Simple Plan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Save_You_(Simple_Plan_song)" title="Save You (Simple Plan song)">"Save You"</a> as a survivor of cancer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2009, Angélil reportedly underwent a heart-related medical procedure to deal with "arterial blockage". The procedure had been "planned for months" and was "not heart surgery".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Angélil underwent another surgery December 2013 for throat cancer,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> with doctors even replacing parts of his tongue to give him the ability to speak<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<p>The following June, Angélil stepped down as Dion's manager to focus on his health. However, he has still been involved in business decisions pertaining to his wife's career.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Angélil is portrayed in an unauthorized TV <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biopic" title="Biopic">biopic</a> about Celine Dion by <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> actor <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Colantoni" title="Enrico Colantoni">Enrico Colantoni</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Extortion_attempt">Extortion attempt</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ren%C3%A9_Ang%C3%A9lil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Extortion attempt">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Angélil was accused by Yun Kyeong Kwon Sung of sexual assault stemming from an incident in 2000 that took place in a <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley" title="Las Vegas Valley">Las Vegas</a> hotel. These charges were later investigated by Las Vegas police. Although Angélil eventually paid $2 million to settle, Angélil never admitted to doing anything wrong, always maintaining the settlement was to avoid negative publicity that might upset his wife <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine Dion</a>.</p>
<p>Yun and her husband, Ae Ho Kwon, were later arrested in 2003 and charged with trying to extort money from Angélil over a false <a href="/wiki/Rape" title="Rape">rape</a> claim. The couple were convicted in 2005.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> However, in early 2008, the Nevada Supreme Court reversed both the convictions of husband and wife as to the extortion and conspiracy charges, on grounds that the couple had not been allowed to submit evidence showing that the sexual assault actually occurred. However, the Court upheld the jury verdict as to the charges of soliciting a bribe. The court held that they should have had a chance to produce evidence of rape against Angélil in their trial.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Defamation_suits">Defamation suits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ren%C3%A9_Ang%C3%A9lil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Defamation suits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2001, Angélil, along with his wife Celine Dion, filed a $5 million defamation lawsuit against Quebec tabloid <i>Allo Vedettes'</i> for printing a story alleging the couple paid $5,000 to rent out the swimming pool of Caesars Palace in Las Vegas so that Celine could sunbathe topless and René could go skinny dipping, allegations they strenuously denied<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>. They later withdrew the case, saying it was not a priority in light of the terrorist events of <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">9/11</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Gambling_fame">Gambling fame</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ren%C3%A9_Ang%C3%A9lil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Gambling fame">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Angélil is an avid <a href="/wiki/Poker" title="Poker">poker</a> player, having qualified for the 2005 <a href="/wiki/World_Series_of_Poker_Tournament_of_Champions" title="World Series of Poker Tournament of Champions">World Series of Poker Tournament of Champions</a>, and finishing <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_poker_terms#I" title="List of poker terms">in the money</a> at the 2007 Mirage Poker Showdown event on the <a href="/wiki/World_Poker_Tour" title="World Poker Tour">World Poker Tour</a>, a series of high stakes tournaments. Angélil is also rumored to be a dedicated gambler away from the poker table. He reportedly gambles upwards of $1 million a week at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caesar%27s_Palace" title="Caesar's Palace">Caesar's Palace</a>, and keeps a line of credit for the same amount at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bellagio_(resort_and_casino)" title="Bellagio (resort and casino)">Bellagio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>A frenzy of speculation as to how much Angélil spends in casinos was created when Jan Jones, former Las Vegas mayor and high ranking executive at Harrah's Communications, was quoted by a reporter from the London-based newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/The_Observer" title="The Observer">The Observer</a></i> as saying, "Celine Dion's husband is a big gambler. He probably gambles $1 million in a week, but he can afford to." A retraction was later issued by Caesar's Palace, which is owned by Harrah's, stating that Jan Jones' previous allegations were totally without merit.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
