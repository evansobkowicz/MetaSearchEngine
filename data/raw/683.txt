<p>Listeners can give to UNICEF simply by clicking on the iconic Swedish pop group’s ballad Chiquitita</p>
</div>
</div>
</div><!-- END .excerpt -->
<div class="the_article">
<div class="row">
<div class="small-12 columns">
<div class="article">
<p><a href="http://cdn.psfk.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/Abba-abba-64000_1024_768.jpg"></a></p>
<p>As charities continue forging new partnerships to take advantage of the Internet and make giving as accessible as possible, the musicians behind the pop band ABBA have found a unique way to empower music lovers to support a vital cause. Working with <a href="https://unicef.se/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">UNICEF Sweden</a>, the four members of the group have agreed to donate all royalties of their 1979 hit Chiquitita to the global children’s’ rights charity, including those earned via clicks on Spotify, Youtube, and iTunes. These donations will be designated for projects that help impoverished young women throughout the world find education, gainful employment, and stability in a new initiative called <a href="http://pressplaytogive.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Press Play to Give.</a></p>
<p>ABBA has a long relationship with UNICEF, dating back to a performance of Chiquitita at a charity event in 1979. In the 35 years since, part of the song’s proceeds have gone to UNICEF. With Press Play to Give, ABBA the Museum and UNICEF launch a revamped fundraising campaign, which includes full royalty contributions on behalf of the band members, Universal Music Publishing, and Polar Music, along with covers of Chiquitita by rising artists to bring the song to new audiences. A new cover will be released every 5 years until 2019 to mark the song’s 40th anniversary, and proceeds from these covers will also go to UNICEF. The first of these covers comes from Swedish singer-songwriter <a href="http://www.laleh.se/us/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Laleh</a>, who performed her version of Chiquitita at the UN General Assembly on November 20th.</p>
<p><a href="http://cdn.psfk.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/tumblr_nfck5bIXIp1u0ls4bo1_1280.jpg" rel="attachment wp-att-258480"><img alt="tumblr_nfck5bIXIp1u0ls4bo1_1280.jpg" class="aligncenter wp-image-258480" height="468" src="http://cdn.psfk.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/12/tumblr_nfck5bIXIp1u0ls4bo1_1280-468x468.jpg" width="468"/></a></p>
<p>Chiquitita’s message of healing and solidarity is fitting as a call to action for the empowerment of young women. As ABBA guitarist and vocalist Björn Ulvaeus explains,</p>
<blockquote><p>I think that “Chiquitita” can serve as a symbol for all the girls and young women around the world who are denied their rightful place in society. We can help educate them, enabling them to stand up to any cultural or religious patriarchy that holds them back.</p></blockquote>
<p>Press Play to Give is one aspect of larger platform Right for Rights, which hopes to transform charitable giving by requesting intellectual rights holders donate all or a portion of their rights to fund a cause, rather than seeking direct donations. <a href="http://pressplaytogive.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank"><br/>
</a></p>
<p><a href="http://pressplaytogive.com/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Press Play to Give</a></p>
