<h3><a name=".2.1.9">
<span id="swiki.2.1.9.1">

Why does the wardrobe provide a gateway to Narnia?

</span>
</a></h3>
<div class="tabs" id="control.2.1.9.tab">
<img class="spin" src="/images/swiki/spin.gif"/>
<button class="b_edit first" id="b.2.1.9.edit" onclick="Swiki.bounce('control.2.1.9.tab','edit',{layout:'.2.1.9',path:'.2.1.9'})"><span>Edit</span></button><button class="b_history" id="b.2.1.9.history" onclick="Swiki.bounce('control.2.1.9.tab','history',{layout:'.2.1.9',path:'.2.1.9'})"><span>History</span></button><button class="b_delete last" id="b.2.1.9.delete" onclick="Swiki.bounce('control.2.1.9.tab','delete',{confirm:'1',layout:'.2.1.9',path:'.2.1.9'})"><span>Delete</span></button>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="display">
<p id="swiki.2.1.9.2">

The answer is found in the prequel story <i>The Magician's Nephew</i>. At the end of <i>The Magician's Nephew</i> Digory is given an apple by Aslan as a reward for showing his faith in accomplishing a task enforced on him. Once back in the real world, Digory uses the apple to heal his sick mother, and then later buries the apple (<i>along with the magic rings which originally took Digory into Narnia</i>) in the garden of his childhood home. The tree that subsequently grew was thus imbued with Narnian magic. When the tree was blown down in a storm many years later, Digory used the wood to build the wardrobe. The wardrobe seen in the movie is carved with symbols and pictures representing many of the key events in <i>The Magican's Nephew</i>.

</p>
</div>
</div>
<div id="swiki.2.1.17">
<div id="swiki.2.1.17.view">
<div class="section">
<h3><a name=".2.1.17">
<span id="swiki.2.1.17.1">

What does the Professor throw to Peter at the end of the film?

</span>
</a></h3>
<div class="tabs" id="control.2.1.17.tab">
<img class="spin" src="/images/swiki/spin.gif"/>
<button class="b_edit first" id="b.2.1.17.edit" onclick="Swiki.bounce('control.2.1.17.tab','edit',{layout:'.2.1.17',path:'.2.1.17'})"><span>Edit</span></button><button class="b_history" id="b.2.1.17.history" onclick="Swiki.bounce('control.2.1.17.tab','history',{layout:'.2.1.17',path:'.2.1.17'})"><span>History</span></button><button class="b_delete last" id="b.2.1.17.delete" onclick="Swiki.bounce('control.2.1.17.tab','delete',{confirm:'1',layout:'.2.1.17',path:'.2.1.17'})"><span>Delete</span></button>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="display">
<p id="swiki.2.1.17.2">

It is the cricket ball from earlier in the film. Prior to all four children entering Narnia for the first, Edmund had accidently smashed a window and knocked over a suit of armour during a game of Cricket outside. The children then fled, being pursued as they thought by Mrs Macready, and came to hide in the wardrobe. After finally leaving Narnia at the end of the movie, the professor playfully throws the cricket ball to Peter, indicating that it had been him, not Mrs Macready, who had followed them into the Wardrobe room. This scene also helps communicate the idea that absolutely no time had passed in the real world whilst the children had been in Narnia.

</p>
</div>
</div>

<h3><a name=".2.1.4">
<span id="swiki.2.1.4.1">

Why did they change the name of the wolf?

</span>
</a></h3>
<div class="tabs" id="control.2.1.4.tab">
<img class="spin" src="/images/swiki/spin.gif"/>
<button class="b_edit first" id="b.2.1.4.edit" onclick="Swiki.bounce('control.2.1.4.tab','edit',{layout:'.2.1.4',path:'.2.1.4'})"><span>Edit</span></button><button class="b_history" id="b.2.1.4.history" onclick="Swiki.bounce('control.2.1.4.tab','history',{layout:'.2.1.4',path:'.2.1.4'})"><span>History</span></button><button class="b_delete last" id="b.2.1.4.delete" onclick="Swiki.bounce('control.2.1.4.tab','delete',{confirm:'1',layout:'.2.1.4',path:'.2.1.4'})"><span>Delete</span></button>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="display">
<p id="swiki.2.1.4.2">

They didn't change it. However, some American editions of the Narnia books have several alterations, one of them being that the character "Maugrim" was renamed to "Fenris Ulf." Since 1994, the US publications of the book have come into line with the original UK edition. The movie producers went with the original UK name "Maugrim" rather than the altered US name "Fenris Ulf."

</p>
</div>
</div>

<p id="swiki.2.1.5.2">

It's a reference to a line from the book: "<i>And shortly after that they looked into a room that was quite empty except for one big wardrobe; the sort that has a looking glass in the door. There was nothing else in the room at all except a dead blue bottle on the window-sill.</i>"
