<p><b>Priscilla Ann Presley</b> (née <b>Wagner</b>; born May 24, 1945) is an American actress and <a href="/wiki/Business_magnate" title="Business magnate">business magnate</a>. She is the ex-wife of the late singer <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a> as well as co-founder and former chairwoman of <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley_Enterprises" title="Elvis Presley Enterprises">Elvis Presley Enterprises</a> (EPE), the company that turned <a href="/wiki/Graceland" title="Graceland">Graceland</a> into one of the top tourist attractions in the United States. In her acting career, Presley starred with <a href="/wiki/Leslie_Nielsen" title="Leslie Nielsen">Leslie Nielsen</a> in the three successful <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naked_Gun" title="Naked Gun">Naked Gun</a></i> films, and played the role of <a href="/wiki/Jenna_Wade" title="Jenna Wade">Jenna Wade</a> on the long-running television series <i><a href="/wiki/Dallas_(1978_TV_series)" title="Dallas (1978 TV series)">Dallas</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancestry_and_early_life">Ancestry and early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Priscilla_Presley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ancestry and early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Priscilla Presley's maternal grandfather, Albert Henry Iversen (born in 1899 in <a href="/wiki/Egersund" title="Egersund">Egersund</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>), emigrated to the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrk.no_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-nrk.no-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> He married Lorraine (who was of <a href="/wiki/Ulster_Scots_people" title="Ulster Scots people">Scots-Irish</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_people" title="English people">English</a> descent).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Finstad_1997_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Finstad_1997-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Their daughter, Anna Lillian Iversen, was born in March 1926.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Finstad_1997_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Finstad_1997-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Later she was called – or her name was changed to – Ann.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Finstad_1997_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Finstad_1997-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> At the age of 19, she gave birth to Priscilla.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Finstad_1997_2-3"><a href="#cite_note-Finstad_1997-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> They both still have cousins in Norway.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vg.no_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-vg.no-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> In a letter to the City Hall of Egersund, Ann asked for information about their relatives, and wrote that Priscilla was interested in knowing about them; Priscilla's parents then visited family members in Norway in 1992.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vg.no_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-vg.no-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Priscilla has a square named after her in Egersund – Priscilla Presleys plass.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-egersund.org_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-egersund.org-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The area is in the street outside the house where her grandfather was born and lived.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-egersund.org_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-egersund.org-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Priscilla and her family were invited by the Lord Mayor of Egersund to the opening ceremony of Priscilla Presleys plass,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrk.no_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-nrk.no-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> which took place August 23, 2008, but were unable to go due to Lisa Marie being pregnant.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-egersund.org_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-egersund.org-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Priscilla's biological father was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Navy" title="US Navy">US Navy</a> pilot James Wagner.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Finstad_1997_2-4"><a href="#cite_note-Finstad_1997-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> His parents were Kathryn and Harold Wagner of German descent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Finstad_1997_2-5"><a href="#cite_note-Finstad_1997-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> On August 10, 1944, at the age of 23, he married Priscilla's mother; they had been dating for more than three years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Finstad_1997_2-6"><a href="#cite_note-Finstad_1997-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> He was killed in a plane crash while returning home on leave when Priscilla was six months old.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Finstad_1997_2-7"><a href="#cite_note-Finstad_1997-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._22_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._22-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> When Priscilla discovered this "family secret"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> while rummaging through an old wooden box of family keepsakes, she was encouraged by her mother to keep it from the other children as she feared it would "endanger our family closeness".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._22_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._22-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1948, her mother met a <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">United States Air Force</a> officer named Paul Beaulieu, from <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, Canada. The couple were married within a year. Beaulieu took over the raising of Priscilla, and was the only father she would ever know. Over the next few years Priscilla grew up quickly, helping to care for the growing family as her father's Air Force career moved them from <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">Connecticut</a> to <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a> to <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">Maine</a>. In her own words, she described herself during this period as "a shy, pretty little girl unhappily accustomed to moving from base to base every two or three years".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._19_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._19-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> Priscilla later recalled that she felt uncomfortable moving so often because she never knew if she could make friends for life, or even if she would fit in with the new people she met on each move.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._19_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._19-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1956, the Beaulieus moved to and settled in <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a>, but soon her father was transferred to <a href="/wiki/Wiesbaden" title="Wiesbaden">Wiesbaden</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">Germany</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_.281985.29.2C_p._24_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_.281985.29.2C_p._24-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> Priscilla was "crushed" by this news, and after finishing Junior High her fears of leaving her friends behind and making new ones were once again at the forefront of her mind.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_.281985.29.2C_p._24_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_.281985.29.2C_p._24-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_in_Germany">Life in Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Priscilla_Presley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Life in Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Initially the Beaulieus stayed at the Helene Hotel when they arrived in Germany, but after three months, living there became too expensive and they looked for a place to rent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._25_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._25-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> The family settled in a large apartment in a "vintage building constructed long before <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>". Soon after moving in, the Beaulieus realized that it was a <a href="/wiki/Brothel" title="Brothel">brothel</a>, but due to scarce housing, they were forced to remain there.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._25_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._25-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Feeling like an outsider once again due to her lack of understanding of the German language, Priscilla frequented the Eagles Club; a place where American families would gather to have dinner and be entertained.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._25_9-2"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._25-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> It was "within walking distance" of their apartment, and proved to be an important discovery for Priscilla.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._25_9-3"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._25-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> She would go there "every day after school" and listen to the jukebox while writing letters back home to her friends in Austin.</p>
<p>At the Eagles Club, Priscilla met Currie Grant, a young American Air Force recruit whose Commanding Officer knew Priscilla's father.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._26_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._26-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Through Grant, she came to meet Elvis Presley. Grant stated that he was a good friend of the singer and that he and his wife would visit him quite often.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._26_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._26-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Priscilla states that Grant offered to introduce her to Elvis, but being cautious and skeptical of such a claim about his friendship with Presley, she said she would have to ask her parents. Currie Grant states that it was Priscilla who asked him to introduce her, as she was curious about meeting Presley.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Finstad_1997_2-8"><a href="#cite_note-Finstad_1997-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>However, over the next two weeks Grant met with Priscilla's parents and assured them that she would be well chaperoned.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._26_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._26-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_with_Elvis">Life with Elvis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Priscilla_Presley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Life with Elvis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Germany">Germany</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Priscilla_Presley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Germany">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Elvis and Priscilla met on September 13, 1959,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> during a party at Elvis' home in <a href="/wiki/Bad_Nauheim" title="Bad Nauheim">Bad Nauheim, Germany</a>, while he was serving in the army.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clutton_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Clutton-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Despite her being 14 years old, she made a huge impression on Elvis with her much older appearance.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldman-306_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Goldman-306-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> Elvis allegedly regressed to acting like an "awkward, embarrassed" boy-next-door figure in front of her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldman-306_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-Goldman-306-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> However, by the end of the evening he had managed to compose himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldman-306_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-Goldman-306-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Despite Priscilla's parents being angered by her late return home during that first meeting and insisting that she would never meet Elvis again,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldman-307_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Goldman-307-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> his eagerness for another meeting, and his promise never to bring her home late again,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldman-307_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Goldman-307-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> led them to relent. They were frequently together until Elvis left Germany in March 1960. After Elvis left Germany, Priscilla was inundated with requests for interviews from media outlets around the world.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley62_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Presley62-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> She received fan mail from Elvis fans, some nice and some not so nice, as well as mail from "lonesome G.I.'s".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley62_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Presley62-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Convinced she would never see Elvis again, and with rumors of his ongoing relationship with <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Sinatra" title="Nancy Sinatra">Nancy Sinatra</a> flying around the gossip magazines, Priscilla resigned herself to the belief that her whirlwind romance was over.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley62_15-2"><a href="#cite_note-Presley62-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Move_to_Graceland">Move to Graceland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Priscilla_Presley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Move to Graceland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After Elvis' return to America, the couple stayed in contact over the phone, though they would not see each other again until the summer of 1961, when Priscilla's parents agreed to let her visit for two weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clutton_12-1"><a href="#cite_note-Clutton-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Priscilla's parents allowed her to go only if Elvis would pay for a first-class round trip, arrange for her to be chaperoned at all times, and that she write home every day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley62_15-3"><a href="#cite_note-Presley62-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> Elvis agreed to all these demands and Priscilla flew to Los Angeles. Elvis told her that they were going to <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas" title="Las Vegas">Las Vegas</a> and, to throw her parents off the scent, he had Priscilla write a postcard for every day they would be gone so that they could be mailed from Los Angeles by a member of his staff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enc415_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-enc415-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>It was during this visit, while on a trip to Las Vegas, that Priscilla first took amphetamines and sleeping pills to keep up with Elvis' lifestyle.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> After another visit at Christmas, Priscilla's parents finally let her move to America for good in March 1962.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clutton_12-2"><a href="#cite_note-Clutton-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Victor415_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Victor415-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> Part of the agreement was that she would attend an all-girls Catholic school, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Immaculate_Conception_Cathedral_High_School_(Memphis,_Tennessee)" title="Immaculate Conception Cathedral High School (Memphis, Tennessee)">Immaculate Conception High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Memphis,_Tennessee" title="Memphis, Tennessee">Memphis, Tennessee</a>, and live with Elvis' father and his stepmother in a separate house on the Graceland estate until she graduated from high school in 1963. Part of the agreement also was that they would eventually marry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Victor415_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-Victor415-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> However, after a few weeks, she was moved into Graceland to be with Elvis, although her parents did eventually agree to her living there if Elvis promised to marry her.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Clutton_12-3"><a href="#cite_note-Clutton-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Priscilla later said, "The move was natural. I was there all the time anyway."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Priscilla was always keen to go to Hollywood with Elvis, but he kept telling her that he was too busy for that and she was made to stay in Memphis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enc415_17-1"><a href="#cite_note-enc415-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> During the filming of <i><a href="/wiki/Viva_Las_Vegas" title="Viva Las Vegas">Viva Las Vegas</a></i>, Elvis began an affair with his co-star <a href="/wiki/Ann-Margret" title="Ann-Margret">Ann-Margret</a>. When Priscilla read of these reports in the press, she confronted Elvis. He told her that they were simply rumors to promote the film and that she should not believe everything that she read in the press.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enc415_17-2"><a href="#cite_note-enc415-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> For the next few years, Elvis would have intimate relationships with many of his leading ladies and co-stars, all the while denying their existence to Priscilla. Eventually she was allowed to visit him in Hollywood, but her visits were kept short.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enc415_17-3"><a href="#cite_note-enc415-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Marriage_and_pregnancy">Marriage and pregnancy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Priscilla_Presley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Marriage and pregnancy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shortly before Christmas 1966, Elvis proposed to Priscilla. Accounts suggest that Priscilla threatened to take her story to the press if Elvis refused to marry her, and that her father threatened to have Elvis charged under the <a href="/wiki/Mann_Act" title="Mann Act">Mann Act</a>; "taking a minor across state lines for sexual purposes".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-down313_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-down313-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Colonel_Tom_Parker" title="Colonel Tom Parker">Colonel Parker</a>, Elvis' manager, also attempted to encourage him to marry by reminding him about his RCA "morals clause" within his record contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-down313_21-1"><a href="#cite_note-down313-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> Priscilla suggested in a 1973 interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Ladies%27_Home_Journal" title="Ladies' Home Journal">Ladies' Home Journal</a></i> that she and Elvis were quite happy to just live together, but "at that time it wasn't nice for people to [just] live together".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> Accounts by Elvis' cook, Alberta, claim that he was so upset about the wedding that she caught him crying about it one day. When she asked why he didn't just cancel the wedding if it upset him so much, he replied "I don't have a choice."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-down313_21-2"><a href="#cite_note-down313-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> Marty Lacker, a close friend to Elvis, has also spoken about Elvis' reluctance to marry,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-down313_21-3"><a href="#cite_note-down313-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> while others such as <a href="/wiki/Joe_Esposito_(author)" title="Joe Esposito (author)">Joe Esposito</a> have asserted that Elvis was excited to marry Priscilla.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-down313_21-4"><a href="#cite_note-down313-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>They got married on May 1, 1967, at the Aladdin Hotel in Las Vegas. The wedding, arranged by Parker to maximize publicity, featured very few guests and was over in only eight minutes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-down313_21-5"><a href="#cite_note-down313-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> It was followed by a quick press conference and a $10,000 breakfast reception, attended by friends, family, and business associates from MGM, RCA, and the William Morris Agency.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-down313_21-6"><a href="#cite_note-down313-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> The wedding caused rifts between Elvis and several of his <a href="/wiki/Memphis_Mafia" title="Memphis Mafia">closest friends</a> who were not invited to the actual wedding ceremony.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gural261_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-gural261-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Red_West" title="Red West">Red West</a>, especially, was furious about the situation. He and his wife had been personally invited by Elvis to Las Vegas for the wedding, had dressed for the occasion, and at the last minute were told that they would not be present.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gural261_23-1"><a href="#cite_note-gural261-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> For Red, who had been with Elvis since the beginning of his rise to fame and had given Elvis the role of best man at his own wedding, this was enough of an insult that he decided to quit his job working for Elvis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gural261_23-2"><a href="#cite_note-gural261-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> Many other friends of Elvis were also disappointed and held resentment towards him for many years to follow, although they mainly blamed Parker for their exclusion rather than Elvis himself.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gural261_23-3"><a href="#cite_note-gural261-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Following the reception, Elvis and Priscilla boarded a private jet and enjoyed a short honeymoon in Palm Springs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-down313_21-7"><a href="#cite_note-down313-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> On May 4, they flew back to Memphis and retreated to their private ranch, just over the Mississippi state line, for a three-week break.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gural261_23-4"><a href="#cite_note-gural261-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> Many of Elvis' inner circle joined them, although for the most part the couple were left alone and were able to enjoy each other's company without the intrusion of the Memphis Mafia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gural261_23-5"><a href="#cite_note-gural261-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> Priscilla reveled in her chance to be a proper wife; cooking, cleaning, and washing for her husband. "I loved playing house" she later remarked, adding "Here was an opportunity to take care of him myself. No maids or housekeepers to pamper us."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gural261_23-6"><a href="#cite_note-gural261-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> In an attempt to heal rifts, Elvis and Priscilla held another reception at Graceland on May 29 for the friends and family who were unable to attend the original ceremonies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gural261_23-7"><a href="#cite_note-gural261-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Soon after, Priscilla found out that she was pregnant. She was upset at such an early pregnancy, certain that it would destroy the closeness she had finally found with Elvis.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-down323_24-0"><a href="#cite_note-down323-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> She had asked him earlier if she could take birth control pills, but Elvis had insisted they weren't perfected yet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-down323_24-1"><a href="#cite_note-down323-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> She considered abortion, and even discussed it with Elvis at one point, but both decided they could not live with themselves if they had gone through with it.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-down323_24-2"><a href="#cite_note-down323-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> Their only child, <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Marie_Presley" title="Lisa Marie Presley">Lisa Marie</a>, was born exactly nine months after their wedding, on February 1, 1968.</p>
<p>Priscilla wrote in 1985 autobiography, <i><a href="/wiki/Elvis_and_Me" title="Elvis and Me">Elvis and Me</a></i>, that around the time Elvis was filming <i><a href="/wiki/Live_a_Little,_Love_a_Little" title="Live a Little, Love a Little">Live a Little, Love a Little</a></i> (1968) she began taking private dance lessons. She found herself deeply attracted to the instructor, known simply as Mark in the book, and she confesses to having a short affair.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._262_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._262-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> She implies regret, however, saying "I came out of it realizing I needed much more out of my relationship with Elvis."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._262_25-1"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._262-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Despite Priscilla's affair and Elvis' on-and-off relationships with his co-stars and leading ladies, the first few years they were married seemed a happy time for the couple. However, when Elvis' career took off again after his <a href="/wiki/Elvis_(1968_TV_program)" title="Elvis (1968 TV program)">1968 television special</a>, he was constantly touring and playing in Las Vegas. Elvis had also been seeing other women on and off, often leaving Priscilla at home with Lisa Marie. Due to Elvis' being away so often, the marriage soured.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Separation_and_divorce">Separation and divorce</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Priscilla_Presley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Separation and divorce">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Elvis was a keen karate student, and persuaded Priscilla to take it up. Priscilla thought it was a good idea, as it would pass the time she spent alone if she had a hobby to concentrate on, and she was also keen to share in Elvis' interests.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> Following suggestions from Elvis, Priscilla began taking lessons from <a href="/wiki/Mike_Stone_(karate)" title="Mike Stone (karate)">Mike Stone</a>, a karate instructor she had met in 1972 backstage at one of Elvis' concerts.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> She soon began an affair with him.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._297_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._297-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> Priscilla states in her book, "My relationship with Mike had now developed into an affair. I still loved Elvis greatly, but over the next few months I knew I would have to make a crucial decision regarding my destiny."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._297_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._297-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> She later states "Elvis must have perceived my new restlessness."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> A couple of months later, she said that Elvis had requested to see her in his hotel suite. It was then that she writes in her book that Elvis "forcefully made love to me", and said, "This is how a real man makes love to his woman."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> She later stated in an interview that she regretted her choice of words in describing the incident, and said it had been an overstatement. She went on to say following the incident, "what really hurt was that he was not sensitive to me as a woman and his attempt at reconciliation had come too late" suggesting that his actions were a deliberate attempt at reconciliation or compensation for his lack of sexual interest in Priscilla which had been a source of hurt and discontent for her for years. Priscilla states in her book "He had mentioned to me before we were married that he had never been able to make love to a woman who had a child"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._259_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._259-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> and she later expressed the personal repercussions of their sexual dysfunction "I am beginning to doubt my own sexuality as a woman. My physical and emotional needs were unfulfilled."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Presley_1985.2C_p._259_31-1"><a href="#cite_note-Presley_1985.2C_p._259-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> After this incident, Priscilla summarized "this was not the gentle, understanding man I grew to love."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Elvis and Priscilla separated on February 23, 1972, and filed for legal separation on July 26.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> To avoid Priscilla's having to make her home address available on the public records and therefore risking the security of both her and Lisa Marie,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> Elvis filed for divorce on January 8, 1973<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup> and it was finalized on October 9, 1973.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guralnick.2FJorgensen.2C_p._329_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guralnick.2FJorgensen.2C_p._329-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> The couple agreed to share custody of their daughter and Priscilla was awarded an outright cash payment of $725,000 as well as spousal support, child support, 5% of Elvis' new publishing companies and half the income from the sale of their Beverly Hills home.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guralnick.2FJorgensen.2C_p._329_36-1"><a href="#cite_note-Guralnick.2FJorgensen.2C_p._329-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup> Originally the couple had agreed upon a much smaller settlement; a $100,000 lump payment, $1,000 a month spousal support, and $500 a month child support.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gural474_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-gural474-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> Notably, Priscilla was keen to make it on her own and prove that her marriage to Elvis was not about money.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gural474_37-1"><a href="#cite_note-gural474-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> However, soon afterwards, her new lawyers had persuaded her to up her demands, pointing out that a star of Elvis' stature could easily afford more for his ex-wife and child.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-down373_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-down373-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Priscilla and Elvis remained close, leaving the courthouse on the day of their divorce hand in hand.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-down373_38-1"><a href="#cite_note-down373-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> Although Priscilla was granted full custody of Lisa Marie, she promised Elvis that he could have access to her any time he wished.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business">Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Priscilla_Presley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley_Enterprises" title="Elvis Presley Enterprises">Elvis Presley Enterprises</a></div>
<p>In 1973, after her split from Elvis, Presley set up a joint venture with her friend and stylist Olivia Bis. Together they opened a clothing boutique in Los Angeles called Bis &amp; Beau.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bis_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-bis-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> Elvis was supportive of Priscilla's venture, and even contacted several friends in public relations to help with promotion for the launch.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> In a 1973 interview to promote the opening of the store, Priscilla said, "After the separation, I had to make up my mind about what I wanted to do, and since I had worked with Olivia for such a long time on my own clothes, I decided to try it professionally. We both do the designing for the shop, and have people who sew for us."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bis_39-1"><a href="#cite_note-bis-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> The shop was a successful venture, with celebrity clients including <a href="/wiki/Cher" title="Cher">Cher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lana_Turner" title="Lana Turner">Lana Turner</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barbra_Streisand" title="Barbra Streisand">Barbra Streisand</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Wood" title="Natalie Wood">Natalie Wood</a> shopping there regularly.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup> The shop closed in 1976.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-az17_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-az17-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>After Elvis' death in 1977,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup> Priscilla acted as executor for his only heir, Lisa Marie (who was then only 9).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ENC_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-ENC-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> Graceland itself cost $500,000 a year in upkeep, and expenses had dwindled Lisa Marie's inheritance to only $1 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ENC_44-1"><a href="#cite_note-ENC-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> Taxes due on the property and other expenses due came to over $500,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ENC_44-2"><a href="#cite_note-ENC-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> Faced with having to sell Graceland, Priscilla examined other famous homes/museums. She hired a CEO, Jack Soden, to turn Graceland into a tourist attraction. Graceland was opened to the public on June 7, 1982. Presley's gamble paid off; only four weeks after opening Graceland's doors, the estate made back all the money it had invested.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ENC_44-3"><a href="#cite_note-ENC-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> Priscilla became the chairwoman and president of <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley_Enterprises" title="Elvis Presley Enterprises">Elvis Presley Enterprises</a> (EPE), stating she would remain in the position until Lisa Marie reached 21 years of age. Under Presley's guidance, the enterprise's fortunes soared and eventually the trust grew to be worth over $100 million.</p>
<p>In 1988, Presley launched her own range of fragrances, and followed this up with a range of linen.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enc415_17-4"><a href="#cite_note-enc415-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> She has also helped produce a couple of films, including <i><a href="/wiki/Breakfast_with_Einstein" title="Breakfast with Einstein">Breakfast with Einstein</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Graceland" title="Finding Graceland">Finding Graceland</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enc415_17-5"><a href="#cite_note-enc415-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> In September 2000, Presley was elected to the board of directors at <a href="/wiki/Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer" title="Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer">Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acting_career">Acting career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Priscilla_Presley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Acting career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Hal_B._Wallis" title="Hal B. Wallis">Hal B. Wallis</a>, a Hollywood producer who had financed many of Elvis' earlier films, had shown an interest in signing Priscilla to a contract.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bodyguardbook226_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-bodyguardbook226-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> Elvis, however, had no intentions of allowing his wife to have a career of any kind; in his opinion, albeit a very common one at the time, "a woman's place was in the home looking after her man".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bodyguardbook226_46-1"><a href="#cite_note-bodyguardbook226-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> Priscilla had shown an interest in dancing and modeling, but her knowledge of Elvis' opinion meant that she kept them as hobbies instead of pursuing them as careers. She did get the opportunity to model for a local store once, but when Elvis heard about it, he asked her to give it up.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enc415_17-6"><a href="#cite_note-enc415-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Presley had originally been offered a role as one of the angels on <i><a href="/wiki/Charlie%27s_Angels" title="Charlie's Angels">Charlie's Angels</a></i>. She turned down the role because she disliked the show.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> Priscilla made her television debut as co-host of <i>Those Amazing Animals</i> in 1980.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-az17_42-1"><a href="#cite_note-az17-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1983, she got her first chance to act professionally on a season 2 episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fall_Guy" title="The Fall Guy">The Fall Guy</a></i> titled "Manhunter".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enc415_17-7"><a href="#cite_note-enc415-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> She then found a role in a television film titled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Love_is_Forever_(film)" title="Love is Forever (film)">Love is Forever</a></i>, starring alongside <a href="/wiki/Michael_Landon" title="Michael Landon">Michael Landon</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-landon_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-landon-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> Although she was treated well by most of the cast and crew, and her acting was praised by several of her co-stars, she found Landon difficult to work with on set.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-landon_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-landon-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup> After the television film aired, Presley landed the role of <a href="/wiki/Jenna_Wade" title="Jenna Wade">Jenna Wade</a> (originally played by <a href="/wiki/Morgan_Fairchild" title="Morgan Fairchild">Morgan Fairchild</a>) on the popular <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prime-time_television" title="Prime-time television">prime-time</a> soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Dallas_(1978_TV_series)" title="Dallas (1978 TV series)">Dallas</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-az17_42-2"><a href="#cite_note-az17-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> She played the role of Wade for five years, leaving the show in 1988 to focus on other parts.</p>
<p>Presley has appeared in a few feature film roles, most notably in 1988 when she starred opposite Leslie Nielsen in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Naked_Gun:_From_the_Files_of_Police_Squad!" title="The Naked Gun: From the Files of Police Squad!">The Naked Gun: From the Files of Police Squad!</a></i> as Jane Spencer. Presley would go on to act in the next two movies in the series: <i><a href="/wiki/The_Naked_Gun_2%C2%BD:_The_Smell_of_Fear" title="The Naked Gun 2½: The Smell of Fear">The Naked Gun 2½: The Smell of Fear</a></i> (1991) and <i><a href="/wiki/Naked_Gun_33%E2%85%93:_The_Final_Insult" title="Naked Gun 33⅓: The Final Insult">Naked Gun 33⅓: The Final Insult</a></i> (1994). All three films were solid at the box office. In the late 1990s, she was a special guest star on the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Melrose_Place" title="Melrose Place">Melrose Place</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Touched_by_an_Angel" title="Touched by an Angel">Touched by an Angel</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Spin_City" title="Spin City">Spin City</a></i></p>
<p>Presley made her <a href="/wiki/Pantomime" title="Pantomime">pantomime</a> debut in <i>Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs</i> at the <a href="/wiki/New_Wimbledon_Theatre" title="New Wimbledon Theatre">New Wimbledon Theatre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wimbledon,_London" title="Wimbledon, London">Wimbledon</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, Christmas 2012, starring opposite <a href="/wiki/Warwick_Davis" title="Warwick Davis">Warwick Davis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup> Presley said she was "delighted" by the opportunity, which she described as an "honor".</p>
<p>Presley reprised her role as the Wicked Queen in <i>Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs</i> at the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Opera_House" title="Manchester Opera House">Opera House</a> in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, Christmas 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Charity_work_and_activism">Charity work and activism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Priscilla_Presley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Charity work and activism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 2003, Presley has been the Ambassador of the <a href="/wiki/Dream_Foundation" title="Dream Foundation">Dream Foundation</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Santa_Barbara,_California" title="Santa Barbara, California">Santa Barbara</a>-based wish-granting organization for terminally ill adults and their families.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Presley is a devoted member of the <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Scientology" title="Church of Scientology">Church of Scientology</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enc415_17-8"><a href="#cite_note-enc415-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> and speaks publicly for the religion's anti-psychiatry <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Front_group" title="Front group">front group</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Citizens_Commission_on_Human_Rights" title="Citizens Commission on Human Rights">CCHR</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2013, Presley spoke out against the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ag-Gag#Tennessee_.282013.29" title="Ag-Gag">Tennessee Ag-Gag Bill</a> in a letter to Tennessee Governor <a href="/wiki/Bill_Haslam" title="Bill Haslam">Bill Haslam</a>. Presley cited her and Elvis' love of horses and expressed her concern that the bill would hinder animal cruelty investigations and reduce protections for horses and other farm animals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Priscilla_Presley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Romantically, Presley has had a number of serious relationships since her divorce in 1973. Immediately afterwards, she lived with karate instructor <a href="/wiki/Mike_Stone_(karate)" title="Mike Stone (karate)">Mike Stone</a>. She then had a six-year live-in relationship with actor and model <a href="/wiki/Michael_Edwards_(actor)" title="Michael Edwards (actor)">Mike Edwards</a>, until he began developing feelings for the teenaged Lisa Marie. Edwards recounts the tale of their relationship in his book <i>Priscilla, Elvis and Me</i> (1988).</p>
<p>Presley's longest relationship has been with Italian screenwriter-director <a href="/wiki/Marco_Garibaldi" title="Marco Garibaldi">Marco Garibaldi</a>, with whom she lived for 22 years. Their son, <a href="/wiki/Navarone_Garibaldi" title="Navarone Garibaldi">Navarone</a>, was born on March 1, 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2006, they ended their relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-enc415_17-9"><a href="#cite_note-enc415-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> She was later rumored to be dating <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Lythgoe" title="Nigel Lythgoe">Nigel Lythgoe</a>, 2006 to 2009.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-express_57-0"><a href="#cite_note-express-57"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Presley, who is single, said in a December 2012 interview that "L.A. is a very difficult town to find someone, because people are too fickle and they have other agendas."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58"><a href="#cite_note-58"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
