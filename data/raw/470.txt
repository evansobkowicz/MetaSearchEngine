<p><i><b>Jurassic Park</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a> novel written by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Crichton" title="Michael Crichton">Michael Crichton</a>. Often considered a <a href="/wiki/Cautionary_tale" title="Cautionary tale">cautionary tale</a> on unconsidered biological tinkering in the same spirit as <a href="/wiki/Mary_Shelley" title="Mary Shelley">Mary Shelley</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Frankenstein" title="Frankenstein">Frankenstein</a></i>, it uses the metaphor of the collapse of an <a href="/wiki/Amusement_park" title="Amusement park">amusement park</a> showcasing <a href="/wiki/Genetic_engineering" title="Genetic engineering">genetically recreated</a> <a href="/wiki/Dinosaur" title="Dinosaur">dinosaurs</a> to illustrate the mathematical concept of <a href="/wiki/Chaos_theory" title="Chaos theory">chaos theory</a> and its philosophical implications. A sequel titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lost_World_(Crichton_novel)" title="The Lost World (Crichton novel)">The Lost World</a></i>, also written by Crichton, was published in 1995. In 1997, both novels were re-published as a single book titled <i>Michael Crichton's Jurassic World</i>, unrelated to the film of the same name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1993, <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">Steven Spielberg</a> adapted the book into the blockbuster film <i><a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park_(film)" title="Jurassic Park (film)">Jurassic Park</a>.</i> The book's sequel, <i>The Lost World</i>, was also adapted by Spielberg into a <a href="/wiki/The_Lost_World:_Jurassic_Park" title="The Lost World: Jurassic Park">film</a> in 1997. A <a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park_III" title="Jurassic Park III">third film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Joe_Johnston" title="Joe Johnston">Joe Johnston</a> and released in 2001 drew several elements, themes and scenes from both books that were ultimately not utilized in either of the previous films, such as the aviary and boat scenes. A <a href="/wiki/Jurassic_World" title="Jurassic World">fourth entry</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Colin_Trevorrow" title="Colin Trevorrow">Colin Trevorrow</a> is set for theatrical release on June 12, 2015.</p>
<p>The novel began as Crichton conceived a <a href="/wiki/Screenplay" title="Screenplay">screenplay</a> about a graduate student who recreates a dinosaur in 1983.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Eventually, given his reasoning that genetic research is expensive and "there is no pressing need to create a dinosaur", Crichton concluded that it would emerge from a "desire to entertain", leading to a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wildlife_park" title="Wildlife park">wildlife park</a> of extinct animals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Originally it was told from the point of view of a child, but Crichton changed it as everyone who read the draft felt it would be better if told by an adult.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_summary">Plot summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jurassic_Park_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The narrative begins in August 1989 by slowly tying together a series of incidents involving strange animal attacks in <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a> and on fictional Isla Nublar,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> the main setting for the story. One of the species, a strange, small, lizard-like creature with three toes (thought at the time to be a new species of basilisk lizard), is identified later as a <i><a href="/wiki/Procompsognathus" title="Procompsognathus">Procompsognathus</a></i>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paleontologist" title="Paleontologist">Paleontologist</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alan_Grant_(Jurassic_Park)" title="Alan Grant (Jurassic Park)">Alan Grant</a> and his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paleobotanist" title="Paleobotanist">paleobotanist</a> graduate student, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Jurassic_Park_characters#Dr._Ellie_Sattler" title="List of Jurassic Park characters">Ellie Sattler</a>, are abruptly whisked away by billionaire <a href="/wiki/List_of_Jurassic_Park_characters#John_Hammond" title="List of Jurassic Park characters">John Hammond</a> — founder and chief executive officer of International Genetic Technologies, or InGen — for a weekend visit to a "biological preserve" he has established on a remote island off the coast of Costa Rica.</p>
<p>Upon arrival, the preserve is revealed to be Jurassic Park, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theme_park" title="Theme park">theme park</a> showcasing <a href="/wiki/Cloning#Artificial_cloning_of_organisms" title="Cloning">cloned</a> dinosaurs. The animals have been recreated using <a href="/wiki/Ancient_DNA" title="Ancient DNA">damaged dinosaur DNA</a> found in blood inside of gnats and ticks fossilized and preserved in <a href="/wiki/Amber" title="Amber">amber</a>. Gaps in the genetic code have been filled in with reptilian, avian, or amphibian DNA. To control the population, all specimens on the island are <a href="/wiki/Lysine" title="Lysine">lysine</a>-deficient females. Hammond proudly touts InGen's advances in <a href="/wiki/Genetic_engineering" title="Genetic engineering">genetic engineering</a> and shows his guests through the island's vast array of automated systems.</p>
<p>Recent events in the park have spooked Hammond's investors. To placate them, Hammond means for Grant and Sattler to act as fresh consultants. They stand in counterbalance to a well-known mathematician and <a href="/wiki/Chaos_theory" title="Chaos theory">chaos theorist</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Jurassic_Park_characters#Dr._Ian_Malcolm" title="List of Jurassic Park characters">Ian Malcolm</a>, and a lawyer representing the investors, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Jurassic_Park#Donald_Gennaro" title="List of characters in Jurassic Park">Donald Gennaro</a>. Both are pessimistic about the park's prospects. Malcolm, having been consulted before the park's creation, is especially emphatic in his prediction that the park will collapse, as it is an unsustainable simple structure bluntly forced upon a <a href="/wiki/Complex_system" title="Complex system">complex system</a>.</p>

<p>Countering Malcolm's dire predictions with youthful energy, Hammond groups the consultants with his grandchildren, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Jurassic_Park#Tim_Murphy" title="List of characters in Jurassic Park">Tim</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Jurassic_Park#Alexis_.22Lex.22_Murphy" title="List of characters in Jurassic Park">Alexis "Lex" Murphy</a>. While touring the park with the children, Grant finds a <i><a href="/wiki/Velociraptor" title="Velociraptor">Velociraptor</a></i> eggshell, which seems to prove Malcolm's earlier assertion that the dinosaurs have been breeding against the geneticists' design. Malcolm suggests a flaw in their method of analyzing dinosaur populations, in that motion detectors were set to search only for the expected number of creatures in the park and not for any higher number. The park's controllers are reluctant to admit that the park has long been operating beyond their constraints. Malcolm also points out the height distribution of the <i><a href="/wiki/Procompsognathus" title="Procompsognathus">Procompsognathus</a></i> forms a <a href="/wiki/Normal_distribution" title="Normal distribution">Gaussian distribution</a>, the curve of a breeding population, rather than the distinctive pattern that a population reared in batches ought to display.</p>
<p>In the midst of this, the corrupt chief programmer of Jurassic Park's controlling software, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Jurassic_Park#Dennis_Nedry" title="List of characters in Jurassic Park">Dennis Nedry</a>, attempts <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Corporate_espionage" title="Corporate espionage">corporate espionage</a> for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Jurassic_Park#Dr._Lewis_Dodgson" title="List of characters in Jurassic Park">Lewis Dodgson</a>, a geneticist and agent of InGen's archrival, Biosyn. By activating a <a href="/wiki/Backdoor_(computing)" title="Backdoor (computing)">backdoor</a> he wrote into the park's computer system, Nedry manages to shut down its security systems and steal frozen embryos, two for each of the park's fifteen species. He then attempts to smuggle them out to a contact waiting at an auxiliary dock deep in the park. However, during a sudden <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">tropical storm</a>, he exits his stolen vehicle to get his bearings and is killed by a <i><a href="/wiki/Dilophosaurus" title="Dilophosaurus">Dilophosaurus</a></i>. Without Nedry to reactivate the park's security, the electrified fences remain off and dinosaurs escape. The adult <i><a href="/wiki/Tyrannosaurus" title="Tyrannosaurus">Tyrannosaurus rex</a></i> attacks the guests on tour, while the juvenile rex attacks public relations manager <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Jurassic_Park#Ed_Regis" title="List of characters in Jurassic Park">Ed Regis</a>, killing him. In the aftermath, Grant and the children become lost in the park.</p>
<p>Malcolm is gravely injured during the incident, but is soon found by Gennaro and park game warden <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Jurassic_Park#Robert_Muldoon" title="List of characters in Jurassic Park">Robert Muldoon</a> and spends the remainder of the novel slowly dying as, between lucid lectures and morphine-induced rants, he tries to help those in the main compound understand their predicament and survive.</p>

<p>The park's upper management — engineer and park supervisor <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Jurassic_Park#John_Arnold" title="List of characters in Jurassic Park">John Arnold</a>, chief geneticist <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Jurassic_Park#Dr._Henry_Wu" title="List of characters in Jurassic Park">Henry Wu</a>, Muldoon, and Hammond — struggle to return power to the park, while the veterinarian, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Jurassic_Park#Dr._Gerry_Harding" title="List of characters in Jurassic Park">Dr. Harding</a>, takes care of the injured Malcolm. For a time they manage to get the park largely back in order, restoring the computer system by shutting down and restarting the power and resetting the system. Unfortunately, a series of errors on their part soon plunge the park into greater chaos. During their time trying to restore the park to working order, they fail to notice that the system has been running on auxiliary power since the restart; this power soon runs out, shutting the park down a second time. Furthermore, since the auxiliary generators did not produce enough electricity to power the fences, the fences were not reactivated when the system was reset, meaning all the fences — including the holding pen containing the park's <i>Velociraptors</i>, quarantined due to their intelligence and aggression — had been offline the whole time. Escaping their enclosure, the <i>Velociraptors</i> kill Wu and Arnold and injure Muldoon, Gennaro, and Harding. Meanwhile, Grant and the children slowly make their way back to the Visitor Center by rafting down the jungle river, carrying news that several young <i>Velociraptors</i>, bred and raised in the island's wilds, were on board the <i>Anne B</i>, the island's supply ship, when it departed for the mainland.</p>
<p>While Ellie distracts the <i>Velociraptors</i>, Grant manages to reactivate the park's main power. After escaping from several <i>Velociraptors</i>, Grant, Gennaro, Tim, and Lex are able to make it to the control room, where Tim is able to contact the <i>Anne B</i> and tell them to return to the island. The survivors are then able to organize themselves and eventually save their own lives. Word soon reaches them that the crew of the <i>Anne B</i> has discovered and killed the <i>Velociraptor</i> stowaways.</p>
<p>Gennaro tries to order the island destroyed as a dangerous asset, but Grant rejects his authority, claiming that even though they cannot control the island, they have a responsibility to understand just what happened and how many dinosaurs have already escaped to the mainland. Grant, Ellie, Muldoon, and (against his will) Gennaro set out into the park to find the wild <i>Velociraptor</i> nests and compare hatched eggs with the island's revised population tally. Cautious in this pursuit, they emerge unharmed. Meanwhile, Hammond, taking a walk around the park and contemplating making a park improving on his previous mistakes, hears the T-Rex roar and falls down a hill where he is eaten by a pack of <i>Procompsognathus</i>. With regard to the dinosaurs' breeding, it eventually transpires that using frog DNA to fill gaps in the dinosaurs' genetic code enabled a measure of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sequential_hermaphrodites" title="Sequential hermaphrodites">dichogamy</a>, in which some of the female animals somehow changed into males in response to the all-female environment.</p>
<p>In the conclusion, before boarding helicopters, the group tell the Costa Rican air force that the dinosaurs had been killing people. The air force then say that the island is dangerous and releases napalm over the island, destroying it and the dinosaurs. It is implied that Malcolm dies. Survivors of the incident are indefinitely detained by the United States and Costa Rican governments at a hotel. Weeks later, Grant is visited by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_characters_in_Jurassic_Park#Dr._Martin_.22Marty.22_Guitierrez" title="List of characters in Jurassic Park">Dr. Martin Guitierrez</a>, an American doctor who lives in Costa Rica and has found a <i>Procompsognathus</i> corpse. Guitierrez informs Grant that an unknown pack of animals has been migrating through the Costa Rican jungle, eating lysine-rich crops and chickens. He also informs Grant that none of them, with the possible exception of Tim and Lex, are going to leave any time soon.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jurassic_Park_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The book became a bestseller and Michael Crichton's signature novel. It also received favorable reviews by critics. The novel became even more famous following the release of the <a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park_(film)" title="Jurassic Park (film)">film</a>, which has grossed more than US$1 billion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
