<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en"><span dir="auto">Music of <i>The Lord of the Rings</i> film series</span></h1>
<div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
<div id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">
					Jump to:					<a href="#mw-head">navigation</a>, 					<a href="#p-search">search</a>
</div>
<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p>The music of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_(film_series)" title="The Lord of the Rings (film series)">The Lord of the Rings</a></i> film series was composed, orchestrated, conducted and produced by <a href="/wiki/Howard_Shore" title="Howard Shore">Howard Shore</a>. Shore wrote many hours of music for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings_(film_series)" title="The Lord of the Rings (film series)">The Lord of the Rings</a></i>, 10 hours of which have been released in the Complete Recordings CD/DVD boxed sets. Additional music, including alternate and unused compositions, was released with the book <i>The Music of the Lord of the Rings films</i> in 2010. Shore composed the music in an emotional, operatic way, threading through the scores over 90 identified <a href="/wiki/Leitmotif" title="Leitmotif">leitmotifs</a>, which are categorized by the Middle-earth cultures to which they relate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Shore began his work on the music for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Fellowship_of_the_Ring" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring">The Fellowship of the Ring</a></i> in late 2000 and recorded the first pieces of music (the <a href="/wiki/Moria_(Middle-earth)" title="Moria (Middle-earth)">Moria</a> sequence) in spring of 2001. Additional music for the extended DVD version was recorded in March 2002. A similar pattern was followed for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Two_Towers" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers">The Two Towers</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Return_of_the_King" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King">The Return of the King</a></i>, with the final sessions taking place in <a href="/wiki/Watford" title="Watford">Watford</a> on March 20, 2004.</p>
<p>The music was performed primarily by the <a href="/wiki/London_Philharmonic_Orchestra" title="London Philharmonic Orchestra">London Philharmonic Orchestra</a> and the <a href="/wiki/London_Voices" title="London Voices">London Voices</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Symphony_Orchestra" title="New Zealand Symphony Orchestra">New Zealand Symphony Orchestra</a> contributing some of the early Moria music. A wide variety of instrumental and vocal soloists, including members of the films' cast, contributed to the scores as well.</p>
<p>The scores for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Fellowship_of_the_Ring" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring">The Fellowship of the Ring</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Return_of_the_King" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King">The Return of the King</a></i> won <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Awards</a> in 2002 and 2004. The latter film also won an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Oscar</a> statuette for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Song" title="Academy Award for Best Song">Best Song</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globe Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Original_Score" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Original Score">Best Original Score</a> and <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Original_Song" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Original Song">Best Original Song</a>. Shore's music for <i>The Lord of the Rings</i> has become the most successful composition of his career and one of the most popular motion picture scores in history.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Principal_leitmotifs">Principal leitmotifs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_of_The_Lord_of_the_Rings_film_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Principal leitmotifs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_appearance_in_The_Fellowship_of_the_Ring">First appearance in <i>The Fellowship of the Ring</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_of_The_Lord_of_the_Rings_film_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: First appearance in The Fellowship of the Ring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>"The History of the Ring": a minor-key string melody plays over the <i>Lord of the Rings</i> title card for all three films. Shore hints in the production commentary for the film that it is a theme pertaining, in fact, to <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">Gondor</a>.</li>
<li>"The Seduction of the Ring": sung by the boy choir</li>
<li>"The Shire and the Hobbits", also known as "<a href="/wiki/Concerning_Hobbits" title="Concerning Hobbits">Concerning Hobbits</a>": the theme for the Shire. On the soundtrack, it starts with a solo tin whistle, followed by a solo fiddle and then the complete orchestra repeating the same phrase. The track then quietens down and the solo tin whistle plays a second melody, again followed by the orchestra. The track ends with the violin and then the tin whistle repeating the first theme. There are four settings:
<ul>
<li>"The Rural Setting": usually heard only in the Shire</li>
<li>"The Pensive Setting": a reflective version of the theme</li>
<li>"The Hymn Setting": first heard when Frodo and Sam set out</li>
<li>"A Hobbit's Understanding": used when the Hobbits come to understand the hardships and struggles of their journey</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>"The Black Rider": a dark piece which features almost all flats.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Middle-earth" title="Middle-earth">Middle-earth</a> motif</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Isengard" title="Isengard">Isengard</a>: usually accompanied by a 5/4 rhythm in the percussion.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rivendell" title="Rivendell">Rivendell</a>: a theme formed by a series of <a href="/wiki/Arpeggio" title="Arpeggio">arpeggios</a>, usually in strings and often with choral accompaniment.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lothl%C3%B3rien" title="Lothlórien">Lothlórien</a>: a soft, ethereal chorus accompanied by <a href="/wiki/Cello" title="Cello">cellos</a>. A more hard-edged, brass-driven version of the theme appears in the second film during the battle of <a href="/wiki/Helm%27s_Deep" title="Helm's Deep">Helm's Deep</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nazg%C3%BBl" title="Nazgûl">Nazgûl</a> motif</li>
<li>"The Evil of the Ring": played on the <a href="/wiki/Rhaita" title="Rhaita">rhaita</a>, associated with <a href="/wiki/Sauron" title="Sauron">Sauron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mordor" title="Mordor">Mordor</a>.</li>
<li>"The Fellowship" theme: a heroic, sweeping piece using principally brass and orchestra. It is heard in various versions during the first film, but after "The Bridge of Khazad-dûm," the last time that the entire Fellowship is together, it can only be heard infrequently and sparingly throughout the next two films until the remainder of The Fellowship charge the Black Gate "For Frodo".</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_appearance_in_The_Two_Towers">First appearance in <i>The Two Towers</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_of_The_Lord_of_the_Rings_film_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: First appearance in The Two Towers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>"<a href="/wiki/Gollum" title="Gollum">Gollum</a>'s Menace": featuring the <a href="/wiki/Cimbalom" title="Cimbalom">cimbalom</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/%C3%89owyn" title="Éowyn">Éowyn</a>'s theme: using <a href="/wiki/Perfect_fifth" title="Perfect fifth">open fifth</a> intervals (lacking the third of the chord – this means that the chords cannot be defined as major or minor).</li>
<li>"The <a href="/wiki/Rohan_(Middle-earth)" title="Rohan (Middle-earth)">Rohan</a> Fanfare": featuring the <a href="/wiki/Hardanger_fiddle" title="Hardanger fiddle">hardanger fiddle</a>.</li>
<li>"Evenstar": a slow, serene piece.</li>
<li>"The White Rider in Nature": A sweeping, full-orchestra crescendo with heavy strings.</li>
<li>Nature's Reclamation (commonly referred to by fans as the Nature theme), played during the "The Last March of the Ents" part of the movie, probably used as a way of interpreting the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ents#The_Last_March_of_the_Ents" title="Ents">Ents' march</a> as Nature's way of fighting back.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The theme was first heard while <a href="/wiki/Gandalf" title="Gandalf">Gandalf</a> was trapped in Isengard when a moth serving as a messenger for the Eagles arrived at the pinnacle of Orthanc; the moth and the Eagles represent nature here as well. A more heroic and robust brass version is heard in the third film when the Rohirrim leave Edoras for Dunharrow and also during Théoden's "Ride now!" speech before the Rohirrim charge at Pelennor Fields; it is possible this is meant to show the union between Men and Nature via the horses of Rohan.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_appearance_in_The_Return_of_the_King">First appearance in <i>The Return of the King</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_of_The_Lord_of_the_Rings_film_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: First appearance in The Return of the King">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Realm of <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">Gondor</a> Theme: the basic version of this was established during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Council_of_Elrond" title="Council of Elrond">Council of Elrond</a> in the <i>The Fellowship of the Ring</i>, and also existed in the Extended Edition of <i>The Two Towers</i> DVD. It occurs in several variants, including Realm of Gondor in Decline and Realm of Gondor in Ascension.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Minas_Tirith" title="Minas Tirith">Minas Tirith</a>/Silver Trumpets Theme: the basic version of this was established during a scene with <a href="/wiki/Aragorn" title="Aragorn">Aragorn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Boromir" title="Boromir">Boromir</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lothl%C3%B3rien" title="Lothlórien">Lothlórien</a> in <i>The Fellowship of the Ring</i>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Flute" title="Flute">Flute</a> solo by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sir_James_Galway" title="Sir James Galway">Sir James Galway</a>.</li>
<li>Mithrandir/Grey Havens theme: featured in "<a href="/wiki/Into_the_West_(song)" title="Into the West (song)">Into the West</a>" by <a href="/wiki/Annie_Lennox" title="Annie Lennox">Annie Lennox</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Instrumentation">Instrumentation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_of_The_Lord_of_the_Rings_film_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Instrumentation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Howard Shore made use of a large orchestra using the following instruments:</p>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Woodwind_instrument" title="Woodwind instrument">Woodwinds</a></b>: 3 <a href="/wiki/Flute" title="Flute">flutes</a> (1st and 2nd doubling on <a href="/wiki/Alto_flute" title="Alto flute">alto flutes</a>, 2nd doubling on <a href="/wiki/Piccolo" title="Piccolo">piccolo</a>, 3rd doubling on <a href="/wiki/Piccolo" title="Piccolo">piccolo</a> and optionally on <a href="/wiki/Tin_whistle" title="Tin whistle">Irish whistle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alto_flute" title="Alto flute">alto flute</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Pan_flute" title="Pan flute">pan flute</a> [wooden flute]), 3 <a href="/wiki/Oboe" title="Oboe">oboes</a> (3rd doubling on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_horn" title="English horn">English horn</a>), 3 <a href="/wiki/Clarinet" title="Clarinet">clarinets</a> (3rd doubling on <a href="/wiki/Bass_clarinet" title="Bass clarinet">bass clarinet</a>), 3 <a href="/wiki/Bassoon" title="Bassoon">bassoons</a> (3rd doubling on <a href="/wiki/Contrabassoon" title="Contrabassoon">double bassoon</a>)</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Brass_instrument" title="Brass instrument">Brass</a></b>: 5 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horn_(instrument)" title="Horn (instrument)">horns</a> in F, 4 <a href="/wiki/Trumpet" title="Trumpet">trumpets</a> in B-Flat (piston and rotary valve), 3 <a href="/wiki/Trombone" title="Trombone">trombones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tuba" title="Tuba">tuba</a></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Percussion_instrument" title="Percussion instrument">Percussion</a></b> (minimum 4 players): <a href="/wiki/Timpani" title="Timpani">timpani</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chimes" title="Chimes">chimes</a>, medium and large <a href="/wiki/Gong" title="Gong">tamtams</a>, 5 <a href="/wiki/Gong" title="Gong">suspended gongs</a> (6", 8", 10", 12", 14"), <a href="/wiki/Cymbal" title="Cymbal">suspended cymbals</a>: large and antique, <i>piatti</i>, 2 metal bell plates (14", 1" thick), distressed <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snare_drum" title="Snare drum">snare drum</a>, field (or side) drum, 2 <a href="/wiki/Bodhr%C3%A1n" title="Bodhrán">bodhráns</a>, Japanese <a href="/wiki/Taiko" title="Taiko">taiko</a> drums: small, medium and large, <a href="/wiki/Bass_drum" title="Bass drum">bass drum</a>, 2 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Log_drum" title="Log drum">log drums</a>, bass <a href="/wiki/Marimba" title="Marimba">marimba</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rattle_(percussion_instrument)" title="Rattle (percussion instrument)">rattle</a></li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Keyboard_instrument" title="Keyboard instrument">Keyboard</a></b>: <a href="/wiki/Piano" title="Piano">piano</a> (doubling on <a href="/wiki/Celesta" title="Celesta">celesta</a>)</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_of_Tolkien_languages">Use of Tolkien languages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_of_The_Lord_of_the_Rings_film_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Use of Tolkien languages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film score for The Lord of the Rings incorporates extensive vocal music blended with the orchestral arrangements. The great majority of the lyrics used are in the invented languages of Middle-earth, representing the various cultures and races in Tolkien's writings. These languages include <a href="/wiki/Quenya" title="Quenya">Quenya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sindarin" title="Sindarin">Sindarin</a> associated with Elves, <a href="/wiki/Ad%C3%BBnaic" title="Adûnaic">Adûnaic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rohirric" title="Rohirric">Rohirric</a> for Men, and <a href="/wiki/Khuzdul" title="Khuzdul">Khuzdul</a> of the Dwarves. Some of these languages had been developed extensively by Tolkien, while others were extrapolated by linguist <a href="/wiki/David_Salo" title="David Salo">David Salo</a> based on the limited examples of vocabulary and linguistic style available. (<a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> was used as an analog for Rohirric.) The lyrical texts were derived from several sources, including songs and poems written by Tolkien, as well as original and adapted material from screenwriters <a href="/wiki/Fran_Walsh" title="Fran Walsh">Fran Walsh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippa_Boyens" title="Philippa Boyens">Philippa Boyens</a>, and others, all translated by Salo.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The vocal music serves primarily to give texture and cultural aesthetic to the score; there is never any translation of the lyrics in the on-screen presentation, and in many cases only fragments of the source texts are used.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Deleted_tracks">Deleted tracks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_of_The_Lord_of_the_Rings_film_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Deleted tracks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There exist several pieces of music written by <a href="/wiki/Howard_Shore" title="Howard Shore">Howard Shore</a> that never made it into the final cut of the film trilogy or any existing soundtrack. Among these are various pieces written for battles throughout the film. There was also a special musical arrangement written for the trailer for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Return_of_the_King" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King">The Return of the King</a></i>, which primarily consisted of principal leitmotifs along with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Movie_trailer" title="Movie trailer">movie trailer</a>-like music. Additionally, there was a song entitled "Use Well the Days" sung by Annie Lennox. The song can be found on a supplementary DVD included with the <i>Return of the King</i> soundtrack in some packages released in 2003.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Original_soundtracks">Original soundtracks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_of_The_Lord_of_the_Rings_film_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Original soundtracks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Recordings of the score were originally issued on single-disc albums, that closely followed the theatrical release dates of the films. All soundtrack albums of the trilogy have been released through <a href="/wiki/Reprise_Records" title="Reprise Records">Reprise Records</a>, Enya's label at that time of the first soundtrack's release. While the cover art for <i>The Fellowship of the Ring</i> uses an original compilation of film characters, the covers for <i>The Two Towers</i> and <i>The Return of the King</i> reflect the respective film posters.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Complete_Recordings"><i>The Complete Recordings</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_of_The_Lord_of_the_Rings_film_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: The Complete Recordings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Starting in 2005, a year after the extended release of <i>The Return of the King</i>, <a href="/wiki/Reprise_Records" title="Reprise Records">Reprise Records</a> began to release one multi-disc set for each part of the trilogy. These annually published collections, titled <i>The Complete Recordings</i>, contain the entire score for the extended versions of the films on <a href="/wiki/Compact_disc" title="Compact disc">CD</a>, along with an additional <a href="/wiki/DVD-Audio" title="DVD-Audio">DVD-Audio</a> disc that offers 2.0 stereo and 5.1 <a href="/wiki/Surround_sound" title="Surround sound">surround</a> mixes of the soundtrack. Each album also comes with extensive <a href="/wiki/Liner_notes" title="Liner notes">liner notes</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Music_journalist" title="Music journalist">music journalist</a> <a href="/wiki/Doug_Adams_(music_journalist)" title="Doug Adams (music journalist)">Doug Adams</a> which reviews all of the tracks and provides information about the process of composing and recording the score, as well as a detailed list of all musical instruments, people and organizations involved. These Annotated Scores have been made freely available by <a href="/wiki/New_Line_Cinema" title="New Line Cinema">New Line</a> on the promotional website for the soundtracks (see <i><a href="#External_links">below</a></i>). The cover artwork uses common elements for the three albums like the film series' logo and an inscription in Tolkien's <a href="/wiki/Tengwar" title="Tengwar">tengwar</a> letters. The background of each album cover differs though in that it shows an aspect from the map of Middle-earth drawn by Tolkien that fits the title of the release and the location of the plot: <i>The Fellowship of the Ring</i> depicts the <a href="/wiki/Shire_(Middle-earth)" title="Shire (Middle-earth)">The Shire</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhudaur" title="Rhudaur">Rhudaur</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eregion" title="Eregion">Eregion</a> in dark red, the cover for <i>The Two Towers</i> shows <a href="/wiki/Rohan_(Middle-earth)" title="Rohan (Middle-earth)">Rohan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fangorn" title="Fangorn">Fangorn</a> in dark blue while <i>The Return of the King</i> shows a map of <a href="/wiki/Gondor" title="Gondor">Gondor</a> in dark green.</p>

<p><i>The Complete Recordings</i> for <i>The Return of the King</i> span almost three hours and fifty minutes on four CDs. The accompanying DVD-audio disc is double-sided to accommodate all of the material. The set was released on November 20, 2007 and is currently the only of the three to be available for <a href="/wiki/Music_download" title="Music download">digital download</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Music_of_the_Lord_of_the_Rings_Films"><i>The Music of the Lord of the Rings Films</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_of_The_Lord_of_the_Rings_film_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: The Music of the Lord of the Rings Films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>The Music of the Lord of the Rings Films</i> (<a class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn" href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/9780739071571">ISBN 978-0-7390-7157-1</a>) is a book which was written by <a href="/wiki/Doug_Adams_(music_journalist)" title="Doug Adams (music journalist)">Doug Adams</a> and released on October 5, 2010. The book contains a detailed look at the themes and leitmotifs in the films' music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> It also contains snippets of sheet music and illustrations. The book was released with a companion CD, <i>The Rarities Archive</i>. The CD has 21 tracks of previously unreleased music created for the films, as well as an audio interview with Howard Shore.</p>

<p>Following the theatrical release of all three films, Howard Shore reworked the film scores of the trilogy into <i>The Lord of the Rings Symphony</i>, a more structured six-movement work for orchestra and choir. This has been performed in various concert halls around the world, accompanied by a light and visual art show by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Lee_(illustrator)" title="Alan Lee (illustrator)">Alan Lee</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Howe_(illustrator)" title="John Howe (illustrator)">John Howe</a>. A DVD titled <i>Howard Shore: Creating the Lord of the Rings Symphony—a composer's journey through Middle Earth</i> has been released. The 50-minute long DVD features extensive excerpts of the concert given by Shore and the Montreal Orchestra and Grand Choir at the "Montreal en Lumiere" Festival, interspersed with spoken commentary by Shore, who recounts his approach in composing the music for the three films and then reworking it into the LOTR symphony.</p>
<p>On September 13, 2011, Shore released "The Lord of the Rings Symphony" on CD and MP3 format. The double-album was recorded in Lucerne, Switzerland and performed by the 21st Century Symphony Orchestra &amp; Chorus under the direction of <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ludwig_Wicki&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ludwig Wicki (page does not exist)">Ludwig Wicki</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Live_to_Projection">Live to Projection</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_of_The_Lord_of_the_Rings_film_series&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Live to Projection">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Live to Projection</i> was a series where <i>The Lord of the Rings</i> films were projected while the music is performed live. It was conducted by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Ludwig_Wicki&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Ludwig Wicki (page does not exist)">Ludwig Wicki</a> and <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Erik_Eino_Ochsner&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Erik Eino Ochsner (page does not exist)">Erik Eino Ochsner</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> and was performed around the world, including Switzerland, Australia and the United States.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
