<p><b><a href="/wiki/Queen_(band)" title="Queen (band)">Queen</a></b> are a British <a href="/wiki/Rock_music" title="Rock music">rock</a> band, which consisted of vocalist <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Mercury" title="Freddie Mercury">Freddie Mercury</a> (d. 1991), guitarist <a href="/wiki/Brian_May" title="Brian May">Brian May</a>, bassist <a href="/wiki/John_Deacon" title="John Deacon">John Deacon</a>, and drummer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roger_Meddows-Taylor" title="Roger Meddows-Taylor">Roger Taylor</a>. Founded in 1970, Queen released their self-titled <a href="/wiki/Queen_(Queen_album)" title="Queen (Queen album)">debut album</a> in 1973. Despite not being an immediate success, Queen gained popularity in Britain with their second self-titled <a href="/wiki/Queen_II" title="Queen II">album</a> in 1974.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic-bio_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-bio-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Their 1975 single, "<a href="/wiki/Bohemian_Rhapsody" title="Bohemian Rhapsody">Bohemian Rhapsody</a>", was number 1 for nine weeks (and a further five weeks in 1991) and is the third biggest selling single of all time in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The band's 1981 <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_(Queen_album)" title="Greatest Hits (Queen album)">Greatest Hits</a></i> album is the biggest selling album in UK history with 6.0 million copies sold by 2014. The band's 1991 compilation <i><a href="/wiki/Greatest_Hits_II_(Queen_album)" title="Greatest Hits II (Queen album)">Greatest Hits II</a></i> is also one of the UK's top ten biggest sellers of all time, with 3.8 million copies sold by 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1972, Queen signed a production deal with <a href="/wiki/Trident_Studios" title="Trident Studios">Trident Studios</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-qz_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-qz-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Later in their career, Queen signed a record contract with <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">EMI</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Elektra_Records" title="Elektra Records">Elektra</a> in the United States. The band would remain with EMI for the rest of their career, though, in 1983, they terminated their American contract with Elektra and signed with <a href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">Capitol Records</a>. However in 1990, they terminated their U.S. contract with Capitol and signed with <a href="/wiki/Disney_Music_Group" title="Disney Music Group">Disney's</a> <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Records" title="Hollywood Records">Hollywood Records</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> which has remained the current owner of Queen's entire music catalog in North America.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1991, Queen's entire catalogue was remastered and released by Hollywood on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compact_Disc" title="Compact Disc">compact disc</a> in the United States, and thirteen albums (all studio albums up to <i>The Works</i> as well as <i>Live Killers</i> and <i>Greatest Hits</i>) were remastered at <a href="/wiki/Abbey_Road_Studios" title="Abbey Road Studios">Abbey Road Studios</a> and released on CD and <a href="/wiki/Compact_Cassette" title="Compact Cassette">cassette</a> in the United Kingdom between July 1993 and March 1994. Queen's entire album back catalogue was remastered and re-released in the UK and rest of the world (excluding the US) through 2011 to commemorate their 40th anniversary (as well as being the 20th anniversary of Mercury's death). The 2011 remasters were released by <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Island_Records" title="Island Records">Island Records</a> label, as the band's contract with EMI ended in 2010.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The 2011 remasters were released on <a href="/wiki/Super_Audio_CD" title="Super Audio CD">SACD</a> by Universal Music Japan, between November 2011 and April 2012. In an interview with BBC Wales, Brian May announced a new compilation album titled <i><a href="/wiki/Queen_Forever" title="Queen Forever">Queen Forever</a></i>, which was later released in November 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-queenforever_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-queenforever-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
