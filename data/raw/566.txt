<p>The albums discography of <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a> starts on July 19, 1954, with the release of his first commercial <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">single</a>, and ends in February 1978. Presley's final album, <i><a href="/wiki/Elvis_in_Concert" title="Elvis in Concert">Elvis in Concert</a></i>, and final two singles, "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/My_Way_(song)" title="My Way (song)">My Way</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Unchained_Melody" title="Unchained Melody">Unchained Melody</a>", although released after his death are considered part of the discographical canon as they were projects Presley himself knew about in the planning stages.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> All other releases are included in the posthumous sections, and obviously all <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compact_Disc" title="Compact Disc">compact disc</a> <a href="/wiki/Box_set" title="Box set">box sets</a> were also released after Presley's death. Without including album reissues, during this period of 1954 to 1978 American Presley releases consisted of 102 singles, 30 <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended-play singles</a>, 70 albums on <a href="/wiki/RCA_Records" title="RCA Records">RCA Records</a>, four albums on the <a href="/wiki/Pickwick_Records" title="Pickwick Records">Pickwick</a> budget label, and five singles on the <a href="/wiki/Sun_Records" title="Sun Records">Sun Records</a> label.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="RIAA_Certifications_and_sales">RIAA Certifications and sales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elvis_Presley_albums_discography&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: RIAA Certifications and sales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> (RIAA) first began tracking sales of Elvis Presley in 1958, who didn't receive his first <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">Gold Album</a> award until 1958. In August 1992, he was awarded with 110 gold, platinum and multi-platinum albums and singles, the largest presentation of gold and platinum record awards in history. According to the latest data from the RIAA website, Elvis has a total of 90 gold, 52 platinum and 25 multi-platinum album awards by the organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>According to RIAA, he has sold over 134.5 million albums units only in the United States, making him the best selling solo albums artist of all time in this country, second overall, behind <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">The Beatles</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> According to Presley's record label, RCA, their estimation is <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_music_artists" title="List of best-selling music artists">the best selling artist of all time</a>, with sales to over 1 billion records worldwide in all formats (600 million only in the United States).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AP_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-AP-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Follow_That_Dream_Records">Follow That Dream Records</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Elvis_Presley_albums_discography&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Follow That Dream Records">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="metadata plainlinks ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation">

<td class="mbox-image">
<div style="width:52px"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg"><img alt="" data-file-height="204" data-file-width="262" height="39" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" width="50"/></a></div>

<td class="mbox-text"><span class="mbox-text-span">This list (and its introduction) <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>. <span class="hide-when-compact">Please help <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Elvis_Presley_albums_discography&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Introduction_to_referencing/1" title="Help:Introduction to referencing/1">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. <p>FTD (Follow That Dream), Sony Music's Official Elvis Presley Collectors label was established in 1999 to serve the dedicated Elvis collector and to complement the commercial and artistic level of RCA's retail release schedule by issuing repertoire that is considered of interest to serious Elvis fans and collectors, material that is generally not part of mainstream RCA label releases to the public at large. There are the original Collectors CDs with CDs ranging from soundboard recorded concerts (Recorded from the mixing desk in mono) to full RCA professionally recorded concerts in stereo and CDs consisting of unreleased material from recording sessions – Such as The Jungle Room Sessions and Memphis Sessions etc., later FTD introduced the Elvis Classic Album Series where they release the original Elvis albums in deluxe 7" size packaging with a booklet and a usually a second CD of out takes from the same period as the recordings on the album. There is also the Movie Soundtrack Series usually released as a single CD but again in deluxe 7" packaging with booklet.</p>
