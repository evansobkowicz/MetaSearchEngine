<div class="article_text">
<font color="#5FA505"><b>Claim:</b></font>   Image shows a risqué shadow sneaked into a scene from <i>Toy Story 3</i>.
<br><br>
<center><img src="/images/content-divider.gif"/></center>
<noindex><table><tr><td valign="CENTER"><img src="/images/red.gif"/></td><td valign="TOP"><font color="#37628D" size="5"><b>FALSE</b></font></td></tr></table></noindex>
<center><img src="/images/content-divider.gif"/></center>
<br>
<font color="#5FA505"><b>Example:</b></font>   <font color="#5FA505"><i>[Collected via e-mail, July 2010]</i></font>
<br><br>
<div class="quoteBlock">
I found this image on Toy Story 3, I was wondering if this scene was real.
<br><br>
<center><img height="281&lt;/CENTER" src="/photos/risque/graphics/toystory.jpg" width="500">
</img></center></br></br></div>
<div class="quoteBlockEnd">   </div>
<br>
<font color="#5FA505"><b>Origins:</b></font>   When we started this web site way back in 1994, the very first articles we published were about various risqué images and messages supposedly slipped into Disney <nobr>films — a</nobr> few of them real, most of them products of viewers' imaginations.  The years since then have seen the addition of a third class of risqué Disney film "Easter 

<table align="RIGHT" border="0" cellpadding="12"><tr><td>


</td></tr></table>

eggs": fakes created through the process of digital image manipulation.
<br><br>
The June 2010 release of <i>Toy Story 3</i>, the second sequel in the popular PIXAR/Disney <i>Toy Story</i> series of films, was quickly followed by the circulation of the image displayed above, which purportedly captured a risqué scenario hidden in the film: the toy characters reacting with wide-eyed shock to the off-camera sight of their now grown-up owner, Andy (who is about to leave home to begin college), engaging in a decidedly adult activity suggested to viewers by the shadow the participants cast on the floor of Andy's room.  (The image was typically posted on the web or e-mailed with a title of "Andy's All Grown Up" or "Andy's <nobr>Room — All</nobr> Grown Up.")
<br><br>
This tableau did not actually appear in any version of <i>Toy Story 3</i>, however.  It was created by simply adding the suggestive shadow image into a scene which originally featured rather ordinary shadowing effects:
