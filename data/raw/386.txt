<p itemprop="articleBody">
</p><p itemprop="articleBody">
ALICE IN WONDERLAND AT 150</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
It's a very merry birthday this year for "Alice's Adventures in Wonderland," marking 150 years since the classic children's book by <a class="r_lapi" href="http://abcnews.go.com/topics/entertainment/books/lewis-carroll.htm">Lewis Carroll</a> was published in 1865.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
Alice150 events including art exhibits, performances, conferences and even tea parties are being held in New York City, the <a class="r_lapi" href="http://abcnews.go.com/topics/news/united-kingdom.htm">United Kingdom</a> and other places from Japan to Brazil.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
In New York City, Columbia University, New York University and the Morgan Library will all host exhibits, including a display of Carrroll's original manuscript at the Morgan. The manuscript will later move to Philadelphia's Rosenbach Museum and Library, which will host several related exhibits.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
Other U.S. venues for Alice150 events include Harvard, the University of Maryland and Vassar College. The Charles M. Schultz Museum in Santa Rosa, California, which honors the creator of the "Peanuts" cartoon, will open a "Peanuts in Wonderland" exhibition in November with illustrations, graphic novels and comics, including Schultz's Cheshire Beagle.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
In England, events range from exhibits at the Royal Horticultural Society Garden and Rosemoor Gardens to Mad Hatter-themed tea parties at Oxford University.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
Details at <a href="http://www.LewisCarroll.org">http://www.LewisCarroll.org</a> or <a href="http://lewiscarrollresources.net/2015/">http://lewiscarrollresources.net/2015/</a> .</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
———</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
LINCOLN FUNERAL 150th</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
Springfield, Illinois, is hosting exhibits, lectures, performances and re-enactments to mark the 150th anniversary of President Lincoln's assassination and funeral.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
Lincoln died April 15, 1865 after being shot at Ford's Theatre in Washington, D.C. He was buried in Springfield's Oak Ridge Cemetery on May 4, 1865. Lincoln lived in Springfield for 17 years and his home there is a national historic site.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
The Springfield commemoration kicked off Feb. 1 and events will roll out over the next few months. The culmination takes place May 1-3 with Civil War encampments, an all-night vigil, a procession to the cemetery and a cannon salute.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
A funeral train transported Lincoln's body from Washington to Springfield, with thousands of people turning out at each stop along the 1,600-mile route. The original funeral car was destroyed in a fire in 1911 but a replica is being built by a train enthusiast in Illinois. A group called the 2015 Lincoln Funeral Train plans to display the funeral car in Springfield at the May commemoration.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
A schedule of events in Springfield can be found at <a href="http://lincolnfuneraltrain.org/">http://lincolnfuneraltrain.org/</a> . Information on the replica train car is at <a href="http://www.The2015LincolnFuneralTrain.com">http://www.The2015LincolnFuneralTrain.com</a> .</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
———</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
NEW ORLEANS BEYOND <a class="r_lapi" href="http://abcnews.go.com/topics/lifestyle/mardi-gras.htm">MARDI GRAS</a></p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
The days leading up to Mardi Gras are among New Orleans' busiest. But there's plenty for tourists to do there after the Feb. 17 holiday.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
The Tennessee Williams New Orleans Literary Festival is scheduled for March 25-29 with master classes, competitions and theater events. The French Quarter Festival takes place April 9-12. The Indy Grand Prix of Louisiana will race in the New Orleans area for the first time on April 12 at NOLA Motorsports Park in Avondale, 14 miles southwest of downtown New Orleans. The New Orleans Jazz Festival takes place April 24-May 3.</p>
<p itemprop="articleBody">
Looking ahead, the Essence Festival takes place July 3-5, and the New Orleans Fringe Festival, a celebration of theatrical arts that began in 2008, is changing this year into faux/real, a new event celebrating arts, literature and food and drink in November.</p>
