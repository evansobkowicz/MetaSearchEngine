<p><b>Nicholas Scott</b> "<b>Nick</b>" <b>Cannon</b> (born October 8, 1980)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allmusic_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-allmusic-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> is an American <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Comedian" title="Comedian">comedian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rapping" title="Rapping">rapper</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entrepreneur" title="Entrepreneur">entrepreneur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Radio_personality" title="Radio personality">radio</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_personality" title="Television personality">television personality</a>. On television, Cannon began as a teenager on <i><a href="/wiki/All_That" title="All That">All That</a></i> before going on to host <i><a href="/wiki/The_Nick_Cannon_Show" title="The Nick Cannon Show">The Nick Cannon Show</a></i>, <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wild_%27N_Out" title="Wild 'N Out">Wild 'N Out</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent" title="America's Got Talent">America's Got Talent</a></i>. He acted in the films <i><a href="/wiki/Drumline_(film)" title="Drumline (film)">Drumline</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Don%27t_Cost_a_Thing_(film)" title="Love Don't Cost a Thing (film)">Love Don't Cost a Thing</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Roll_Bounce" title="Roll Bounce">Roll Bounce</a></i>. As a rapper, he released his debut <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon_(album)" title="Nick Cannon (album)">self-titled album</a> in 2003 with the hit single "<a href="/wiki/Gigolo_(Nick_Cannon_song)" title="Gigolo (Nick Cannon song)">Gigolo</a>", a collaboration with singer <a href="/wiki/R._Kelly" title="R. Kelly">R. Kelly</a>. In 2007 he played the role of the fictional footballer TJ Harper in the film <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Goal_2" title="Goal 2">Goal 2</a>. In 2006, Cannon recorded singles "Dime Piece" and "My Wife" for a planned album <i>Stages</i>, which was never released. Cannon married American R&amp;B/pop singer <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> on April 30, 2008.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cannon was born on October 8, 1980, in <a href="/wiki/San_Diego" title="San Diego">San Diego</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, the son of Beth (née Hackett), an accountant, and James Cannon, a motivational televangelist.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Growing up, he divided his time between San Diego, where his mother lived, and <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">Charlotte, North Carolina</a>, where his father had worked. At age 11, Cannon received his first break doing stand-up comedy on his father's local cable access program.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Peoples_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Peoples-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>After graduating in 1998 from <a href="/wiki/Monte_Vista_High_School_(Spring_Valley,_California)" title="Monte Vista High School (Spring Valley, California)">Monte Vista High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spring_Valley,_San_Diego_County,_California" title="Spring Valley, San Diego County, California">Spring Valley, California</a>, Cannon attained a gig as a warm-up comedian for <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a>'s studio audience in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>, most notably <i><a href="/wiki/All_That" title="All That">All That</a>,</i> where he also made guest appearances.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Music_career">Music career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Music career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1999.E2.80.932006:_Music_beginnings.2C_Nick_Cannon_.26_Stages">1999–2006: Music beginnings, <i>Nick Cannon</i> &amp; <i>Stages</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1999–2006: Music beginnings, Nick Cannon &amp; Stages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a teenager, Cannon formed the rap group "Da G4 Dope Bomb Squad" with his friend Steve Groves; they opened for the likes of <a href="/wiki/Will_Smith" title="Will Smith">Will Smith</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lyte_Funky_Ones" title="Lyte Funky Ones">LFO</a>, <a href="/wiki/98_Degrees" title="98 Degrees">98 Degrees</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Montell_Jordan" title="Montell Jordan">Montell Jordan</a>.</p>
<p>After signing with <a href="/wiki/Jive_Records" title="Jive Records">Jive Records</a> in 2001, he was featured alongside <a href="/wiki/Romeo_Miller" title="Romeo Miller">Romeo Miller</a> and <a href="/wiki/3LW" title="3LW">3LW</a> on the <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jimmy_Neutron:_Boy_Genius_(film)#Soundtrack_album_track_listing" title="Jimmy Neutron: Boy Genius (film)">Jimmy Neutron: Boy Genius</a></i> soundtrack with a <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover</a> of the 1988 hit song "<a href="/wiki/Parents_Just_Don%27t_Understand" title="Parents Just Don't Understand">Parents Just Don't Understand</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> His debut <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon_(album)" title="Nick Cannon (album)">self titled</a> album was released in 2003 and included the single "Your Pops Don't Like Me".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, Cannon formed his own record label, Can I Ball Records, with plans to release his second studio album, entitled <i>Stages</i>, later that year.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The album's first single, "Can I Live?", a pro-life song, was released in July 2005<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nick_Cannon_Discography_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-Nick_Cannon_Discography-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> followed by the second single "Dime Piece" in March 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nick_Cannon_Discography_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-Nick_Cannon_Discography-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> But it was never released because of Nick Cannon's acting career.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2009.E2.80.9312:_N.27Credible_Entertainment.2C_Slick_Nick_.26_Child_of_the_Corn">2009–12: N'Credible Entertainment, Slick Nick &amp; <i>Child of the Corn</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2009–12: N'Credible Entertainment, Slick Nick &amp; Child of the Corn">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2009 Nick Cannon formed a new label after cancelling his old label Can-I-Ball Records entitled N'Credible Entertainment. In 2010, Cannon created a parody of hip hop pioneer <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a> entitled "Slick Nick". He released two songs of the character such as "I'm A Slick Rick" in which he attempted to diss Hip-Hop mega-star <a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>, it was a freestyle of <a href="/wiki/Cali_Swag_District" title="Cali Swag District">Cali Swag District</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Teach_Me_How_to_Dougie" title="Teach Me How to Dougie">Teach Me How to Dougie</a>" &amp; "Nick's Story" which was a Freestyle rap version of <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Children%27s_Story" title="Children's Story">Children's Story</a>". On December 6, 2010 Nick Cannon released his debut mixtape entitled <i>Child Of The Corn</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013.E2.80.93present:_White_People_Party_Music">2013–present: <i>White People Party Music</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2013–present: White People Party Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 26, 2013 Nick Cannon premiered his new single "Me Sexy" &amp; announced after 7 years from the shelving of his album <i>Stages</i> he was working on a new second studio album entitled <i><a href="/wiki/White_People_Party_Music" title="White People Party Music">White People Party Music</a></i>, and will feature collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Afrojack" title="Afrojack">Afrojack</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pitbull_(rapper)" title="Pitbull (rapper)">Pitbull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Future_(rapper)" title="Future (rapper)">Future</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Polow_da_Don" title="Polow da Don">Polow da Don</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hotnewhiphop1_10-0"><a href="#cite_note-hotnewhiphop1-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rap-up1_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-rap-up1-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The album's second single "Looking for a Dream" was released on February 11, 2014. The song features <a href="/wiki/Afrojack" title="Afrojack">Afrojack</a>. The music video was released on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/VEVO" title="VEVO">VEVO</a> February 13.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_ventures">Other ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other ventures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hosting">Hosting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Hosting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2005, Cannon produced and hosted the <a href="/wiki/MTV" title="MTV">MTV</a> <a href="/wiki/Improvisation" title="Improvisation">improv</a> comedy series <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wild_%27N_Out" title="Wild 'N Out">Wild 'N Out</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> That same year he hosted the so-called slime stunt on <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a>'s <a href="/wiki/2005_Kids%27_Choice_Awards" title="2005 Kids' Choice Awards">2005 Kids' Choice Awards</a> in which a human cannonball was shot into slime. He hosted the fourth, fifth, sixth, seventh, and eighth seasons,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> and is currently hosting the ninth season, of <i><a href="/wiki/America%27s_Got_Talent" title="America's Got Talent">America's Got Talent</a></i>. He also hosted the first and only season of Nickelodeon's <i>Friday Night Slimetime</i>. In addition, he was the DJ on <a href="/wiki/Ellen_DeGeneres" title="Ellen DeGeneres">Ellen DeGeneres</a>'s <i>Bigger, Longer, And Wider</i> show. On December 11, 2009, Cannon hosted the <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon_HALO_Awards" title="Nickelodeon HALO Awards">Nickelodeon HALO Awards</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hayden_Panettiere" title="Hayden Panettiere">Hayden Panettiere</a>, <a href="/wiki/LeBron_James" title="LeBron James">LeBron James</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kelly_Rowland" title="Kelly Rowland">Kelly Rowland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alicia_Keys" title="Alicia Keys">Alicia Keys</a>.</p>
<p>On July 4, 2011, Cannon hosted the <a href="/wiki/Nathan%27s_Hot_Dog_Eating_Contest" title="Nathan's Hot Dog Eating Contest">Nathan's Hot Dog Eating Contest</a>, an annual American competitive eating competition.</p>
<p>On November 4, 2013, Cannon was the master of ceremonies as well as an honoree at the annual <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ebony_Magazine" title="Ebony Magazine">Ebony Magazine</a> Power 100 Awards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In spring 2014, Cannon will be one of the three hosts of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World%27s_Got_Talent" title="World's Got Talent">World's Got Talent</a></i> with <a href="/wiki/Ant_%26_Dec" title="Ant &amp; Dec">Anthony McPartlin and Declan Donnelly</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Business">Business</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Business">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The former Nickelodeon star and MTV persona is now the <a href="/wiki/Chairman" title="Chairman">Chairman</a> of <a href="/wiki/TeenNick" title="TeenNick">TeenNick</a> and the development and creative consultant of the network since 2009. In 2012, Nick Cannon created a sketch-comedy series, <i><a href="/wiki/Incredible_Crew" title="Incredible Crew">Incredible Crew</a></i>, starring six teenagers as Cannon wrote and produced the theme song. The show was produced by <a href="/wiki/Cartoon_Network_Studios" title="Cartoon Network Studios">Cartoon Network Studios</a>, in association with Ncredible Entertainment.</p>
<p>On November 12, 2012, <a href="/wiki/MTV2" title="MTV2">MTV2</a> announced that they will be reviving Nick Cannon's <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_%27n_Out" title="Wild 'n Out">Wild 'n Out</a></i>. The revived show is been produced by Nick's Ncredible Entertainment, the series will premiere in 2013 a few returning faces joined by a roster of fresh talents. When asked about the show coming back, Nick said, "With our show's original cast, everyone from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kevin_Hart_(actor)" title="Kevin Hart (actor)">Kevin Hart</a> to <a href="/wiki/Katt_Williams" title="Katt Williams">Katt Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Affion_Crockett" title="Affion Crockett">Affion Crockett</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taran_Killam" title="Taran Killam">Taran Killam</a> becoming household names, I am looking forward to working with MTV2 as we work to introduce the biggest comedic stars of tomorrow with the return of Wild 'N Out."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Rapper <a href="/wiki/Big_Boi" title="Big Boi">Big Boi</a> of <a href="/wiki/Outkast" title="Outkast">Outkast</a> has confirmed in a tweet on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> he is shooting an episode on January 29, 2013 in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">NYC</a> and for people to join in the audience.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On November 30, 2012, a picture of Nick was posted on the Ncredible Entertainment website, which reported that Nick signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/NBCUniversal" title="NBCUniversal">NBCUniversal</a> to produce scripted and unscripted material for the network.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radio_hosting">Radio hosting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Radio hosting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 19, 2010, Cannon started hosting the morning show (6-10AM) with co-hosts Nikki and Southern Sarah Lee at <a href="/wiki/WBMP_(FM)" title="WBMP (FM)">92.3 NOW FM (WXRK-FM)</a> in New York.</p>
<p>Cannon has a weekly chart program called <i>Cannon's Countdown</i> that is syndicated by <a href="/wiki/CBS_Radio" title="CBS Radio">CBS Radio</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On February 17, 2012, Cannon stepped down from 92.3 NOW citing health issues.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Comedy">Comedy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Comedy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 13, 2010, Cannon announced that he was going on a comedy tour in fall 2010, beginning at the Just for Laughs festival in Montreal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In early 2011, Cannon recorded his first stand-up comedy special titled <i>Mr. Showbiz</i> at the Palms Casino Resort in Las Vegas. The special premiered on Showtime on May 14, 2011. A digital release of <i>Mr. Showbiz</i> became available on iTunes beginning May 16, 2011, with a physical release of the album on May 31, 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With no prior announcement on April 30, 2008, Cannon married singer <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> at her private estate on <a href="/wiki/Windermere_Island" title="Windermere Island">Windermere Island</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">The Bahamas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtvvid2_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtvvid2-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In April 2011, Carey gave birth to fraternal twins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> The daughter is named Monroe Cannon (after <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Monroe" title="Marilyn Monroe">Marilyn Monroe</a>), the son, Moroccan Scott Cannon (because Cannon proposed to Carey in her Moroccan-style room; Scott is Cannon's middle name and his grandmother's maiden name). Monroe, like her mother, has no middle name.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On January 4, 2012, Cannon was hospitalized after suffering from mild kidney failure. He was then hospitalized on February 17, 2012, for blood clots in his lungs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> On March 5, 2012, he announced that he had been diagnosed with an autoimmune disease, <a href="/wiki/Lupus_nephritis" title="Lupus nephritis">lupus nephritis</a>, which was the cause of his recent kidney problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On May 1, 2013, Cannon and Carey lived out a fairy-tale "Fantasy" wedding at the <a href="/wiki/Disneyland_Resort" title="Disneyland Resort">Disneyland Resort</a>, where they marked their fifth wedding anniversary with a renewal of vows and celebrated their twins' second birthday.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2014 Cannon confirmed after much speculation that he and Carey had separated a few months earlier.</p>
<p>September 2014, via his twitter account He posted: <i>I love @MariahCarey and that will never change!!! (sic)</i> Nick also defended Mariah, 44, insisting the criticism she has received in the wake of their separation is completely uncalled for. He added in a series of tweets: <i>What infuriates me most is to hear people slander @MariahCarey .</i></p>
<p>"I will forever be in debted to her for blessing me with our children ... I will always love her unconditionally for this and so much more. @MariahCarey is an amazing Mother and I trust her wholeheartedly <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Charitable_work">Charitable work</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Charitable work">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011, Cannon filmed a public service announcement for <a href="/wiki/Do_Something" title="Do Something">Do Something</a> encouraging teens to find a cause about which they are passionate and take action in their communities.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Since 2011, Cannon has served as the celebrity spokesperson for the <a href="/wiki/National_Association_of_Letter_Carriers" title="National Association of Letter Carriers">National Association of Letter Carriers</a>' annual national food drive, which is held throughout the United States on the second Saturday in May.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Eminem">Eminem</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nick_Cannon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Eminem">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Eminem" title="Eminem">Eminem</a>'s 2009 album, <i><a href="/wiki/Relapse_(Eminem_album)" title="Relapse (Eminem album)">Relapse</a></i>, contained a track titled "Bagpipes from Baghdad", poking fun at Cannon's wife <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a>. Cannon responded with tweets via his <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> page, while Mariah released a song and video titled "<a href="/wiki/Obsessed_(Mariah_Carey_song)" title="Obsessed (Mariah Carey song)">Obsessed</a>", with lyrics and visuals aimed at Eminem. Eminem responded with a mixtape-style track called "<a href="/wiki/The_Warning_(Eminem_song)" title="The Warning (Eminem song)">The Warning</a>" in which he addressed both Cannon and Mariah, and later commented that he was finished talking about the subject.</p>
<p>The feud was thought to have been over until in September 2010 when Cannon responded with the song "I'm a Slick Rick" which in <a href="/wiki/Slick_Rick" title="Slick Rick">Slick Rick</a>'s flow he takes shots at Eminem over the instrumental for <a href="/wiki/Cali_Swag_District" title="Cali Swag District">Cali Swag District</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Teach_Me_How_to_Dougie" title="Teach Me How to Dougie">Teach Me How to Dougie</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> This was followed by a campaign via <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>, Twitter, and his radio show proposing a boxing ring fight between Eminem and Cannon for charity.</p>
<p>In late September 2011, over a year after "I'm A Slick Rick" was released, Cannon took another lyrical jab at Eminem by releasing a song called "I Remember."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On October 3, 2011, Cannon released another diss song called "Warning (Remix)" which also features <a href="/wiki/Uncle_Murda" title="Uncle Murda">Uncle Murda</a> aimed at Eminem, radio host Charlamagne Tha God, and the feud between R&amp;B singer <a href="/wiki/Ray_J" title="Ray J">Ray J</a> and rapper <a href="/wiki/Fabolous" title="Fabolous">Fabolous</a>. Cannon fires lyrical shots at Eminem and also featured an audio clip of Eminem with <a href="/wiki/Tim_Westwood" title="Tim Westwood">Tim Westwood</a> back in 2009 where Eminem backtracked on the beef saying: “I had no idea Nick Cannon was going to start wiling out on me" which Eminem referred to Cannon's then canceled <i><a href="/wiki/Wild_%27n_Out" title="Wild 'n Out">Wild 'n Out</a></i> television show. Cannon retorted to the clip by saying: “You said it Marshall, that’s all you had to do!” and that Slim Shady is “forgiven."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-youtube1_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-youtube1-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hiphopwired1_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-hiphopwired1-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-taletela1_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-taletela1-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
