<p><b>Robin Hugh Gibb</b>, <span style="font-size:85%;"><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commander_of_the_Most_Excellent_Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Commander of the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire">CBE</a></span> (22 December 1949 – 20 May 2012) was a singer, songwriter and record producer, best known as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Bee_Gees" title="Bee Gees">Bee Gees</a>. He gained worldwide fame with his brothers <a href="/wiki/Barry_Gibb" title="Barry Gibb">Barry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Gibb" title="Maurice Gibb">Maurice Gibb</a>. Besides, their younger brother <a href="/wiki/Andy_Gibb" title="Andy Gibb">Andy</a> was also a singer. Gibb joined his first band <a href="/wiki/The_Rattlesnakes_(1955_band)" title="The Rattlesnakes (1955 band)">the Rattlesnakes</a> which formed in <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>, England.</p>
<p>Gibb was born on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a> to English parents, <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Gibb" title="Hugh Gibb">Hugh</a> and Barbara Gibb; the family later moved to <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a> (where Andy was born) before settling in <a href="/wiki/Redcliffe,_Queensland" title="Redcliffe, Queensland">Redcliffe</a>, a suburb of <a href="/wiki/Brisbane" title="Brisbane">Brisbane</a>, Australia. Gibb began his career as part of the family trio (Barry-Maurice-Robin). When the group found their first success, they returned to England where they achieved worldwide fame. In 2002, the Bee Gees were appointed as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commander_of_the_Most_Excellent_Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Commander of the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire">CBEs</a> for their "contribution to music",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBE_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-CBE-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> however investiture at Buckingham Palace was delayed until 2004.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBE_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-CBE-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>With record sales estimated in excess of 200 million units, the Bee Gees became one of the most successful pop groups of all time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Music historian <a href="/wiki/Paul_Gambaccini" title="Paul Gambaccini">Paul Gambaccini</a> described Gibb as "one of the major figures in the history of British music" and "one of the best white soul voices ever".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_death_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_death-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> From 2008 to 2011, Gibb was President of the Heritage Foundation, honouring figures in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_culture" title="British culture">British culture</a>. After a career spanning six decades, Gibb last performed on stage in February 2012 supporting injured British servicemen and women at a charity concert at the <a href="/wiki/London_Palladium" title="London Palladium">London Palladium</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Palladium_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Palladium-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> On 20 May 2012, Gibb died at the age of 62 from liver and kidney failure brought on by <a href="/wiki/Colorectal_cancer" title="Colorectal cancer">colorectal cancer</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Toronto_Sun_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Toronto_Sun-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>As an instrumentalist, he primarily played a variety of keyboards, such as piano, organ and <a href="/wiki/Mellotron" title="Mellotron">Mellotron</a> on Bee Gees albums <i><a href="/wiki/Bee_Gees%27_1st" title="Bee Gees' 1st">Bee Gees' 1st</a></i> (1967)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Odessa_(Bee_Gees_album)" title="Odessa (Bee Gees album)">Odessa</a></i>, he also played guitar on his debut solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Robin%27s_Reign" title="Robin's Reign">Robin's Reign</a></i> (1970), as well as the scene in <i><a href="/wiki/Sgt._Pepper%27s_Lonely_Hearts_Club_Band_(film)" title="Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band (film)">Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-february_10-1"><a href="#cite_note-february-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>Robin Hugh Gibb was born on 22 December 1949 in Jane Crookall Maternity Home in <a href="/wiki/Douglas,_Isle_of_Man" title="Douglas, Isle of Man">Douglas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Gibb" title="Hugh Gibb">Hugh</a> and Barbara Gibb (née Pass). He was the fraternal twin of <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Gibb" title="Maurice Gibb">Maurice Gibb</a> and was the older of the two by 35 minutes. Apart from Maurice, he had one sister, Lesley Evans and two brothers, <a href="/wiki/Barry_Gibb" title="Barry Gibb">Barry</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andy_Gibb" title="Andy Gibb">Andy</a>. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> As a kid, Gibb loved to play with fire and would take boxes of matches from the kitchen. His mother said he would start fires anywhere he wanted and called him a "firebug". One fire he set even went to the point of police attention.</p>
<p>In 1953, the Gibbs watched the coronation of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_II" title="Queen Elizabeth II">Queen Elizabeth II</a> on the television. Their neighbour in Willaston, <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>, Marie Beck who was the friend of his mother and her sister Peggy. Another neighbour, Helen Kenney was living in Douglas Head as Kenney recalls "Barry and the twins used to come into Mrs. Beck's house and we would mind them, Robin once said to me, 'We're going to be rich one day, we're going to form a band!' "Little did I realise he meant it".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-first-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Gibb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1955.E2.80.9358:_The_Rattlesnakes">1955–58: The Rattlesnakes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Gibb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1955–58: The Rattlesnakes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/The_Rattlesnakes_(1955_band)" title="The Rattlesnakes (1955 band)">The Rattlesnakes (1955 band)</a></div>
<p>In 1955, when the Gibbs moved back to their home town of <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-manchester_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-manchester-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> the Gibb brothers formed <a href="/wiki/The_Rattlesnakes_(1955_band)" title="The Rattlesnakes (1955 band)">The Rattlesnakes</a>. The band consisted of Barry on guitar and vocals, Robin and Maurice on vocals, Paul Frost on drums and Kenny Horrocks on <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tea-chest_bass" title="Tea-chest bass">tea-chest bass</a>, and the quintet performed in local theatres in Manchester,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-manchester_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-manchester-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> their influences at that time such as <a href="/wiki/The_Everly_Brothers" title="The Everly Brothers">The Everly Brothers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Richard" title="Cliff Richard">Cliff Richard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Anka" title="Paul Anka">Paul Anka</a>. In May 1958, it was disbanded as Frost and Horrocks left, and the name changed to Wee Johnny Hayes and the Blue Cats. In August 1958, the family travelled to Australia on the same ship as Australian musician <a href="/wiki/Red_Symons" title="Red Symons">Red Symons</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1958.E2.80.9369:_Bee_Gees">1958–69: Bee Gees</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Gibb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1958–69: Bee Gees">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Bee_Gees" title="Bee Gees">Bee Gees</a></div>
<p>The name was finally changed to <a href="/wiki/Bee_Gees" title="Bee Gees">Bee Gees</a>, when they lived in <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>, Australia.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> The Bee Gees' debut television appearance was in 1960 on <a href="/wiki/Desmond_Tester" title="Desmond Tester">Desmond Tester</a>'s <i>Strictly for Moderns</i> when they performed "Time Is Passing By".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> When they signed to <a href="/wiki/Festival_Records" title="Festival Records">Festival Records</a> at the start of 1963 (but they were assigned to <a href="/wiki/Leedon_Records" title="Leedon Records">Leedon Records</a>) they released their debut single, "<a href="/wiki/The_Battle_of_the_Blue_and_the_Grey" title="The Battle of the Blue and the Grey">The Battle of the Blue and the Grey</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> Their 1964 single "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Claustrophobia_(song)" title="Claustrophobia (song)">Claustrophobia</a>" is notable for being the first song that features Gibb as an instrumentalist playing melodica.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> The first Bee Gees record on which he sang lead was "I Don't Think It's Funny" in 1965.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1966, he wrote his first song "I Don't Know Why I Bother With Myself" that was credited to himself. Also in 1966, Gibb and his brother Barry took more solo vocals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<div class="quotebox" style="float: right; clear: right; margin: 0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em; width:25%; padding: 6px; border: 1px solid #aaa; font-size: 88%; background-color: #F9F9F9; padding:8px;">
<div class="" style="position: relative; text-align: left;">
<div>
<p>"Robin is such a highly strung person, he was bound to snap eventually. We have just come back from holiday but he never moved out of his hotel room. He also went with Barry to <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nairobi" title="Nairobi">Nairobi</a>. But in both places, while Barry went sightseeing, Robin just stayed in his room writing songs. Robin seems totally unable to relax. He always has to be writing. Yet, instead of sitting back after his songs have been recorded, he immediately turns to new writing. So he gets tense and tired, with the result that he collapsed last week. We may be twins, but I'm not a bit like him."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-first-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</div>
</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">—Maurice Gibb, press conference, 1968</div>
</div>
<p>The group's first period of British success in the late 1960s started with "<a href="/wiki/New_York_Mining_Disaster_1941" title="New York Mining Disaster 1941">New York Mining Disaster 1941</a>" and the band added drummer <a href="/wiki/Colin_Petersen" title="Colin Petersen">Colin Petersen</a> and guitarist <a href="/wiki/Vince_Melouney" title="Vince Melouney">Vince Melouney</a> to make the group as a band. They toured the Europe in 1967 and 1968 as well as US in August 1968. The band's first UK #1 was "<a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_(Bee_Gees_song)" title="Massachusetts (Bee Gees song)">Massachusetts</a>" which features Gibb on lead vocal. On the day it reached number one, 5 November 1967, Robin Gibb recalled to <i>The Mail on Sunday</i> on 1 November 2009;</p>
<table align="center" class="cquote2" role="presentation" style="border-collapse:collapse; border-style:none; background-color:transparent;">
<tr>
<td style="vertical-align:top;color:#B2B7F2;font-size:40px;font-family:serif;font-weight:bold;text-align:left;padding:10px 10px;" width="20">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align:top;padding:4px 10px;">This was a bittersweet victory. The day it went to No. one it was Bonfire Night and I was in the <a href="/wiki/Hither_Green_rail_crash" title="Hither Green rail crash">Hither Green rail crash</a> in Lewisham. Forty-nine people died and it was one of Britain's worst rail disasters. Luckily I didn't get injured. I remember sitting at the side of the carriage, watching the rain pour down, fireworks go off and blue lights of the ambulances whirring. It was like something out of a Spielberg film. I thought, at least there is one consolation, we have our first UK number one.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></td>
<td style="vertical-align:bottom;color:#B2B7F2;font-size:40px;font-family:serif;font-weight:bold;text-align:right;padding:10px 10px;" width="20">”</td>
</tr>
</table>
<p>Gibb spoke of the effects of seeing injured people being carried into the hospital on stretchers in a 2012 interview, "It all got too much for me. I knew that I couldn’t help anyone, and I couldn’t cope with that. I had to get away from the hospital. So Molly [his then girlfriend] and I checked ourselves out, and went to stay at the London home of Robert Stigwood, our manager, then to my parents’ house in Buckinghamshire. I had a delayed reaction; in the days that followed, I started crying and crying and couldn’t stop".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 13 June 1968, seven songs was recorded including "Indian Gin and Whisky Dry" all of the songs was credited to Gibb alone just his vocals and guitar and the song was later appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/Idea_(album)" title="Idea (album)">Idea</a></i>. On 27 July 1968, Gibb collapsed and fell unconscious. He later admitted to a London nursing home suffering from nervous exhaustion as the group's American tour was postponed. Gibb later moved to <a href="/wiki/Sussex" title="Sussex">Sussex</a> on 31 July to continue his recuperation. Harley Madison of <i>Hullabaloo</i> also made a comment on Gibb's nervous exhaustion. When Gibb collapsed it was only a few hours before the Bee Gees began the tour. Four dates were cancelled and Gibb had another illness called relapse and had to fly back to England to rest a few days. Gibb co-wrote "<a href="/wiki/Only_One_Woman" title="Only One Woman">Only One Woman</a>", <a href="/wiki/The_Marbles_(duo)" title="The Marbles (duo)">The Marbles</a>' debut and successful hit especially in Europe and New Zealand. The single "<a href="/wiki/I_Started_a_Joke" title="I Started a Joke">I Started a Joke</a>" on which he sang lead and co-wrote made the Bee Gees' debut on the US Top 10. Although Gibb claimed that the melody of the song was inspired by the sounds he heard in a jet engine.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-first-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In August, the band started to record <i><a href="/wiki/Odessa_(Bee_Gees_album)" title="Odessa (Bee Gees album)">Odessa</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> In January 1969, Gibb co-wrote another Marbles single "<a href="/wiki/The_Walls_Fell_Down" title="The Walls Fell Down">The Walls Fell Down</a>" and co-produced the sessions that same month.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-february_10-2"><a href="#cite_note-february-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> However, the rivalry with Barry eventually prompted Robin to leave the group and begin a solo career (three months after guitarist <a href="/wiki/Vince_Melouney" title="Vince Melouney">Vince Melouney</a> left the band) after his song "<a href="/wiki/Lamplight" title="Lamplight">Lamplight</a>" was relegated to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/B-side" title="B-side">B-side</a> of Barry's song "<a href="/wiki/First_of_May_(Bee_Gees_song)" title="First of May (Bee Gees song)">First of May</a>". Meanwhile, there were rumours during this period that he was dealing with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drug_abuse" title="Drug abuse">drug</a> problems, allegedly leading his parents to threaten legal action to make him a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ward_of_court" title="Ward of court">ward of court</a> (the UK age of majority at that time being 21, while Gibb was only 19).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> The Bee Gees' last recording session with Robin was on February 1969.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-february_10-3"><a href="#cite_note-february-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Gibb's last performance with the group was on <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Tom_Jones_Show" title="The Tom Jones Show">The Tom Jones Show</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Top_of_the_Pops" title="Top of the Pops">Top of the Pops</a></i> before leaving the group.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1969.E2.80.9370:_Solo_career">1969–70: Solo career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Gibb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 1969–70: Solo career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>On 19 March 1969, he announced that he was leaving the Bee Gees the same day as the band recorded "<a href="/wiki/Tomorrow_Tomorrow_(Bee_Gees_song)" title="Tomorrow Tomorrow (Bee Gees song)">Tomorrow Tomorrow</a>" and that became their first single without Robin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-february_10-4"><a href="#cite_note-february-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> In his solo career, Gibb was initially successful with a number 2 UK hit, "<a href="/wiki/Saved_by_the_Bell_(song)" title="Saved by the Bell (song)">Saved by the Bell</a>", which sold over one million copies and received a <a href="/wiki/Music_recording_sales_certification" title="Music recording sales certification">gold disc</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-The_Book_of_Golden_Discs_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-The_Book_of_Golden_Discs-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> As he performs that song on the German TV show <i><a href="/wiki/Beat-Club" title="Beat-Club">Beat-Club</a></i>. Also in 1969, Gibb co-produced "Love for Living", the song was performed by <a href="/wiki/Clare_Torry" title="Clare Torry">Clare Torry</a> and was released as a single.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup> He also starts a mini-tour, making television appearances in dozen countries to promote "Saved by the Bell". By 19 July 1969, <i>New Musical Express</i> announced "Tonight, [Robin Gibb] is fronting a 97-piece orchestra and a 60-piece choir in a recording of his latest composition, 'To Heaven And Back' which was inspired by the Apollo 11 moonshot. It is an entirely instrumental piece, with the choir being used for astral effects. The single will be billed as by 'The Robin Gibb Orchestra and Chorus and it will be rush-released as soon as possible by <a href="/wiki/Polydor_Records" title="Polydor Records">Polydor</a>". At that time, he was doing a musical score for <i>Henry The Eighth</i> and he's making his own film called <i>Family Tree</i>. Later, it was reported on <i>NME</i> that Gibb wrote dozen of songs for <a href="/wiki/Tom_Jones_(singer)" title="Tom Jones (singer)">Tom Jones</a>, a meeting between Gibb and Jones was said to be arranged for Gibb's return from a three-day promo trip to Germany.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-first_1-4"><a href="#cite_note-first-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> In 31 January and 1 February 1970, Gibb performs in <a href="/wiki/Auckland" title="Auckland">Auckland</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. However, Robin's first solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Robin%27s_Reign" title="Robin's Reign">Robin's Reign</a></i> (1970) was less successful and he soon found that being a solo artist was unsatisfying. Maurice played bass on the song "<a href="/wiki/Mother_and_Jack" title="Mother and Jack">Mother and Jack</a>", but was subsequently removed from the project by producer <a href="/wiki/Robert_Stigwood" title="Robert Stigwood">Robert Stigwood</a>. Also in that year, <a href="/wiki/Colin_Petersen" title="Colin Petersen">Colin Petersen</a> produced "Make a Stranger Your Friend" performed by <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Kelly" title="Jonathan Kelly">Jonathan Kelly</a>, on which Gibb sang on the chorus with <a href="/wiki/Mick_Taylor" title="Mick Taylor">Mick Taylor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Klaus_Voormann" title="Klaus Voormann">Klaus Voormann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madeline_Bell" title="Madeline Bell">Madeline Bell</a>, three members of <a href="/wiki/The_Family_Dogg" title="The Family Dogg">The Family Dogg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Lomax" title="Jackie Lomax">Jackie Lomax</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sellers" title="Peter Sellers">Peter Sellers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spike_Milligan" title="Spike Milligan">Spike Milligan</a> and others. By January 1970, Gibb started to record his second solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Sing_Slowly_Sisters" title="Sing Slowly Sisters">Sing Slowly Sisters</a></i> until February but was still unreleased. He wanted "Great Caesar's Ghost" to be release as a single around 1970 with "Engines, Aeroplanes" as the B-side but the two songs was not included on that album and was unreleased until this day.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-demo_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-demo-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 13 June, Gibb and Maurice reunited and they recorded four songs, the two of the four tracks was released on their upcoming album <i>2 Years On</i>, the session was originally for Maurice alone as he bought Gibb to the sessions. On 21 June sessions, the pair recorded another five songs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-demo_33-1"><a href="#cite_note-demo-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1970.E2.80.9379:_Comeback_for_the_Bee_Gees">1970–79: Comeback for the Bee Gees</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Gibb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 1970–79: Comeback for the Bee Gees">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In August, the pair returned to studio and they announced that the Bee Gees were back, with or without Barry's contribution, one of the fourteen songs, "Back Home" and "I'm Weeping" was also released on <i>2 Years On</i>. In 21 August, it was announced that Barry rejoined the group and record together, and the first song after the announcement was "<a href="/wiki/Lonely_Days" title="Lonely Days">Lonely Days</a>" which reached #3 in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Billboard_Hot_100" title="US Billboard Hot 100">US Billboard Hot 100</a>. On the <i><a href="/wiki/2_Years_On" title="2 Years On">2 Years On</a></i> album contains Gibb's compositions including "Alone Again", he also co-wrote and sang lead vocals on the title track as well as "Man For All Seasons". In December 1970, Gibb recorded a demo "After the Laughter".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-demo_33-2"><a href="#cite_note-demo-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> The Bee Gees made their first US #1 single "<a href="/wiki/How_Can_You_Mend_a_Broken_Heart" title="How Can You Mend a Broken Heart">How Can You Mend a Broken Heart</a>", Gibb contributed on the song, writing with Barry and sang lead vocals on its first verse.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In April 1972, two months after the departure of drummer Geoff Bridgford, he wrote his last solo composition on a Bee Gees record "Never Been Alone" (until 1999).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1976, on the group's <i><a href="/wiki/Children_of_the_World" title="Children of the World">Children of the World</a></i> album, he sang lead on "<a href="/wiki/Love_Me_(Bee_Gees_song)" title="Love Me (Bee Gees song)">Love Me</a>" as well as doing <a href="/wiki/Falsetto" title="Falsetto">falsetto</a> on the track's coda, and he also used his falsetto on his lead vocal part on the song "Lovers" as Barry provided lead vocals on the entire song. On the <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Fever_(soundtrack)" title="Saturday Night Fever (soundtrack)">Saturday Night Fever</a></i> soundtrack, he did not sing lead vocals on any Bee Gees song on that soundtrack unlike their previous and next albums.</p>
<p>In 1978, Gibb performed on the <i><a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street_Fever" title="Sesame Street Fever">Sesame Street Fever</a></i> album for the <a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">Sesame Street</a> children's TV program. He was one of the singers on the "Sesame Street Fever" title track, he sang a song called "Trash" for the character <a href="/wiki/Oscar_the_Grouch" title="Oscar the Grouch">Oscar the Grouch</a>, and spoke with <a href="/wiki/Cookie_Monster" title="Cookie Monster">Cookie Monster</a> at the beginning of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C_is_for_Cookie" title="C is for Cookie">C is for Cookie</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1980.E2.80.9390:_Collaboration_with_other_artists">1980–90: Collaboration with other artists</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Gibb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 1980–90: Collaboration with other artists">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In January 1980, Gibb co-wrote and co-produced with <a href="/wiki/Blue_Weaver" title="Blue Weaver">Blue Weaver</a> <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Ruffin" title="Jimmy Ruffin">Jimmy Ruffin</a>'s 1980 album <i><a href="/wiki/Sunrise_(Jimmy_Ruffin_album)" title="Sunrise (Jimmy Ruffin album)">Sunrise</a></i>. Also in 1980, he duetted with <a href="/wiki/Marcella_Detroit" title="Marcella Detroit">Marcy Levy</a> on the song "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Help_Me!" title="Help Me!">Help Me!</a>" (reached No. 50 in the US) featured on the <a href="/wiki/Times_Square_(soundtrack)" title="Times Square (soundtrack)">soundtrack</a> of the film <i><a href="/wiki/Times_Square_(film)" title="Times Square (film)">Times Square</a></i> the other artists were performed on the film, including <a href="/wiki/Gary_Numan" title="Gary Numan">Gary Numan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roxy_Music" title="Roxy Music">Roxy Music</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ramones" title="Ramones">Ramones</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Cure" title="The Cure">The Cure</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Cars" title="The Cars">The Cars</a>. Also in 1980, most of <a href="/wiki/Barbra_Streisand" title="Barbra Streisand">Barbra Streisand</a>'s album <i><a href="/wiki/Guilty_(Barbra_Streisand_album)" title="Guilty (Barbra Streisand album)">Guilty</a></i> was co-written by Gibb with Barry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup> In February 1981, the Bee Gees returned to the studio and recorded <i><a href="/wiki/Living_Eyes_(Bee_Gees_album)" title="Living Eyes (Bee Gees album)">Living Eyes</a></i>, unlike the previous album, this album was not a successful as their fans described it as a worst album. After the <a href="/wiki/Dionne_Warwick" title="Dionne Warwick">Dionne Warwick</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Heartbreaker_(Dionne_Warwick_album)" title="Heartbreaker (Dionne Warwick album)">Heartbreaker</a></i> and before recording songs for the film <i><a href="/wiki/Staying_Alive_(1983_film)" title="Staying Alive (1983 film)">Staying Alive</a></i></p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Initial_solo_success">Initial solo success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Gibb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Initial solo success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>He recorded his second solo album with Maurice's participation, <i><a href="/wiki/How_Old_Are_You%3F_(album)" title="How Old Are You? (album)">How Old Are You?</a></i>. The lead single "<a href="/wiki/Juliet_(Robin_Gibb_song)" title="Juliet (Robin Gibb song)">Juliet</a>" was a success in Europe as well as "<a href="/wiki/Another_Lonely_Night_in_New_York" title="Another Lonely Night in New York">Another Lonely Night in New York</a>" and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/How_Old_Are_You_(Robin_Gibb_song)" title="How Old Are You (Robin Gibb song)">title track</a>. In 1984, he released his fourth solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Secret_Agent_(Robin_Gibb_album)" title="Secret Agent (Robin Gibb album)">Secret Agent</a></i>, a new wave/synthpop-influenced LP<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> (reached No. 97 in the US, No. 31 in Germany and No. 20 in Switzerland) The album's lead and first single "<a href="/wiki/Boys_Do_Fall_in_Love" title="Boys Do Fall in Love">Boys Do Fall in Love</a>" did reach the <i><a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">Billboard</a></i> Magazine top 40 list of hits, the song reached No. 70 in the UK, No. 7 in South Africa and No. 10 in Italy. Other singles "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Secret_Agent_(song)" title="Secret Agent (song)">title track</a> and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/In_Your_Diary" title="In Your Diary">In Your Diary</a>" not repeated the success of the first single. Due to the success of "Boys Do Fall in Love", he performed the song in several TV shows including <i>Eldorado</i> (Danish TV).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 1985, he released his fifth solo album <i><a href="/wiki/Walls_Have_Eyes" title="Walls Have Eyes">Walls Have Eyes</a></i> with the singles "Like a Fool" and "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Toys_(song)" title="Toys (song)">Toys</a>", both songs were not charted in the US or UK. These three albums were more successful in Europe than in the UK or US. In 1986, Gibb joined <a href="/wiki/Thompson_Twins" title="Thompson Twins">Thompson Twins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zak_Starkey" title="Zak Starkey">Zak Starkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Richard" title="Cliff Richard">Cliff Richard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bonnie_Tyler" title="Bonnie Tyler">Bonnie Tyler</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Parr" title="John Parr">John Parr</a> and <a href="/wiki/Holly_Johnson" title="Holly Johnson">Holly Johnson</a> under the name <i>Anti-Heroin Project</i> to record a charity single called "Live-In World". In late 1986, the Bee Gees began to writing and recording songs for their album <i><a href="/wiki/E.S.P._(Bee_Gees_album)" title="E.S.P. (Bee Gees album)">E.S.P.</a></i> to be released in 1987.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="1991.E2.80.932009:_Touring_years">1991–2009: Touring years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Gibb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 1991–2009: Touring years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1992, <a href="/wiki/Lulu_(singer)" title="Lulu (singer)">Lulu</a> recorded "Let Me Wake Up In Your Arms" on which he co-wrote. Gibb later contributed vocals on the Brazilian duo Chitãozinho e Xororó and José y Durval's "Palavras/Palabros" (a Spanish or Portuguese version of "Words").<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1998, the Bee Gees recorded their own version of "<a href="/wiki/Ellan_Vannin_(poem)" title="Ellan Vannin (poem)">Ellan Vannin</a>" with lead vocals by Gibb. It was later released as a single as a limited edition CD to benefit the Manx Children in Need charity.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001, the Bee Gees released their last album <i><a href="/wiki/This_Is_Where_I_Came_In" title="This Is Where I Came In">This Is Where I Came In</a></i> and features his last composition on a Bee Gees record "Embrace". On 27 January 2003, fifteen days after Maurice's death, Robin released a solo album, <i><a href="/wiki/Magnet_(album)" title="Magnet (album)">Magnet</a></i> in Germany on <a href="/wiki/SPV_GmbH" title="SPV GmbH">SPV GmbH</a>, and worldwide shortly afterwards. <i>Magnet</i> featured the Bee Gees song "Wish You Were Here" (from the 1989 album <i><a href="/wiki/One_(Bee_Gees_album)" title="One (Bee Gees album)">One</a></i>) in a new acoustic version. The lead single, "<a href="/wiki/Please_(Robin_Gibb_song)" title="Please (Robin Gibb song)">Please</a>", had coincidental lyrics about 'loss'. In recent years, Gibb sang the vocals to the opening titles to the British <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> show <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dame_Edna_Treatment" title="The Dame Edna Treatment">The Dame Edna Treatment</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup> In August 2003, Gibb announced the release of a new single of "My Lover's Prayer", a song first recorded by the Bee Gees in 1997 on the album <i><a href="/wiki/Still_Waters_(Bee_Gees_album)" title="Still Waters (Bee Gees album)">Still Waters</a></i>, with lead vocals by Gibb and singers <a href="/wiki/Wanya_Morris" title="Wanya Morris">Wanya Morris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Bass" title="Lance Bass">Lance Bass</a>. That version was played on the radio but was never actually released. In October 2003, Gibb recorded a second version of the song with <a href="/wiki/Alistair_Griffin" title="Alistair Griffin">Alistair Griffin</a>, a-runner up in the UK television program <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fame_Academy_2" title="Fame Academy 2">Fame Academy</a></i> on which Gibb appeared as a judge. In January 2004, the new version of that song was released in the UK as a double A side CD single. It eventually reached number 5 in the UK music charts. In late 2004, Gibb embarked a solo tour of Germany, Russia and Asia with singer <a href="/wiki/Alistair_Griffin" title="Alistair Griffin">Alistair Griffin</a> as the opening act. On his return to the UK, Gibb released a CD and DVD of live recordings from the German leg of the tour, backed by the <a href="/wiki/Neue_Philharmonie_Frankfurt" title="Neue Philharmonie Frankfurt">Frankfurt Neue Philharmonic Orchestra</a> of <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt" title="Frankfurt">Frankfurt</a>, Germany. In 2005, Gibb made a solo tour of Latin America.</p>
<p>In January 2005, Gibb joined his brother Barry and several other artists under the name <i><a href="/wiki/One_World_Project" title="One World Project">One World Project</a></i> to record a charity single in aid of Asian <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">tsunami</a> relief, titled "Grief Never Grows Old". Other artists who performed on the single included <a href="/wiki/Boy_George" title="Boy George">Boy George</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Winwood" title="Steve Winwood">Steve Winwood</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jon_Anderson" title="Jon Anderson">Jon Anderson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rick_Wakeman" title="Rick Wakeman">Rick Wakeman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Richard" title="Cliff Richard">Sir Cliff Richard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Wyman" title="Bill Wyman">Bill Wyman</a>, <a href="/wiki/America_(band)" title="America (band)">America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kenney_Jones" title="Kenney Jones">Kenney Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago_(band)" title="Chicago (band)">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brian_Wilson" title="Brian Wilson">Brian Wilson</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Beach_Boys" title="The Beach Boys">The Beach Boys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russell_Watson" title="Russell Watson">Russell Watson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Davy_Spillane" title="Davy Spillane">Davy Spillane</a>. In June 2005, Gibb joined <a href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(UK_series_1)" title="The X Factor (UK series 1)">The X Factor</a> runner up band <a href="/wiki/G4_(band)" title="G4 (band)">G4</a> at a sell-out concert at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Albert_Hall" title="Royal Albert Hall">Royal Albert Hall</a> in London, singing the Bee Gees song "First of May". In December 2005, a recordings of this performance was released as part of double A side single, credited as "G4 feat Robin Gibb" together with G4's <a href="/wiki/Cover_version" title="Cover version">cover version</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Mathis" title="Johnny Mathis">Johnny Mathis</a> song "When a Child is Born". "First of May" also appeared on the platinum selling album <i>G4 &amp; Friends</i>, which reached number 6 in the UK album charts. In the same year, Gibb presented <a href="/wiki/Master_class" title="Master class">master classes</a> at <a href="/wiki/Paul_McCartney" title="Paul McCartney">Paul McCartney</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Institute_for_Performing_Arts" title="Liverpool Institute for Performing Arts">Liverpool Institute for Performing Arts</a> and oversaw the selection for release of thesis works by music graduates for the next two terms. On 20 February 2006, Gibb and Barry performed at a concert for Diabetes Research Institute of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Miami" title="University of Miami">University of Miami</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood,_Florida" title="Hollywood, Florida">Hollywood, Florida</a>. This was their first joint performance since Maurice's death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miami_charity_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-miami_charity-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup> In March 2006, Gibb announced plans for more solo concerts in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shanghai,_China" title="Shanghai, China">Shanghai, China</a> and <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>. In May 2006, Gibb took part in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince%27s_Trust" title="Prince's Trust">Prince's Trust</a> 30th birthday Concert at the <a href="/wiki/Tower_of_London" title="Tower of London">Tower of London</a> along with Barry. They sang three songs: "<a href="/wiki/Jive_Talkin%27" title="Jive Talkin'">Jive Talkin'</a>", "<a href="/wiki/To_Love_Somebody_(song)" title="To Love Somebody (song)">To Love Somebody</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/You_Should_Be_Dancing" title="You Should Be Dancing">You Should Be Dancing</a>". In September 2006, Gibb performed "<a href="/wiki/Stayin%27_Alive" title="Stayin' Alive">Stayin' Alive</a>" at the <a href="/wiki/Miss_World" title="Miss World">Miss World</a> 2006 contest finals in <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a>, Poland. In November 2006, Gibb released his sixth album <i><a href="/wiki/My_Favourite_Christmas_Carols" title="My Favourite Christmas Carols">My Favourite Christmas Carols</a></i> the last album released in his lifetime, backed by <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Serlo_Concert&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Serlo Concert (page does not exist)">The Serlo Concert</a>, a London choir.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> This album featured a new song by Gibb called "Mother of Love", which was released in Europe as a download single. The song was inspired by Maurice and was Gibb's new composition since Maurice died. Gibb donated all <a href="/wiki/Royalties" title="Royalties">royalties</a> from "Mother of Love" to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BKWSU" title="BKWSU">Janki Foundation for Global Healthcare</a>", and dedicated the song to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BKWSU" title="BKWSU">Dadi Janki</a>, the organisation's spiritual leader. Gibb dedicated the album to his mother, Barbara Gibb. <i>My Favourite Christmas Carols</i> has a bonus DVD disc titled <i>A Personal Christmas Moment with Robin Gibb</i>. Also in November 2006, Gibb performed a solo concert, entitled 'Bee Gees – Greatest Hits' at the Araneta Coliseum (now <a href="/wiki/Smart_Araneta_Coliseum" title="Smart Araneta Coliseum">Smart Araneta Coliseum</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Metro_Manila" title="Metro Manila">Manila</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. Gibb marked his return to his birthplace by playing a concert at the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man_TT" title="Isle of Man TT">Isle of Man TT</a> festival in 2007. Gibb donated all of his share of the money from this concert to the children's ward at Noble's Hospital, Isle of Man, and invited all emergency service staff and marshals for the TT to attend for free. He also was a special guest of the <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(United_States)" title="Independence Day (United States)">United States Independence Day</a> Concert called "<a href="/wiki/A_Capitol_Fourth" title="A Capitol Fourth">A Capitol Fourth</a>" held on the west lawn of the US Capitol as the lead singer of "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/How_Deep_is_Your_Love" title="How Deep is Your Love">How Deep is Your Love</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/Stayin%27_Alive" title="Stayin' Alive">Stayin' Alive</a>".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>

<p>On 18 May 2008, Gibb released the song "<a href="/wiki/Alan_Freeman_Days" title="Alan Freeman Days">Alan Freeman Days</a>" in tribute to the Australian DJ <a href="/wiki/Alan_Freeman" title="Alan Freeman">Alan Freeman</a>. The song was issued as a download only track, although a promotional CD was issued by Academy Recordings. In December 2008, "Alan Freeman Days" was followed by another downloadable song titled "Wing and a Prayer", which shared the same name as a song from the 1989 <i><a href="/wiki/One_(Bee_Gees_album)" title="One (Bee Gees album)">One</a></i> album. However, the new song was actually a reworking of the song, "Sing Slowly Sisters", that had remained unreleased since 1970.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup> Later in December, Gibb issued another song, "Ellan Vannin (Home Coming Mix)", featuring the <a href="/wiki/King_William%27s_College" title="King William's College">King William's College</a> Choir from the Isle of Man. ("Ellan Vannin" is the <a href="/wiki/Manx_language" title="Manx language">Manx</a> name for the Isle of Man.) On 8 September 2007, Gibb performed at a concert in <a href="/wiki/Salt_Lake_City" title="Salt Lake City">Salt Lake City</a>, Utah at <a href="/wiki/EnergySolutions_Arena" title="EnergySolutions Arena">EnergySolutions Arena</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Nu_Skin_Enterprises" title="Nu Skin Enterprises">Nu Skin Enterprises</a> Convention, singing a set of Bee Gees hits. On 25 October 2007, Gibb performed at the <a href="/wiki/National_Palace_of_Culture" title="National Palace of Culture">National Palace of Culture</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sofia" title="Sofia">Sofia</a>, Bulgaria and sang the Bee Gees' most famous songs.</p>
<p>In 2008, Gibb completed a new solo album entitled <i>50 St. Catherine's Drive</i>, but it was never released. The song "Instant Love" was a collaboration with Gibb's son, Robin-John both having written the music and vocals. "Instant Love" featuring Robin-John on lead vocals appeared in a short film called <i>Bloodtype: The Search</i> in which Robin-John appeared.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48"><span>[</span>48<span>]</span></a></sup> On 25 October 2008, to mark the 30th anniversary of the song "Saturday Night Fever" topping the UK charts, Gibb performed with special guests including <a href="/wiki/Ronan_Keating" title="Ronan Keating">Ronan Keating</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stephen_Gateley" title="Stephen Gateley">Stephen Gateley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sam_Sparro" title="Sam Sparro">Sam Sparro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sharleen_Spiteri" title="Sharleen Spiteri">Sharleen Spiteri</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gabriella_Climi" title="Gabriella Climi">Gabriella Climi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bryn_Christopher" title="Bryn Christopher">Bryn Christopher</a> at the London music festival <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BBC_Electric_Proms" title="BBC Electric Proms">BBC Electric Proms</a>. Gibb went back to the top of the UK charts in 2009 when he collaborated with singers <a href="/wiki/Ruth_Jones" title="Ruth Jones">Ruth Jones</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rob_Brydon" title="Rob Brydon">Rob Brydon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Jones_(singer)" title="Tom Jones (singer)">Tom Jones</a> on a new version of "<a href="/wiki/Islands_in_the_Stream_(song)" title="Islands in the Stream (song)">Islands in the Stream</a>", written by Gibb, Barry and Maurice. The new version, inspired by the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> comedy TV show <i><a href="/wiki/Gavin_%26_Stacey" title="Gavin &amp; Stacey">Gavin &amp; Stacey</a></i>, was created to benefit the charity <a href="/wiki/Comic_Relief" title="Comic Relief">Comic Relief</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49"><a href="#cite_note-49"><span>[</span>49<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010.E2.80.9312:_Final_years">2010–12: Final years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Gibb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2010–12: Final years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2010, Gibb was also a guest mentor on the Australian version of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_X_Factor_(Australia)" title="The X Factor (Australia)">The X Factor</a></i>, alongside TV host <a href="/wiki/Kyle_Sandilands" title="Kyle Sandilands">Kyle Sandilands</a>, actress/singer <a href="/wiki/Natalie_Imbruglia" title="Natalie Imbruglia">Natalie Imbruglia</a>, and singers <a href="/wiki/Ronan_Keating" title="Ronan Keating">Ronan Keating</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guy_Sebastian" title="Guy Sebastian">Guy Sebastian</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50"><a href="#cite_note-50"><span>[</span>50<span>]</span></a></sup> Also in 2010, Gibb toured in Australia with <a href="/wiki/Bonnie_Tyler" title="Bonnie Tyler">Bonnie Tyler</a> as his supporting guest.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51"><span>[</span>51<span>]</span></a></sup> Together they performed at <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, Sydney, <a href="/wiki/Brisbane" title="Brisbane">Brisbane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Perth" title="Perth">Perth</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52"><span>[</span>52<span>]</span></a></sup> In September 2011, Gibb recorded the Bee Gees classic "<a href="/wiki/I%27ve_Gotta_Get_a_Message_to_You" title="I've Gotta Get a Message to You">I've Gotta Get a Message to You</a>" with <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> men <a href="/wiki/The_Soldiers" title="The Soldiers">The Soldiers</a> for a charity single in the UK, it was produced with his son Robin John Gibb and the video for which was produced by <a href="/wiki/Vintage_TV_(TV_channel)" title="Vintage TV (TV channel)">Vintage TV</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53"><span>[</span>53<span>]</span></a></sup> Gibb was the subject of an edition of the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> <a href="/wiki/Genealogy" title="Genealogy">genealogy</a> documentary series <i><a href="/wiki/Who_Do_You_Think_You_Are%3F_(UK_TV_series)" title="Who Do You Think You Are? (UK TV series)">Who Do You Think You Are?</a></i> first broadcast on 21 September 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54"><span>[</span>54<span>]</span></a></sup> On 30 January 2012, Gibb announced his intention to appear on stage at the Coming Home Concert at the <a href="/wiki/London_Palladium" title="London Palladium">London Palladium</a> in February to benefit British soldiers returning home from <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55"><a href="#cite_note-55"><span>[</span>55<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-in_coma_56-0"><a href="#cite_note-in_coma-56"><span>[</span>56<span>]</span></a></sup> It would be his last performance on stage.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Palladium_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Palladium-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> Over a period of two years, Gibb and Robin-John wrote the score for <i>The <a href="/wiki/RMS_Titanic" title="RMS Titanic">Titanic</a> Requiem</i>, recorded by <a href="/wiki/Royal_Philharmonic_Orchestra" title="Royal Philharmonic Orchestra">Royal Philharmonic Orchestra</a> to commemorate the 100th Anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Sinking_of_the_RMS_Titanic" title="Sinking of the RMS Titanic">sinking of the <i>Titanic</i></a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57"><span>[</span>57<span>]</span></a></sup> Gibb was due to attend the piece's premier on 10 April 2012 at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Westminster_Central_Hall" title="Westminster Central Hall">Central Hall, Westminster</a>, London but his failing health kept him away.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-O.27Mahoney_2012_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-O.27Mahoney_2012-58"><span>[</span>58<span>]</span></a></sup> He died the next month.</p>
<p>His final studio album <i><a href="/wiki/50_St._Catherine%27s_Drive" title="50 St. Catherine's Drive">50 St. Catherine's Drive</a></i> was released posthumously in September 29, 2014 in the UK and September 30, 2014 in the US, the album reached #70 in the UK and #39 in Germany, the album features his unreleased recordings from 2007 and mostly in 2008 while the others were previously released.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Gibb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1968 Gibb married Molly Hullis, a secretary in <a href="/wiki/Robert_Stigwood" title="Robert Stigwood">Robert Stigwood</a>'s organisation. The couple had both survived the <a href="/wiki/Hither_Green_rail_crash" title="Hither Green rail crash">Hither Green rail crash</a>, which killed 49 people on 5 November 1967.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59"><span>[</span>59<span>]</span></a></sup> They had two children together, <a href="/wiki/Spencer_Gibb" title="Spencer Gibb">Spencer</a> (b. 1972) and Melissa (b. 1974). The couple divorced in 1980 after years of living separate lives, as Hullis filed for divorce in 22 May 1980, with Gibb almost permanently in the U.S. and Hullis remaining in the UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60"><span>[</span>60<span>]</span></a></sup> On 9 September 1983, Gibb was arrested and sentenced to 14 days in prison for speaking to the press about his previous marriage in breach of a Court Order.</p>
<p>Gibb's second marriage, from 1985 until his death,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tel_o_61-0"><a href="#cite_note-Tel_o-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup> was to Dwina Murphy, an author and artist. She is interested in the <a href="/wiki/Neo-Druidism" title="Neo-Druidism">Druidry</a> religion and is a follower of the neo-<a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brahma_Kumaris" title="Brahma Kumaris">Brahma Kumaris</a> movement. The couple had a son, Robin-John (known as RJ (b. 1983). Robin-John's first major musical project is the <i>Titanic Requiem</i> (2012), written with Gibb and first performed at the Central Hall, Westminster, London, on 10 April 2012, by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Philharmonic_Orchestra" title="Royal Philharmonic Orchestra">Royal Philharmonic Orchestra</a> and RSVP Voices. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62"><span>[</span>62<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Gibb had an eight-year affair with his housekeeper Claire Yang that produced his fourth child, Snow Evelyn Robin Juliet Gibb born November 4, 2008.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tel_o_61-1"><a href="#cite_note-Tel_o-61"><span>[</span>61<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63"><span>[</span>63<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Gibb and his wife divided their time between their homes in <a href="/wiki/Peel,_Isle_of_Man" title="Peel, Isle of Man">Peel, Isle of Man</a>, their mansion in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" title="Miami, Florida">Miami, Florida</a> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64"><span>[</span>64<span>]</span></a></sup> and their main residence in <a href="/wiki/Thame" title="Thame">Thame</a>, Oxfordshire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65"><a href="#cite_note-65"><span>[</span>65<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On 10 March 1988, younger brother <a href="/wiki/Andy_Gibb" title="Andy Gibb">Andy</a> died in <a href="/wiki/Oxford" title="Oxford">Oxford</a>, England, of <a href="/wiki/Myocarditis" title="Myocarditis">myocarditis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66"><span>[</span>66<span>]</span></a></sup> On 12 January 2003, twin brother <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Gibb" title="Maurice Gibb">Maurice</a> died in <a href="/wiki/Miami_Beach,_Florida" title="Miami Beach, Florida">Miami Beach, Florida</a>, of complications from a twisted intestine.</p>
<p>Politically, Gibb was a supporter of New Labour, the British <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour Party</a> under Tony Blair. He launched a rally in <a href="/wiki/Huddersfield" title="Huddersfield">Huddersfield</a>, West Yorkshire, ahead of the 2005 General Election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67"><span>[</span>67<span>]</span></a></sup> He was a close friend of the former Labour Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Tony_Blair" title="Tony Blair">Tony Blair</a>, who was criticised for staying over at Gibb's Miami mansion during Christmas 2006.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68"><span>[</span>68<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2008 Gibb publicly stated that he continued to get on "like a house on fire" with Blair, and claimed that the then Labour Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Brown" title="Gordon Brown">Gordon Brown</a> regularly listened to the Bee Gees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69"><span>[</span>69<span>]</span></a></sup> "He listens to our music every day. Gordon likes our music and I like Gordon," he told The Times.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70"><span>[</span>70<span>]</span></a></sup> In a tribute upon his death, longtime friend Tony Blair said: "Robin was not only an exceptional and extraordinary musician and songwriter, he was a highly intelligent, interesting and committed human being. He was a great friend with a wonderful open and fertile mind and a student of history and politics."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71"><span>[</span>71<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72"><span>[</span>72<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Robin Gibb worked on behalf of several charities. He was the organiser of the Sunseeker Ball in aid of the <a href="/wiki/Outward_Bound" title="Outward Bound">Outward Bound</a> Trust. For the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Confederation_of_Societies_of_Authors_and_Composers" title="International Confederation of Societies of Authors and Composers">International Confederation of Societies of Authors and Composers</a> (CISAC), Gibb served as president from 2007 to 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73"><span>[</span>73<span>]</span></a></sup> He was also the longest serving president (2008–2011) of the Heritage Foundation, which honours figures of British culture and facilitated his campaign on behalf of the Bomber Command Memorial Appeal.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74"><span>[</span>74<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75"><span>[</span>75<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_problems_and_death">Health problems and death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Gibb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Health problems and death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 14 August 2010, while performing in <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, Gibb began to feel abdominal pains. On 18 August, at an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oxford,_England" title="Oxford, England">Oxford</a> hospital, he underwent emergency surgery for a blocked <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intestine" title="Intestine">intestine</a>, the same condition that his brother, Maurice, suffered from (Maurice died from a heart attack while awaiting surgery for the condition).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-colon_76-0"><a href="#cite_note-colon-76"><span>[</span>76<span>]</span></a></sup> Gibb recovered and returned to perform concerts in <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> and Australia. During this time, Gibb was also involved in promoting fund-raising for the <a href="/wiki/RAF_Bomber_Command_Memorial" title="RAF Bomber Command Memorial">memorial</a> dedicated to <a href="/wiki/RAF_Bomber_Command" title="RAF Bomber Command">RAF Bomber Command</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_Park,_London" title="Green Park, London">Green Park, London</a>. Gibb also wrote <i>The Titanic Requiem</i> with his son Robin-John, which was recorded by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Philharmonic_Orchestra" title="Royal Philharmonic Orchestra">Royal Philharmonic Orchestra</a> to commemorate the 100th anniversary of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sinking_of_the_Titanic" title="Sinking of the Titanic">sinking of the Titanic</a> in 2012.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Telegraph_Titanic_77-0"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph_Titanic-77"><span>[</span>77<span>]</span></a></sup> Gibb continued to make television appearances and other events following his surgery, but in April 2011 he was forced by health problems to cancel his tour of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-colon_76-1"><a href="#cite_note-colon-76"><span>[</span>76<span>]</span></a></sup> Another concert in Paris was cancelled in October 2011. On 14 October, Gibb was due to perform the charity single with the Soldiers, but was again rushed to hospital with severe abdominal pains.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78"><span>[</span>78<span>]</span></a></sup> On 18 October, following his release from the hospital, Gibb appeared on <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Alan_Titchmarsh_Show" title="The Alan Titchmarsh Show">The Alan Titchmarsh Show</a></i> looking gaunt and frail.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79"><span>[</span>79<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On 27 October 2011, Gibb cancelled an appearance only minutes before he was due to perform at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poppy_Appeal" title="Poppy Appeal">Poppy Appeal</a> Concert in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80"><span>[</span>80<span>]</span></a></sup> Later the same week however, Gibb was seen in London and quoted as saying he felt "absolutely great".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81"><span>[</span>81<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In November 2011, he was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Colorectal_cancer" title="Colorectal cancer">colorectal cancer</a>, which had metastasised to his liver several months earlier.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82"><span>[</span>82<span>]</span></a></sup> In an interview published in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Mail_on_Sunday" title="The Mail on Sunday">The Mail on Sunday</a></i> on 22 January 2012, Gibb spoke for the first time of the cancer.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"For more than 18 months, I had lived with an inflammation of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colon_(anatomy)" title="Colon (anatomy)">colon</a>; then I was diagnosed with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colon_cancer" title="Colon cancer">colon cancer</a>, which spread to the <a href="/wiki/Liver" title="Liver">liver</a>. I have undergone <a href="/wiki/Chemotherapy" title="Chemotherapy">chemotherapy</a>, however, and the results — to quote my doctor — have been 'spectacular'. It’s taken a toll, naturally, but the strange thing is that I've never felt seriously ill. I’ve mostly felt great. There have been many false claims around, which I'd like to dispel. I am not and have never been 'at death's door'. Nor do I have a team of <a href="/wiki/Alternative_medicine" title="Alternative medicine">alternative</a> doctors working on my health. That's not true, although I'm not averse to healthy remedies for any illness. I feel they can go together with conventional medicine. I do eat <a href="/wiki/Health_food" title="Health food">health foods</a> and drink <a href="/wiki/Herbal_tea" title="Herbal tea">herbal teas</a> made for me by Dwina, my wife and RJ's mother. Other than that, I am under the care of Dr. Peter Harper at <a href="/wiki/The_London_Clinic" title="The London Clinic">The London Clinic</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83"><span>[</span>83<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
</blockquote>
<p>In March 2012, Gibb was hospitalised for intestinal surgery and cancelled scheduled appearances while recovering.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84"><span>[</span>84<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85"><span>[</span>85<span>]</span></a></sup> In April, however, he contracted <a href="/wiki/Pneumonia" title="Pneumonia">pneumonia</a> and fell into a <a href="/wiki/Coma" title="Coma">coma</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86"><span>[</span>86<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Donnelly_2012_87-0"><a href="#cite_note-Donnelly_2012-87"><span>[</span>87<span>]</span></a></sup> Although he came out of his coma later in April,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88"><span>[</span>88<span>]</span></a></sup> his colorectal cancer had advanced<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SMH_89-0"><a href="#cite_note-SMH-89"><span>[</span>89<span>]</span></a></sup> and he died in London on 20 May 2012 at the age of 62 from liver and kidney failure.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_death_13-1"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_death-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Toronto_Sun_15-1"><a href="#cite_note-Toronto_Sun-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_90-0"><a href="#cite_note-Time-90"><span>[</span>90<span>]</span></a></sup> His funeral was held on 8 June 2012 and he was buried at the Church of St Mary the Virgin, near his home in <a href="/wiki/Thame" title="Thame">Thame</a>, Oxfordshire.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91"><span>[</span>91<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-92"><a href="#cite_note-92"><span>[</span>92<span>]</span></a></sup> In September of the same year, a blue plaque was placed on the house.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-93"><a href="#cite_note-93"><span>[</span>93<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Gibb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/The_Who" title="The Who">The Who</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Roger_Daltrey" title="Roger Daltrey">Roger Daltrey</a> recalls: "A lovely, lovely guy. I hear everyone talking about the success of their career but I haven't heard many talk about him as a singer and I used to think he was one of the best. To me, singing is about moving people and Robin's voice had something about it that could move me and, I'm sure, millions of others. It was almost like his heart was on the outside." <a href="/wiki/John_Travolta" title="John Travolta">John Travolta</a> recalls, "I thought Robin was one of the most wonderful people, gifted, generous and a real friend to everyone he knew. And we'll miss him."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-94"><a href="#cite_note-94"><span>[</span>94<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tim_Rice" title="Tim Rice">Tim Rice</a> described Gibb as "a charming man". Rice adds: "I saw him only a couple of weeks ago, He wasn't at all well but was putting up a marvelous fight. It's a terrible loss for the music industry." Gibb's friend <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Richard" title="Cliff Richard">Cliff Richard</a> says, "We are a fraternity of people who sing pop and rock and Robin is another one of us who's gone too soon, too early." <a href="/wiki/Ringo_Starr" title="Ringo Starr">Ringo Starr</a> told the BBC: "Robin will be remembered as a musician and as a singer and part of the Bee Gees." <a href="/wiki/Dionne_Warwick" title="Dionne Warwick">Dionne Warwick</a> says: "He was wonderful. He was a jokester. He had an incredibly witty sense of humour and was fun to be around."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-joy_95-0"><a href="#cite_note-joy-95"><span>[</span>95<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kenny_Rogers" title="Kenny Rogers">Kenny Rogers</a> adds: "Robin was a part of something unique. Mostly, Robin was a good guy who didn't deserve to die this young. We will all miss him for what he was as a person and what he brought to music."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-joy_95-1"><a href="#cite_note-joy-95"><span>[</span>95<span>]</span></a></sup> Other artists paid tribute to Gibb including <a href="/wiki/Justin_Timberlake" title="Justin Timberlake">Justin Timberlake</a> (who played Robin in the <i><a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">SNL</a></i> skit <a href="/wiki/The_Barry_Gibb_Talk_Show" title="The Barry Gibb Talk Show">The Barry Gibb Talk Show</a>), <a href="/wiki/Ronan_Keating" title="Ronan Keating">Ronan Keating</a>, <a href="/wiki/Liam_Gallagher" title="Liam Gallagher">Liam Gallagher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hanson_(band)" title="Hanson (band)">Hanson</a>, David Dramain of <a href="/wiki/Disturbed_(band)" title="Disturbed (band)">Disturbed</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atmosphere_(band)" title="Atmosphere (band)">Atmosphere</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paula_Abdul" title="Paula Abdul">Paula Abdul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jake_Shears" title="Jake Shears">Jake Shears</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Scissor_Sisters" title="The Scissor Sisters">The Scissor Sisters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jermaine_Dupri" title="Jermaine Dupri">Jermaine Dupri</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Frampton" title="Peter Frampton">Peter Frampton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adam_Hills" title="Adam Hills">Adam Hills</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Andre" title="Peter Andre">Peter Andre</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Marx" title="Richard Marx">Richard Marx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taboo_(rapper)" title="Taboo (rapper)">Taboo</a> of the <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Eyed_Peas" title="The Black Eyed Peas">The Black Eyed Peas</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Doors" title="The Doors">The Doors</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Mars" title="Bruno Mars">Bruno Mars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sam_Sparro" title="Sam Sparro">Sam Sparro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jamey_Jasta" title="Jamey Jasta">Jamey Jasta</a> of <a href="/wiki/Hatebreed" title="Hatebreed">Hatebreed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Duran_Duran" title="Duran Duran">Duran Duran</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Script" title="The Script">The Script</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Adams" title="Bryan Adams">Bryan Adams</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-96"><a href="#cite_note-96"><span>[</span>96<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acclaim_and_recognition">Acclaim and recognition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Robin_Gibb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Acclaim and recognition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:RobinGibbPlaque.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2448" data-file-width="3264" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/RobinGibbPlaque.jpg/220px-RobinGibbPlaque.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/RobinGibbPlaque.jpg/330px-RobinGibbPlaque.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/RobinGibbPlaque.jpg/440px-RobinGibbPlaque.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:RobinGibbPlaque.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Blue_plaque" title="Blue plaque">Blue plaque</a> of the Heritage Foundation commemorating Robin Gibb at his home in <a href="/wiki/Thame" title="Thame">Thame</a>, Oxfordshire</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In 1994, Gibb was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Songwriters_Hall_of_Fame" title="Songwriters Hall of Fame">Songwriters Hall of Fame</a> at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy_Museum" title="Grammy Museum">Grammy Museum</a> in Los Angeles, California. In 1997, Gibb was inducted into the <a href="/wiki/Rock_and_Roll_Hall_of_Fame" title="Rock and Roll Hall of Fame">Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a> in <a href="/wiki/Cleveland" title="Cleveland">Cleveland</a>, Ohio, US as a member of the Bee Gees. At the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1997_BRIT_Awards" title="1997 BRIT Awards">1997 BRIT Awards</a> held in <a href="/wiki/Earls_Court_Exhibition_Centre" title="Earls Court Exhibition Centre">Earls Court</a>, London on 24 February, the Bee Gees received the award for Outstanding Contribution to Music.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-97"><a href="#cite_note-97"><span>[</span>97<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/2002_New_Year_Honours" title="2002 New Year Honours">New Year Honours 2002</a> Gibb was appointed as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Commander_of_the_Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Commander of the Order of the British Empire">Commander of the Order of the British Empire</a> (CBE) along with his brothers Maurice and Barry. However, the official presentation ceremony at <a href="/wiki/Buckingham_Palace" title="Buckingham Palace">Buckingham Palace</a> in London was delayed until 2004 due to Maurice's death.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CBE_11-2"><a href="#cite_note-CBE-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2004, Gibb and his brother Barry both received honorary <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Music" title="Doctor of Music">doctorates of music</a> from The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Manchester" title="University of Manchester">University of Manchester</a>, England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-98"><a href="#cite_note-98"><span>[</span>98<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2005, Gibb received the <a href="/wiki/Steiger_Award" title="Steiger Award">Steiger Award</a> (Miner Award) in <a href="/wiki/Bochum" title="Bochum">Bochum</a>, Germany for accomplishments in the arts. On 10 July 2009, both brothers were also made <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_the_City" title="Freedom of the City">Freemen</a> of the Borough of Douglas, Isle of Man. The award was also bestowed posthumously on Maurice, therefore confirming the freedom of the town of their birth to Gibb, Barry and Maurice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-99"><a href="#cite_note-99"><span>[</span>99<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The radio and television presenter <a href="/wiki/Paul_Gambaccini" title="Paul Gambaccini">Paul Gambaccini</a> has stated that the Bee Gees were "second only to Lennon and McCartney as the most successful songwriting unit in British popular music", and recognised Gibb as "one of the major figures in the history of British music [and] one of the best white soul voices ever".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_death_13-2"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_death-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> Gibb was a fellow of the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_of_Songwriters,_Composers_and_Authors" title="British Academy of Songwriters, Composers and Authors">British Academy of Songwriters, Composers and Authors</a> (BASCA).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100"><a href="#cite_note-100"><span>[</span>100<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
