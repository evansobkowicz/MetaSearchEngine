<div class="row gdl-page-row-wrapper"><div class="gdl-page-left mb0 page-left-right-sidebar"><div class="row"><div class="gdl-page-item mb0 pb55 twelve columns"><div class="blog-content-wrapper"><div class="gdl-blog-full"><h1 class="blog-title"><a href="http://attitude.co.uk/abbas-frida-reunion-tour-answer/">Abba’s Frida on reunion tour: ‘The answer is no’</a></h1><div class="blog-info-wrapper gdl-item-border"><div class="blog-date"><span class="head">Posted On</span> <a href="http://attitude.co.uk/2014/04/08/">08 Apr 2014 16:38</a></div><div class="blog-author"><span class="head">By :</span> <a href="http://attitude.co.uk/author/nick-levine/" rel="author" title="Posts by Nick Levine">Nick Levine</a></div><div class="blog-tag"><span class="head">Tags: </span> <a href="http://attitude.co.uk/tag/abba/" rel="tag">Abba</a>, <a href="http://attitude.co.uk/tag/music/" rel="tag">music</a></div><div class="clear"></div></div><div class="blog-content"><p><a href="https://attitude.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/ABBA.jpg"><img alt="ABBA" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-18234" height="211" src="//attitude.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2014/04/ABBA-300x211.jpg" width="300"/></a>Frida Lyngstad has ruled out the possibility of Abba ever getting back together to perform live.</p>
<p>In recent years it has been rumoured that the legendary Swedish four-piece, who disbanded in 1982, have received offers of up to $1 billion to reunite for a world tour.</p>
<p>“We only have one answer and that is no — we’re not going to do it,” Frida told London’s <a href="http://www.standard.co.uk/showbiz/no-money-money-money-will-get-abba-on-tour-9246420.html/" target="_blank">Evening Standard</a>​ last night (April 7).</p>
<p>She then pointed to a picture of the band in its 1970s prime and continued: “That’s how we looked at that time, with all the energy, excitement and motivation. It’s not exactly the same nowadays, if you know what I mean.”</p>
<p>The singer went on to add that “no amount of money would change our minds”, before hinting that the band might, however, be tempted to record a song together.</p>
<p>“Maybe we sometimes say it would be good to do a song together again, just a recording and nothing else, but I don’t know if that will happen — so don’t say that we will!” she said.</p>
<p>Frida and her former bandmate Björn Ulvaeus attended a party at London’s Tate Modern last night (April 7) to mark the 40th anniversary of the band’s famous Eurovision triumph and launch <em>ABBA — The Official Photo Book</em>.</p>
