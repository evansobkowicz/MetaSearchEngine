"The Catcher in the Rye" film version – should it happen?</h1>

<h2 class="eza-summary" id="summary" itemprop="description">
<p>"The Catcher in the Rye" as movie? Some fans go on hoping, while others are horrified. </p></h2>
<h3 id="byline">
<span class="eza-authors outer_block" id="authors">
<span class="author">By</span>
<span class="csm_staff"><a data-staff-node-id="114329" href="#"><span class="author" itemprop="author">Marjorie Kehe</span><span class="down_arrow" title="author bio"></span></a></span>
</span>
<time class="eza-publish_date outer_block all_caps" datetime="2010-08-18T11:41:56-04:00" id="date-published" itemprop="datePublished" title="Published: August 18, 2010 11:41 am EDT">August 18, 2010</time> </h3>
</div>
<div id="main-art-hole">
<div class="jcarousel">
<ul>
<li>
<figure class="eza-images" id="image-top-0" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject">
<img alt="" src="//images.csmonitor.com/csmarchives/2010/08/sal8.gif?alias=standard_600x400"/>
<figcaption class="clearfix">
<div class="icon clickable pull_right" id="image-caption-close-btn-0">
<img alt="close" src="/extension/csm_base/design/csm_design/images/close_btn_10x11.png" title="hide caption"/>
</div>
<div class="text" id="image-caption-text-0" itemprop="caption">
Is "The Catcher in the Rye" an "unfilmable" book? </div>
<div class="relateds" id="image-top-0-relateds">
</div>
</figcaption>
<div class="caption_bar clearfix" id="caption_bar-0">
<div class="view_caption pull_left clickable" id="view_caption-0">View Caption</div> </div>
</figure>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
<div class="eza-body clearfix p402_premium" id="story-body">
<p>To many fans, the mere idea of <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/J.D.+Salinger" rel="nofollow" target="_self">J.D. Salinger</a>'s classic "<a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/The+Catcher+in+the+Rye" rel="nofollow" target="_self">The Catcher in the Rye</a>" being turned into a movie seems wrong. After all, didn't protagonist <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Holden+Caulfield" rel="nofollow" target="_self">Holden Caulfield</a> very clearly state: "If there's one thing I hate, it's the movies. Don't even mention them to me"?</p><p>But since Salinger's death in January of this year, <a href="http://blogs.wsj.com/speakeasy/2010/01/28/will-jd-salingers-the-catcher-in-the-rye-finally-be-filmed/" target="_blank">speculation has been brewing</a>. While Salinger definitively shut the door on the idea in his lifetime (he once wrote to a movie producer that “Holden Caulfield himself, in my undoubtedly super-biased opinion, is essentially unactable”), he did also add in the same letter that he might think of "leaving the unsold rights to my wife and daughter as a kind of insurance policy."</p><p>Will his heirs take the bait? There has been further speculation this year that <a href="http://www.slashfilm.com/2010/06/18/could-tax-debt-finally-see-catcher-in-the-rye-rights-land-at-a-hollywood-studio/" target="_blank">questions about a tax loophole</a> might pressure them to do so – and do so quickly. </p><div class="multiplier_story">
					Recommended: 	
																				
															
						
		
	
				
	
				
	

 

	

	
	
																																				

						
			
	<a href="/USA/2011/0104/Could-you-pass-a-US-citizenship-test/Who-signs-bills"><span class="story_link">
			Could you pass a US citizenship test?
		</span></a>
</div><p>A new <a href="http://www.predicto.com/index.cfm?fuse=surveys&amp;surveyName=Will-a-film-adaptation-of-JD-Salingers-classic-book-The-Catcher-in-the-Rye-be-announced-this-year" target="_blank">survey by vote-by text poll site Predicto </a>indicates that 65 percent of voters believe that a film version will be made. </p><div class="pull_right p402_hide" id="story-embed-column"><aside id="story-inset"><div class="ezc-csm_quiz" id="story-inset-0"><div class="inset_block ezc-csm_quiz">
<div class="story_thumbnail"><a href="/USA/2011/0104/Could-you-pass-a-US-citizenship-test"><img alt="" src="//images.csmonitor.com/csmarchives/2011/01/110103-citizenship.jpg?alias=standard_218x145"/></a></div>
<div class="title">
<a href="/csmlists/quizzes"><span class="kicker">Test your knowledge</span></a><a href="/USA/2011/0104/Could-you-pass-a-US-citizenship-test/Who-signs-bills"><span class="story_link">
			Could you pass a US citizenship test?
		</span></a>
</div>
</div></div>

			Photos of the day 02/08
		</span></a>
</div>
</div></div></aside></div><p>Those who say yes are, perhaps, hoping for a miracle along the lines of "<a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/To+Kill+a+Mockingbird" rel="nofollow" target="_self">To Kill a Mockingbird</a>," in which a great book was turned into an equally great movie. But how many times in the average lifetime does such a thing happen?</p><p>Already, pundits are pleading for Hollywood to keep its hands off Holden. "Is 'The Catcher in the Rye' really unfilmable?" asks <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Stuart+McGurk" rel="nofollow" target="_self">Stuart McGurk</a> in <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/film/filmblog/2010/feb/10/catcher-in-the-rye-film" target="_blank">The Guardian</a>, only to conclude that, when it comes to casting Holden, "even the more realistic candidates don't feel quite right." <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/Matt+Zoller+Seitz" rel="nofollow" target="_self">Matt Zoller Seitz</a>, writing for <a href="http://www.salon.com/entertainment/movies/film_salon/2010/06/23/catcher_in_the_rye_movie" target="_blank">Salon</a>, is even more definitive. Even after conceding a film probably will be made, Seitz declares: " 'The Catcher in the Rye' should never be made into a movie. Period."</p><p>You have only to go on <a class="inform_link" href="/tags/topic/YouTube+LLC" rel="nofollow" target="_self">YouTube</a>, of course, to see <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Hfl_HHRL6m4" target="_blank">one</a> or <a href="http://vimeo.com/5796178" target="_blank">two</a> of the visions that some have had for such a project. Whether these horrify or entice you – well, that's probably a personal matter between Holden and you.</p><p><i>Marjorie Kehe is the Monitor's book editor.</i></p><p><i>Should 'The Catcher in the Rye' be turned into a movie? Join the Monitor's book discussion on <a href="http://www.facebook.com/pages/Marjorie-Kehe/235853723852" target="_self">Facebook</a> and <a href="http://twitter.com/MarjorieKehe" target="_self">Twitter</a>.</i><span class="eos_marker" id="end-of-story" title="-30-"></span></p>
