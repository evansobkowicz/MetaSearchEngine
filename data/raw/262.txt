</table><i><b>Avatar: The Last Airbender</b></i>, also known as <i><b>Avatar: The Legend of Aang</b></i> in PAL regions, is an Emmy award-winning American animated television series that aired for three seasons on <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> and the Nicktoons Network. The series was created and produced by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dante_DiMartino" title="Michael Dante DiMartino">Michael Dante DiMartino</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Konietzko" title="Bryan Konietzko">Bryan Konietzko</a>, who served as executive producers along with <a href="/wiki/Aaron_Ehasz" title="Aaron Ehasz">Aaron Ehasz</a>. <i>Avatar'</i>s setting is in an <a href="/wiki/Influences_on_the_Avatar_series" title="Influences on the Avatar series">Asian-influenced</a> <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">world</a> of martial arts and elemental manipulation. The show drew on elements from East Asian, South Asian, and Western culture, making it a mixture of what were previously traditionally separate categories of Japanese anime and Western domestic cartoons.
<p>The series follows the adventures of the main protagonist <a href="/wiki/Aang" title="Aang">Aang</a> and his friends, who must save the world by defeating <a href="/wiki/Fire_Lord" title="Fire Lord">Fire Lord</a> <a href="/wiki/Ozai" title="Ozai">Ozai</a> and ending the <a href="/wiki/Hundred_Year_War" title="Hundred Year War">destructive war</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Fire_Nation" title="Fire Nation">Fire Nation</a>. The show first aired on February 21, 2005 and the series concluded with a widely-lauded <a href="/wiki/Sozin%27s_Comet:_The_Final_Battle" title="Sozin's Comet: The Final Battle">two-hour television movie</a> on July 19, 2008. The show is now available on DVD, the iTunes Store, the Xbox Live Marketplace, the PlayStation Network, and the Zune Marketplace, along with one of Nickelodeon's spinoff networks, Nicktoons.
</p><p><i>Avatar: The Last Airbender</i> was popular with both audiences and critics, garnering 5.6 million viewers on its best-rated showing and receiving high ratings in the Nicktoons lineup, even outside its 6–11-year-old demographic. <i>Avatar</i> has been nominated for and won awards from the Annual Annie Awards, the Genesis Awards and the primetime Emmy Awards, among others. The first season's success prompted Nickelodeon to order second and third seasons. The first part of a movie trilogy titled <i><a href="/wiki/Film:The_Last_Airbender" title="Film:The Last Airbender">The Last Airbender</a></i> was released on July 1, 2010.
</p><p>Merchandise based on the series includes scaled action figures, a <a href="/wiki/Avatar:_The_Last_Airbender_Trading_Card_Game" title="Avatar: The Last Airbender Trading Card Game">trading card game</a>, three video games based individually on each season, stuffed animals distributed by Paramount Parks, and <a href="/wiki/LEGO_Avatar:_The_Last_Airbender" title="LEGO Avatar: The Last Airbender">two LEGO sets</a>. The series' popularity spawned a sequel series, titled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Legend_of_Korra" title="The Legend of Korra">The Legend of Korra</a></i>, which takes place seventy years after the original series.
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Dante_DiMartino" title="Michael Dante DiMartino">Michael Dante DiMartino</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Konietzko" title="Bryan Konietzko">Bryan Konietzko</a> began work on the series at <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> Animation Studios in Burbank, California. According to Bryan Konietzko, the program was conceived in the spring of 2001 when he took an old sketch of a balding, middle-aged man and re-imagined the character as a child. Konietzko drew the character herding bison in the sky and showed the sketch to Mike DiMartino. At the time, DiMartino was studying a documentary about explorers trapped in the South Pole.
</p>

<p><br/>
The co-creators successfully pitched the idea to Nickelodeon VP and executive producer Eric Coleman just two weeks later.
</p><p>The show was first revealed to the public in a teaser reel at San Diego Comic-Con International 2004 and aired February 21, 2005. In the United States, the first two episodes of the series were shown together in a one-hour premiere event. A second twenty-episode season ran from March 17, 2006 through December 1. A third and final season, beginning September 21, 2007, featured twenty-one episodes rather than the usual twenty. The final four episodes were packaged as a two-hour movie.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Premise"> Premise </span></h3>
<figure class="article-thumb tright show-info-icon" style="width: 200px"> <a class="image image-thumbnail" href="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/avatar/images/b/b7/Avatar_World_map.png/revision/latest?cb=20131005142135"><img alt="Avatar World map" class="thumbimage lzy lzyPlcHld " data-image-key="Avatar_World_map.png" data-image-name="Avatar World map.png" data-src="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/avatar/images/b/b7/Avatar_World_map.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width/200?cb=20131005142135" height="113" onload="if(typeof ImgLzy==='object'){ImgLzy.load(this)}" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" width="200"><noscript><img alt="Avatar World map" class="thumbimage " data-image-key="Avatar_World_map.png" data-image-name="Avatar World map.png" height="113" src="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/avatar/images/b/b7/Avatar_World_map.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width/200?cb=20131005142135" width="200"/></noscript></img></a> <figcaption> <a class="sprite info-icon" href="/wiki/File:Avatar_World_map.png"></a> <p class="caption">The series takes place in a world where civilization is divided into <a href="/wiki/Four_nations" title="Four nations">four nations</a>, each country having their own corresponding element and characteristics.</p> </figcaption> </figure>
<p><i>Avatar: The Last Airbender</i> takes place in a fantasy world that is home to humans, fantastic animals, and spirits. Human civilization is divided into <a href="/wiki/Four_nations" title="Four nations">four nations</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Water_Tribe" title="Water Tribe">Water Tribes</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Earth_Kingdom" title="Earth Kingdom">Earth Kingdom</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Air_Nomads" title="Air Nomads">Air Nomads</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Fire_Nation" title="Fire Nation">Fire Nation</a>. Each nation has its own natural element, on which it bases its society, and within each nation exist people known as "benders" who have the innate power and ability to control and manipulate the eponymous element of their nation. The show's creators assigned each bending art its own style of martial arts, causing it to inherit the advantages and weaknesses of the martial arts it was assigned. The four types of <a href="/wiki/Bending_arts" title="Bending arts">bending arts</a> are <a href="/wiki/Waterbending" title="Waterbending">waterbending</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earthbending" title="Earthbending">earthbending</a>, <a href="/wiki/Firebending" title="Firebending">firebending</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Airbending" title="Airbending">airbending</a>.
</p><p>Each generation yields one person who is capable of controlling and manipulating all four elements, the <a href="/wiki/Avatar" title="Avatar">Avatar</a>. When an Avatar dies, they are reincarnated into the next nation in the <a href="/wiki/Avatar#Avatar_Cycle" title="Avatar">Avatar Cycle</a>. The Avatar Cycle parallels the seasons: autumn for the Air Nomads, winter for the Water Tribe, spring for the Earth Kingdom and summer for the Fire Nation. Legend holds the Avatar must master each bending art in order, starting with his or her native element. This can sometimes be compromised when the situation requires it, as <a href="/wiki/Aang" title="Aang">Aang</a> demonstrates in the show. For the Avatar, learning to bend the element opposite his native element can be extremely challenging and difficult. This is because opposing bending arts are based on opposing fighting styles and disciplines. Firebending and waterbending are opposites, as are earthbending and airbending.
</p><p>The Avatar possesses a unique power and ability called the <a href="/wiki/Avatar#Avatar_State" title="Avatar">Avatar State</a>; a defense mechanism which endows the Avatar with all of the knowledge, powers and abilities of all of the past Avatars and acts as a self-triggering defense mechanism, although it can be made subject to the will if the user opens his bodily <a href="/wiki/Chakras" title="Chakras">chakras</a>. If an Avatar is killed in the Avatar State, the reincarnation cycle will be broken, and the Avatar will cease to exist. Through the ages, countless incarnations of the Avatar have served to keep the four nations in harmony and maintain world peace and order. The Avatar also serves as the bridge between the physical world and the <a href="/wiki/Spirit_World" title="Spirit World">Spirit World</a>, allowing him or her to solve problems that normal benders cannot. Another ability that nobody but the Avatar can do is <a href="/wiki/Energybending" title="Energybending">energybending</a>, which Aang demonstrates in the fight with Fire Lord Ozai at <a href="/wiki/Wulong_Forest" title="Wulong Forest">Wulong Forest</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_influences"> Cultural influences </span></h2>
<dl><dd><i>Main article: <a href="/wiki/Influences_on_the_Avatar_series" title="Influences on the Avatar series">Influences on the Avatar series</a></i>
</dd></dl>
<figure class="article-thumb tleft show-info-icon" style="width: 200px"> <a class="image image-thumbnail" href="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/avatar/images/3/30/Writing.png/revision/latest?cb=20090102201443"><img alt="Writing" class="thumbimage lzy lzyPlcHld " data-image-key="Writing.png" data-image-name="Writing.png" data-src="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/avatar/images/3/30/Writing.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width/200?cb=20090102201443" height="150" onload="if(typeof ImgLzy==='object'){ImgLzy.load(this)}" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIABAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAEALAAAAAABAAEAQAICTAEAOw%3D%3D" width="200"><noscript><img alt="Writing" class="thumbimage " data-image-key="Writing.png" data-image-name="Writing.png" height="150" src="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/avatar/images/3/30/Writing.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width/200?cb=20090102201443" width="200"/></noscript></img></a> <figcaption> <a class="sprite info-icon" href="/wiki/File:Writing.png"></a> <p class="caption">Writing implements as depicted in the series had notable eastern influences.</p> </figcaption> </figure>
<p><i>Avatar</i> is notable for borrowing extensively from Asian art and mythology to create its fictional universe. The show's character designs are influenced by both American cartoons and anime; the show, however, is not considered an "anime" because of its origination in the United States. Explicitly stated influences include Chinese art and history, Korean clothing and folk tales, Japanese anime, Hinduism, Taoism, Buddhism, and yoga. The production staff employed a cultural consultant, Edwin Zane, to review scripts.
</p><p>Traditional <a href="/wiki/Writing_in_the_World_of_Avatar" title="Writing in the World of Avatar">East Asian calligraphy</a> styles are used for nearly all the writing in the show. For each instance of calligraphy, an appropriate style is used, ranging from seal script (more archaic) to clerical script. The show employs calligrapher Siu-Leung Lee as a consultant and translator.
</p><p>The choreographed martial art bending moves were profoundly affected by Asian cinema. Western film series such as <a class="extiw" href="http://starwars.wikia.com/wiki/Star_Wars" title="w:c:starwars:Star Wars"><i>Star Wars</i></a>, and literature series such as <a class="extiw" href="http://harrypotter.wikia.com/wiki/Harry_Potter_%28book_series%29" title="w:c:harrypotter:Harry Potter (book series)"><i>Harry Potter</i></a> and <a class="extiw" href="http://lotr.wikia.com/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings" title="w:c:lotr:The Lord of the Rings"><i>The Lord of the Rings</i></a>, were a heavy influence in developing the story of <i>Avatar</i>. In an interview, the creators revealed that they wanted to tell their own epic "legend &amp; love story".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Avatar"> Avatar </span></h3>
<p>The term "Avatar" comes from the Sanskrit word Avatāra, which means "descent". In Hinduism, the gods manifest themselves into Avatars to restore balance on earth, usually during a period of great evil. The Chinese characters that appear at the top of the show's title card mean "the divine spiritual medium who has descended upon the mortal world".
</p><p>When Aang was young, he unknowingly revealed that he was the Avatar when he chose <a href="/wiki/Avatar_relics" title="Avatar relics">four toys</a> out of thousands, each of which were the childhood toys of the previous Avatars. In Tibetan Buddhism, there is a similar test for reincarnations of a Tulku Lama. In <i>Magic and Mystery in Tibet</i>, Alexandra David-Neel writes that "a number of objects such as rosaries, ritualistic implements, books, tea-cups, etc., are placed together, and the child must pick out those which belonged to the late <i>tulku</i>, thus showing that he recognizes the things which were <i>theirs</i> in their previous life". Each successor is expected to show signs of continuity with the previous Avatar, such as being born within a week of the previous Avatar's death.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Elements"> Elements </span></h3>
<p><i>Avatar</i> draws on the four classical elements common to most ancient philosophies (rather than the five classical Chinese elements) for its bending arts: water, earth, fire and air. Although each has its own variation, most ancient philosophies incorporate these four elements in some way: examples include the classical Hindu, Buddhist, Greek and Japanese elemental traditions.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Fighting_styles"> Fighting styles </span></h3>
<p>The fighting choreography of the show draws from martial arts; the fighting styles and weaponry are based on Chinese martial arts, with each bending art corresponding to a certain real-world style. The creators referred to Baguazhang for airbending, Hung Gar for earthbending, Northern Shaolin for firebending, and Tai Chi for waterbending. The only exception to this is <a href="/wiki/Toph_Beifong" title="Toph Beifong">Toph</a>, who employs a Chu Gar Southern Praying Mantis style. The series employed Sifu <a href="/wiki/Kisu" title="Kisu">Kisu</a> of the Harmonious Fist Chinese Athletic Association as a martial arts consultant.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characters"> Characters </span></h2>
<ul><li> <b><a href="/wiki/Aang" title="Aang">Aang</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Zach_Tyler_Eisen" title="Zach Tyler Eisen">Zachary Tyler Eisen</a>) is the fun-loving, 112-year-old protagonist of the series, who is biologically twelve years old but was frozen in an iceberg for one hundred years. He is the current incarnation of the Avatar, the spirit of the world manifested into human form, whose duty is to maintain balance among the nations of the world. Aang is a reluctant hero, who would prefer adventure over his job as the Avatar and making friends over fighting the Fire Nation.
</li></ul>
<ul><li> <b><a href="/wiki/Katara" title="Katara">Katara</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Mae_Whitman" title="Mae Whitman">Mae Whitman</a>) is a fourteen-year-old female waterbender of the Southern Water Tribe, the only waterbender in the tribe. Katara discovers and frees Aang from an iceberg in which he had been trapped for a hundred years. With her fifteen-year-old brother Sokka, she accompanies Aang on his quest to defeat the Fire Lord and bring peace to the world. In the original <a href="/wiki/Unaired_pilot" title="Unaired pilot">unaired pilot</a> episode, Katara's name was Kya; this later used as her mother's name.
</li></ul>

<ul><li> <b><a href="/wiki/Sokka" title="Sokka">Sokka</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Jack_DeSena" title="Jack DeSena">Jack DeSena</a>) is a fifteen-year-old warrior of the Southern Water Tribe. With his sister Katara, he accompanies Aang on his quest to defeat the Fire Lord. The joker of the group, Sokka describes himself as "meat-loving" and "sarcastic". Unlike his companions, Sokka cannot bend an element, but the series, though it often makes him the victim of comedy at his expense, frequently grants him opportunities to use his ingenuity and weapons, including his trusty boomerang and a sword he forged from a meteorite.
</li></ul>
<ul><li> <b><a href="/wiki/Toph_Beifong" title="Toph Beifong">Toph Beifong</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Jessie_Flower" title="Jessie Flower">Jessie Flower</a>) is a twelve-year-old blind earthbender. In Book Two, she leaves her wealthy family and comfortable home to join Aang on his quest, with a plan to teach him earthbending. Though blind, Toph "sees" by feeling the vibrations in the ground through her feet. She becomes the first earthbender to learn to bend metal and is considered one of the most powerful earthbenders.
</li></ul>
<ul><li> <b><a href="/wiki/Zuko" title="Zuko">Zuko</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Dante_Basco" title="Dante Basco">Dante Basco</a>) is the sixteen-year-old exiled prince of the Fire Nation and original antagonist of the series. Due to events in Zuko's past, his father, Fire Lord Ozai, deems him a complete failure, and Zuko feels he must capture the Avatar to regain his honor. Over time, Zuko struggles to deal with his anger, self-pity, and familial relationships; meanwhile, he grows sympathetic to the peoples his nation has terrorized. In Book Three, he defects from the Fire Nation and joins Aang and the team in order to teach Aang firebending. At the end of the series, he is crowned ruler of the Fire Nation.
</li></ul>
<ul><li> <b><a href="/wiki/Suki" title="Suki">Suki</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Jennie_Kwan" title="Jennie Kwan">Jennie Kwan</a>) is the fifteen-year-old leader of the <a href="/wiki/Kyoshi_Warriors" title="Kyoshi Warriors">Kyoshi Warriors</a> and the last to join Aang's quest. Introduced early and briefly at her home on <a href="/wiki/Kyoshi_Island" title="Kyoshi Island">Kyoshi Island</a>, she is seen twice more, again briefly, in Book Two, before permanently joining <a href="/wiki/Team_Avatar" title="Team Avatar">Team Avatar</a> after Sokka and Zuko rescue her from a Fire Nation prison in Book Three. Like Sokka, she is not a bender but is quite capable of handling herself in a fight with sword, war fan, and acrobatic skills. By the end of the series, she is the evident love interest of Sokka.
</li></ul>
<ul><li> <b><a href="/wiki/Iroh" title="Iroh">Iroh</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Mako_Iwamatsu" title="Mako Iwamatsu">Mako</a> in Books One and Two, <a href="/wiki/Greg_Baldwin" title="Greg Baldwin">Greg Baldwin</a> in Book Three) is a retired Fire Nation general, known as the Dragon of the West, and Prince Zuko's uncle and mentor. Iroh was the original heir to the Fire Nation throne until his brother usurped the throne after Fire Lord <a href="/wiki/Azulon" title="Azulon">Azulon</a>'s death. On the surface, Iroh is a cheerful, kind, and optimistically eccentric old man, but he still remains a competent warrior and a devoted surrogate parent to Zuko. Iroh is a Grand Master of the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_White_Lotus" title="Order of the White Lotus">Order of the White Lotus</a>, a secret society of men from all nations and helps retake <a href="/wiki/Ba_Sing_Se" title="Ba Sing Se">Ba Sing Se</a> during the series finale. Unlike most firebenders, Iroh does not use fury as the source of his strength; instead he uses the original firebending skills learned from the <a href="/wiki/Dragon" title="Dragon">dragons</a>. He too, is one of the few firebenders with the rare ability to generate lightning and redirect it as well (a technique he developed himself).
</li></ul>
<ul><li> <b><a href="/wiki/Ozai" title="Ozai">Ozai</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Mark_Hamill" title="Mark Hamill">Mark Hamill</a>) is the tyrannical ruler of the Fire Nation during the final years of the Hundred Year War and the central antagonist of the series. Like every Fire Lord before him, Ozai is a firebending master, and prior to the ending of the War, he is believed to be one of the most powerful firebenders in the <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_the_World_of_Avatar" title="Geography of the World of Avatar">World</a>. Ozai is willing to go to any lengths to achieve his goal of world domination, even sacrificing his own people and committing genocide.
</li></ul>
<ul><li> <b><a href="/wiki/Azula" title="Azula">Azula</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Grey_DeLisle" title="Grey DeLisle">Grey DeLisle</a>) is the fourteen-year-old princess of the Fire Nation. She is Zuko's younger sister and one of the major antagonists of the series. Azula is a firebending prodigy and is one of the few living firebenders capable of summoning <a href="/wiki/Lightning_generation" title="Lightning generation">lightning</a>. She has no qualms about bullying and threatening her relatives, reserving any familial loyalty for her father. She is first introduced at the end of Book One, although she appears in the background in an earlier episode.
</li></ul>
<ul><li> <b><a href="/wiki/Zhao" title="Zhao">Zhao</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Jason_Isaacs" title="Jason Isaacs">Jason Isaacs</a>) is the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Military_of_the_Fire_Nation#Navy" title="Military of the Fire Nation">Fire Nation Navy</a> and the secondary antagonist of Book One. He is a master firebender and, as one of the most powerful men in the Fire Nation. Zhao seeks to become a Fire Nation hero and go down in his nation's history as a legend. To achieve this end, Zhao will go to any lengths even killing a <a href="/wiki/Spirit_World" title="Spirit World">Spirit</a>.
</li></ul>
<ul><li> <b><a href="/wiki/Long_Feng" title="Long Feng">Long Feng</a></b> (<a href="/wiki/Clancy_Brown" title="Clancy Brown">Clancy Brown</a>) is the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Secretariat_of_Ba_Sing_Se" title="Grand Secretariat of Ba Sing Se">Grand Secretariat of Ba Sing Se</a>, the leader of the <a href="/wiki/Dai_Li" title="Dai Li">Dai Li</a>, and the secondary antagonist of Book Two. As <a href="/wiki/Earth_monarch" title="Earth monarch">Earth King</a> <a href="/wiki/Kuei" title="Kuei">Kuei</a>'s closest personal adviser, Long Feng hid the Hundred Year War's existence outside the Earth Kingdom city's walls while the Dai Li supplanted the king's power over his people. Long Feng will go to any lengths to maintain his control over Ba Sing Se including, but not limited to, blackmail, brainwashing, and kidnapping.
</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Series_synopsis"> Series synopsis </span></h2>
<p>One hundred years before the start of the series, a twelve year old airbender <a href="/wiki/Aang" title="Aang">Aang</a> learns that he is the new Avatar. Fearful of the heavy responsibilities of stopping an impending world war and with the impending separation from his mentor Monk <a href="/wiki/Gyatso" title="Gyatso">Gyatso</a>, Aang flees from home on his flying bison <a href="/wiki/Appa" title="Appa">Appa</a>. During a fierce storm, they crash into the ocean, and Aang's Avatar state freezes them in a state of suspended animation inside an iceberg.
</p>

He realizes that he must fulfill his destiny of becoming a fully realized Avatar and return balance to the world by defeating the Fire Nation. Aang sets out to master the three unlearned elements: water, earth, and fire. With Katara and Sokka, Aang decides to head first to the North Pole to find a waterbending master.
</p><p>Aang soon discovers that Sozin's Comet, which Fire Lord <a href="/wiki/Sozin" title="Sozin">Sozin</a> used as a power supply to start the Hundred Year War, will return in the coming summer, giving the Fire Nation enough power to ultimately accomplish victory. Aang realizes that he must master all four elements and end the War before this time. For most of their journey to the North Pole, the group is pursued by <a href="/wiki/Zuko" title="Zuko">Zuko</a>, a banished <a href="/wiki/Fire_Nation" title="Fire Nation">Fire Nation</a> prince and son of Fire Lord Ozai who is obsessed with capturing Aang to restore his lost honor.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Response"> Response </span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ratings"> Ratings </span></h3>
<p>When the show debuted, it was rated the best animated television series in its demographic; new episodes averaged 1.1 million viewers each. A one-hour special showing of "The Secret of the Fire Nation" which aired on September 15, 2006, consisting of "<a href="/wiki/The_Serpent%27s_Pass" title="The Serpent's Pass">The Serpent's Pass</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/The_Drill" title="The Drill">The Drill</a>", gathered an audience of 4.1 million viewers. According to Nielsen Media Research, the special was the best performing cable television show airing in that week. In 2007, <i>Avatar</i> was syndicated to more than 105 countries worldwide and was one of Nickelodeon's top rated programs. The series was ranked first on Nickelodeon in Germany, Indonesia, Malaysia, Belgium, and Colombia.
</p><p>The series finale, <i><a href="/wiki/Sozin%27s_Comet:_The_Final_Battle" title="Sozin's Comet: The Final Battle">Sozin's Comet: The Final Battle</a></i>, received the highest ratings of the series. Its July 19, 2008 premiere averaged 5.6 million viewers, 95% more viewers than Nickelodeon had received in mid-July 2007. During the week of July 14, it ranked as the most-viewed program for the under-14 demographic. <i>Sozin's Comet</i> also appeared on <a class="external text exitstitial" href="http://iTunes.com" rel="nofollow">iTunes</a>' top ten list of best-selling television episodes during that same week. <i>Sozin's Comet</i>'s popularity affected online media as well; "<a href="/wiki/Rise_of_the_Phoenix_King" title="Rise of the Phoenix King">Rise of the Phoenix King</a>", a <a href="/wiki/Nick.com%27s_Avatar_Index" title="Nick.com's Avatar Index">Nick.com</a> online game based on <i>Sozin's Comet</i>, generated almost 815,000 game plays within three days.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Anime_or_cartoon"> Anime or cartoon </span></h3>
<dl><dd><i>Main article: <a href="/wiki/Influences_on_the_Avatar_series#Anime" title="Influences on the Avatar series">Influences on the Avatar series#Anime</a></i>
</dd></dl>
<p>The debate over <i>Avatar</i> being considered an anime is a controversial one; one reviewer commented that "<i>Avatar</i> blurs the line between anime and (US) domestic cartoons until it becomes irrelevant".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SFT_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-SFT-1">[2]</a></sup> <i>Avatar</i> has many features typical of anime, such as a color palette distinctive from most American cartoons.
</p>
<div class="quote">
<dl><dd><span>"<i>The best anime balances great action sequences with humor and emotion, something we try to do on </i>Avatar<i>. We love all the films of Hayao Miyazaki, especially </i>Spirited Away<i> and </i>Princess Mononoke<i>. Both movies deal with spirituality and the environment in an entertaining way. Also, there's a lot of great animation.</i>"</span>
</dd><dd>―<i>Avatar</i> creators <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Konietzko" title="Bryan Konietzko">Bryan Konietzko</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dante_DiMartino" title="Michael Dante DiMartino">Michael Dante DiMartino</a> confirming a particular anime influence in a magazine interview.</dd></dl></div>

<p><br/>
According to an interview with the artists of <i>Avatar</i>, Appa's design was based on the Catbus in <i>My Neighbor Totoro</i>, due to the peculiar task of creating a mammal with six legs.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AAS_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-AAS-2">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Avatar</i> draws inspiration from Shinichiro Watanabe's <i>Cowboy Bebop</i> and <i>Samurai Champloo</i>, as well as <i>FLCL</i> (<i>Fooly Cooly</i>) of Gainax. Other various studios from which inspiration was drawn include Studio 4 °C, Production I.G, and Studio Ghibli. Bryan has commented that some of his most cherished Watanabe fight scenes were the fight between <i>Bebop's</i> Spike Spiegel and a drug smuggler in "Asteroid Blues" as well as the duel between Mugen and a blind female Jojutsu-user in the <i>Champloo</i> episode "Elegy of Entrapment (Verse 2)". <i>Avatar</i> director Giancarlo Volpe also claims the staff "were all ordered to buy <i>FLCL</i> and watch every single episode of it".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_media"> Other media </span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion_and_merchandising"> Promotion and merchandising </span></h3>
<p><i>Avatar'</i>s success has led to some promotional advertising with third-party companies, such as Burger King and Upper Deck Entertainment. <i>Avatar</i>-themed roller coasters at Kings Island and at Nickelodeon Universe in the Mall of America also appeared. During the show's runtime, Nickelodeon published two special issues of <i>Nick Mag Presents</i> dedicated entirely to the show. Various members of the <i>Avatar</i> staff and cast appeared at the 2006 San Diego Comic-Con International convention, while Michael Dante DiMartino and Bryan Konietzko appeared with Martial Arts Consultant Sifu Kisu at the Pacific Media Expo on October 28, 2006. <i>Avatar</i> also has its own line of t-shirts, <a href="/wiki/LEGO_Avatar:_The_Last_Airbender" title="LEGO Avatar: The Last Airbender">LEGO playsets</a>, toys, a trading card game, a cine-manga, and three video games. Also in September; <i><a href="/wiki/Legends_of_the_Arena" title="Legends of the Arena">Legends of the Arena</a></i>, an MMO, was released online.
</p><p>The Mattel-produced action figure toy line generated some controversy with its exclusion of any female characters. Mattel came to release information stating that they have taken account of Katara's increased role within the program, and that she would be included in the figure assortment for a mid-2007 release. The figure ultimately went unreleased, however, as the entire line was canceled before she could be produced.
</p><p>Nickelodeon executives have since released optimistic plans for upcoming marketing strategies in regards to <i>Avatar</i>. Nickelodeon President Cyma Zarghami openly stated his belief that the franchise "could become their <i>Harry Potter</i>". They expected consumers to spend about $121 million in 2007, rising to $254 million by 2009. The marketing plans were to be coincided with the release of the first live-action film based on the series in 2010, which was to be the first film in a trilogy.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feature_film"> Feature film </span></h3>
<dl><dd><i>Main article: <a href="/wiki/Film:The_Last_Airbender" title="Film:The Last Airbender">Film:The Last Airbender</a></i>
</dd></dl>

<p>On January 8, 2007, Paramount Pictures' MTV Films and <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> Movies announced that they have signed <a href="/wiki/Film:M._Night_Shyamalan" title="Film:M. Night Shyamalan">M. Night Shyamalan</a> to write, direct and produce a trilogy of live-action films based on the series; the first of these films encompasses the main characters' adventures in Book One. The film was in a dispute with James Cameron's film <i>Avatar</i> regarding title ownership, which resulted in the film being titled <i>The Last Airbender</i>. It was released on July 1, 2010. Shyamalan was attracted to the series because of its inspiring martial arts and spiritual theme, after being introduced to the program by his children. Filming began in March 2009 and take place in Philadelphia and Greenland. Producer <a href="/wiki/Film:Frank_Marshall" title="Film:Frank Marshall">Frank Marshall</a> stated the film may be moved to later in 2010 or even to early 2011, and that some filming could happen in the Far East.
</p><p>According to an interview with the co-creators in <i>SFX Magazine</i>, Shyamalan came across <i>Avatar</i> when his daughter wanted to be <a href="/wiki/Katara" title="Katara">Katara</a> for Halloween. Intrigued, Shyamalan researched and watched the series with his family. "Watching <i>Avatar</i> has become a family event in my house ... so we are looking forward to how the story develops in season three," said Shyamalan. "Once I saw the amazing world that <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dante_DiMartino" title="Michael Dante DiMartino">Mike</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Konietzko" title="Bryan Konietzko">Bryan</a> created, I knew it would make a great feature film."
</p><p><i>Avatar</i> co-creators <a href="/wiki/Michael_Dante_DiMartino" title="Michael Dante DiMartino">Mike DiMartino</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Konietzko" title="Bryan Konietzko">Bryan Konietzko</a> voiced their opinion within an interview regarding M. Night Shyamalan writing, directing and producing the film. The two displayed much enthusiasm over Shyamalan's decision for the adaptation, stating that they admire his work and, in turn, he respects their material. M. Night Shyamalan said he will write the <a href="/wiki/Film:The_Last_Airbender_2" title="Film:The Last Airbender 2">second film</a> while preparing to shoot the first. <a href="/wiki/Film:James_Newton_Howard" title="Film:James Newton Howard">James Newton Howard</a>, who had composed all of Shyamalan's films since <i>The Sixth Sense</i>, composed <i>The Last Airbender</i>.
</p><p>Shyamalan originally offered the roles of <a href="/wiki/Film:Aang" title="Film:Aang">Aang</a> to karate-trained Texan <a href="/wiki/Film:Noah_Ringer" title="Film:Noah Ringer">Noah Ringer</a>; <a href="/wiki/Film:Sokka" title="Film:Sokka">Sokka</a> to <a href="/wiki/Film:Jackson_Rathbone" title="Film:Jackson Rathbone">Jackson Rathbone</a> (<i>Twilight</i>); <a href="/wiki/Film:Katara" title="Film:Katara">Katara</a> to <a href="/wiki/Film:Nicola_Peltz" title="Film:Nicola Peltz">Nicola Peltz</a> (<i>Deck the Halls</i>); and <a href="/wiki/Film:Zuko" title="Film:Zuko">Prince Zuko</a> to Jesse McCartney. The casting of white actors triggered negative fan reaction marked by accusations of racism, a letter-writing campaign, and a protest outside of a Philadelphia casting call for movie extras. Rathbone dismissed the complaints, saying "I think it's one of those things where I pull my hair up, shave the sides, and I definitely need a tan. It's one of those things where, hopefully, the audience will suspend disbelief a little bit." In February 2009, <a href="/wiki/Film:Dev_Patel" title="Film:Dev Patel">Dev Patel</a> replaced McCartney, whose tour dates conflicted with a boot camp scheduled for the cast to train in martial arts. <a href="/wiki/Film:Aasif_Mandvi" title="Film:Aasif Mandvi">Aasif Mandvi</a> plays <a href="/wiki/Film:Zhao" title="Film:Zhao">Commander Zhao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Film:Shaun_Toub" title="Film:Shaun Toub">Shaun Toub</a> plays <a href="/wiki/Film:Iroh" title="Film:Iroh">Uncle Iroh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Film:Cliff_Curtis" title="Film:Cliff Curtis">Cliff Curtis</a> plays <a href="/wiki/Film:Ozai" title="Film:Ozai">Fire Lord Ozai</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Film:Keong_Sim" title="Film:Keong Sim">Keong Sim</a> plays the <a href="/wiki/Film:Earthbending_father" title="Film:Earthbending father">earthbending father</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Games"> Games </span></h3>
<dl><dd><i>See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Avatar_games" title="List of Avatar games">List of Avatar games</a></i>
</dd></dl>
<p>A video game trilogy about <i>Avatar</i> has been created: <a href="/wiki/Avatar:_The_Last_Airbender_(video_game)" title="Avatar: The Last Airbender (video game)"><i>Avatar: The Last Airbender</i></a>, the video game, was released on October 10, 2006; <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar:_The_Last_Airbender_%E2%80%93_The_Burning_Earth" title="Avatar: The Last Airbender – The Burning Earth">Avatar: The Last Airbender – The Burning Earth</a></i> was released on October 16, 2007' and <i><a href="/wiki/Avatar:_The_Last_Airbender_%E2%80%93_Into_the_Inferno" title="Avatar: The Last Airbender – Into the Inferno">Avatar: The Last Airbender – Into the Inferno</a></i> was released on October 13, 2008. The three games were loosely based on seasons one, two and three, respectively. Players can select characters and complete quests to gain experience and advance the storyline. Despite lackluster critical reviews, the games did extremely well commercially; for example, Avatar: The Last Airbender was THQ's top selling Nickelodeon game in 2006 and even reached Sony CEA's "Greatest Hits" status.
</p><p><i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Avatar:_Legends_of_the_Arena" title="Avatar: Legends of the Arena">Avatar: Legends of the Arena</a></i>, a video game for Microsoft Windows launched on September 25, 2008 by <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a>. Each user is able to create their own character, choose a nation, and interact with others across the globe.
</p>

</li></ul>
