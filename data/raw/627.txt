<p>The singles discography of English musician/singer-songwriter <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a> consists of 128 official singles and 17 as featured artist, as well as 40 other non-single guest appearances. Across his career, John has yielded 56 top 40 hits in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, only second to <a href="/wiki/Elvis_Presley" title="Elvis Presley">Elvis Presley</a> in total,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ejofficial_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-ejofficial-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> with 27 of those hitting the top ten and 9 achieving number-one. In his native <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, he has accumulated 71 top 40 singles, including 33 top tens and 8 number-ones. In 1997, John released the <a href="/wiki/A-side_and_B-side#Double_A-side" title="A-side and B-side">double A-side</a> single "<a href="/wiki/Something_About_the_Way_You_Look_Tonight" title="Something About the Way You Look Tonight">Something About the Way You Look Tonight</a>"/"<a href="/wiki/Candle_in_the_Wind_1997" title="Candle in the Wind 1997">Candle in the Wind 1997</a>" in dedication to the memory of <a href="/wiki/Diana,_Princess_of_Wales" title="Diana, Princess of Wales">Diana, Princess of Wales</a>. It subsequently hit number-one in every country that it charted in and became the biggest selling single of all-time since the UK and US charts began in the 1950s, with sales of 33 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guinness_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Guinness-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> John has sold 100 million singles worldwide.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
