<p><b>Jake Matthew Lloyd</b> (born March 5, 1989) is an American former <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actor</a> who gained worldwide fame when he was chosen by <a href="/wiki/George_Lucas" title="George Lucas">George Lucas</a> to play the young <a href="/wiki/Darth_Vader#prequel_trilogy" title="Darth Vader">Anakin Skywalker</a> in the 1999 film <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_I:_The_Phantom_Menace" title="Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace">Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace</a></i>, the first film in the <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars#Prequel_trilogy" title="Star Wars"><i>Star Wars</i> prequel trilogy</a>. He reprised this role in five subsequent <i>Star Wars</i> video games.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jake_Lloyd&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lloyd was born in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fort_Collins" title="Fort Collins">Fort Collins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">Colorado</a>, the son of William 'Bill' Lloyd, an <a href="/wiki/Emergency_medical_technician" title="Emergency medical technician">E.M.T.</a> set <a href="/wiki/Medic" title="Medic">medic</a>, and Lisa Riley, an entertainment agent.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Carmel_High_School_(Carmel,_Indiana)" title="Carmel High School (Carmel, Indiana)">Carmel High School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Carmel,_Indiana" title="Carmel, Indiana">Carmel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>.</p>
<p>In 1996, Lloyd began his acting career playing Jimmy Sweet in 4 episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/ER_(TV_series)" title="ER (TV series)">ER</a></i>. Afterwards, he was cast as Jake Warren in <i><a href="/wiki/Unhook_the_Stars" title="Unhook the Stars">Unhook the Stars</a></i>. He got his big break playing Jamie Langston in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jingle_All_The_Way" title="Jingle All The Way">Jingle All The Way</a></i>. Afterwards, he played Mark Armstrong in <i><a href="/wiki/Apollo_11_(film)" title="Apollo 11 (film)">Apollo 11</a></i>.</p>
<p>After portraying Anakin in a chain of related video games, Lloyd retired from acting in 2001, citing bullying on the part of classmates and the stress of doing up to 60 interviews a day as his reasons for doing so. He left Hollywood for Chicago, and dropped out after a semester at <a href="/wiki/Columbia_College_Chicago" title="Columbia College Chicago">Columbia College Chicago</a>. He studies film and Psychology at his college.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NBCNews_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-NBCNews-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>He continues to make appearances, however, at sci-fi and comic-book festivals.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NBCNews_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-NBCNews-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> He currently lives in <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">Indianapolis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>. Lloyd claimed in a 2012 interview that director <a href="/wiki/George_Lucas" title="George Lucas">George Lucas</a> ruined his childhood and his acting career, and that Lloyd destroyed all of his Star Wars memorabilia out of anger toward the film.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2012 he announced he was directing a documentary highlighting the Tibetan refugees in India, who are attempting to escape the persecution of the Chinese.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> He was commissioned in 2012 to create a promo for singer Mallory Low's song, and <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_II:_Attack_of_the_Clones" title="Star Wars Episode II: Attack of the Clones">Star Wars Episode II: Attack of the Clones</a></i> actor <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Logan" title="Daniel Logan">Daniel Logan</a> was cast as the star in his video. Logan is the actor who played the younger <a href="/wiki/Boba_Fett" title="Boba Fett">Boba Fett</a> in <i>Attack of the Clones</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
