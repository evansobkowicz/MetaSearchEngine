<p><b>Sydney Carton</b> is a central character in the novel <i><a href="/wiki/A_Tale_of_Two_Cities" title="A Tale of Two Cities">A Tale of Two Cities</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Dickens" title="Charles Dickens">Charles Dickens</a>. He is a shrewd young <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">Englishman</a> and sometime junior to his fellow <a href="/wiki/Barrister" title="Barrister">barrister</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/C.J._Stryver" title="C.J. Stryver">C.J. Stryver</a>. In the novel, he is seen to be a drunkard, self-indulgent and self-pitying because of his wasted life. He has a strong, <a href="/wiki/Unrequited_love" title="Unrequited love">unrequited love</a> for <a href="/wiki/Lucie_Manette" title="Lucie Manette">Lucie Manette</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Carton.27s_character">Carton's character</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sydney_Carton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Carton's character">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sydney Carton is introduced into the novel <i><a href="/wiki/A_Tale_of_Two_Cities" title="A Tale of Two Cities">A Tale of Two Cities</a></i> as a young, sloppy but brilliant lawyer who bears an uncanny likeness to <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darnay" title="Charles Darnay">Charles Darnay</a>, the prisoner he is defending. He uses his great skill to save Darnay from death, passing his case to his colleague Stryver, who takes all the glory for saving Darnay. It is then revealed that Carton both likes and hates Darnay, as he sees him as everything he should be but is not. Carton is called a "<a href="/wiki/Jackal" title="Jackal">jackal</a>" because it appears that, while Mr. Stryver very deftly presents each case, it is Carton's legal acumen that helps win them, though Stryver gets all the credit—a reference to how the jackals help the lions with the kill, while the lions take all the glory. It is also seen that Carton is an alcoholic who faces a great lack of self-confidence. He develops an unrequited love for Lucie Manette, which he tells her about. He says that he would do anything for her or for anybody that she loves.</p>
<p>Darnay returns to France, and is arrested for being an aristocrat (his original name is Charles Evrémonde). Before his execution by guillotine, Carton steps in and tricks Darnay into trading places with him, for Lucie and for the sake of their friendship. This is done with the help of <a href="/wiki/John_Barsad" title="John Barsad">John Barsad</a>, an English spy working at one of the French prisons, after a conversation described as a "hand of cards". His final words (or what Dickens says would have been his final words) are among the most famous in English literature:</p>

<tr>
<td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">“</td>
<td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">It is a far, far better thing that I do, than I have ever done; it is a far, far better rest that I go to than I have ever known.</td>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Interpretations">Interpretations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sydney_Carton&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Interpretations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li>The historical novel <i>A Far Better Rest</i> (2000) by American author <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Susanne_Alleyn&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Susanne Alleyn (page does not exist)">Susanne Alleyn</a> is a re-telling of <i>A Tale of Two Cities</i> from Carton's perspective.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (December 2010)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup></li>
<li>The historical novel <i>The Carton Chronicles : The Curious Tale of Flashman's true father</i> (2010) by Keith Laidler imagines that Sydney Carton had a last minute change of heart, escaped the guillotine and went on to work as a spy for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robespierre" title="Robespierre">Robespierre</a> whilst attempting to win Lucie Manette / Darnay's heart. In his narrative Carton also confesses to being the real father of <a href="/wiki/Harry_Flashman" title="Harry Flashman">Harry Flashman</a> the roguish hero of the series of books created by <a href="/wiki/George_MacDonald_Fraser" title="George MacDonald Fraser">George MacDonald Fraser</a> who in turn borrowed him from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tom_Brown%27s_Schooldays" title="Tom Brown's Schooldays">Tom Brown's Schooldays</a> by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Hughes" title="Thomas Hughes">Thomas Hughes</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
