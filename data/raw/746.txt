<p>It's official: a human being other than Celine Dion now owns <a href="http://josephmontanaro.com/details.php?lang=en&amp;page=0&amp;lid=238&amp;country&amp;state&amp;range&amp;type=0" target="_hplink">the singer's majestic Quebec mansion.</a></p><p>It's unknown who bought the luxurious home and how much he or she offered, but the asking price was almost $30 million, <a href="http://www.usatoday.com/story/money/business/2013/08/31/celine-dion-mansions/2749033/" target="_hplink">according to USA Today.</a></p><p>The French-Canadian singer had listed the 24,000 square-foot home ‚Äî built in 2001 on a private island in Laval, Que., and chock full of antique furniture ‚Äî in May 2012.</p><p>She decided to sell off the property, as well as her $72-million Florida compound, due to her <a href="http://news.nationalpost.com/2012/05/18/celine-dion-selling-29-7m-mansion-on-private-island/" target="_hplink">‚Äúintensive performance schedule‚Äù</a>in Las Vegas, according to the National Post.</p><p>In 2011, Dion started a three-year contract at The Colosseum at Caesars Palace in Las Vegas, and <a href="http://www.usatoday.com/story/money/business/2013/08/31/celine-dion-mansions/2749033/" target="_hplink">recently extended it until 2019.</a></p><p>Check out photos from both of the mansions in the slideshow below:</p>
