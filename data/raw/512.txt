<p><i><b>Paracanthurus hepatus</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of Indo-Pacific <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surgeonfish" title="Surgeonfish">surgeonfish</a>. A popular fish in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_aquaria" title="Marine aquaria">marine aquaria</a>, it is the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monotypic" title="Monotypic">only member</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><b>Paracanthurus</b></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FishBase_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-FishBase-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ITIS_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-ITIS-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> A number of common names are attributed to the species, including <b>regal tang</b>, <b>palette surgeonfish</b>, <b>blue tang</b> (leading to confusion with the Atlantic <i><a href="/wiki/Acanthurus_coeruleus" title="Acanthurus coeruleus">Acanthurus coeruleus</a></i>), <b>royal blue tang</b>, <b>hippo tang</b>, <b>flagtail surgeonfish</b>, <b>Pacific regal blue tang</b> and <b>blue surgeonfish</b>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paracanthurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Paracanthurus hepatus</i> has a royal blue body, yellow tail, and black 'palette' design. The lower body is yellow in the west-central <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-name_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-name-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> It grows to 30 cm (12 in.).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FishBase_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-FishBase-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> This fish is rather flat, like a <a href="/wiki/Pancake" title="Pancake">pancake</a>, with a circular body shape, a pointed snout-like nose, and small scales. The blue tang has 9 dorsal spines, 26–28 dorsal soft rays, 3 anal spines, and 24–26 anal soft rays.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paracanthurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The species' range is broad, but it is common nowhere. It can be found throughout the <a href="/wiki/Indo-Pacific" title="Indo-Pacific">Indo-Pacific</a>. It is seen in reefs of <a href="/wiki/East_Africa" title="East Africa">East Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">Samoa</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia" title="New Caledonia">New Caledonia</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Great_Barrier_Reef" title="Great Barrier Reef">Great Barrier Reef</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FishBase_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-FishBase-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The regal blue tang is one of the most common and most popular marine aquarium fish all over the world. They live in pairs, or in a small groups of up to 10 or 12 individuals. These fish reach sexual maturity at 9–12 months of age.</p>
<p>The regal blue tang is ranked LC (least concern) by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_Conservation_Union" title="World Conservation Union">World Conservation Union</a> (IUCN), but is of low vulnerability.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FishBase_1-3"><a href="#cite_note-FishBase-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paracanthurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a juvenile, its diet consists primarily of <a href="/wiki/Plankton" title="Plankton">plankton</a>. Adults are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Omnivorous" title="Omnivorous">omnivorous</a> and feed on plankton, but will also graze on algae. Spawning occurs during late afternoon and evening hours. This event is indicated by a change in color from a uniform dark blue to a pale blue.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_cycle">Life cycle</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paracanthurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Life cycle">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Males aggressively court female members of the school, leading to a quick upward spawning rush toward the surface of the water during which eggs and sperm are released. The eggs are small, approximately 0.8 mm in diameter. The eggs are <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pelagic" title="Pelagic">pelagic</a>, each containing a single droplet of oil for flotation. The fertilized eggs hatch in twenty-four hours, revealing small, translucent larvae with silvery abdomens and rudimentary caudal spines. Regal blue tangs can also, when faced with danger or dark spaces, make themselves semi-transparent, in order to help with evasion and light passivity, respectively.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Importance_to_humans">Importance to humans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paracanthurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Importance to humans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The regal blue tang is of minor commercial fisheries importance, however, it is a <a href="/wiki/Bait_fish" title="Bait fish">bait fish</a>. The flesh has a strong odor and is not highly prized. This fish may cause <a href="/wiki/Ciguatera" title="Ciguatera">ciguatera</a> poisoning if consumed by humans. However, regal blue tangs are collected commercially for the aquarium trade. Handling the tang risks the chances of being badly cut by the <b>caudal spine</b>. These spines, on both sides of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Caudal_peduncle" title="Caudal peduncle">caudal peduncle</a>, are extended from the body when the fish becomes excited. The quick, thrashing sideways motion of the tail can produce deep wounds that result in swelling and discoloration, posing a risk of infection. It is believed that some species of <i><a href="/wiki/Acanthurus" title="Acanthurus">Acanthurus</a></i> have venom glands while others do not. The spines are used only as a method of protection against aggressors.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aquarium_life">Aquarium life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paracanthurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Aquarium life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The regal tang is commonly found in the aquarium trade, despite being one of the more fragile popular fish. In particular, it needs a source of spirulina to regularly graze from, and achieves a respectable size of 30cm (12 inches). In addition, it is prone to parasitic infections and lateral line disease.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Popular_culture">Popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Paracanthurus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A regal blue tang appeared in a 1989 <a href="/wiki/Maybelline" title="Maybelline">Maybelline</a> television commercial featuring <a href="/wiki/Lynda_Carter" title="Lynda Carter">Lynda Carter</a>.</p>
<p>In the 2003 <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Pictures" title="Walt Disney Pictures">Disney</a>/<a href="/wiki/Pixar" title="Pixar">Pixar</a> film, <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Nemo" title="Finding Nemo">Finding Nemo</a></i> and its 2016 sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Dory" title="Finding Dory">Finding Dory</a></i>, the character Dory (voiced by <a href="/wiki/Ellen_DeGeneres" title="Ellen DeGeneres">Ellen DeGeneres</a>) is a regal blue tang.</p>
