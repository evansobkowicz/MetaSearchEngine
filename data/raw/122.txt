<h2>Differences Between Book &amp; The Bible</h2>
<p>Here are only a few of the errors represented as fact in Dan Brown's novel, <em>The Da Vinci Code</em>. </p>
<table border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<tr>
<td class="largecopy" width="171"><h2>Da Vinci Code says... </h2>
<p>Jesus is a great man or prophet in the earliest historical sources but was later proclaimed divine at the Council of Nicaea.<br>
<br>
<br>
</br></br></br></p></td>
<td class="sectionDivider" rowspan="2" width="11"><img height="1" src="/templates/images/spacer.gif" width="10"/></td>
<td class="largecopy" width="175"><h2>The Bible Says... </h2>
<p> Jesus is called “God” (theos) 7 times in the New Testament. He is called “Lord” (kyrios) in the divine sense numerous times. No serious historian  argues that these  texts postdate the Council of Nicaea.</p></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td class="largecopy" width="50%"><h2>Da Vinci Code says... </h2>
<p>The Dead Sea Scrolls along with the Nag Hammadi documents are the earliest   Christian records. </p></td>
<td class="largecopy" width="50%"><h2>History  Says... </h2>
