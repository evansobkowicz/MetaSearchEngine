<section class="review read-more" itemprop="review" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">
<meta content="" itemprop="reviewRating" itemref="microdata-rating" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Rating">
<h4 class="review-author headline">
            Review
                            by <span itemprop="author">Heather Phares</span>
</h4>
<div class="text" itemprop="reviewBody">
<p>
                    Following in the footsteps of the original <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/shrek-mw0000004137">Shrek</a> soundtrack, <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/shrek-2-mw0000333159">Shrek 2</a> emphasizes the crossover potential of the movie and its music. Unfortunately, though, the album doesn't have as many surprises as its predecessor; none of the songs here have the same left-field feel as <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/rufus-wainwright-mn0000854854">Rufus Wainwright</a>'s version of "Hallelujah" (or the <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/john-cale-mn0000224638">John Cale</a> version that actually appeared in the film), although <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/tom-waits-mn0000615119">Tom Waits</a>' mischievous, middle-of-the-night "Little Drop of Poison" and <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/nick-cave-mn0000397880">Nick Cave</a>'s dignified ballad "People Ain't No Good" come close. On the other hand, <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/shrek-2-mw0000333159">Shrek 2</a> doesn't include anything as obnoxious or omnipresent as <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/smash-mouth-mn0000029600">Smash Mouth</a>'s "All Star" or "I'm a Believer," although it does feature of plenty of covers, including <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/frou-frou-mn0000988448">Frou Frou</a>'s odd techo-pop revision of <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/bonnie-tyler-mn0000097173">Bonnie Tyler</a>'s "Holding Out for a Hero," which turns the song from a fun if overblown AOR anthem into a chilly yet slick lament. <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/butterfly-boucher-mn0000629243">Butterfly Boucher</a>'s "Changes" and <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/pete-yorn-mn0000844043">Pete Yorn</a>'s "Ever Fallen in Love," meanwhile, have the dubious distinction of being average renditions of great songs; not even <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/david-bowie-mn0000531986">David Bowie</a>'s cameo on "Changes" can elevate it (although his duet with <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/mick-jagger-mn0000892823">Mick Jagger</a> on "Dancin' in the Streets" remains his worst collaboration). And while <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/eddie-murphy-mn0000142208">Eddie Murphy</a> and <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/antonio-banderas-mn0000587204">Antonio Banderas</a>' version of "Livin' la Vida Loca" isn't as thorough a deconstruction of a <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/ricky-martin-mn0000359026">Ricky Martin</a> song as <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/william-hung-mn0000680119">William Hung</a>'s infamous "She Bangs" (or as funny -- albeit unintentionally so -- as his own single "Party All the Time"), it is a dose of silly fun on a soundtrack that is sorely lacking that quality. <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/jennifer-saunders-mn0000265382">Jennifer Saunders</a>' "Fairy Godmother Song" -- which sounds like her Absolutely Fabulous character making up words to the Cinderella classic "Bibbidi Bobbidi Boo" -- and "Believe"-esque version of "Holding Out for a Hero" also liven up the soundtrack a bit. However, most of <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/shrek-2-mw0000333159">Shrek 2</a> features mopey love songs like <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/dashboard-confessional-mn0000574102">Dashboard Confessional</a>'s "As Lovers Go [Ron Fair Remix]," <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-eels-mn0000174752">the Eels</a>' "I Need Some Sleep," and <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/rich-price-mn0000849357">Rich Price</a>'s "I'm on My Way"; they reflect the film's romantic misadventures between Shrek and Princess Fiona, but these songs don't make the soundtrack an especially fun listen, particularly considering that the movie is, ostensibly, first and foremost aimed at kids. But <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/counting-crows-mn0000128495">Counting Crows</a>' "Accidentally in Love" -- which sounds like a cross between one of their old hits and <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-bodeans-mn0000047789">the BoDeans</a>' "Closer to Free" -- and <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/joseph-arthur-mn0000224925">Joseph Arthur</a>'s "You're So True" are aimed at those kids' older siblings and parents, making <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/shrek-2-mw0000333159">Shrek 2</a> a lukewarm compromise that isn't likely to satisfy either audience.                 </p>
