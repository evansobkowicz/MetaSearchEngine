<p><i><b>The Lion King: Original Motion Picture Soundtrack</b></i> is the original motion picture <a href="/wiki/Film_score" title="Film score">soundtrack</a> for the 1994 <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Animation_Studios" title="Walt Disney Animation Studios">Disney animated film</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Lion_King" title="The Lion King">The Lion King</a></i>. It contains songs from the film written by <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tim_Rice" title="Tim Rice">Tim Rice</a>, and a score composed by <a href="/wiki/Hans_Zimmer" title="Hans Zimmer">Hans Zimmer</a>. Elton John has a dual role of performer for several tracks. Additional performers include <a href="/wiki/Carmen_Twillie_(actress)" title="Carmen Twillie (actress)">Carmen Twillie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jason_Weaver" title="Jason Weaver">Jason Weaver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rowan_Atkinson" title="Rowan Atkinson">Rowan Atkinson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Whoopi_Goldberg" title="Whoopi Goldberg">Whoopi Goldberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Irons" title="Jeremy Irons">Jeremy Irons</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cheech_Marin" title="Cheech Marin">Cheech Marin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Cummings" title="Jim Cummings">Jim Cummings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nathan_Lane" title="Nathan Lane">Nathan Lane</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ernie_Sabella" title="Ernie Sabella">Ernie Sabella</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sally_Dworsky" title="Sally Dworsky">Sally Dworsky</a>. The album was released on May 30, 1994 on CD and <a href="/wiki/Compact_Cassette" title="Compact Cassette">audio cassette</a>. The soundtrack was recorded in three different countries: the U.S., the U.K. and South Africa. It is the best-selling soundtrack album to an animated film in the United States with over 7 million copies sold, with 4,934,000 copies sold in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sales_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-sales-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>An expanded version of <i>The Lion King</i> soundtrack, featuring 30 minutes of previously-unreleased material, was released as part of the <i><a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Records:_The_Legacy_Collection" title="Walt Disney Records: The Legacy Collection">Walt Disney Records: The Legacy Collection</a></i> series on June 24, 2014. In 2014, Hot Topic released a vinyl picture disc of the soundtrack. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAToday_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Legacy_Collection_release"><i>The Legacy Collection</i> release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Lion_King_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The Legacy Collection release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="hatnote relarticle mainarticle">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Records:_The_Legacy_Collection#The_Lion_King" title="Walt Disney Records: The Legacy Collection">Walt Disney Records: The Legacy Collection § The Lion King</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Records" title="Walt Disney Records">Walt Disney Records</a> released an expanded edition of <i>The Lion King</i> soundtrack containing an additional thirty minutes of previously-unheard material from the film, as well as a collection of demo versions of cues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USAToday_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-USAToday-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> This version of the album contains a new <a href="/wiki/Audio_mastering" title="Audio mastering">remastered mix</a> by Alan Meyerson.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Chart_and_commercial_success">Chart and commercial success</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Lion_King_(soundtrack)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chart and commercial success">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The soundtrack shipped over 10 million copies in the U.S. and eventually went 10x platinum in 1995, thus becoming the biggest-selling soundtrack ever from an animated film. Its certification was promoted to <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">Diamond</a> when the award was instituted in 1999. According to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nielsen_Soundscan" title="Nielsen Soundscan">Nielsen Soundscan</a> the soundtrack, as of April 2014, has sold a total of 7.873 million copies in the US.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sales_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-sales-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> It is also the best-selling vinyl album in the Nielsen SoundScan era (starting 1991), with 1,043,000 copies sold as of June 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2010, <a href="/wiki/Rhapsody_(online_music_service)" title="Rhapsody (online music service)">Rhapsody</a> called it one of the all-time great Disney &amp; <a href="/wiki/Pixar" title="Pixar">Pixar</a> Soundtracks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
