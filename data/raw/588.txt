<p><b>Guy Stuart Ritchie</b> (born 10 September 1968) is an English filmmaker known for his crime films. He left high school and got entry-level jobs in the film industry in the mid-1990s. He eventually graduated to directing commercials. He made his first film in 1995, a 20-minute short which impressed investors who backed his first feature film, the crime comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Lock,_Stock_and_Two_Smoking_Barrels" title="Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels">Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels</a></i> (1998). He then directed another crime comedy, <i><a href="/wiki/Snatch_(film)" title="Snatch (film)">Snatch</a></i> (2000). His first two films were a commercial and critical success. His next two films, the romantic comedy <i><a href="/wiki/Swept_Away_(2002_film)" title="Swept Away (2002 film)">Swept Away</a></i> (2002) and the crime drama <i><a href="/wiki/Revolver_(2005_film)" title="Revolver (2005 film)">Revolver</a></i> (2005) were not a commercial or critical success. The next crime drama, <i><a href="/wiki/RocknRolla" title="RocknRolla">RocknRolla</a></i> (2008), received mixed reviews and a modest box office return. In 2009, he directed his first films in the action mystery genre, with <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)" title="Sherlock Holmes (2009 film)">Sherlock Holmes</a></i> (2009) and its sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes:_A_Game_of_Shadows" title="Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows">Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows</a></i> (2011). Both films were major box office successes, and the two films received positive, and moderately positive reviews, respectively.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guy_Ritchie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ritchie was born in <a href="/wiki/Hatfield,_Hertfordshire" title="Hatfield, Hertfordshire">Hatfield, Hertfordshire</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-b-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> the second of two children born to Amber (née Parkinson) and Captain John Vivian Ritchie (b. 1928), former <a href="/wiki/Seaforth_Highlanders" title="Seaforth Highlanders">Seaforth Highlanders</a> serviceman and advertising executive. John's father was Major Stewart Ritchie, who died in France, in 1940, during World War Two.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> John's mother was Doris Margaretta McLaughlin (b. 1896), daughter of Vivian Guy McLaughlin (b. 1865) and Edith Martineau (b. 1866). The McLaughlins have a pedigree going back to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Edward_I_of_England" title="King Edward I of England">King Edward I of England</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Ritchie's mother, Amber, later married a baronet.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> His father's second marriage was to <a href="/wiki/Shireen_Ritchie,_Baroness_Ritchie_of_Brompton" title="Shireen Ritchie, Baroness Ritchie of Brompton">Shireen Ritchie, Baroness Ritchie of Brompton</a>, a former model and later Conservative politician and life peer.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Ritchie, who is <a href="/wiki/Dyslexia" title="Dyslexia">dyslexic</a>, was expelled from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stanbridge_Earls" title="Stanbridge Earls">Stanbridge Earls School</a> at the age of 15.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-b-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> He has stated that drug use was the reason for the expulsion; his father has said that it was because his son was caught "cutting class and entertaining a girl in his room."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated304_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated304-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> He also attended <a href="/wiki/Sibford_School" title="Sibford School">Sibford School</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Windlesham_House_School" title="Windlesham House School">Windlesham House School</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>In addition to his elder sister, Tabitha, a dance instructor, Ritchie has a half-brother, Kevin Bayton, who was born to Amber Parkinson when she was a teenager and given up for adoption.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> From 1973 until 1980, when they divorced,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated304_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated304-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Ritchie's mother was married to Sir Michael Leighton, 11th baronet. As a divorcée, she is correctly styled as Amber, Lady Leighton.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Directing_career">Directing career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guy_Ritchie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Directing career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ritchie directed a 20-minute short film in 1995 entitled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hard_Case" title="The Hard Case">The Hard Case</a></i>. In 1998, Ritchie and his dad contacted their friend <a href="/wiki/Peter_Morton" title="Peter Morton">Peter Morton</a>, of the <a href="/wiki/Hard_Rock_Cafe" title="Hard Rock Cafe">Hard Rock Cafe</a> chain, wondering if he had any potential investors for a debut film, Morton knew his nephew <a href="/wiki/Matthew_Vaughn" title="Matthew Vaughn">Matthew Vaughn</a> had been studying film production in Los Angeles. Peter informed Vaughn of Ritchie's new film idea, and Vaughn knew he could help out with his own found production ideas he had gained from his travels to America. Matthew, John, Guy and Peter all contacted their mutual acquaintance, <a href="/wiki/Trudie_Styler" title="Trudie Styler">Trudie Styler</a>, who they knew had enough money to invest in the production of Ritchie's second film. Styler informed them that she had previously seen The Hard Case, and decided that co-funding the project would be a worthwhile opportunity. After about eight months, the production of the film, <i><a href="/wiki/Lock,_Stock_and_Two_Smoking_Barrels" title="Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels">Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels</a></i> was completed, and it was released in 1998 to a national audience, then later went global after its positive reviews. Richie was introduced to <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a> when the soundtrack for the film was issued on her <a href="/wiki/Maverick_Records" title="Maverick Records">Maverick Records</a> label. The film, whose main actor was <a href="/wiki/Nick_Moran" title="Nick Moran">Nick Moran</a>, also introduced actors <a href="/wiki/Jason_Statham" title="Jason Statham">Jason Statham</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/The_Transporter" title="The Transporter">The Transporter</a></i>), <a href="/wiki/Jason_Flemyng" title="Jason Flemyng">Jason Flemyng</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dexter_Fletcher" title="Dexter Fletcher">Dexter Fletcher</a> to worldwide audiences, as well as introducing former footballer <a href="/wiki/Vinnie_Jones" title="Vinnie Jones">Vinnie Jones</a> to a new career as a film star. In 2000 Ritchie won an <a href="/wiki/Edgar_Award" title="Edgar Award">Edgar Award</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Mystery_Writers_of_America" title="Mystery Writers of America">Mystery Writers of America</a> for Best Motion Picture Screenplay. Ritchie created and produced a spin-off television series called <i><a href="/wiki/Lock,_Stock..." title="Lock, Stock...">Lock, Stock...</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>His second feature film was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snatch_(movie)" title="Snatch (movie)"><i>Snatch</i></a>, released in the year 2000. Originally known as <i>Diamonds</i>, it was another caper comedy, this time backed by a major studio. The cast featured such Hollywood big names as <a href="/wiki/Brad_Pitt" title="Brad Pitt">Brad Pitt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benicio_del_Toro" title="Benicio del Toro">Benicio del Toro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Farina" title="Dennis Farina">Dennis Farina</a>, along with the returning Vinnie Jones and Statham. Similar to <i>Lock, Stock and Two Smoking Barrels</i> in featuring a complex and inventive storyline in which the characters weave in and out of each other's lives, the film also plays with time, depicting events from various perspectives. It currently has a rating of 73% on Rotten Tomatoes.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Following his marriage to Madonna, Ritchie began focusing his filmmaking on his wife, directing her in both a music video (for the song "<a href="/wiki/What_It_Feels_Like_for_a_Girl" title="What It Feels Like for a Girl">What It Feels Like for a Girl</a>", a controversial video that showed Madonna engaging in violent behaviour, ostensibly directed at men, including T-boning a car with three men in it, tasering and robbing a man at an ATM, scratching a police car and shooting two officers with a water gun, driving her car through a group of men playing street hockey and incinerating a man by throwing a lighter into a pool of gasoline) and a short film, <i>Star</i>, for the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BMW_films" title="BMW films">BMW films</a> series. Ritchie's next film, also featuring Madonna, was a remake of the 1974 <a href="/wiki/Lina_Wertm%C3%BCller" title="Lina Wertmüller">Lina Wertmüller</a> hit <i><a href="/wiki/Swept_Away_(1974_film)" title="Swept Away (1974 film)">Swept Away</a></i> (also entitled <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swept_Away_(2002)" title="Swept Away (2002)">Swept Away</a></i>). Ritchie cast Madonna as a rich, rude socialite who, after a shipwreck, is trapped on a deserted island with a slovenly Communist sailor who humiliates her. Ritchie renamed the woman Amber Leighton after his mother. This film was both a critical and commercial disappointment.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Ritchie's next project was a Vegas-themed heist film entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Revolver_(2005_film)" title="Revolver (2005 film)">Revolver</a></i>, which was critically panned in the US and UK.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> Ritchie was involved with a hidden camera show called <i>Swag</i>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> for <a href="/wiki/Channel_5_(UK)" title="Channel 5 (UK)">Channel Five</a> in the UK, which turned the table on criminals and opportunists by using stunts to trap them in the act. Ritchie has also written and directed <i><a href="/wiki/RocknRolla" title="RocknRolla">RocknRolla</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Gerard_Butler" title="Gerard Butler">Gerard Butler</a>. It scores 60% on Rotten Tomatoes and was generally received well.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Ritchie intends to develop the film into a trilogy, with the next entry being "The Real RocknRolla," as is stated at the end of "RocknRolla." Ritchie will also direct a film based on a comic book series he created with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Virgin_Comics" title="Virgin Comics">Virgin Comics</a> entitled <i><a href="/wiki/Guy_Ritchie%27s_Gamekeeper" title="Guy Ritchie's Gamekeeper">Guy Ritchie's Gamekeeper</a></i>. The film rights were acquired by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warner_Brothers" title="Warner Brothers">Warner Brothers</a> in July 2007. The film's being produced by <a href="/wiki/Silver_Pictures" title="Silver Pictures">Silver Pictures</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2008, Ritchie directed a commercial for <a href="/wiki/Nike,_Inc." title="Nike, Inc.">Nike</a> called "Take It To The Next Level", about a young Dutch footballer who signs for <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a>, showing the progression of his career from his viewpoint, until he makes his debut for <a href="/wiki/Netherlands_national_football_team" title="Netherlands national football team">the Netherlands</a>. The commercial features cameo appearances from some football players with music by <a href="/wiki/Eagles_of_Death_Metal" title="Eagles of Death Metal">Eagles of Death Metal</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> Ritchie's movie <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes_(2009_film)" title="Sherlock Holmes (2009 film)">Sherlock Holmes</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Robert_Downey,_Jr." title="Robert Downey, Jr.">Robert Downey, Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jude_Law" title="Jude Law">Jude Law</a>, received its theatrical release on 25 December 2009. The film was given generally positive reviews<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> and grossed more than $520 million worldwide,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> becoming Ritchie's most successful film financially.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup> The sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes:_A_Game_of_Shadows" title="Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows">Sherlock Holmes: A Game of Shadows</a></i>, was released on 16 December 2011.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> In June 2012 it was announced that Ritchie would direct an adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Treasure_Island" title="Treasure Island">Treasure Island</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Louis_Stevenson" title="Robert Louis Stevenson">Robert Louis Stevenson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> On 29 October 2012, Ritchie produced a game trailer for <i><a href="/wiki/Call_of_Duty:_Black_Ops_II" title="Call of Duty: Black Ops II">Call of Duty: Black Ops II</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On 18 March 2013, <i><a href="/wiki/Deadline.com" title="Deadline.com">Deadline.com</a></i> reported that <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Man_From_U.N.C.L.E." title="The Man From U.N.C.L.E.">The Man From U.N.C.L.E.</a></i> was in production with Ritchie directing.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tom_25-0"><a href="#cite_note-tom-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> On 31 July 2013, it was announced that Ritchie's adaptation would start filming in September 2013 in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> Warner Bros. set the film for a 16 January 2015 release.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-January16th-2015_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-January16th-2015-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In January 2014, Warner Bros set Ritchie to direct a new multi-film version of the <i>King Arthur</i> legend, which is scheduled for an 22 July 2016 release with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lionel_Wigram_(cinema)" title="Lionel Wigram (cinema)">Lionel Wigram</a> as producer and <a href="/wiki/Joby_Harold" title="Joby Harold">Joby Harold</a> as screenwriter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Idris_Elba" title="Idris Elba">Idris Elba</a> is in talks to play a Merlin-esque figure who trains and mentors Arthur.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Hunnam" title="Charlie Hunnam">Charlie Hunnam</a> will play King Arthur by Ritchie's choice.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guy_Ritchie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>On 18 May 2000, Ritchie was arrested by the police after he assaulted a 20-year-old man outside the <a href="/wiki/Kensington" title="Kensington">Kensington</a> home he shared with Madonna, causing actual bodily harm.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> Ritchie started training in <a href="/wiki/Shotokan" title="Shotokan">Shotokan karate</a> at the age of seven at the <a href="/wiki/Budokwai" title="Budokwai">Budokwai</a> in London, where he later achieved a black belt in <a href="/wiki/Judo" title="Judo">judo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup> He also has a brown belt in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brazilian_Jiu-Jitsu" title="Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu">Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In February 2011 a £6m house he owns in London's <a href="/wiki/Fitzrovia" title="Fitzrovia">Fitzrovia</a> was occupied briefly by members of The Really Free School, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Squatter" title="Squatter">squatter</a> organization.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Guy_Ritchie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 22 December 2000, Guy married American singer <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a> at <a href="/wiki/Skibo_Castle" title="Skibo Castle">Skibo Castle</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup> They have a son, Rocco John Ritchie (born 11 August 2000 in Los Angeles) and adopted a Malawian baby boy in 2006, David (born 24 September 2005). On 15 October 2008, British media reported that a split was "imminent" between Ritchie and Madonna.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span>[</span>39<span>]</span></a></sup> The split was confirmed by their spokesperson<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span>[</span>40<span>]</span></a></sup> and Ritchie and Madonna went public with the split because they "can’t bear to live with the pretense any longer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span>[</span>41<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>On 15 December 2008, it was announced by Madonna's spokeswoman that the singer had agreed to a divorce settlement with Ritchie, the terms of which grant him between £50 million and £60 million, a figure that includes the value of the couple's <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Punch_Bowl_(Mayfair)" title="The Punch Bowl (Mayfair)">London pub</a> and residence and <a href="/wiki/Ashcombe_House,_Wiltshire" title="Ashcombe House, Wiltshire">Wiltshire estate</a> in England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span>[</span>42<span>]</span></a></sup> Madonna and Guy Ritchie's marriage was dissolved by District Judge Reid by <i><a href="/wiki/Decree_nisi" title="Decree nisi">decree nisi</a></i> at the clinical Principal Registry of the Family Division in High Holborn, London. Madonna and Ritchie entered into a compromise agreement for Rocco and David, then aged eight and three respectively, and divided the children's time between Ritchie's London home and Madonna's in New York, where the two will be joined by her daughter, Lourdes, from a previous relationship.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43"><span>[</span>43<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44"><span>[</span>44<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Richie is engaged to model <a href="/wiki/Jacqui_Ainsley" title="Jacqui Ainsley">Jacqui Ainsley</a> with whom he has three children: son Rafael (born 5 September 2011), daughter Rivka (born 29 November 2012) and a third child born in 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45"><span>[</span>45<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46"><span>[</span>46<span>]</span></a></sup> <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47"><a href="#cite_note-47"><span>[</span>47<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
