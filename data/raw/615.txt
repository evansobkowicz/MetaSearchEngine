<p>This article contains the albums discography of the American pop artist <b><a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a></b>. It consists of eleven <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Studio_album" title="Studio album">studio albums</a> and a partial list of <a href="/wiki/Live_album" title="Live album">live albums</a>, <a href="/wiki/Compilation_album" title="Compilation album">compilations</a>, <a href="/wiki/Extended_play" title="Extended play">extended plays</a>, <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack" title="Soundtrack">soundtracks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Remix_album" title="Remix album">remix albums</a>. Also listed are peak chart positions from 10 selected countries (markets), with relative sales and certifications. Sales in parentheses are of albums not certified. The countries selected are United States, Australia, Canada, France,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>a<span>]</span></a></sup> Germany, the Netherlands,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>b<span>]</span></a></sup> New Zealand, Spain, Switzerland and the United Kingdom. Some of the countries changed their levels of certifications at various times.</p>
<p>Jackson made his debut at the age of six with <a href="/wiki/The_Jackson_5" title="The Jackson 5">The Jackson 5</a> (later known as The Jacksons), who were prominent performers during the 1970s. Jackson's early solo career that same decade included albums such as <i><a href="/wiki/Got_to_Be_There" title="Got to Be There">Got to Be There</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Ben_(album)" title="Ben (album)">Ben</a></i>. His two follow-ups, <i><a href="/wiki/Music_%26_Me" title="Music &amp; Me">Music &amp; Me</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Forever,_Michael" title="Forever, Michael">Forever, Michael</a></i>, failed to sell well. In 1979, Jackson released <i><a href="/wiki/Off_the_Wall_(album)" title="Off the Wall (album)">Off the Wall</a></i>, which became his first best-selling album. Three years later, Jackson issued his best-selling album to date, <i><a href="/wiki/Thriller_(Michael_Jackson_album)" title="Thriller (Michael Jackson album)">Thriller</a></i>, which topped several international charts and became the <a href="/wiki/List_of_best-selling_albums" title="List of best-selling albums">best-selling album of all time</a>. Jackson's follow-ups, <i><a href="/wiki/Bad_(album)" title="Bad (album)">Bad</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dangerous_(Michael_Jackson_album)" title="Dangerous (Michael Jackson album)">Dangerous</a></i>, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HIStory:_Past,_Present_and_Future,_Book_1" title="HIStory: Past, Present and Future, Book 1">HIStory: Past, Present and Future, Book 1</a></i>, also became international successes. Jackson's tenth and final studio album, <i><a href="/wiki/Invincible_(Michael_Jackson_album)" title="Invincible (Michael Jackson album)">Invincible</a></i>, was released in 2001 and topped international charts though its sales paled in comparison to his previous albums, with total confirmed sales around thirteen million copies worldwide.</p>
<p>Following <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Michael_Jackson" title="Death of Michael Jackson">Jackson's death</a> in 2009, sales of his previous work skyrocketed, with his compilation album, <i><a href="/wiki/Number_Ones_(Michael_Jackson_album)" title="Number Ones (Michael Jackson album)">Number Ones</a></i>, becoming one of the best-selling albums internationally in 2009.</p>

<li id="cite_note-1"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-1">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">In France, from 1989, greatest hits/compilations of the artists charted in a compilation chart. From 2002 to 2005, albums with price under €10 were charted in a mid-price chart. In 2005, the rules changed again, with the mid-price list that became a back-catalog chart (generally albums that are at least 2 years old, sometimes also less). In this page, <i>Top Albums</i> (from 2002 full price/new albums list) and <i>Top Compilations</i> were used.</span></li>
