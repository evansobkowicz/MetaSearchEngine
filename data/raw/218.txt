<h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en"><span dir="auto">Watership Down, Hampshire</span></h1>

<p><b>Watership Down</b> is a hill, or <a href="/wiki/Downland" title="Downland">down</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Ecchinswell" title="Ecchinswell">Ecchinswell</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Civil_parishes_in_England" title="Civil parishes in England">civil parish</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ecchinswell,_Sydmonton_and_Bishops_Green" title="Ecchinswell, Sydmonton and Bishops Green">Ecchinswell, Sydmonton and Bishops Green</a> in the <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> county of <a href="/wiki/Hampshire" title="Hampshire">Hampshire</a>. It rises fairly steeply on its northern flank (the <a href="/wiki/Escarpment" title="Escarpment">scarp</a> side), but to the south the slope is much gentler (the <a href="/wiki/Escarpment" title="Escarpment">dip</a> side). <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_national_grid_reference_system" title="British national grid reference system">grid reference</a> <span style="white-space: nowrap"><a class="external text" href="http://tools.wmflabs.org/os/coor_g/?pagename=Watership_Down,_Hampshire&amp;params=SU497570_region%3AGB_scale%3A25000">SU497570</a></span>.</p>
<p>The Down is best known as the setting for <a href="/wiki/Richard_Adams" title="Richard Adams">Richard Adams</a>' 1972 novel about <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rabbits" title="Rabbits">rabbits</a>, also called <i><a href="/wiki/Watership_Down" title="Watership Down">Watership Down</a></i>. The area is popular with cyclists and walkers. A <a href="/wiki/Bridle_path" title="Bridle path">bridleway</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Wayfarer%27s_Walk" title="Wayfarer's Walk">Wayfarer's Walk</a> cross county footpath, runs along the ridge of the Down which lies at the south-eastern edge of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Wessex_Downs_AONB" title="North Wessex Downs AONB">North Wessex Downs Area of Natural Beauty</a>. Other nearby features include <a href="/wiki/Ladle_Hill" title="Ladle Hill">Ladle Hill</a>, on Great Litchfield Down, immediately to the west. Part of the hill is a 10.37 hectares (25.6 acres) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biological_SSSI" title="Biological SSSI">biological SSSI</a>, first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSSI_notification" title="SSSI notification">notified</a> in 1978. The hill has a partially completed <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age" title="Iron Age">Iron Age</a> <a href="/wiki/Hill_fort" title="Hill fort">hill fort</a> on its summit, and the surrounding area is rich in Iron Age tumuli, enclosures, lynchets and field systems. Further to the west lies <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beacon_Hill,_Burghclere" title="Beacon Hill, Burghclere">Beacon Hill</a>.</p>
<p>Watership Down is accessible via the large village of <a href="/wiki/Kingsclere" title="Kingsclere">Kingsclere</a>.</p>
