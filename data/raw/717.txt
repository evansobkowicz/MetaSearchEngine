<p><b>Thomas Daniel</b> "<b>Tommy</b>" <b>Mottola</b> (born July 14, 1949) is an American music executive. He is the co-owner of <a href="/wiki/Casablanca_Records" title="Casablanca Records">Casablanca Records</a> in a joint venture with the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Music_Group" title="Universal Music Group">Universal Music Group</a>. He headed <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony Music Entertainment</a>, parent of the <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">Columbia</a> label, for nearly 15 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Tommy Mottola entered in the music scene in the mid 60s as a recording artist for <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Records" title="Columbia Records">CBS Records</a> under the name "T.D. Valentine". After his attempt to become a recording star himself failed, Mottola started working for publishing powerhouse Chappell Publishing and started his own management company, Champion Entertainment Organization. His role at Chappell put him in touch with many artists, and soon he signed his first successful management clients, <a href="/wiki/Hall_%26_Oates" title="Hall &amp; Oates">Hall &amp; Oates</a>.</p>
<p>Mottola helped Hall and Oates land a record deal and several high-profile endorsements. He was also recognized for successfully using new mediums for promotion, like music videos and corporate sponsorship for music tours.</p>
<p>Apart from Hall and Oates, Mottola is known as a mentor and former talent manager. His most famous protégés were <a href="/wiki/Carly_Simon" title="Carly Simon">Carly Simon</a>, <a href="/wiki/John_Mellencamp" title="John Mellencamp">John Mellencamp</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mtv_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-mtv-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Diana_Ross" title="Diana Ross">Diana Ross</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Taylor_Dayne" title="Taylor Dayne">Taylor Dayne</a> in the 1980s, <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> (whom he married) in the 1990s, <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Estefan" title="Gloria Estefan">Gloria Estefan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anastacia" title="Anastacia">Anastacia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a> in the early 2000s. He is married to Mexican telenovela actress and singer <a href="/wiki/Thal%C3%ADa" title="Thalía">Thalía</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sony.2FColumbia">Sony/Columbia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tommy_Mottola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Sony/Columbia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1988, Mottola was hired by Sony Music (then known as CBS Records) by <a href="/wiki/Walter_Yetnikoff" title="Walter Yetnikoff">Walter Yetnikoff</a> (who had been at the company from 1975 to 1990) to run its U.S. operations (it had recently been acquired by the Japan-based <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a> corporation). In 1990, he replaced Yetnikoff as Chairman CEO of the newly named Sony Music. During his tenure, he transformed Sony into one of the most successful global music companies, expanding its businesses into over 60 countries, while creating one of the strongest management teams in the industry. He revitalized Sony Music's publishing division by making such acquisitions as the Beatles catalogue and enabled Sony to become the first major music company to make available commercial digital downloads. Additionally, Mottola was the architect for some of Sony's biggest global brands such as Celine Dion and Mariah Carey. During his 15 year tenure, Mottola built Sony up from a company with revenues of $800 million a year to one with over $6 billion of annual revenue by the year 2000.</p>
<p>He is widely well known for signing, developing, and nurturing the careers of <a href="/wiki/Celine_Dion" title="Celine Dion">Celine Dion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gloria_Estefan" title="Gloria Estefan">Gloria Estefan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Destiny%27s_Child" title="Destiny's Child">Destiny's Child</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shakira" title="Shakira">Shakira</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Dixie_Chicks" title="Dixie Chicks">Dixie Chicks</a>, etc., and for releasing digitally remastered <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Compact_discs" title="Compact discs">compact discs</a> of the older recordings made by <a href="/wiki/Barbra_Streisand" title="Barbra Streisand">Barbra Streisand</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen" title="Bruce Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Billy_Joel" title="Billy Joel">Billy Joel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andy_Williams" title="Andy Williams">Andy Williams</a>, among others. He also is credited with creating the Latin Explosion in popular music by championing such Sony artists as <a href="/wiki/Jennifer_Lopez" title="Jennifer Lopez">Jennifer Lopez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marc_Anthony" title="Marc Anthony">Marc Anthony</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ricky_Martin" title="Ricky Martin">Ricky Martin</a>. He also worked with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a> from the time he began recording his "Dangerous" (1991) album. Jackson would later state that his relationship with Mottola dissolved based on corruption in Mottola's working practices, and once charged him with being a "racist who exploited black talent". <p>In June 2010, he decided to sell his ranch in Colorado, for $27.5 million, as he was not spending time in it anymore and wanted to be closer to his family.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="After_Sony">After Sony</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tommy_Mottola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: After Sony">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tommy Mottola was the head of <a href="/wiki/Sony_Music_Entertainment" title="Sony Music Entertainment">Sony Music Entertainment</a> until January 2003. Since leaving his post as chief of Sony Music, Mottola has been building a new entertainment company, complete with recorded music, television production, theater, and fashion, alongside a branding and management company that had recently launched. He was instrumental in re-launching the various careers of such artists as Grammy winner and Latin pop star <a href="/wiki/Marc_Anthony" title="Marc Anthony">Marc Anthony</a>, and teen sensation <a href="/wiki/Lindsay_Lohan" title="Lindsay Lohan">Lindsay Lohan</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Later signings included <a href="/wiki/Cassie_Ventura" title="Cassie Ventura">Cassie Ventura</a> in 2006, and <a href="/wiki/Mika_(singer)" title="Mika (singer)">Mika</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> who in 2007 had a #1 single in the UK.</p>
<p>A collaboration with former <a href="/wiki/Miramax" title="Miramax">Miramax</a> chief <a href="/wiki/Harvey_Weinstein" title="Harvey Weinstein">Harvey Weinstein</a> has them teamed on two current projects for live theater, namely producers of Pink Floyd's <i>The Wall</i>. Mottola's TV production arm is currently producing and developing many shows for cable and network television.</p>
<p>Mottola bought out the rights to <a href="/wiki/Casablanca_Records" title="Casablanca Records">Casablanca Records</a> (a then-diminished imprint of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polygram" title="Polygram">Polygram</a>), and resurrected it, signing actress <a href="/wiki/Lindsay_Lohan" title="Lindsay Lohan">Lindsay Lohan</a>, and Mika.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tommy_Mottola&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Mottola has been married three times. He was married to his first wife Lisa Clark from 1971 to 1990; they had a son and a daughter. On June 5, 1993 Mottola married his second wife <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">Mariah Carey</a> in an infamous <a href="/wiki/Diana,_Princess_of_Wales" title="Diana, Princess of Wales">Princess Diana</a>-inspired wedding. The couple divorced on March 5, 1998. He went on to date other stars before dating his third wife, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexican</a> <a href="/wiki/Telenovela" title="Telenovela">telenovela</a> star and recording artist <a href="/wiki/Thal%C3%ADa" title="Thalía">Thalía</a>. They were married on December 2, 2000 at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Patrick%27s_Cathedral,_New_York" title="St. Patrick's Cathedral, New York">St. Patrick's Cathedral</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>. They have two children: a daughter and a son.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-people-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
