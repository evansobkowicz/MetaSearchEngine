<div class="article-content js-sticky-ad" itemprop="articleBody" itemscope="">
<p><a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/artists/ac-dc">AC/DC</a> set the <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/grammy-awards-2015">Grammy Awards</a> on their own "Highway to Hell" with their explosive opening performance, starting as LL Cool J introduced them. The Aussie hard rockers blasted the audience with the title track of their latest album, last year's <em><a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/ac-dc-rock-or-bust-20141202">Rock or Bust</a></em>. In standard form for the group, frontman Brian Johnson gyrated in his signature Andy Capp hat, as lead guitarist Angus Young headbanged and duck-walked in his schoolboy uniform.</p>
<aside class="related-article">
<h4>Sidebar</h4>
<a data-track="" data-track-action="Interstitial" data-track-label="URL" href="/music/lists/grammy-awards-2015s-21-best-and-worst-moments-20150209">
<div class="image-holder"> <img alt="Prince, Mary J. Blige and Sam Smith" class="" src="http://assets.rollingstone.com/assets/2015/list/grammy-awards-2015s-21-best-and-worst-moments-20150209/185256/small_rect/1423489798/300x169-grammy.jpg"/>
</div>
        Grammy Awards 2015's 21 Best and Worst Moments »
    </a>
</aside>
<p>As soon as they finished with that tune, Young blared out the opening chords to "Highway to Hell" and Johnson grinned from ear to ear. The whole audience donned light-up devil horns, including Katy Perry who gave the devil-horns with her fingers, as some pyro lit things up behind them. Even Tony Bennett –who, along with his date for the night, Lady Gaga, was not be-horned – seemed to be enjoying himself. When LL Cool J came back to host, he called the performance "devilishly good."</p>
<p>AC/DC have won only one Grammy Award in their career, for the <em><a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/black-ice-20081030">Black Ice</a></em> song "War Machine" in 2010. In November, the Australian hard rockers released <em>Rock or Bust</em>, their first album without rhythm guitarist Malcolm Young, who backed out of the band to <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/malcolm-youngs-family-confirms-ac-dc-guitarists-dementia-20140930">battle dementia</a>. His nephew, Stevie Young, played on the album and is performing live with the group.</p>
<p>The release was marred by the <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/ac-dcs-phil-rudd-arrested-for-attempting-to-procure-a-murder-20141105">arrest</a> of drummer Phil Rudd who was <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/murder-for-hire-charge-dropped-against-ac-dcs-phil-rudd-20141106">charged with drug possession and threatening to kill</a> in New Zealand last November. His role in the band has been a point of speculation since then. "I'm going back to work with AC/DC, and I don't care who likes it and who doesn't," Rudd said in an interview with <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/acdc-phil-rudd-i-want-my-job-back-20141222">New Zealand's One News</a> a month after the arrest.</p>
<p>The medley marked the group's first new performance with past AC/DC drummer Chris Slade. Prior to their Grammys performance, rumors sparked online that Slade, who played on the band's 1990 LP <em>The Razors Edge</em> and departed in 1994, would be behind the kit at the awards show.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/drummer-chris-slade-rumored-to-have-rejoined-acdc/" target="_blank">Blabbermouth</a> reported that the drummer, who had recently been playing in an AC/DC tribute group, had postponed a meet-and-greet (oddly scheduled at an optometrist's clinic) because of "big news" regarding his career. The drummer confirmed the news the day before the Grammys, according to <a href="http://www.blabbermouth.net/news/chris-slade-confirms-return-to-acdc-this-is-an-amazing-opportunity-for-me/" target="_blank">Blabbermouth</a>.</p>
<p>Slade also confirmed that he will join the band on their upcoming tour, which will take place after a headlining gig at <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/coachella-2015-lineup-ac-dc-jack-white-drake-to-headline-20150106">Coachella</a> in April. He'll replace Rudd, whose next <a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/ac-dc-drummer-phil-rudds-trial-delayed-after-lawyer-quits-20150205">court appearance </a>is scheduled for April.</p>
<p>In happier news, 2015 marks the 35th anniversary of <em><a href="http://www.rollingstone.com/music/albumreviews/back-in-black-20050210">Back in Black</a></em>, the band's first with lead singer Brian Johnson.</p>
