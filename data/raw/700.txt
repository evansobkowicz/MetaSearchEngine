<h1>Tom Petty, U2, Pink Floyd, Metallica, Neil Young Fall on Grammy Night</h1>
<div class="post_meta">
<span class="the_author"><span>by </span><a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/author/nickderiso/" title="Nick DeRiso">Nick DeRiso</a></span>
<span class="the_date">February 8, 2015 11:41 PM</span> </div> <div class="social-group-top large"><div class="social_icons size_large">
<a class="facebook share_link" href="http://www.facebook.com/sharer.php?u=http%3A%2F%2Fultimateclassicrock.com%2Fclassic-rock-grammy-losers%2F%3Ftrackback%3Dfbshare_top&amp;t=Tom+Petty%2C+U2%2C+Pink+Floyd%2C+Metallica%2C+Neil+Young+Fall+on+Grammy+Night" target="_blank" title="Share on Facebook">

<span class="social-submenu"></span></div></div> <div class="the_content clearfix">
<figure class="aligncenter" id="attachment_225469"><img alt="" class="size-large wp-image-225469" height="420" src="http://wac.450F.edgecastcdn.net/80450F/ultimateclassicrock.com/files/2015/02/Tom-Petty-U2-Pink-Floyd-Metallica--630x420.jpeg" title="Tom Petty U2 Pink Floyd Metallica" width="630"/><figcaption style="width:630px;">Jerod Harris / Matthias Nareyek / MJ Kim / Ian Gavan, Getty Images</figcaption></figure>
<p>The Grammy Awards are hailed as “music’s biggest night,” but it proved to be a big disappointment for classic-rock artists, instead. <a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tags/tom-petty/">Tom Petty</a>, <a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tags/u2/">U2</a> and the <a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tags/black-keys/">Black Keys</a> were all nominated for Best Rock Album, but came up short to <a href="http://diffuser.fm/tags/beck/" target="_blank">Beck</a>. In fact, the Black Keys suffered a string of losses throughout the night.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, <a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tags/metallica/">Metallica</a>‘s ‘Through the Never’ suffered a similar fate in the Best Music Film category, finishing second to (the admittedly awesome) ’20 Feet From Stardom.’ <a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tags/pink-floyd/">Pink Floyd</a>‘s ‘The Divison Bell: 20th Anniversary Deluxe Box Set’ had also been nominated for Best Surround Sound Album, though <a href="http://popcrush.com/tags/beyonce/" target="_blank">Beyonce</a>‘s self-titled album took the prize. <a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tags/neil-young/">Neil Young</a>‘s ‘A Letter Home’ vinyl box set was nominated for Best Boxed Set or Special Limited Edition Package, as was the <a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tags/the-grateful-dead/">Grateful Dead</a>‘s ‘Spring 1990 (The Other One),’ but the award went to ‘The Rise and Fall of Paramount Records, Volume One.’</p>
<p><a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tags/gregg-allman/">Gregg Allman</a> lost the Best American Roots Performance award to <a href="http://tasteofcountry.com/tags/rosanne-cash/" target="_blank">Rosanne Cash</a>. Paul Epworth, who had been nominated in part for his work with <a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tags/paul-mccartney/">Paul McCartney</a>, was bested by Max Martin for Producer of the Year, Non-Classical honors. <a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tags/jack-white/">Jack White</a> and the Black Keys were nominated for Best Rock Song, but that statue went to <a href="http://popcrush.com/tags/paramore/" target="_blank">Paramore</a> for ‘Ain’t It Fun.’ White also fell to St. Vincent in the Best Alternative Album category.</p>
<p>It wasn’t all bad. <a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tags/pearl-jam/">Pearl Jam</a> claimed a Grammy for Best Recording Package, in honor of ‘Lightning Bolt.’ <a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tags/johnny-winter/">Johnny Winter</a> earned a posthumous Best Blues Album nod for ‘Step Back.’ White’s ‘Lazaretto’ actually won for Best Rock Performance, but he bested a group that included (again!) the Black Keys. Poor guys.</p>
<p>As we’ve previously discussed/groused about, <a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tags/tenacious-d/">Tenacious D</a> also <a href="http://ultimateclassicrock.com/tenacious-d-metal-grammy/">claimed a metal statue</a> over some bands many consider far more worthy.</p>
