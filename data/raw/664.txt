<p><b>Vikas Bahl</b> (born 1971) is an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_film" title="Indian film">Indian film</a> producer, screenwriter, and director, know for his works predominantly in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hindi_Cinema" title="Hindi Cinema">Hindi Cinema</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> He produces films under <a href="/wiki/Phantom_Films" title="Phantom Films">Phantom Films</a>, and was the former head of <i>UTV Spot Boy</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> He has garnered two <a href="/wiki/National_Film_Awards_(India)" title="National Film Awards (India)">National Film Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vikas_Bahl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bahl was born and brought up in <a href="/wiki/Lajpat_Nagar" title="Lajpat Nagar">Lajpat Nagar</a> in Delhi, and his father worked with the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Oil_Corporation" title="Indian Oil Corporation">Indian Oil Corporation</a>. After his graduation from <a href="/wiki/Ramjas_College" title="Ramjas College">Ramjas College</a>, Delhi, he shifted to Mumbai to pursue MBA degree from <a href="/wiki/Narsee_Monjee_Institute_of_Management_Studies" title="Narsee Monjee Institute of Management Studies">Narsee Monjee Institute of Management Studies</a> (NMIMS).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-times-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vikas_Bahl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Bahl started his career with advertising for several years as a client servicing, before joining UTV Spotboy. In 2011, he started <a href="/wiki/Phantom_Films" title="Phantom Films">Phantom Films</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Anurag_Kashyap" title="Anurag Kashyap">Anurag Kashyap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vikramaditya_Motwane" title="Vikramaditya Motwane">Vikramaditya Motwane</a> and Madhu Mantena. Over the years as a director he has worked with film directors like Vishal Bhardwaj, Anurag Kashyap, Rajkumar Gupta and Vikramaditya Motwane.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Also in 2011, he co-directed <i><a href="/wiki/Chillar_Party" title="Chillar Party">Chillar Party</a></i> and won the <a href="/wiki/National_Film_Award_for_Best_Screenplay" title="National Film Award for Best Screenplay">National Film Award for Best Screenplay</a>, along with Nitesh Tiwari and Vijay Maurya. The film has also garnered the <a href="/wiki/National_Film_Award_for_Best_Children%27s_Film" title="National Film Award for Best Children's Film">National Film Award for Best Children's Film</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59thaward_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-59thaward-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2014 came his independent directorial venture, <i><a href="/wiki/Queen_(film)" title="Queen (film)">Queen</a></i> starring <a href="/wiki/Kangana_Ranaut" title="Kangana Ranaut">Kangana Ranaut</a>, which received critical and popular acclaim.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toi_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-toi-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-times_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-times-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>His upcoming project is <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shaandar" title="Shaandar">Shaandar</a></i>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Destination_wedding" title="Destination wedding">destination wedding</a> film, wherein an Indian family goes to Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-toi_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-toi-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> In October 2014, he directed a short film, <i>Going Home</i> featuring <a href="/wiki/Alia_Bhatt" title="Alia Bhatt">Alia Bhatt</a> on women's safety, which went viral on social media.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
