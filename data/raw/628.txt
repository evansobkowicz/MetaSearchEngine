<p><b>David James Furnish</b> (born 25 October 1962) is a Canadian/British filmmaker, former advertising executive, and now a film director and producer most known for his documentary <i><a href="/wiki/Elton_John:_Tantrums_%26_Tiaras" title="Elton John: Tantrums &amp; Tiaras">Elton John: Tantrums &amp; Tiaras</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_and_career">Life and career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=David_Furnish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life and career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>David Furnish was born in <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_Ontario" title="Scarborough, Ontario">Scarborough</a>, Ontario, Canada, to Jack Furnish (retired, former director of Bristol-Myers) and Gladys Furnish (retired, homemaker). He has an older brother, John, and a younger brother, Peter. Furnish graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Sir_John_A._Macdonald_Collegiate_Institute" title="Sir John A. Macdonald Collegiate Institute">Sir John A. Macdonald Collegiate Institute</a> in 1981 and received an Honours Business Administration undergraduate degree from the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Richard_Ivey_School_of_Business" title="Richard Ivey School of Business">Richard Ivey School of Business</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Western_Ontario" title="University of Western Ontario">University of Western Ontario</a> in London, Ontario in 1985. He was recruited by the advertising agency <a href="/wiki/Ogilvy_%26_Mather" title="Ogilvy &amp; Mather">Ogilvy &amp; Mather</a>, eventually transferred to the London, England, office and was appointed to their Board.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Furnish is co-chief of Rocket Pictures along with John.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Furnish serves on the board of the Elton John AIDS Foundation, attending fundraisers and other events in support of that cause.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Furnish is a contributing editor for <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tatler_(1901)" title="Tatler (1901)">Tatler</a></i> magazine and also is a regular columnist for <i><a href="/wiki/Interview_(magazine)" title="Interview (magazine)">Interview</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/GQ" title="GQ">GQ</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>Furnish began a relationship with singer <a href="/wiki/Elton_John" title="Elton John">Elton John</a> in 1993. John proposed to Furnish in May 2005 at a dinner party with friends and family at one of their homes in <a href="/wiki/Old_Windsor" title="Old Windsor">Old Windsor</a>. Furnish and John entered into a civil partnership on 21 December 2005, the first day that civil partnerships could be performed in England, in the town of <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Berkshire" title="Windsor, Berkshire">Windsor, Berkshire</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn122810_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-cnn122810-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Their first child, son Zachary Jackson Levon Furnish-John, was born in December 2010 in California via <a href="/wiki/Surrogacy" title="Surrogacy">surrogacy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn122810_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-cnn122810-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> In January 2013, the couple's second son, Elijah Joseph Daniel Furnish-John, was born through the same surrogate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> After <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gay_marriage" title="Gay marriage">gay marriage</a> <a href="/wiki/Marriage_(Same_Sex_Couples)_Act_2013" title="Marriage (Same Sex Couples) Act 2013">became legal</a> in England and Wales in March 2014, John and Furnish married in <a href="/wiki/Windsor,_Berkshire" title="Windsor, Berkshire">Windsor, Berkshire</a> on 21 December 2014, the nine-year anniversary of their civil partnership.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2015, he was named one of <a href="/wiki/GQ" title="GQ">GQ</a>'s 50 best dressed British men. <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
