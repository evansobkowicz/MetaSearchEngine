<h1 class="header">Quotes</h1>
</div>
<div class="header" id="quotes_content">

<div class="quote soda sodavote odd" id="qt0478188"><div class="sodatext">
<p>
<a href="/name/nm0000225/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">Adso of Melk</span></a>:
Master? Have you ever been in love? </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000125/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">William of Baskerville</span></a>:
In love? Yeah, many times. </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000225/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">Adso of Melk</span></a>:
You were? </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000125/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">William of Baskerville</span></a>:
Yes, of course. Aristotle, Ovid, Vergil... </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000225/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">Adso of Melk</span></a>:
No, no, no. I meant with a... </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000125/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">William of Baskerville</span></a>:
Oh. Ah. Are you not confusing love with lust? </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000225/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">Adso of Melk</span></a>:
Am I? I don't know. I want only her own good. I want her to be happy. I want to save her from her poverty. </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000125/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">William of Baskerville</span></a>:
Oh, dear. </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000225/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">Adso of Melk</span></a>:
Why "oh dear"? </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000125/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">William of Baskerville</span></a>:
You *are* in love. </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000225/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">Adso of Melk</span></a>:
Is that bad? </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000125/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">William of Baskerville</span></a>:
For a monk, it does present certain problems. </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000225/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">Adso of Melk</span></a>:
But doesn't St. Thomas Aquinas praise love above all other virtues? </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000125/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">William of Baskerville</span></a>:
Yes, the love of God, Adso. The love of God. </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000225/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">Adso of Melk</span></a>:
Oh... And the love of woman? </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000125/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">William of Baskerville</span></a>:
Of woman? Thomas Aquinas knew precious little, but the scriptures are very clear. Proverbs warns us, "Woman takes possession of a man's precious soul", while Ecclesiastes tells us, "More bitter than death is woman". </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000225/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">Adso of Melk</span></a>:
Yes, but what do you think, Master? </p><p>
<a href="/name/nm0000125/?ref_=tt_trv_qu"><span class="character">William of Baskerville</span></a>:
Well, of course I don't have the benefit of your experience, but I find it difficult to convince myself that God would have introduced such a foul being into creation without endowing her with *some* virtures. Hmm? How peaceful life would be without love, Adso, how safe, how tranquil, and how dull. </p></div>
