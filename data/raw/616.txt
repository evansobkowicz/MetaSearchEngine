<p><b>Deborah Jeanne</b> "<b>Debbie</b>" <b>Rowe</b> (born December 6, 1958)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> is an American nurse known for her marriage to <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a>, with whom she had two children. She lives in <a href="/wiki/Palmdale,_California" title="Palmdale, California">Palmdale</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Debbie_Rowe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rowe was born in Spokane, Washington, the daughter of Barbara Chilcutt and Gordon Rowe. Her father divorced her mother a few weeks before her second birthday. Rowe had a lonely childhood.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> She was raised by her mother, a few aunts, and maternal grandmother. She has one half-sister named Loretta Scarlett Rowe who was born in 1961. She was raised in a <a href="/wiki/Middle_class" title="Middle class">middle class</a> home, and was later adopted by a millionaire couple from Malibu, California.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_with_Michael_Jackson">Relationship with Michael Jackson</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Debbie_Rowe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Relationship with Michael Jackson">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="hatnote boilerplate seealso">See also: <a href="/wiki/Personal_relationships_of_Michael_Jackson#Debbie_Rowe" title="Personal relationships of Michael Jackson">Relationship of Michael Jackson and Debbie Rowe</a></div>
<p>Rowe met <a href="/wiki/Michael_Jackson" title="Michael Jackson">Michael Jackson</a> while working as a nurse in Dr. <a href="/wiki/Arnold_Klein" title="Arnold Klein">Arnold Klein</a>'s dermatology office, where Jackson was being treated for <a href="/wiki/Vitiligo" title="Vitiligo">vitiligo</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-people-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> She recalled that after Jackson's divorce from <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Marie_Presley" title="Lisa Marie Presley">Lisa Marie Presley</a> in 1996, he was upset at the possibility that he might never become a father. Rowe, a longtime Jackson fan, proposed to bear his children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-outtakes_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-outtakes-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> In an interview with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Playboy_Magazine" title="Playboy Magazine">Playboy</a>, Lisa Marie stated that she knew at the time that she and Jackson were married, that Rowe wanted to have his children and that Rowe had "a crush on him".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Playboyinterview1_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-Playboyinterview1-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Children_and_marriage">Children and marriage</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Debbie_Rowe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Children and marriage">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Michael_Jackson3_2006_(cropped_version).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="495" data-file-width="597" height="182" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Michael_Jackson3_2006_%28cropped_version%29.jpg/220px-Michael_Jackson3_2006_%28cropped_version%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Michael_Jackson3_2006_%28cropped_version%29.jpg/330px-Michael_Jackson3_2006_%28cropped_version%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Michael_Jackson3_2006_%28cropped_version%29.jpg/440px-Michael_Jackson3_2006_%28cropped_version%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a>

<p>It was announced she was pregnant in 1996, and the two were later married on November 14, 1996, in <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. It was her second marriage, her first being to Richard Edelman in 1982.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-people_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-people-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Three months after Rowe and Jackson's marriage she gave birth to a son, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Michael_Jackson_I" title="Prince Michael Jackson I">Michael Joseph Jackson Jr.</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Certs_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Certs-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> (born February 13, 1997),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> who was subsequently known as Prince.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> The next year she gave birth to a daughter, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris-Michael_Katherine_Jackson" title="Paris-Michael Katherine Jackson">Paris-Michael Katherine Jackson</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Certs_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Certs-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> (born April 3, 1998).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Jackson took full responsibility for raising the children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-outtakes_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-outtakes-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Divorce">Divorce</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Debbie_Rowe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Divorce">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rowe, who described herself as a private person and almost never gave interviews, was overwhelmed by the publicity that came with being married to Jackson.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-outtakes_8-2"><a href="#cite_note-outtakes-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The couple divorced on October 8, 1999, with Rowe giving full custody rights of the children to Jackson. Rowe received an $8-million settlement, and a house in <a href="/wiki/Beverly_Hills,_California" title="Beverly Hills, California">Beverly Hills, California</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Los_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Los-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> Court documents indicated she had signed a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prenuptial" title="Prenuptial">prenuptial</a> agreement and therefore could not obtain an equal division of <a href="/wiki/Community_property" title="Community property">community property</a> under California law.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2001, Rowe went to a <a href="/wiki/Alternative_dispute_resolution" title="Alternative dispute resolution">private judge</a> to have her <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Parental_rights" title="Parental rights">parental rights</a> for the two children terminated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Los_14-1"><a href="#cite_note-Los-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2004, after Jackson was charged with 10 counts of child abuse, she went to court to have the decision reversed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sue_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-sue-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> According to the <a href="/wiki/Jewish_Telegraphic_Agency" title="Jewish Telegraphic Agency">Jewish Telegraphic Agency</a> Rowe, who converted<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> to Judaism from Catholicism, sought the reversal in part because she feared the nanny and some of Jackson's siblings were exposing the children to teachings of the <a href="/wiki/Nation_of_Islam" title="Nation of Islam">Nation of Islam</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> 2005 court documents noted that “Because she is Jewish, Deborah feared the children might be mistreated if Michael continued the association.”<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup> On the stand, in the 2005 <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People_v._Jackson" title="People v. Jackson">People v. Jackson</a> case, she explained that she had been allowed limited visits to her children, for eight hours every 45 days.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2005, Rowe sold her Beverly Hills house for $1.3 million, and bought a <a href="/wiki/Ranch" title="Ranch">ranch</a> in Palmdale.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> In 2006, she sued Jackson for one immediate payment of $195,000 and one payment of $50,000 to pursue a child custody case.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sue_16-1"><a href="#cite_note-sue-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Jackson was ordered to pay her $60,000 in legal fees.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="After_Jackson.27s_death">After Jackson's death</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Debbie_Rowe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: After Jackson's death">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Michael_Jackson" title="Death of Michael Jackson">Jackson's death</a> on June 25, 2009, Rowe made statements through her attorney to deny a series of gossip reports, including reports that she was not the children's biological mother<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NBC_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-NBC-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> and that she was attempting to bargain her parental rights for money.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> Several gossip outlets reported that Debbie Rowe was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Surrogate_mother" title="Surrogate mother">surrogate mother</a> for the children and not their biological mother.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In July, 2009, she filed a lawsuit for <a href="/wiki/Defamation" title="Defamation">defamation</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Invasion_of_privacy" title="Invasion of privacy">invasion of privacy</a> against a source who handed over alleged private e-mails to the television entertainment news program <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra_(TV_series)" title="Extra (TV series)">Extra</a></i><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> and on March 3, 2010 she was successful in the defamation lawsuit. She was awarded $27,000 in damages, although she had originally sought $500,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In August 2009, Rowe reached a settlement with <a href="/wiki/Katherine_Jackson" title="Katherine Jackson">Katherine Jackson</a>, the children's guardian, under which she has rights to supervised visitations. Mrs. Jackson's attorney stated that the negotiations were "never about money" and the settlement was in the best interests of the children.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Debbie_Rowe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: In popular culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rowe was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/April_Telek" title="April Telek">April Telek</a> in the 2004 film <i><a href="/wiki/Man_in_the_Mirror:_The_Michael_Jackson_Story" title="Man in the Mirror: The Michael Jackson Story">Man in the Mirror: The Michael Jackson Story</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Rowe was parodied in actress/comedian Tracey Ullman's series <i><a href="/wiki/Tracey_Ullman%27s_State_of_the_Union" title="Tracey Ullman's State of the Union">Tracey Ullman's State of the Union</a></i>.</p>
