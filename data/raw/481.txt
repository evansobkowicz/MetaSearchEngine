<p>Amy Pascal has stepped down as the co-chair of Sony production house. She reportedly took the decision of leaving her esteemed position at the studio after her private emails were leaked in a  Sony cyber hack.</p>
<p>According to Dave McNary of <a href="http://variety.com/2015/film/news/amy-pascals-exit-could-affect-sonys-spider-man-franchise-dragon-tattoo-1201425102/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Variety</a>, Pascal’s exit could affect the future of “Spider-Man” franchise. She was reportedly responsible for expanding the Spider-Man universe. It is being speculated post Pascal’s departure, the future of the “Spider-Man” franchise can be re-examined.</p>
<p>Last year at Comic-Con, the studio revealed its plans to go ahead with the “Spider-Man” spinoff, “Sinister Six.” The film will reportedly focus on the main villains of the Spidey universe and is scheduled for a 2016 release.  Meanwhile, “The Amazing Spider-Man 3,” is reportedly planned for a 2018 release. There is nothing confirmed in regards to the “Amazing Spider-Man” franchise as the last instalment in the series did not prove as successful as expected. The film starred Andrew Garfield and Emma Stone in the leading role.</p>
<p>The Sony hack has also revealed that Garfield may be ousted as Spiderman in the future films, according to <a href="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-2874218/Andrew-Garfield-booted-Spider-Man-movie-series-according-latest-Sony-email-leaks.html" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">Daily Mail</a>. There was a major buzz that Marvel is planning to take over the “Spider-Man” franchise and they may start the series from a clean slate. Though nothing is confirmed in this regard. Meanwhile, apart from the “Spider-Man” series, other instalments, which could get affected by Pascal’s exit, include “Girl With the Dragon Tattoo” and the “Cleopatra” project. </p>
<p>Pascal’s new career plans include the launch of her own production company. “I have spent almost my entire professional life at Sony Pictures and I am energized to be starting this new chapter based at the company I call home," said Ms Pascal in a statement, reports <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/business-31157107" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">BBC</a>. Pascal has been planning to switch to production for quite some time, according to the website. Sony will be funding her production company for four years but will retain distribution rights of the films. It is still not known who will replace Pascal as the Sony co-chair. PE Motion Picture Group president Doug Belgrad could possibly replace Pascal, reports Variety. Other executives who could step into Pascal’s shoes include Tom Rothman, of TriStar Pictures; and Michael De Luca of Columbia Pictures. </p>
