<p><b>Pernilla August</b> (born <b>Mia Pernilla Hertzman-Ericson</b>; 13 February 1958<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup>) is a <a href="/wiki/Swedes" title="Swedes">Swedish</a> <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">actress</a>, <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">director</a> and <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">screenwriter</a>. Being one of <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>'s leading actresses and a longtime collaborator with director <a href="/wiki/Ingmar_Bergman" title="Ingmar Bergman">Ingmar Bergman</a>, she won the <a href="/wiki/Best_Actress_Award_(Cannes_Film_Festival)" title="Best Actress Award (Cannes Film Festival)">Best Actress Award</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1992_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="1992 Cannes Film Festival">1992 Cannes Film Festival</a> for her role in his <i><a href="/wiki/The_Best_Intentions" title="The Best Intentions">The Best Intentions</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pernilla_August&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Actor">Actor</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pernilla_August&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Actor">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>August started acting during her childhood in theatre and at school. Her professional acting career started in 1975 when director <a href="/wiki/Roy_Andersson" title="Roy Andersson">Roy Andersson</a> cast her in a minor role in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Giliap" title="Giliap">Giliap</a></i> the same year, followed from 1979 by films by other directors, <a href="/wiki/Vilgot_Sj%C3%B6man" title="Vilgot Sjöman">Vilgot Sjöman</a> (among them, the film about <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Nobel" title="Alfred Nobel">Alfred Nobel</a>, 1983) and <a href="/wiki/Lasse_Hallstr%C3%B6m" title="Lasse Hallström">Lasse Hallström</a>. She studied acting at <a href="/wiki/Swedish_National_Academy_of_Mime_and_Acting" title="Swedish National Academy of Mime and Acting">Swedish National Academy of Mime and Acting</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a> 1979–82. Before finishing her studies, she attracted the attention of <a href="/wiki/Ingmar_Bergman" title="Ingmar Bergman">Ingmar Bergman</a>, who cast her in his film <i><a href="/wiki/Fanny_and_Alexander" title="Fanny and Alexander">Fanny and Alexander</a></i> (1982), playing the nanny in the director's romanticised portrait of his childhood. That marked the beginning of two decades of collaboration, collecting several international awards, including television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Best_Intentions" title="The Best Intentions">The Best Intentions</a></i> (1991), where she portrayed Bergman's mother and met her second husband to be, director <a href="/wiki/Bille_August" title="Bille August">Bille August</a>, and TV-productions <i><a href="/wiki/Private_Confessions" title="Private Confessions">Private Confessions</a></i> (1996), directed by <a href="/wiki/Liv_Ullmann" title="Liv Ullmann">Liv Ullmann</a> and Bergman's own <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Presence_of_a_Clown" title="In the Presence of a Clown">In the Presence of a Clown</a></i> (1997).</p>
<p>She also starred in <a href="/wiki/Bo_Widerberg" title="Bo Widerberg">Bo Widerberg</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Serpent%27s_Way" title="The Serpent's Way">The Serpent's Way</a></i> (1986) as well as his TV-production of <a href="/wiki/Henrik_Ibsen" title="Henrik Ibsen">Henrik Ibsen</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wild_Duck" title="The Wild Duck">The Wild Duck</a></i> (1989). Among the many <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavian</a> and international films are also Bille August's <i><a href="/wiki/Jerusalem_(1996_film)" title="Jerusalem (1996 film)">Jerusalem</a></i> (1996), <a href="/wiki/Richard_Hobert" title="Richard Hobert">Richard Hobert</a>'s <i>Where the Rainbow Ends</i> (1999) [granted her a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guldbagge_Award" title="Guldbagge Award">Guldbagge Award</a> as Best actress] and <i>The Birthday</i> (2000], <i><a href="/wiki/I_Am_Dina" title="I Am Dina">I Am Dina</a></i> (2002), <a href="/wiki/Bj%C3%B6rn_Runge" title="Björn Runge">Björn Runge</a>'s <i>Om jag vänder mig om</i>/<i>If I Turn Around</i> (2003) [a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silver_Bear" title="Silver Bear">Silver Bear</a> at <a href="/wiki/Berlin_International_Film_Festival" title="Berlin International Film Festival">Berlin International Film Festival</a> 2004 and more Best actress awards], <a href="/wiki/Per_Fly" title="Per Fly">Per Fly</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Manslaughter_(2005_film)" title="Manslaughter (2005 film)">Manslaughter</a></i> (2005), Swedish-<a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwanese</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Miss_Kicki" title="Miss Kicki">Miss Kicki</a></i> (2009) and <a href="/wiki/Jan_Troell" title="Jan Troell">Jan Troell</a>'s <i>Truth and Consequence</i> (2012).</p>
<p>At the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dramatic_Theatre" title="Royal Dramatic Theatre">Royal Dramatic Theatre</a> in Stockholm, she has acted in several plays, starting 1981, several directed by Ingmar Bergman and touring internationally. These include <a href="/wiki/Ophelia" title="Ophelia">Ophelia</a> in <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Hamlet" title="Hamlet">Hamlet</a></i> (1986) [a part she has also played in another Swedish television film, 1985], <a href="/wiki/August_Strindberg" title="August Strindberg">August Strindberg</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/A_Dream_Play" title="A Dream Play">A Dream Play</a></i> (1986), Nora in <a href="/wiki/Henrik_Ibsen" title="Henrik Ibsen">Henrik Ibsen</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/A_Doll%27s_House" title="A Doll's House">A Doll's House</a></i> (1989), Hermione in Bergman's special version of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/A_Winter%27s_Tale" title="A Winter's Tale">A Winter's Tale</a></i> (1994), the title role in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Schiller" title="Schiller">Schiller</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Mary_Stuart_(play)" title="Mary Stuart (play)">Mary Stuart</a></i> (2000) and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Helene_Alving" title="Helene Alving">Helene Alving</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ibsen" title="Ibsen">Ibsen</a>'s <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ghosts" title="Ghosts">Ghosts</a></i> (2002). She also worked with <a href="/wiki/Russians" title="Russians">Russian</a> director Jurij Ljubimov in <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Pushkin" title="Alexander Pushkin">Alexander Pushkin</a>'s <i>A Feast in the Time of Plague</i> (1996). In 1983-84, she worked at Folkteatern i Gävleborg (The Folk Theatre in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/G%C3%A4vleborg" title="Gävleborg">Gävleborg</a>) with director <a href="/wiki/Peter_Oskarson" title="Peter Oskarson">Peter Oskarson</a> in <a href="/wiki/Anton_Chekhov" title="Anton Chekhov">Anton Chekhov</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Three_Sisters_(play)" title="Three Sisters (play)">Three Sisters</a></i>. In 2008, she acted in the stage production of <i><a href="/wiki/Steel_Magnolias_(play)" title="Steel Magnolias (play)">Steel Magnolias</a></i> in Stockholm.</p>
<p>Many in the <a href="/wiki/English-speaking_world" title="English-speaking world">English-speaking world</a> know her best as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Star_Wars_characters#S" title="List of Star Wars characters">Shmi Skywalker</a>, the mother of <a href="/wiki/Darth_Vader" title="Darth Vader">Anakin Skywalker</a>, from <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_I:_The_Phantom_Menace" title="Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace">Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace</a></i> (1999) and its sequel <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_II:_Attack_of_the_Clones" title="Star Wars Episode II: Attack of the Clones">Attack of the Clones</a></i> (2002). She also appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Young_Indiana_Jones_Chronicles" title="The Young Indiana Jones Chronicles">The Young Indiana Jones Chronicles</a></i>. She shared the role as Virgin Mary in <i><a href="/wiki/Mary,_Mother_of_Jesus_(film)" title="Mary, Mother of Jesus (film)">Mary, Mother of Jesus</a></i> (1999) with <a href="/wiki/Melinda_Kinnaman" title="Melinda Kinnaman">Melinda Kinnaman</a>. She played a bomber in the Swedish film <i><a href="/wiki/Deadline_(2001_film)" title="Deadline (2001 film)">Sprängaren</a></i> (2001). In 2011, she reprised her role as Shmi Skywalker in the third season of <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_(2008_TV_series)" title="Star Wars: The Clone Wars (2008 TV series)">Star Wars: The Clone Wars</a></i> for the episode "Overlords", which aired on 28 January.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Director">Director</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pernilla_August&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Director">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After her directorial debut with the 2005 short film <i>Time Bomb</i> (<i>Blindgångare</i>), she debuted as feature-film director and <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">screenwriter</a> in 2010 with <i><a href="/wiki/Beyond_(2010_film)" title="Beyond (2010 film)">Beyond</a></i>, starring <a href="/wiki/Noomi_Rapace" title="Noomi Rapace">Noomi Rapace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ola_Rapace" title="Ola Rapace">Ola Rapace</a>. The film was selected as the Swedish entry for the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Foreign_Language_Film" title="Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film">Best Foreign Language Film</a> at the <a href="/wiki/84th_Academy_Awards" title="84th Academy Awards">84th Academy Awards</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84th_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-84th-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> and was much awarded with Swedish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guldbagge_Award" title="Guldbagge Award">Guldbagge Awards</a> and more; August won for Best Director and Best Script. In October 2011, August was asked to direct a new Danish drama series, <i>Arvingerne</i>, an offer she accepted. The series premiered on <a href="/wiki/DR_(broadcaster)" title="DR (broadcaster)">DR</a> on 1 January 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Her two youngest daughters have cameos in the series.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awards">Awards</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pernilla_August&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Awards">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Among many international awards and nominations, at the <a href="/wiki/1992_Cannes_Film_Festival" title="1992 Cannes Film Festival">1992 Cannes Film Festival</a> she won the award for <a href="/wiki/Best_Actress_Award_(Cannes_Film_Festival)" title="Best Actress Award (Cannes Film Festival)">Best Actress</a>, for her role in <a href="/wiki/Bille_August" title="Bille August">Bille August</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/The_Best_Intentions" title="The Best Intentions">The Best Intentions</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-festival-cannes.com_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-festival-cannes.com-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> For the same film she also won the <a href="/wiki/Guldbagge_Award_for_Best_Actress_in_a_Leading_Role" title="Guldbagge Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role">Best Actress</a> award at the <a href="/wiki/28th_Guldbagge_Awards" title="28th Guldbagge Awards">28th Guldbagge Awards</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28thGuldbagge_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-28thGuldbagge-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2002 she was honored with the Royal Swedish medal <a href="/wiki/Litteris_et_Artibus" title="Litteris et Artibus">Litteris et Artibus</a> for her artistic work.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pernilla_August&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>August was born in <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">Stockholm</a>. She has been married twice and changed her name both times. Her first marriage was to <a href="/wiki/Klas_%C3%96stergren" title="Klas Östergren">Klas Östergren</a> and the second to <a href="/wiki/Bille_August" title="Bille August">Bille August</a> (1991–1997). She has three daughters.</p>
