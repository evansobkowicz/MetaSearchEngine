<div class="entry-headline">
<p>Only members of The Rolling Stones can afford this collection of photos, of The Rolling Stones.</p>
</div>
<div class="entry-content">
<p>The Stones are one of the most-photographed bands of all time, attracting some of the best photographers in the world. Ethan Russell. Jim Marshall. Anton Corbijn. Annie Leibovitz</p>
<p>There’s now a 518-page collection of the best of those shots taken from about 1965 to 1992. And as with all things Stones, it ain’t cheap.</p>
<p><a href="http://i0.wp.com/ajournalofmusicalthings.com/wp-content/uploads/rolling_stones_su_gb_signpage001_02616_1410221638_id_845217.jpg"><img alt="rolling_stones_su_gb_signpage001_02616_1410221638_id_845217" class="alignnone wp-image-63744" height="485" src="http://i0.wp.com/ajournalofmusicalthings.com/wp-content/uploads/rolling_stones_su_gb_signpage001_02616_1410221638_id_845217.jpg?resize=485%2C485" width="485"/></a></p>
<p>The “basic” version of the book runs $150 USD while limited editions go for $5,000 (“sumo-sized” with foldouts and silk-screened printed chapter folder) and $10,000 (six “Art Editions,”limited to just 75 copies) The publisher is <a href="http://www.taschen.com/pages/en/catalogue/photography/all/02616/facts.rolling_stones.htm" target="_blank">Taschen</a>, no strangers to high-end photo books.</p>
<p><a href="http://i2.wp.com/ajournalofmusicalthings.com/wp-content/uploads/Rolling-Stones-Mick-and-cat.jpg"><img alt="Rolling Stones  - Mick and cat" class="alignnone wp-image-63743" height="321" src="http://i2.wp.com/ajournalofmusicalthings.com/wp-content/uploads/Rolling-Stones-Mick-and-cat.jpg?resize=486%2C321" width="486"/></a></p>
<p> </p>
