<div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><p><b>Martin Rosen</b> is an American-born filmmaker and theater producer. Rosen is known for having directed the animated film adaptation of <i><a href="/wiki/Watership_Down_(film)" title="Watership Down (film)">Watership Down</a></i> (1978) from the <a href="/wiki/Richard_Adams" title="Richard Adams">Richard Adams</a> novel.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Martin_Rosen_(director)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rosen originally worked as a theater agent and talent scout, before he moved with his wife to the United Kingdom.</p>
<p>His first produced the Canadian feature <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=A_Great_Big_Thing&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="A Great Big Thing (page does not exist)">A Great Big Thing</a></i> (1968) and later co-produced <a href="/wiki/Ken_Russell" title="Ken Russell">Ken Russell</a>'s film version of <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Women_in_Love_(1969_film)" title="Women in Love (1969 film)">Women in Love</a></i> (1969), which won Academy Awards for <a href="/wiki/Glenda_Jackson" title="Glenda Jackson">Glenda Jackson</a> (actress) and <a href="/wiki/Billy_Williams_(cinematographer)" title="Billy Williams (cinematographer)">Billy Williams</a> (cinematography).</p>
<p>Rosen was originally the producer of <i><a href="/wiki/Watership_Down_(film)" title="Watership Down (film)">Watership Down</a></i> but took over as director after <a href="/wiki/John_Hubley" title="John Hubley">John Hubley</a> fell back and died, he also wrote the screenplay for it. This was the first of two novels by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Adams" title="Richard Adams">Richard Adams</a> he adapted. In 1982 he also produced, directed and wrote the screenplay for another animated feature based on an Adams novel, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Plague_Dogs_(film)" title="The Plague Dogs (film)">The Plague Dogs</a></i> (1982). Rosen produced <i><a href="/wiki/Smooth_Talk" title="Smooth Talk">Smooth Talk</a></i> (1986), which won the Sundance Grand Prize. His last film as director was <i><a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Stacking_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Stacking (film) (page does not exist)">Stacking</a></i> (1987). His last project as producer was the animated <i><a href="/wiki/Watership_Down_(TV_series)" title="Watership Down (TV series)"><i>Watership Down</i></a></i> TV series in 1999.</p>
<p>Rosen has also contributed on theater production, he was the originating producer of Michael Weller's <i><a href="/wiki/Moonchildren" title="Moonchildren">Moonchildren</a></i> first presented at London's Royal Court Theater before transferring to the US. He was the originating producer of Maxine Hong Kingston's "The Woman Warrior" presented in association with The Brekeley Rep, Boston's Huntington Theater, and the Doolittle Theater in Los Angeles.</p>
<p>Rosen is married to Elisabeth Payne Rosen, an author and ordained deacon in the <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_Church_(United_States)" title="Episcopal Church (United States)">Episcopal Church</a>.</p>
