<p><b>Lee Unkrich</b> (born August 8, 1967) is an American <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">director</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_editor" title="Film editor">film editor</a> and <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">screenwriter</a>. He is a longtime member of the creative team at <a href="/wiki/Pixar" title="Pixar">Pixar</a>, where he started in 1994 as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_editor" title="Film editor">film editor</a>. He later began directing, first as co-director of <i><a href="/wiki/Toy_Story_2" title="Toy Story 2">Toy Story 2</a></i>. After co-directing <i><a href="/wiki/Monsters,_Inc." title="Monsters, Inc.">Monsters, Inc.</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Finding_Nemo" title="Finding Nemo">Finding Nemo</a></i>, Unkrich made his solo directorial debut with <i><a href="/wiki/Toy_Story_3" title="Toy Story 3">Toy Story 3</a></i>, for which he won the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a> for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Animated_Feature" title="Academy Award for Best Animated Feature">Best Animated Film</a> in 2011.</p>
<p>A native of <a href="/wiki/Chagrin_Falls,_Ohio" title="Chagrin Falls, Ohio">Chagrin Falls, Ohio</a>, Unkrich spent his youth acting at <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_Play_House" title="Cleveland Play House">The Cleveland Play House</a>. Unkrich graduated from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Southern_California" title="University of Southern California">University of Southern California</a> <a href="/wiki/USC_School_of_Cinematic_Arts" title="USC School of Cinematic Arts">School of Cinematic Arts</a> in 1990.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USCalum_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-USCalum-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Before joining Pixar in 1994, Unkrich worked for several years in television as an editor and director. Unkrich is married to Laura Century and they have three children: Hannah, Alice, and Max.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cleveland_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cleveland-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Unkrich is the 2011 recipient of University of Southern California's Mary Pickford Distinguished Alumni Award recognizing alumni contributions to the cinematic arts.</p>
