<p><b>Jar Jar Binks</b> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fictional_character" title="Fictional character">fictional character</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a> saga created by <a href="/wiki/George_Lucas" title="George Lucas">George Lucas</a>. A major character in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_I:_The_Phantom_Menace" title="Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace">Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace</a></i>, he also appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_II:_Attack_of_the_Clones" title="Star Wars Episode II: Attack of the Clones">Episode II: Attack of the Clones</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_III:_Revenge_of_the_Sith" title="Star Wars Episode III: Revenge of the Sith">Episode III: Revenge of the Sith</a></i>, and the television series <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Clone_Wars_(2008_TV_series)" title="Star Wars: The Clone Wars (2008 TV series)">Star Wars: The Clone Wars</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Star_Wars_Databank_-_Binks.2C_Jar_Jar_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-Star_Wars_Databank_-_Binks.2C_Jar_Jar-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The first lead <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Computer_generated_imagery" title="Computer generated imagery">computer generated</a> character of the franchise, he was portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Ahmed_Best" title="Ahmed Best">Ahmed Best</a> in most of his appearances.</p>
<p>Jar Jar's primary role in <i>Episode I</i> was to provide <a href="/wiki/Comic_relief" title="Comic relief">comic relief</a> for the audience, and was generally met with extremely negative comments from both critics and viewers. He is often acknowledged as one of the worst and most hated characters of the <i>Star Wars</i> franchise and the entire film industry at large.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scifimoviepage.com_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-scifimoviepage.com-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Appearances">Appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jar_Jar_Binks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Films">Films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jar_Jar_Binks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jar Jar Binks first appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_I:_The_Phantom_Menace" title="Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace">Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace</a></i> as a bumbling, foolish <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Star_Wars_races_(F%E2%80%93J)#Gungan" title="List of Star Wars races (F–J)">Gungan</a> from the planet <a href="/wiki/Naboo" title="Naboo">Naboo</a>. Banished by his tribe through Boss Rugor Nass for his clumsiness, he is nearly killed by a Federation transport, only to be saved at the last minute by <a href="/wiki/Jedi" title="Jedi">Jedi Knight</a> <a href="/wiki/Qui-Gon_Jinn" title="Qui-Gon Jinn">Qui-Gon Jinn</a> (<a href="/wiki/Liam_Neeson" title="Liam Neeson">Liam Neeson</a>). Qui-Gon and his <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Padawan" title="Padawan">padawan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Obi-Wan_Kenobi" title="Obi-Wan Kenobi">Obi-Wan Kenobi</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ewan_McGregor" title="Ewan McGregor">Ewan McGregor</a>), persuade Jar Jar's tribe to release him to their custody as a guide. He later goes with the Jedi and Queen <a href="/wiki/Padm%C3%A9_Amidala" title="Padmé Amidala">Padmé Amidala</a> (<a href="/wiki/Natalie_Portman" title="Natalie Portman">Natalie Portman</a>) to the planet <a href="/wiki/Tatooine" title="Tatooine">Tatooine</a>, where he meets and befriends <a href="/wiki/Darth_Vader" title="Darth Vader">Anakin Skywalker</a> (<a href="/wiki/Jake_Lloyd" title="Jake Lloyd">Jake Lloyd</a>).</p>
<p>Jar Jar later appears in the film's climactic battle scene, where he leads his fellow Gungans, as a general in the Gungan army, in defeating the <a href="/wiki/Trade_Federation" title="Trade Federation">Trade Federation</a>. After the battle he appears at the funeral of Qui-Gon Jinn and in the ending parade with his fellow Gungans.</p>
<p>Jar Jar's role in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_II:_Attack_of_the_Clones" title="Star Wars Episode II: Attack of the Clones">Attack of the Clones</a></i> is much smaller, but his actions are significant. Ten years after helping to save his planet, he is a delegate to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galactic_Senate" title="Galactic Senate">Galactic Senate</a> and as such, plays a role in bringing his old friends, Obi-Wan and Anakin (<a href="/wiki/Hayden_Christensen" title="Hayden Christensen">Hayden Christensen</a>) back to <a href="/wiki/Coruscant" title="Coruscant">Coruscant</a>, where he greets them with enthusiasm. Later, on the behalf of the Naboo, he gives a speech to the assembled Senate in favor of granting <a href="/wiki/Palpatine" title="Palpatine">Chancellor Palpatine</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ian_McDiarmid" title="Ian McDiarmid">Ian McDiarmid</a>) vast <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emergency_powers" title="Emergency powers">emergency powers</a>. These are granted, giving Palpatine the necessary power he needs to subsequently overthrow the senate and bring the galaxy into the dictatorial control of the <a href="/wiki/Sith" title="Sith">Sith</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Galactic_Empire_(Star_Wars)" title="Galactic Empire (Star Wars)">Galactic Empire</a>.</p>
<p>Jar Jar appears in only a few scenes in <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_III:_Revenge_of_the_Sith" title="Star Wars Episode III: Revenge of the Sith">Revenge of the Sith</a></i>, and has no dialogue (besides a brief "'scuse me" at one point). He was originally given some dialogue in the beginning, but this was cut.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> He is most prominently featured in Padmé Amidala's funeral procession at the end of the film.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Clone_Wars"><i>Clone Wars</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jar_Jar_Binks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Clone Wars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jar Jar Binks is a supporting character in the animated series <i>The Clone Wars</i>, once again voiced by Best, although BJ Hughes voiced the character in a handful of season one episodes. In this series, he is a Senate representative who sometimes accompanies the main characters—Anakin, Ahsoka, Obi-Wan, and Padmé—on their adventures. He and master <a href="/wiki/Mace_Windu" title="Mace Windu">Mace Windu</a> are the two main characters of the episodes "The Disappeared" part 1 and 2. In which they had to search for missing elders and rescue a princess, who was Jar Jar's past love.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jar_Jar_Binks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Even before the release of <i>The Phantom Menace</i>, Jar Jar Binks became the subject of a great deal of media and popular attention, though not in the way his creators intended. Binks became symbolic of what many reviewers such as Brent Staples (<i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> David Edelstein (<i><a href="/wiki/Slate_(magazine)" title="Slate (magazine)">Slate</a></i>),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> and Eric Harrison (<i><a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles_Times" title="Los Angeles Times">Los Angeles Times</a></i>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> considered to be creative flaws of the film. The character was widely rejected and often ridiculed<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scifimoviepage.com_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-scifimoviepage.com-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> by people who felt that Jar Jar was included in the film solely to appeal to children. One fan, <a href="/wiki/Mike_J._Nichols" title="Mike J. Nichols">Mike J. Nichols</a>, created and distributed, free of charge, a modified version of the film, entitled <i><a href="/wiki/The_Phantom_Edit" title="The Phantom Edit">The Phantom Edit</a></i>, which cut out several scenes featuring what Nichols dubbed 'Jar Jar antics.' The character was also lampooned on an episode of the television show <i><a href="/wiki/South_Park" title="South Park">South Park</a></i> entitled "<a href="/wiki/Jakovasaurs" title="Jakovasaurs">Jakovasaurs</a>", in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fairly_OddParents" title="The Fairly OddParents">The Fairly OddParents</a></i> (Episode: "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abra-Catastrophe!" title="Abra-Catastrophe!">Abra-Catastrophe!</a>"), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">The Simpsons</a></i> (Episode: "<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Co-Dependent%27s_Day" title="Co-Dependent's Day">Co-Dependent's Day</a>"), as well as the parody <i><a href="/wiki/Robot_Chicken:_Star_Wars" title="Robot Chicken: Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Robot_Chicken:_Star_Wars_Episode_II" title="Robot Chicken: Star Wars Episode II">episodes</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Robot_Chicken" title="Robot Chicken">Robot Chicken</a></i>, in which Best reprised the role in voice-over form.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Along with film critics, many have also accused the film's creators of excessive <a href="/wiki/Commercialization" title="Commercialization">commercialization</a> directed at young children (a criticism first leveled with the introduction of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ewoks" title="Ewoks">Ewoks</a> in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Star_Wars_Episode_VI:_Return_of_the_Jedi" title="Star Wars Episode VI: Return of the Jedi">Star Wars Episode VI: Return of the Jedi</a></i>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-scifimoviepage.com_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-scifimoviepage.com-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Star Wars</i> creator <a href="/wiki/George_Lucas" title="George Lucas">George Lucas</a> stated that he feels there is a section of the fanbase who get upset with aspects of <i>Star Wars</i> because "[t]he movies are for children but they don't want to admit that... There is a small group of fans that do not like comic <a href="/wiki/Sidekick" title="Sidekick">sidekicks</a>. They want the films to be tough like <i><a href="/wiki/The_Terminator" title="The Terminator">The Terminator</a></i>, and they get very upset and opinionated about anything that has anything to do with being childlike."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rob_Coleman" title="Rob Coleman">Rob Coleman</a>, who was the lead on the <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Light_%26_Magic" title="Industrial Light &amp; Magic">Industrial Light &amp; Magic</a> animation team, warned Lucas that the team thought Jar Jar's character came across poorly. Lucas told him that he specifically put Jar Jar in the film to appeal to small children twelve or under.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Allegations_of_racial_caricature">Allegations of racial caricature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jar_Jar_Binks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Allegations of racial caricature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Joe_Morgenstern" title="Joe Morgenstern">Joe Morgenstern</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i> described the character as a "<a href="/wiki/Rastafari_movement" title="Rastafari movement">Rastafarian</a> <a href="/wiki/Stepin_Fetchit" title="Stepin Fetchit">Stepin Fetchit</a> on platform hoofs, crossed annoyingly with <a href="/wiki/Butterfly_McQueen" title="Butterfly McQueen">Butterfly McQueen</a>."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Patricia_J._Williams" title="Patricia J. Williams">Patricia J. Williams</a> suggested that many aspects of Jar Jar's character are highly reminiscent of the archetypes portrayed in <a href="/wiki/Blackface" title="Blackface">blackface</a> <a href="/wiki/Minstrel_show" title="Minstrel show">minstrelsy</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Williams_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Williams-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup> while others have suggested the character is a "laid-back clown character" representing a black <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> stereotype.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> George Lucas has denied any racist implications.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> Ahmed Best also rejected the allegations, saying that "Jar Jar has nothing to do with the Caribbean".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_game_appearance">Video game appearance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jar_Jar_Binks&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Video game appearance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jar Jar appears as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LEGO" title="LEGO">LEGO</a> mini-figure in the <i><a href="/wiki/Lego_Star_Wars" title="Lego Star Wars">Lego Star Wars</a></i> video games. He also appears as an Angry Bird with a hook move in <i><a href="/wiki/Angry_Birds_Star_Wars_II" title="Angry Birds Star Wars II">Angry Birds Star Wars II</a></i>.</p>
