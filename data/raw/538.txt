<p><i><b>Inception: Music from the Motion Picture</b></i> is the <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack" title="Soundtrack">soundtrack</a> to the 2010 <a href="/wiki/Inception" title="Inception">film of the same name</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Nolan" title="Christopher Nolan">Christopher Nolan</a>, released under <a href="/wiki/Reprise_Records" title="Reprise Records">Reprise Records</a> on July 13, 2010. <a href="/wiki/Hans_Zimmer" title="Hans Zimmer">Hans Zimmer</a> scored the film, marking his third collaboration with Nolan following <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_Begins" title="Batman Begins">Batman Begins</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_(film)" title="The Dark Knight (film)">The Dark Knight</a></i>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Inception:_Music_from_the_Motion_Picture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to Zimmer, it is "a very electronic score".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Nolan asked Zimmer to compose and finish the score as he was shooting the film. The composer said, "He wanted to unleash my imagination in the best possible way".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassidy_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cassidy-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> At one point, while composing the score, Zimmer incorporated a guitar sound reminiscent of <a href="/wiki/Ennio_Morricone" title="Ennio Morricone">Ennio Morricone</a> and was interested in having <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Marr" title="Johnny Marr">Johnny Marr</a>, former guitarist in the influential 80s rock band <a href="/wiki/The_Smiths" title="The Smiths">The Smiths</a>, play these parts. Zimmer's reported inspiration was finding a synthesizer track which he had written similar to Marr's guitar style. Nolan agreed with Zimmer's suggestion, and then Zimmer approached Marr, who accepted his offer. Marr spent four 12-hour days working on the score, playing notes written by Zimmer with a 12-string guitar.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Martens_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-Martens-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>For inspiration, Zimmer read <i><a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6del,_Escher,_Bach" title="Gödel, Escher, Bach">Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid</a></i> by <a href="/wiki/Douglas_Hofstadter" title="Douglas Hofstadter">Douglas Hofstadter</a> because it combined "the idea of playfulness in mathematics and playfulness in music".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassidy_2-1"><a href="#cite_note-Cassidy-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Zimmer did not assemble a temp score but "every now and then they would call and say 'we need a little something here.' But that was OK because much of the music pieces aren't that scene specific. They fall into little categories".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cassidy_2-2"><a href="#cite_note-Cassidy-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> While writing the screenplay, Nolan wrote in <a href="/wiki/%C3%89dith_Piaf" title="Édith Piaf">Édith Piaf</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/Non,_je_ne_regrette_rien" title="Non, je ne regrette rien">Non, je ne regrette rien</a>" but almost took it out when he cast <a href="/wiki/Marion_Cotillard" title="Marion Cotillard">Marion Cotillard</a>, who had just completed an Oscar-winning turn as Piaf in the 2007 film <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/La_Vie_en_rose_(film)" title="La Vie en rose (film)">La Vie en rose</a></i>. Zimmer convinced Nolan to keep it in the film and also integrated elements of the song into his score;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Martens_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-Martens-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> in particular, the film's iconic brass instrument fanfare resembles a slowed-down version of the song's instrumentation.</p>
<p>The trailers for the film feature specially composed music by <a href="/wiki/Zack_Hemsey" title="Zack Hemsey">Zack Hemsey</a>, which does not appear on the official soundtrack.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The soundtrack was nominated for several awards, including an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academy_Award" title="Academy Award">Academy Award</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grammy" title="Grammy">Grammy</a> and a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BAFTA" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a>.</p>

<p>The score received mostly positive reception. Regarding Zimmer's previous collaborations with Nolan, Archie Watt of MovieCues said "it's very obvious to anybody who has heard his previous scores for the director that this score is built firmly on their foundations. The compositions are very simple ones, but here the familiar elements of those previous scores are pulled off in a more energetic and complex way. That’s what puts this score a good distance ahead of them". Whilst praising several elements of the score, his review was largely negative, saying "Despite the fact that certain music on this album is inarguably good, the majority of it is rather dull and mundane".</p>
<p>More enthusiastic were James Southall of Movie Wave, who awarded the score five stars, calling it "Zimmer’s finest work in a number of years", and Jim Lochner of Film Score Click Track, who said "Inception is one thrilling and trippy musical ride", awarding the score four and a half stars out of five.</p>
