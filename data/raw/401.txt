<p>The <b>Batplane</b>, later known as the <b>Batwing</b>, is the fictional <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Comic_book" title="Comic book">comic book</a> <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">superhero</a> <a href="/wiki/Batman" title="Batman">Batman</a>. The vehicle was introduced in "Batman Versus The Vampire, I", published in <i><a href="/wiki/Detective_Comics" title="Detective Comics">Detective Comics</a></i> #31 in 1939, a story which saw Batman travel to <a href="/wiki/Continental_Europe" title="Continental Europe">continental Europe</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> In this issue it was referred to as the "Batgyro", and according to <a href="/wiki/Les_Daniels" title="Les Daniels">Les Daniels</a> was "apparently inspired by <a href="/wiki/Igor_Sikorsky" title="Igor Sikorsky">Igor Sikorsky</a>'s first successful helicopter flight" of the same year. Initially based upon either an <a href="/wiki/Autogyro" title="Autogyro">autogyro</a> or <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a>, with a rotor, the Batgyro featured a bat motif at the front. The writers gave the Batgyro the ability to be "parked" in the air by Batman, hovering in such a way as to maintain its position and allow Batman to return.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dc-ency_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-dc-ency-3"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Batgyro was soon replaced by the Batplane, which debuted in <i>Batman</i> #1, and initially featured a machine gun.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The vehicle was now based on a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fixed_wing" title="Fixed wing">fixed wing</a> airplane rather than a helicopter, with a propeller at the front, although a bat motif was still attached to the nose-cone. The Batplane has undergone constant revision since its first appearance, and has even been depicted as having the capability to traverse underwater.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daniels_1995._p78_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Daniels_1995._p78-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> With the launch of the <a href="/wiki/Tim_Burton" title="Tim Burton">Tim Burton</a> directed <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_(1989_film)" title="Batman (1989 film)">Batman</a></i> film of 1989, the Batplane became known as the Batwing, a name which was carried over into the comics. The 2012 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_Rises" title="The Dark Knight Rises">The Dark Knight Rises</a></i> adapted the Batplane to film once again.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batplane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Batman once maintained aircraft in his original <a href="/wiki/Batcave" title="Batcave">Batcave</a>. However, launching these planes so close to <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Manor" title="Wayne Manor">Wayne Manor</a>'s neighboring estates threatened to compromise Batman's secret identity. The Caped Crusader now "borrows" specially-modified jets and helicopters from <a href="/wiki/Wayne_Enterprises#Wayne_Aerospace" title="Wayne Enterprises">Wayne Aerospace's</a> business and military contracts.</p>
<p><b>Batplane I</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> and the Bat-Rocket favored Batman's signature look over sleek <a href="/wiki/Aerodynamics" title="Aerodynamics">aerodynamics</a>.</p>

<p><b>Batplane II</b><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ourworld_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-ourworld-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ourworld_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-ourworld-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> was a retooled Wayne Aeropsace W4 <i>Wraith</i> fighter that married style with substance. In terms of design, it shares features with the Grumman <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F9F_Cougar" title="F9F Cougar">F9F Cougar</a> and McDonnell <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/F-101" title="F-101">F-101</a> Voodoo. When the Batplane is stolen and triplicated by smugglers in <i>Batman</i> #61, Batman and Robin upgrade the Batplane to jet propulsion, adding at least "100 miles per hour" to its maximum speed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Daniels_1995._p78_5-1"><a href="#cite_note-Daniels_1995._p78-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Batplane_III">Batplane III</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batplane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Batplane III">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Batplane III</b> is a modified Wayne Aerospace <i>SlipStream</i> (<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">$</a>46 million sans "extras"). It is detailed to resemble a standard mid-size corporate jet during take-offs and landings. Some of its features and capabilities are as follows:</p>
<ul>
<li>At cruising <a href="/wiki/Altitude" title="Altitude">altitude</a> (35,000-45,000 ft.), telescoping wings retract. Exterior sections of tail and nose-cone envelop cockpit and cabin fuselage for higher altitude pressurization.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Gaining further altitude (45,000-55,000 ft.) delta fins in the tail and snub winglets elongate to increase efficiency and stability as speeds approach <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Supersonic" title="Supersonic">supersonic</a>.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>At ceiling altitudes (55,000-60,000 ft.) "smart" paint on exterior radar-shielding ceramics responds to dropping air pressure and temperature, thus camouflaging the Batplane's exterior to stealthy black.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Avionics" title="Avionics">Avionics</a> include ergonomic "at-a-glance" viewing levels for all electronics and multifunction displays. The breakaway <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_canopy" title="Aircraft canopy">canopy</a> allows for pilot/co-pilot emergency ejection. The reinforced <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acrylic_glass" title="Acrylic glass">acrylic glass</a> canopy windows polarize at stealth altitude.</li>
</ul>
<p>The aircraft's specifications are:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<dl>
<dd>Height: 14.5 ft.</dd>
<dd>Length: 57.7 ft.</dd>
<dd>Wingspan: 47.6 ft. - The wings are protected by a <a href="/wiki/Bleed_air" title="Bleed air">bleed-air</a> anti-icing system.</dd>
<dd>Altitude Ceiling: 60,000 ft.</dd>
<dd>Maximum Speed: 4,400 mph</dd>
<dd>Range: 2,486 n m</dd>
<dd>Take-Off Distance: 5,230 ft.</dd>
<dd>Landing Distance: 2,984 ft.</dd>
<dd>Payload: 2,670 lb.</dd>
<dd>Refueling Time: 7.8 minutes</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_media">In other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batplane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Live-action_films">Live-action films</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batplane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Live-action films">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The Batwing appears in the movies <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_(1989_film)" title="Batman (1989 film)">Batman</a></i>, used to combat <a href="/wiki/Joker_(comics)" title="Joker (comics)">the Joker</a>. When the Joker launches a deadly Smilex gas attack through balloons, Batman came in the Batwing and destroyed the balloons, angering Joker. Batman then fired off several missiles and two miniguns, killing many henchmen but missing Joker, who in turn shot it down with one bullet from his long barreled revolver. After this, it was rebuilt and upgraded by the events of <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_Forever" title="Batman Forever">Batman Forever</a></i> to combat <a href="/wiki/Riddler" title="Riddler">the Riddler</a>. As demonstrated when Riddler shot him down with a powerful green laser, the Batwing actually has an ejection pod which also doubles as a mini sub. Both models of the plane are roughly bat-shaped. In the comic book adaptation of the <i>Batman Forever</i>, the Batwing actually folds up into the Batboat.</p>
<p>An entirely new version of the Batwing appears in the film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dark_Knight_Rises" title="The Dark Knight Rises">The Dark Knight Rises</a></i> referred to simply as the <b>Bat</b>. It is an unconventional, lightweight <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volantor" title="Volantor">volantor</a>-like craft with a ventrally mounted rotor. Developed by <a href="/wiki/Lucius_Fox" title="Lucius Fox">Lucius Fox</a>, the Bat was originally intended for close-quarters urban military operations but instead becomes Batman's new primary vehicle. Fox notes that the Bat's autopilot function does not work but suggests that Bruce might be capable of fixing it. The vehicle is armed with machine guns, cannons and a variation of the EMP device he used chasing Bane's men. Batman first uses the Bat to escape pursuing police officers after being cornered in a dark alley. Immediately after, he uses it to rescue <a href="/wiki/Catwoman" title="Catwoman">Selina Kyle</a> from <a href="/wiki/Bane_(comics)" title="Bane (comics)">Bane</a>'s henchmen. Later in the film's climax, Batman uses the Bat in the fight to retake Gotham City from Bane and he uses the Bat to haul a fusion bomb away from <a href="/wiki/Gotham_City" title="Gotham City">Gotham City</a> where it detonates over the ocean and presumably kills him. Later, while completing work on the Bat, Fox discovers that Wayne had fixed the autopilot function months before and may have escaped before the bomb detonated. In designing the Bat, Nathan Crowley approached it as if it were an actual military project, emphasising the need for it to "fit into the same family" as the <a href="/wiki/Batmobile" title="Batmobile">Tumbler</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Batcycle" title="Batcycle">Batpod</a>. The final version of the Bat takes its design cues from the <a href="/wiki/Harrier_Jump_Jet" title="Harrier Jump Jet">Harrier Jump Jet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bell_Boeing_V-22_Osprey" title="Bell Boeing V-22 Osprey">Bell Boeing V-22 Osprey</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Boeing_AH-64_Apache" title="Boeing AH-64 Apache">Boeing AH-64 Apache</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ProductionNotes_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ProductionNotes-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> Chris Corbould described the Bat's size and shape as presenting a major challenge for filming given <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Nolan" title="Christopher Nolan">Christopher Nolan</a>'s emphasis on practical effects over <a href="/wiki/Computer-generated_imagery" title="Computer-generated imagery">computer-generated imagery</a>. In order to make the Bat "fly", it was variously supported by wires, suspended from cranes and helicopters, and mounted on a purpose-built vehicle with hydraulic controls to simulate movement.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ProductionNotes_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-ProductionNotes-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Design3_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-Design3-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Design6_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Design6-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Animation">Animation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batplane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Animation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Batplane has appeared in the <a href="/wiki/Super_Friends" title="Super Friends">Super Friends</a> cartoon series, where it was revealed to be a <a href="/wiki/Jet_aircraft" title="Jet aircraft">jet</a>, and it was most often referred to as the <b>Batjet</b>, except for in season one. The Batwing also appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_The_Animated_Series" title="Batman: The Animated Series">Batman: The Animated Series</a></i>, shaped like a stylized bat with very long wings that jut out past the "head" of the plane. The Batplane in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Batman_Adventures" title="The New Batman Adventures">The New Batman Adventures</a></i> takes on a smaller, sleeker design shaped like a rocket with a curved wing on each side which also appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League" title="Justice League">Justice League</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Justice_League_Unlimited" title="Justice League Unlimited">Justice League Unlimited</a></i>. In <i><a href="/wiki/Batman_Beyond" title="Batman Beyond">Batman Beyond</a></i>, flying cars are commonplace and thus the Batmobile used by <a href="/wiki/Batman_(Terry_McGinnis)" title="Batman (Terry McGinnis)">Terry McGinnis</a> doubles as a plane. The classic Bat-Plane appears frequently in <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_The_Brave_and_the_Bold" title="Batman: The Brave and the Bold">Batman: The Brave and the Bold</a></i>.</p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/The_Batman_(TV_series)" title="The Batman (TV series)"><i>The Batman</i></a>, the Batwing is created in the episode "Thunder" to defeat <a href="/wiki/Maxie_Zeus" title="Maxie Zeus">Maxie Zeus</a>. In the episode "Artifacts", <a href="/wiki/Dick_Grayson" title="Dick Grayson">Nightwing</a> uses it to save a plane and crashes it on <a href="/wiki/Mr._Freeze" title="Mr. Freeze">Freeze</a>.</p>
<p>The Batwing briefly appeared in the <i><a href="/wiki/Young_Justice_(TV_series)" title="Young Justice (TV series)">Young Justice</a></i> episode "Revelation", where Batman used it to help combat a giant plant monster created by the <a href="/wiki/Injustice_League" title="Injustice League">Injustice League</a>.The Batwing is later seen again in the episode "Coldhearted",where Batman is using it to fly to the flying fortresses and is also comunicating with the team.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_appearances">Other appearances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Batplane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other appearances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lego" title="Lego">Lego</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lego_Batman" title="Lego Batman">Lego Batman</a> line includes one set which features an incarnation of the Batplane, though it goes by the name of "The Batwing" (7782-The Batwing: The Joker's Aerial Assault). The set is featured alongside the <a href="/wiki/Joker_(comics)" title="Joker (comics)">Joker's</a> helicopter.</p>
<p>A vehicle largely reminiscent of the one seen on screen in the 1989 Batman film is also seen in <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Batman:_Revenge_(The_Batman_LEGO_Film)" title="Batman: Revenge (The Batman LEGO Film)">Batman: Revenge</a></i> (2003).</p>
<p>In the Batman-themed <a href="/wiki/Motion_simulator" title="Motion simulator">motion simulator</a> ride <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Batman_Adventure_%E2%80%93_The_Ride_2" title="Batman Adventure – The Ride 2">Batman Adventure – The Ride 2</a> in <a href="/wiki/Warner_Bros._Movie_World" title="Warner Bros. Movie World">Warner Bros. Movie World</a>, riders seemingly follow the Batplane in pursuit of <a href="/wiki/Joker_(comics)" title="Joker (comics)">the Joker</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mr_Freeze" title="Mr Freeze">Mr Freeze</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Catwoman" title="Catwoman">Catwoman</a>.</p>
<p>At <a href="/wiki/Six_Flags_America" title="Six Flags America">Six Flags America</a>, there is a roller coaster named "Batwing" and themed after Batman's personal flight vehicle. As riders enter the station, announcements are played welcoming them to test pilot Wayne Enterprise's newest vehicle. Batman then interrupts and explains that he needs help saving Gotham City from the Joker. Guests then board the train and ride the ride.</p>
<p>In the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_Arkham_Asylum" title="Batman: Arkham Asylum">Batman: Arkham Asylum</a></i>, a Batplane-like vehicle can be seen hanging from the ceiling and covered by a tarp inside the hidden <a href="/wiki/Batcave" title="Batcave">Batcave</a> under Arkham Island. Later in the game, Batman remotely pilots the Batplane to his location and has it drop off one of his gadgets, the Line Launcher. At the end of the game, Batman calls it again to leave for Gotham and apprehend <a href="/wiki/Two-Face" title="Two-Face">Two-Face</a>. When unlocked, the character trophy is called 'Batwing'.</p>
<p>The Batwing makes a brief cameo in <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_Arkham_City" title="Batman: Arkham City">Batman: Arkham City</a></i>, dropping off Batman's <a href="/wiki/Batsuit" title="Batsuit">Batsuit</a> in the beginning of the game. It also makes a cameo in the Batcave DLC.</p>
<p>The Batwing has a more important role in the prequel <i><a href="/wiki/Batman:_Arkham_Origins" title="Batman: Arkham Origins">Batman: Arkham Origins</a></i>, where Batman is able to get to a section of Gotham City quicker. However, in certain areas he needs to dismantle enemy towers that block the summoning.</p>
