Word Origin and History for <span class="u-headword">inception</span> <button class="button-source" type="button">Expand</button>

<div class="source-data">
<div class="def-list">
<span class="dbox-italic">n.</span> <section class="def-pbk">
<div class="def-set">
<p>early 15c., "beginning, starting," from Middle French <span class="dbox-italic">incepcion</span> and directly from Latin <span class="dbox-italic">inceptionem</span> (nominative <span class="dbox-italic">inceptio</span>) "a beginning, undertaking," noun of action from past participle stem of <span class="dbox-italic">incipere</span> "begin, take in hand," from <span class="dbox-italic">in-</span> "in, on" (see <a class="dbox-xref" href="/browse/in-">in-</a> (2)) + <span class="dbox-italic">cipere</span> comb. form of <span class="dbox-italic">capere</span> "take, seize" (see <a class="dbox-xref" href="/browse/capable">capable</a>).</p>
